Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 948: Conspiring Together

Ning Zhuo had not completely refined the Blood Ember Fire Seed, so its usage had a time limit.

"If the related sacrificial texts can be completed, perhaps it can be thoroughly refined."

"However, this is ultimately the Red-Robed Guest's belonging. Acquiring it through trade will probably require paying quite a high price."

After testing it out for a while, Ning Zhuo put away the Blood Ember Fire Seed.

"Battles are never won by relying solely on a single treasure."

"Liu Jinke will definitely have a large number of new methods in the third battle. The Blood Ember Fire Seed can suppress his blood, but it might not necessarily break through all his arrangements. What is truly worth doing has always been making my own combat system more complete."

Focusing only on countering is, to some extent, neglecting the root to pursue the branches.

On this point, Ning Zhuo's thoughts coincidentally aligned with the intentions of the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak who were sponsoring Liu Jinke this time. Both sides were firmly resolved to revolve around their own paths and continue walking down them unceasingly.

Ning Zhuo chose to delve into the inheritance of the [Empty Valley Note Azure Mechanism Cylinder].

After the efforts of generations of inheritors, this mechanical inheritance had been continuously improved, updated, and optimized. It had become extremely complete, with most of its contents reaching a state where no further modifications could be made.

Therefore, the inheritance naturally included mechanical arts.

Empty Valley Echo Transmission Art: It allows mechanical constructs to pass sound commands to one another, drastically reducing the divine sense burden on the main controller. Getting started with this art isn't the hardest, but it heavily emphasizes foundations, requiring the mechanical's sound holes, reeds, and bamboo bones to all be responsive.

Azure Mechanism Reed Vibration Art: It can cause a mechanism's movements to skyrocket with astonishing explosive power. However, the price is increased wear and tear on the reeds. It is suitable for life-and-death struggles but shouldn't be used frequently.

Bamboo Aperture Sound Concealment Art: It allows a sound command to be hidden within a mechanism in advance, triggering automatically upon encountering specific situations. Its greatest difficulty lies in "hiding without leaking, triggering without chaos." If the sound command is too shallow, enemy interference will scatter it; if too deep, it will be hard to trigger, wasting the effort entirely.

Green Bamboo Patching Art: It can temporarily mend a mechanism's skeleton, making it especially suitable for damages to bamboo bones, brackets, or wing blades. This art isn't a true repair; instead, it uses spiritual energy as a temporary substitute, allowing for forced sustained combat capabilities on the battlefield. The price is severe spiritual energy consumption.

The Sounding Node Synchronization Art could unify the rhythms of different mechanisms. The Valley Echo Edge Convergence Art could pool the power of multiple mechanisms into a single strike. The Empty Valley Returning Nest Art could assist in recalling scattered mechanisms. The Myriad Bamboo Heavenly Sound Art was even more of a top-tier amplification method for large-scale mechanical swarms.

Ning Zhuo had started his research early on and was not unfamiliar with the contents of these mechanical arts.

Unable to pry into Liu Jinke's latest intelligence, the youth could only estimate: "In the next battle, Liu Jinke will inevitably continue to target my mechanical bird swarm. The Flock-Startling Whistle is already a precedent, and the Faith Moves Mountains Art has also appeared. The probability of the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak continuing to raise the stakes and target me is extremely high."

"My time is too tight, and my energy is scattered across many areas. It's impossible for me to master all the mechanical arts."

"Sigh, the appearance of the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar disrupted my rhythm. I could have been much more unhurried."

"But now, it's as if I've been dragged onto the battlefield by Chunyangzi, greatly accelerating the pace!"

"One cannot bite off more than they can chew. I will first choose those that most easily yield results."

Ning Zhuo ultimately selected three arts, preparing to practice them.

They were: the Empty Valley Echo Transmission Art, the Bamboo Aperture Sound Concealment Art, and the Green Bamboo Patching Art.

The youth first practiced the Empty Valley Echo Transmission Art.

He released twelve mechanical birds, dividing them into three columns, suspended in the space within the practice formation.

Ning Zhuo's fingertips landed on the strings, and the zither sounded softly. A clear note was sent into the body of the Neck-Spear Crane at the very front. The crane's bamboo bones vibrated, the reed inside its abdomen let out a soft hum, and the sound command was transmitted backward along the resonance between similar mechanical birds.

The first time, the mechanical birds in the rear were all half a beat slow.

The second time, the third Neck-Spear Crane flapped its wings prematurely, nearly crashing into the Sleeve-Dart Harrier beside it.

The third time, the Joint-Hugging Pheasant misheard the lingering echo and landed a breath too early.

Ning Zhuo was not discouraged. He repeatedly calibrated the pitch, beat, and lingering resonance, gradually suppressing the zither's sound to a fine point until only a sliver of empty valley echo remained. That echo entered one mechanism first, then spread to the others, like a wisp of wind passing through a bamboo grove - when the front bamboo sounded, the rear bamboo naturally responded.

By the nineteenth time, the twelve mechanical birds finally turned in unison.

Feathers spread and bamboo bones hummed softly. Although the Neck-Spear Cranes, Sleeve-Dart Harriers, and Joint-Hugging Pheasants were of different models, they achieved the most basic synchronization amidst this transmitted echo.

Ning Zhuo gave a slight nod.

After continuing his practice for a while, he began attempting the Bamboo Aperture Sound Concealment Art.

Ning Zhuo played the zither, invoking syllables, and compressed a "side-sweeping dodge" sound command into it.

After deploying the mechanism, he deliberately interfered with chaotic sounds, then commanded a small mechanical puppet to launch a surprise attack from the side.

The Sleeve-Dart Falcon's flight stalled momentarily, as if disrupted by the chaotic sounds.

But just as the mechanical puppet lunged close, the bamboo aperture in its abdomen suddenly emitted a soft hum. The concealed sound triggered automatically. The Sleeve-Dart Falcon swept past at a slanted angle, grazing right past the puppet's blade. The iron plate of its tail feathers struck backward, sparking a string of fire against the puppet's shoulder.

As Ning Zhuo practiced, he pondered: "This mechanical art heavily tests a cultivator's combat experience. The richer the experience and the stronger the combat intuition, the better one can set up methods in advance to prevent an enemy's powerful moves. Quite interesting!"

Lastly was the Green Bamboo Patching Art.

Ning Zhuo intentionally broke the leg bone of a Joint-Hugging Pheasant and forced it to run. The pheasant had only taken three steps when crisp cracking sounds came from the broken bone, and its entire posture slanted heavily.

Ning Zhuo lifted a finger and pointed. A wisp of azure spiritual energy sprouted like a bamboo shoot, rapidly extending from the fracture to condense into a semi-transparent temporary bamboo bone. That bamboo bone wasn't hard, but it was extremely resilient, propping up the upper and lower mechanism frames and allowing the Joint-Hugging Pheasant to regain its footing.

The next moment, the Joint-Hugging Pheasant sprang forward. Although its posture was slightly awkward, it could still complete a charge.

Ning Zhuo continuously practiced the three arts until the night grew deep.

In the martial demonstration room, the mechanical birds rose and fell endlessly. The humming of bamboo, the zither's notes, and the vibrations of the reeds intertwined, gradually forming a bit of the true intent of the Empty Valley inheritance.

Ning Zhuo stood among them, his white robes shifting slightly. The sacrificial runes on his palms were as silent as ash, but the bright light in his eyes did not diminish.

Meanwhile, a negotiation had reached its final critical juncture.

The gazes of the two Nascent Soul cultivators, Chunyangzi and Nine Fires Dragon Lord, met.

One was upright and majestic; the other carried profound dragon might.

Nine Fires Dragon Lord suddenly chuckled. "You're quite willing to part with things."

Chunyangzi said calmly, "What must be given up, will be given up. If one cannot bear to part with these branches and leaves, how can one take the entire treasure land?"

Nine Fires Dragon Lord nodded. "Fine, I will help you with this."

Chunyangzi gave a slight nod.

Convincing the Nine Fires Dragon Lord was not difficult for him. Even before making the trip, he was confident.

The reason was simple: during the minor trial to repair the Naming Fire Furnace, Nine Fires Dragon Lord had invested too much and incurred a massive debt. Furthermore, what he owed was to the behind-the-scenes boss of the black market, a debt he dared not refuse to repay.

Seizing upon this point, Chunyangzi expressed his willingness to give Nine Fires Dragon Lord a vast amount of wealth to help him immediately alleviate the pressure of repayment.

Once this condition was laid out, it immediately tempted the Nine Fires Dragon Lord endlessly.

Ultimately, after some haggling, Chunyangzi threw in a few extra incentives, making Nine Fires Dragon Lord feel like he had made quite a profit, thus finally achieving his goal of convincing this demon cultivator.

"Compared to the Dragon Lord, convincing the Red-Robed Guest is much harder. I wonder how Ning Zhuo is faring on his end?"

A heavy stone still hung unresolved in Chunyangzi's heart.

Therefore, after leaving Nine Fires Dragon Lord's cave manor, he didn't return to his own residence but went straight to Ning Zhuo's place.

Ning Zhuo had long anticipated that Chunyangzi would come looking for him. Showing no surprise and keeping no suspense, he informed him of his success the moment they met.

Hearing this, Chunyangzi's gaze immediately fell upon Ning Zhuo, involuntarily lingering for two extra breaths of time.

He knew the Red-Robed Guest harbored a deep-seated hatred for him, and his temperament was unruly and hard to tame. If he had gone to persuade him personally, things would likely have gone wrong. Yet not only did Ning Zhuo succeed, but judging from his expression, it wasn't a forced submission - he had genuinely made the Red-Robed Guest accept the matter.

This made it even more extraordinary!

In his heart, Chunyangzi's evaluation of Ning Zhuo quietly rose another level.

"You have solved a major problem for me," Chunyangzi said, his tone a bit softer than usual. "I will remember this favor."

Ning Zhuo did not claim the credit, cupping his fists as he said, "This junior also did it for the Naming Stronghold."

Chunyangzi chuckled softly, picked up his teacup, and took a sip. The heavy stone in his heart had already safely fallen.

Becoming much more relaxed, he looked at Ning Zhuo and suddenly asked, "Tell me, how should we contest for the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar next?"

This wasn't a simple request for advice.

It was more like a test.

Ning Zhuo's fingertips gently traced the rim of his cup. The lamplight reflected in his eyes as he smiled faintly: "There is only one key point."

Chunyangzi's brows twitched slightly. "What key point?"

But Ning Zhuo didn't give the answer immediately.

He blinked. "Senior, why don't we each write a single character in our palms and then reveal them at the same time? If our thoughts align, it will save us a lot of breath."

Chunyangzi was slightly taken aback, then laughed heartily. "Interesting."

Of course, it wasn't the game that was interesting, but rather the fact that Ning Zhuo used this method to quietly elevate his own status, attempting to speak with him on equal footing.

If Ning Zhuo had just spoken the answer, it would have validated Chunyangzi's intention of "testing" the youth, cementing the situation where Chunyangzi held the higher status.

This could be considered a minor clash of wits.

Chunyangzi and Ning Zhuo then each wrote their key character in their palms.

The two extended their palms simultaneously.

Written on Chunyangzi's palm was the character: Fast!

Written on Ning Zhuo's palm was the character: Direct.

Their gazes met, and they both smiled at the same time.

The characters were different, but their meanings were virtually identical.

'Fast' meant giving no time for the Territory Expansion Alliance, White Cloud Village, and the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak to slowly squabble and lay out their plans.

'Direct' meant no detours, no probing, no building up momentum or sending out countless diplomatic notes in advance. Instead, it meant striking straight at the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar, seizing the vital point with the momentum of a thunderbolt.

To be slow was to invite unexpected changes.

To detour was to lose the opportunity.

Now that the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar had been exposed, it was no longer a secret treasure land unknown to the world.

The Territory Expansion Alliance had operated it for many years, White Cloud Village held the rightful title, the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak lurked in the shadows, and even the main peaks and halls backing them might reach out their hands to intervene.

If the Naming Stronghold still wanted to plot unhurriedly, waiting for them to sort out the rightful claims, transfer personnel, and set up new formations, it would be equal to handing over the best sliver of opportunity on a silver platter.

And now, the Naming Stronghold boasted five Nascent Soul cultivators, did not lack elite experts among the Golden Cores, and among the Foundation Establishment cultivators, there were geniuses like Ning Zhuo, Situ Xing, Shen Xi, Lin Jinglong, and Mu Yuehua, all of whom could rival Golden Cores. They could absolutely be called an army of strong soldiers and sturdy steeds!

With such strength, they should strike fast and attack fiercely, going straight to the point.

Chunyangzi withdrew his palm, the appreciation in his gaze toward Ning Zhuo no longer concealed. "What an excellent 'Direct'."

Ning Zhuo also replied, "Senior's 'Fast' shows even more spirit."

Chunyangzi said, "Since we must be fast and direct, the vanguard is the most important. If the first strike fails to pierce through, no matter how much planning follows, it will all be empty talk."

Ning Zhuo nodded.

The two fell silent simultaneously.

The tea steam in the study curled past the lamplight like a fine line of cloud.

Who should take on the role of the vanguard?

A name surfaced in Chunyangzi's heart.

A name also surfaced in Ning Zhuo's heart.

They didn't speak it aloud immediately, but instead glanced at each other.

Seeing the calm certainty in Ning Zhuo's eyes, Chunyangzi's heart suddenly stirred, and a feeling welled up - this youth before him once again shared his exact thoughts.

Sure enough, when the two compared notes, both pointed to the same person - Tan Zhu!

Chunyangzi laughed heartily and took the initiative to extend his palm.

Knowing his cue, Ning Zhuo immediately reached out as well, high-fiving Chunyangzi with a smile.

Chunyangzi laughed for a moment, then restrained his expression. Staring fixedly at Ning Zhuo with piercing eyes, he suddenly said, "Ning Zhuo, if you weren't in the mid-Foundation Establishment Stage, I would have thought you had wielded power in a great orthodox sect for a hundred years."

Ning Zhuo smiled. "This junior just owes too many debts, so I have no choice but to think a little more."

Chunyangzi: "Shall we go together?"

Ning Zhuo nodded: "I will follow in Senior's wake."

The stone cave Tan Zhu stayed in was very empty.

There were no curios, no flowers or plants, and no superfluous furnishings. There was only a black stone meditation mat and a bronze lamp that had been burning for who knew how long. Its faint flame illuminated the four walls, making them look as pale as sickly bones. In the corner sat several medicine jars, their mouths sealed with talisman paper, yet a bitter scent still slowly seeped out.

Tan Zhu sat cross-legged on the mat.

His figure was gaunt, his face withered and pallid, and his eye sockets slightly sunken. There were already large patches of ashen white in his black hair. A purple-black robe was draped over his body, empty and loose, as if he were wearing a layer of smoke that was about to disperse. He didn't look like the fierce demonic path cultivator who had just thrown White Cloud Village's Flowing Cloud Ferry into chaos and left several Nascent Soul cultivators completely helpless. Instead, he looked more like an old man suffering from a prolonged illness, quietly waiting for his time to end.

But the more he appeared this way, the more it chilled one's heart.

The most terrifying aspect of the Poison Annihilation Wanderer, Tan Zhu, was not his arrogance, but his tranquility.

It was as if life and death, gain and loss, fame, and enmity had all faded from his eyes. All that remained was a sliver of obsession buried who knew where, still supporting him to this day.

Tan Zhu raised his eyes to look at Chunyangzi, then at Ning Zhuo. "You two arrived a bit slower than I expected."

Chunyangzi's expression did not change, but a ripple swiftly flashed through the depths of Ning Zhuo's eyes.

Chunyangzi found a nearby rock and slowly sat down, his bearing still upright and majestic. "Since Daoist Tan knew we would come, you must also know our purpose."

Tan Zhu nodded. "The Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar."

Thoughts couldn't help but surge in Ning Zhuo's mind.

Tan Zhu polluted White Cloud Village's Flowing Cloud Ferry. The several Nascent Soul cultivators of White Cloud Village couldn't resolve it and could only hold their noses and beg him. When Tan Zhu withdrew his poison smoke, he just so happened to break the cloud qi circulation, causing the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar to be exposed.

Now looking at Tan Zhu's expression, Ning Zhuo couldn't help but further verify his suspicion - Tan Zhu's actions were not a coincidence!

Chunyangzi looked at Tan Zhu and got straight to the point. "The Naming Stronghold wishes to contest for the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar and requires a vanguard."

Tan Zhu said calmly, "I will go."

He didn't ask for compensation.

He didn't bargain.

He didn't even ask about the plan.

He agreed far too readily!

It was so fast that neither Chunyangzi nor Ning Zhuo showed any trace of joy; instead, their hearts sank simultaneously.

Chunyangzi's gaze condensed slightly.

Ning Zhuo gently lowered his eyelids, concealing the contemplation in his eyes.

After a moment of thought, Ning Zhuo asked warmly, "Senior Tan, won't you ask a single question?"

Tan Zhu looked at him. His gaze was very faint, yet not at all murky. "Ask what?"

"Ask about the risks of this trip, ask what the Naming Stronghold can give Senior, and ask what Senior can obtain after taking action."

Tan Zhu fell silent for a moment, then suddenly smiled.

His smile was very light; it couldn't even really be called a smile, just a slight tug at the corners of his lips.

"My lifespan is nearing its end. I have no descendants, and no attachments. To me, risks are no longer something worth worrying about. As for what I gain..."

He slowly raised his hand. A wisp of purple-black smoke at his fingertips condensed into a thin thread before quickly dispersing.

"Since I have joined the Naming Stronghold, I will naturally act on its behalf."
