Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 928: Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest

"Tie Kuang's envoy?" Upon hearing this, Ning Zhuo gently rubbed the edge of his teacup with his fingertips, his gaze flickering.

Mu Yuehua tactfully stood up: "Since Young Master has important matters, I will not disturb you."

Ning Zhuo looked up and stood to see her out: "I have noted your words today. As for Daoist Mu's request, the Nanming Stronghold will handle it according to the rules."

Mu Yuehua nodded slightly. The hem of her moon-white skirt brushed over the threshold, dissipating from Ning Zhuo's line of sight like a wisp of cold radiance.

Ning Zhuo had Gongsun Yan see the guest out.

Ning Zhuo, meanwhile, paced in the study, pondering the purpose of the envoy sent by Tie Kuang.

Only thin wisps of tea vapor remained in the study. A sliver of lamplight flickered, casting his elongated shadow.

Before long, the envoy entered.

He was a deacon of the Artifact Refining Hall. Looking to be in his forties with a calm countenance, his robes were not luxurious but exceptionally well-tailored. An emblem of a black iron furnace was embroidered on his cuffs. The furnace emblem did not glow with golden light, yet it possessed a profound heaviness, as if an iron seal had been pressed onto the garment.

Stepping into the study, he did not rush to speak. Instead, he first offered a solemn bow to Ning Zhuo.

His etiquette was impeccable and meticulously performed.

"On the orders of the Hall Master, I greet Young Master Ning." The envoy was at the Golden Core-level, yet before Ning Zhuo, who was in the mid Foundation Establishment Stage, he referred to himself with the humble "I".

Ning Zhuo understood. It was because of his battle with Liu Jinke that the public had confirmed he possessed Golden Core battle power.

This made cultivators of the Golden Core-level truly treat Ning Zhuo as an equal.

Ning Zhuo returned the greeting and invited him to sit.

The envoy did not sit immediately. Instead, he took a small black iron token from his sleeve, held it in both hands, and placed it on the desk.

The front of the token was engraved with the words "Artifact Refining Hall," while the back featured a fire furnace. The furnace's patterns were ancient and rugged, lacking the brilliant luster of pearls or jade, but it sat on the desk like a block of heavy iron, causing the atmosphere in the study to feel a bit heavier.

This was the personal token of the Master of the Artifact Refining Hall.

Tie Kuang had not arrived, but Tie Kuang's imposing aura had.

Tie Kuang was the master of one of the Myriad Manifestations Sect's sixteen halls. In terms of status and position, he was on equal footing with Zhong Dao of the Demon Subjugation Hall, absolutely incomparable to ordinary Golden Core or Foundation Establishment cultivators.

Although Ning Zhuo had established the Nanming Stronghold today and his prestige had skyrocketed, in terms of actual power within the Sect, he absolutely could not be mentioned in the same breath as such figures.

The envoy sat down, his posture perfectly upright.

He spoke, "The Hall Master ordered me to inform Young Master. The Naming Fire Furnace still has its final twenty percent of damage remaining. If Young Master is willing, the Hall Master is willing to take a step back and no longer mention the previous proposal regarding the ownership of twenty percent of the Naming Fire Furnace."

Ning Zhuo's expression remained unmoved.

The envoy continued, "The Hall Master is willing to personally preside over the restoration. The personnel, earth fire, furnace rooms, and precious materials of the Artifact Refining Hall can all be mobilized. As for the expenses required for the final twenty percent - whether it be Spirit Stones, fire materials, formation materials, or patching materials for the furnace wall - the Hall Master is willing to bear them all alone."

The study fell silent for a moment.

The final twenty percent damage of the Naming Fire Furnace was currently the heaviest stone pressing on the Nanming Stronghold. With Tie Kuang willing to shoulder the expenses alone, if it were anyone else, their heart would likely be moved on the spot.

But Ning Zhuo simply lowered his eyes to look at his tea.

The tea broth rippled slightly, reflecting a speck of lamplight.

Tie Kuang's words seemed like a concession, but they were actually an advance.

He did not want twenty percent ownership of the Naming Fire Furnace, yet he wanted to independently bear the final twenty percent of the restoration. Once this was accomplished, a massive portion of the credit for the complete restoration of the Naming Fire Furnace would be attributed to Tie Kuang's name.

By then, Tie Kuang would no longer be an ordinary creditor, but the greatest contributor to the restoration of the Naming Fire Furnace.

And also Ning Zhuo's largest creditor.

This was not assistance.

This was another way to take control of the Nanming Stronghold!

Ning Zhuo recalled when Tie Kuang had sought him out for negotiations after the initial test of the furnace. At that time, Tie Kuang was the first to see through his weakness, immediately pouncing like a fierce beast catching the scent of blood. This man appeared gruff and unrefined, but he actually possessed significant political acumen. How could someone who managed to sit on the throne of the Artifact Refining Hall Master simply be a brute who only knew how to swing a hammer and strike iron?

Furthermore, Tie Kuang had also held the matter of the secret contract over his head at that time.

During that encounter, Tie Kuang's words had been full of thinly veiled threats.

Ning Zhuo's impression of Tie Kuang had never been that of a "crude man."

Today, Pure Yangzi, Nine Fires Dragon Lord, and the Red-Robed Guest vying for seats still allowed the cultivators of the Nanming Stronghold to keep each other in check. If Tie Kuang leveraged the momentum of the Artifact Refining Hall to suppress everyone in one fell swoop, then Ning Zhuo's influence within the Nanming Stronghold would undoubtedly be greatly weakened.

How could Ning Zhuo easily agree?

He slowly put down his teacup and said, "I appreciate Hall Master Tie's good intentions."

The envoy, with a serious expression, listened quietly.

Ning Zhuo said, "However, at the beginning of the Nanming Stronghold's alliance, all the creditors are present, and the rules have just been established. The matter of restoring the furnace concerns both the foundation of the Nanming Stronghold and everyone's creditor rights. It is very difficult for a single party to monopolize it."

"If Hall Master Tie is willing to assist, I naturally welcome it. But if the Artifact Refining Hall wishes to solely take on the final twenty percent, I'm afraid everyone from top to bottom in the Nanming Stronghold will not agree, and I cannot make this decision on my own right now."

Ning Zhuo's tone was gentle, and his phrasing was highly respectful.

But the intent of refusal was crystal clear.

The clever point was that Ning Zhuo brought out the various cultivators of the Nanming Stronghold. The weight of five Nascent Soul-level figures and several Golden Core experts was immense!

Meanwhile, he did not explicitly state his own refusal, greatly alleviating the conflict between himself and Tie Kuang.

The envoy was not surprised.

He nodded slightly and said, "Before I came, the Hall Master said that Young Master would likely not agree."

Ning Zhuo's eyebrows twitched slightly.

The envoy said, "The Hall Master said that Young Master established the alliance and stronghold today, determined the seating based on debt, and constrained the cultivators with rules. You may appear young, but in truth, you are deeply versed in the methods of the Righteous Path. Such a person would never easily hand over the final twenty percent of the Naming Fire Furnace to the Artifact Refining Hall on a silver platter."

He paused, a hint of respect entering his tone.

"The Hall Master also said that he previously underestimated Young Master."

Ning Zhuo did not reply, his heart sinking slightly.

Since Tie Kuang had seen through this layer yet still dispatched an envoy, it meant his true message was yet to come.

Sure enough, the envoy took out a red-patterned Jade Slip from his sleeve.

The Jade Slip was entirely fiery red, with a thread of golden-red light slowly swimming within it, like a curled-up nestling.

"The Hall Master has another strategy to propose to Young Master."

Ning Zhuo: "Please speak."

The envoy: "Since the Nanming Stronghold intends to charge the peak, it cannot solely focus on establishing a name; it must also consider gaining a foothold. On Flowing Cloud Peak, the Cloud Layers are the densest. The mountain exhales and inhales cloud qi, constantly generating countless cloud phenomena year-round. The various small hilltops are entangled with one another, with old powers and new organizations intricately interlocked. If you merely occupy an ordinary cave manor, even if you successfully plant your flag, it will be difficult to maintain operations in the long run."

"It would be one thing for other minor factions to join Flowing Cloud Peak, but today, the Nanming Stronghold possesses the battle power of five Nascent Souls! For the Nanming Stronghold to settle on Flowing Cloud Peak, it would be a battle between a crossing dragon and a nest of local snakes. If mishandled, it might draw a siege from various factions, which would be extremely disastrous and could even cause the entire stronghold to fall apart."

Ning Zhuo wore a solemn expression.

These words hit the nail on the head, striking precisely at the worries hidden deep in his heart.

The Nanming Stronghold was formidable, but having just been established, it was entirely ununited and possessed very poor stress tolerance. It was best suited for fighting battles where they had the upper hand.

Today's alliance was a success, but it had also greatly aroused the vigilance of the large and small factions on Flowing Cloud Peak. Everyone knew that the Nanming Stronghold posed a massive threat. Every faction, and even every itinerant cultivator, would worry that the Nanming Stronghold might target their territory and drive them out!

Such a political situation was actually quite severe.

The envoy infused spiritual energy into the Jade Slip.

The Jade Slip emitted a faint white light, projecting a partial map of Flowing Cloud Peak onto the desk.

Amidst the swirling clouds and mist, a fire vein within the mountain's belly was marked. That place was neither the summit nor the area with the most abundant spiritual energy, but rather located on the southwest side of Flowing Cloud Peak, behind a sheer cliff. Outside the cliff, the Cloud Layer was extremely thick, hanging down like a curtain year-round; inside the cliff, however, a faint red light could be seen, as if an unextinguished ember lay hidden deep within the clouds and mist.

The envoy said, "This place is named Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest."

Ning Zhuo concentrated his gaze on it.

The envoy continued, "Flowing Cloud Peak has dense Cloud Layers. The mountain exhales and inhales cloud qi, giving rise to countless cloud phenomena year-round. This particular location is special: the cloud qi presses downward while the earth fire surges upward. The two clash but do not disperse, instead nurturing a warm fire nest within the mountain's belly."

"Within the nest, there are three things that are most suitable for the Naming Fire Furnace."

"The first is Warm Cloud Marrow."

"This item is not a fierce fire material, but rather a substance to nourish artifact spirits. In the past, the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit was utterly terrified by the furnace explosion. Although Young Master has pacified and forcefully controlled it, traces of its terror still remain. When Warm Cloud Marrow enters the furnace, it doesn't mend the furnace walls, but rather nourishes the furnace spirit. It can soothe the artifact spirit's mind from disturbance, reduce its fear, and help it grow close to the furnace's main body once again."

Ning Zhuo's gaze shifted.

He was the most aware of the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit's condition. The latter was merely being forcefully controlled by the Life Hanging by a Thread Divine Ability. Its terrified and restless state was far more severe than outsiders guessed. If he could obtain this treasure, it would resolve this problem. It was indeed a good thing.

The envoy added, "The second is Vermilion Feather Flame-Generating Sand."

"This sand is produced on the inner walls of the Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest. It is colored like crimson feathers, and its fire attribute is not volatile; it primarily promotes growth and continuation. The Vermilion Bird belongs to fire, but it is not a fire of mere destruction. For the final twenty percent of the Naming Fire Furnace, if overbearing and fierce fire materials are still used to forcefully mend it, the furnace body might be complete, but the artifact spirit might not feel at ease. The Vermilion Feather Flame-Generating Sand can allow severed fire veins to slowly regenerate, making the mended marks connect seamlessly like the veins of a feather, avoiding the drawbacks of a forced amalgamation."

Ning Zhuo nodded slightly.

The Vermilion Bird governed growth, representing a vigorous upward vitality.

If this sand was truly as the envoy described, it was indeed perfectly suited for the Naming Fire Furnace.

The envoy spoke again, "The third is the Crimson Paulownia Root."

"This comes from an inverted Fire Paulownia tree. Its roots delve deep into the boundary between cloud and fire, and the tips of the roots look like phoenix claws, entwined in crimson rosy clouds."

"This root can be refined into the array patterns at the bottom of the furnace, helping the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit establish a sense of returning to its nest. The furnace is its body, and the furnace core is its nest. The Vermilion Bird artifact spirit currently fears fire, fears the furnace, and fears the past event of the furnace explosion. If it lacks the feeling of returning to its nest, even if it can ignite the Nanming Vermilion Bird Fire in the future, it will inevitably suffer from repeated palpitations of terror."

The envoy withdrew his hand and said, "The Hall Master said that the difficulty of the final twenty percent of the Naming Fire Furnace lies not merely in materials. If it were just about patching the furnace body, the Artifact Refining Hall has plenty of methods. What is truly difficult is achieving the unification of the three: the furnace body, the fire veins, and the artifact spirit."

"The Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest happens to have these three treasures, which can alleviate this worry, allowing the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit to find true peace and the Naming Fire Furnace to be completely restored."

Ning Zhuo remained silent.

He sighed in his heart: "This Tie Kuang is truly formidable. Gruff on the outside, but he actually understands human nature profoundly, just as a Grandmaster artifact refiner masters the heat of the fire. The moment the alliance was successfully formed today, he immediately changed his tactics, specifically targeting my current situation."

Ning Zhuo was indeed moved.

This location could not only aid in the restoration of the final twenty percent of the Naming Fire Furnace, but it would also allow the Nanming Stronghold to occupy a truly valuable foundation on Flowing Cloud Peak. If managed properly, the Nanming Stronghold would not merely be planting a flag temporarily, but could support its people with resources, repair the furnace using the fire veins, and secure a lasting foothold relying on the geographical advantage.

But Tie Kuang would inevitably use this to insert himself into the situation.

If the Nanming Stronghold obtained this territory because of Tie Kuang's scheme, then in the future, the three matters of repairing the furnace, defending the territory, and mining materials would never be able to bypass the Artifact Refining Hall.

Ning Zhuo picked up his teacup and took a light sip.

The tea had grown slightly cold.

He put down the teacup and said, "What Hall Master Tie has said carries immense weight."

The envoy bowed his head: "The Hall Master also said that with Young Master's brilliance, you would certainly be able to see the weight of this clearly!"

On the surface, these words seemed like appreciation, but in reality, they implied that Tie Kuang had figured Ning Zhuo out completely, naturally placing himself one level higher than Ning Zhuo.

Ning Zhuo looked at the envoy, smiled lightly, and probed, "A treasured land like the Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest is probably not easy to claim, is it?"

The envoy said frankly, "That is indeed the case. The Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest is not ownerless land; it has been managed by a hidden faction for many years. As for exactly who it is, it is inconvenient for the Hall Master to explicitly state it here."

The envoy raised his hand, retracting the cloud map back into the Jade Slip.

"The Hall Master ordered me to pass this on to Young Master: He has secret allies who can help the Nanming Stronghold enter the board and reduce the resistance of charging the peak. If Young Master is willing to discuss further with the Hall Master, he can help the Nanming Stronghold place the first piece in the matter of the Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest, as a show of sincerity for our cooperation."

Having finished speaking, the envoy fixed his gaze on Ning Zhuo and said no more.

Ning Zhuo pondered for a long time before finally saying, "I already understand Hall Master Tie's intentions."

The envoy asked, "Does Young Master have a reply?"

Ning Zhuo let out a long sigh and said, "The matter of the Flame-Dwelling Cloud Nest is of massive consequence. I cannot make a decision based on just a map and a few words. Please tell Hall Master Tie that Ning Zhuo will give it thorough consideration."

The envoy did not rush him, merely nodding. "The Hall Master said that if Young Master agreed on the spot, you wouldn't be Ning Zhuo."

Ning Zhuo chuckled involuntarily.

The envoy stood up and retrieved the small black iron token, but left the cloud-patterned Jade Slip on the desk.

"The Hall Master gifts this map to Young Master. As for what is true, false, real, or illusory, Young Master can investigate and verify for yourself."

Ning Zhuo stood to see him out.

Not long after, a cultivator concealed his presence and secretly entered the Cave Manor to meet with Ning Zhuo.

It was none other than Tu Yuanzi.

Ning Zhuo harbored quite a few reservations about this mysterious Nascent Soul.

However, Tu Yuanzi, who appeared as a youth in yellow robes, was extremely straightforward, directly handing Ning Zhuo a letter of introduction.

The letter was from Cang Yazi.

After Ning Zhuo read it, the massive boulder in his heart dropped. Revealing a sincere smile, he stepped forward, reached out his arms, and firmly grasped Tu Yuanzi's shoulders: "Daoist Tu Yuanzi, so we are on the same side after all!"

Tu Yuanzi was taken aback by Ning Zhuo's actions, but he was quickly infected by the latter's abundant enthusiasm and couldn't help but smile as well. "Yes, my master sent me to help you."

"I will also help you fulfill your wish!" Ning Zhuo said. "I know that Senior Cang Yazi wants to use the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit for Spirit Awakening. If it were me and I had a companion who hadn't taken form yet, I would be extremely anxious and worried too."

"If they had spent day and night with me for many years like that, they would be just like family!"

Tu Yuanzi nodded heavily. "Yes, exactly, they are my family. Daoist Ning Zhuo, you really are different from most cultivators. My master did not misjudge you!"

Ning Zhuo laughed heartily, "Hahaha."

"Come." He took Tu Yuanzi's hand and led him to a seat. "What kind of tea do you like to drink?"

Tu Yuanzi stared blankly for a moment. "What kind of tea tastes good?"

Cang Yazi lived in isolation and had no particular hobbies regarding food and drink. Furthermore, Tu Yuanzi was not originally a cultivator and usually did not come into contact with such things.

Ning Zhuo smiled and said, "That depends on personal taste. But there is no rush; from now on, we can drink every kind of tea available to buy, and then you'll know, Tu Yuanzi."

"Oh, by the way, do you drink alcohol or not?"

Tu Yuanzi was taken aback again. "I know of alcohol. But I don't know what kind of alcohol tastes good."

Ning Zhuo laughed heartily once again, patting Tu Yuanzi on the shoulder. "No problem, I'll treat you to some right now!"
