Chapter 858: Wu An's Hunt

The ninth level of the Cloud Prison.

The blood had already coagulated, corpses were being carried away, and the shattered Cloud Astral Rock walls were being repaired.

The guards cleaned up the battlefield in twos and threes; some were silent, some whispered, and some were still trembling slightly.

Zhong Dao stood in place with his hands behind his back.

A burly cultivator with a rugged face and reeking of alcohol ran over in a panic, dropping straight to his knees in front of Zhong Dao.

It was none other than the Cloud Prison Commander, Wu An.

Wu An pressed his forehead to the ground, kowtowing so hard it made loud thuds.

"Hall Master, your subordinate deserves death! Your subordinate deserves death! Last night, your subordinate drank too much and was dead drunk. By the time I woke up... everything was already... already..."

Zhong Dao looked down at him and said coldly, "Thirty-seven guards died in the Cloud Prison last night, and one hundred and fifty-two were injured. What do you have to say for yourself?"

Wu An's mouth opened and closed a few times, unable to utter a word of defense.

Zhong Dao continued, "Your drunkenness ruined matters, leading to the escape of over twenty heavy-duty prisoners from the ninth level of the Cloud Prison, resulting in heavy casualties among the guards, and causing our Myriad Manifestations Sect to lose tremendous face at the Flying Cloud Assembly. According to the rules of the Demon Subjugation Hall, how should this be dealt with?"

Wu An closed his eyes, his voice hoarse. "It warrants... execution."

Zhong Dao nodded. "Good. Men!"

As soon as the words left his mouth, people came forward to plead for mercy.

"Lord Hall Master!"

"Lord Hall Master, please don't."

"Lord Wu An is usually diligent and conscientious. It's just that this time, we have been completely renovating the Cloud Prison and coordinating the Formations, which was simply too exhausting."

"Your subordinates... beg the Hall Master to be lenient."

More and more guard cultivators stepped forward to plead. Some were covered in blood and filth, and some were heavily injured, yet they all struggled to their feet.

It was evident just how deeply Wu An had won their hearts.

Zhong Dao's gaze was icy as he swept it across the room. "You all want to plead for him?"

The majority of the guard cultivators fell dead silent, showing signs of shrinking back, but they ultimately stood their ground without moving.

Among them, someone gritted their teeth. "Commander Wu... is indeed at fault. But regarding last night's events, we subordinates also bear responsibility. The Formation had flaws that we failed to discover; the patrols had loopholes that we didn't notice; our complete lack of vigilance makes us even more to blame."

Someone else chimed in, "If anyone deserves death, we subordinates... all deserve death!"

"This subordinate as well!"

"This subordinate as well!"

"We implore the Hall Master to spare our Commander's life!"

In an instant, people knelt all over outside the door, every one of them confessing their sins and begging for mercy.

Zhong Dao's gaze was like a blade. "Do you think that begging for mercy will absolve him of his crimes?"

Someone gathered their courage. "Hall Master, although Commander Wu is fond of drinking, he treats us subordinates well on normal days and has always been dedicated to his duties. We subordinates are also at fault for last night's events; we cannot let Commander Wu shoulder this alone."

He suddenly knelt and kowtowed. "We subordinates are willing to share the blame and punishment for Commander Wu!"

Immediately, the surrounding cultivators followed suit.

"We subordinates are willing!"

"We subordinates are willing!"

Zhong Dao remained silent for a moment before suddenly letting out a cold snort. "You are quite united."

He looked at Wu An. The latter knelt on the ground, tears streaming down his face, his lips trembling so much he couldn't speak.

Zhong Dao said slowly, "Since so many people are pleading for you, I will give you a chance."

Wu An's head snapped up.

Zhong Dao said coldly, "The death penalty can be avoided, but punishment is inescapable. Lead a squad of men to hunt down those escaped Demonic Cultivators. Within three days, if you can capture all of them, dead or alive, I will spare your life. If you cannot, you will be executed!"

The many cultivators who had pleaded for mercy widened their eyes.

Someone grew anxious and wanted to protest, "In a mere three days, how can we possibly capture everyone?"

This seemed like a chance to atone for his crimes, but in reality, it was still a near-certain death sentence.

Wu An gritted his teeth tightly and, with a face full of shame, kowtowed heavily. "Your subordinate obeys!"

Seeing this performance, Zhong Dao was inwardly satisfied and gave a slight nod. "Get lost."

Wu An hurriedly took his leave.

A moment later, Wu An led a group of Golden Core elites from the Cloud Prison, walking hurriedly out of the facility.

Every single person wore a grave expression.

"Three days is extremely limited; we must race against the clock!"

"But this is simply an impossible mission!!"

"There's still hope. I looked up information regarding the Lord of the Infernal Rift and discovered that the spatial rifts the Demonic Cultivators used yesterday teleport them randomly and over a limited distance. Our Myriad Manifestations Sect's main mountain gate is hidden within the sea of clouds. If they teleported outside and directly entered the sea of clouds, it would mean certain death. Therefore, the survivors can only cower within our Sect's main mountain gate, lingering on their last breath and hiding desperately."

"That is true..."

"But even so, how do we find these Demonic Cultivators? Even if we can find them, with this much time having passed, given the abilities and cunning of these Demonic Cultivators, they will certainly do everything in their power to cause massive trouble for us. Once we start a battle within the Sect, I'm afraid the commotion will be even greater, the casualties will be higher, and it will smear even more mud on the Sect's reputation!"

"Sigh!!!"

The cultivators looked at one another and simultaneously let out deep sighs, all feeling that their future was bleak.

"Perhaps, Lord Hall Master..." A cultivator realized something and looked at Wu An, swaying unsteadily.

Wu An turned to look at the man and gave a slight nod. "I know what you want to say. With such a mess happening in the Cloud Prison, Lord Zhong Dao must clean it up. But cleaning up this mess would likely have an even worse impact. Therefore, he chose not to kill me directly, but to let me take the fall, and ultimately execute me anyway, which can be considered making use of trash."

The cultivators behind him opened their mouths but said nothing, tacitly agreeing.

Wu An let out a long sigh and slowly swept his gaze over everyone. "Those of you who came out with me under these circumstances are all my brothers!"

"I'll speak from the bottom of my heart! Even if this is the case, I, Wu An, am willing."

"This Cloud Prison incident was indeed my dereliction of duty! It was my greed for alcohol that implicated so many of our brothers in the Cloud Prison."

"With so many dead and so many injured, I cannot escape the blame. If I hadn't been drunk and had intervened early, such a massive disaster would never have happened!"

"I have also failed Lord Zhong Dao."

"Therefore, even if I am destined to die, I prefer to do it this way. Moving forward, I will do everything in my power to capture the Demonic Cultivators, fight to the death, and make up for my mistakes. Once success is achieved, I will return to Lord Zhong Dao, confess my sins, and face death! I will bear all the consequences of this operation alone."

"Of course!"

"If any brother here has doubts, you may leave right now. Just being able to follow me this far is enough to earn my, Wu's, respect and remembrance for a lifetime! Leaving now is not deserting in the face of battle, but doing so for the sake of your families and friends. No one will look down on you."

Wu An's eyes were bloodshot, and his voice was resounding.

His words moved all the cultivators present deeply. No one hesitated, and no one backed down. Instead, their morale was boosted, and they shouted one after another that they would go all out to help Wu An overcome this crisis!

Wu An was also incredibly moved, bowing deeply to the crowd on the spot. "For me, Wu An, to have you brothers abandon your futures to help me, I have not lived this life in vain!"

The crowd discussed for a while, all of them developing the resolve of an army fighting with their backs to the wall. However, just as they were about to step forward, confusion arose - the road ahead was long, and they didn't have a single clue in their hands. In which direction should they pursue?

Just at that moment, a streak of light sped over from the direction of the Cloud Prison.

The light was incredibly fast, like a shooting star tearing through the sky, heading straight for Wu An.

It was a flying letter!

Wu An reached out and caught the flying letter, only to see that the bird-shaped letter was also carrying a small bell in its beak.

Wu An unrolled the flying letter and swept his divine sense over it, taking it all in at a glance.

The letter stated: "The escaped Demonic Cultivators were all struck by the chime of my Soul-Mourning Bell. This small bell resonates with the Soul-Mourning Bell and can sense their locations. Where the bell rings, the Demonic Cultivators are there. Go quickly, do not delay."

Clearly, this was Zhong Dao's flying letter.

In an instant, Wu An's eyes burned, and his throat choked up. He wanted to say something, but he couldn't utter a single word.

When the bell rings once, evil is repelled; when the bell rings thrice, ten thousand ghosts weep in sorrow.

It turned out that Zhong Dao's bell chime attack had not only eradicated many Demonic Cultivators but had also left behind a crucial clue for tracking them!

Clutching the bell tightly in his hand, Wu An roared hoarsely, "Brothers, follow me!"

The group instantly pierced through the white clouds and sped off.

Along the way, the bell in Wu An's hand constantly vibrated, emitting soft chimes. That sound alternated between urgent and slow, dense and sparse, continuously guiding their direction.

The ringing of the bell grew denser and more urgent.

Wu An's heartbeat quickened in tandem; he knew they were getting closer to their target.

Ahead of them appeared an uninhabited small mountain peak.

The bell chimed as densely as a sudden downpour.

Without the slightest hesitation, Wu An dove down from high in the sky, taking the lead. The rest of the cultivators followed closely behind.

A black-robed Demonic Cultivator, who was meditating in the dense forest of the peak taking an emergency rest, heard the commotion and abruptly looked up.

"Damn it!" His expression twisted as he angrily fought back.

Bang, bang, bang.

Boom, boom!

The Demonic Cultivator refused to surrender and was quickly slain by Wu An and the others.

Wu An left one or two behind to clean up the battlefield and immediately marched away with the rest of his men.

Thus, moving from place to place, either killing or capturing, several Demonic Cultivators were apprehended one after another. Wu An's results were outstanding.

Some of these Demonic Cultivators hid in deep mountains and old forests, some hid in the crowds of the black market, some disguised themselves as ordinary slaves, and some even infiltrated the disciples of the Myriad Manifestations Sect, but not a single one could escape the bell's detection.

"Did you guys notice that the strength of these escaped Demonic Cultivators is actually much weaker than when they fought in the Cloud Prison?"

"Could it be that this is the price of teleportation?"

"It's more likely that something was devoured by the Lord of the Infernal Rift, right? Although they kept their lives, they paid other invisible prices!"

Harboring such speculations, the group encountered the Old Demon Withered Bone.

Wu An's hairs stood on end, and he braced himself with full focus, preparing for a fight to the death. Who would have expected that Old Demon Withered Bone would just surrender directly?

"Hold on, this old man will go back with you," Old Demon Withered Bone conceded.

Wu An and the others naturally wouldn't be so easily convinced.

"Don't attack, don't attack. Bring out your sealing talismans, and I will seal myself to show my sincerity," Old Demon Withered Bone immediately added.

A moment later, Old Demon Withered Bone not only sealed himself, but his entire body was also solidly bound by various methods executed by Wu An and the others.

After ensuring there was nothing to worry about, Wu An voiced his doubts.

Old Demon Withered Bone first cursed Zhong Dao vehemently before finally revealing the truth.

As it turned out, although he had teleported away and temporarily gained his freedom, he discovered that his soul had been struck by the bell chime. Every so often, the bell would chime three times. After each round of chimes, his soul would become unstable and suffer severe damage.

Old Demon Withered Bone exhausted his wits and methods but couldn't dispel the bell chime. Upon seeing Wu An, he was both shocked and delighted, hurriedly surrendering.

"That heaven-damned Zhong Dao!"

"I know he has always wanted to execute the Demonic Cultivators in the Cloud Prison, finding us to be a waste of resources."

"He deliberately let us escape so that we would all lose our lives because of this."

"I have to hand it to his ruthlessness this time; even I fell for his scheme!"

"He is itching for me to die. Heh heh, then I just won't give him what he wants. I'm willing to bind myself; as long as the green hills remain, there will be no shortage of firewood!"

Old Demon Withered Bone's words left Wu An and the others in profound shock.

Only then did they understand a part of Zhong Dao's plotting.

Anyone capable of becoming the head of a faction naturally wouldn't be a simple character. Zhong Dao had managed the Demon Subjugation Hall for many years and presumably upheld the principle of eradicating evil entirely. However, he was only one of the higher-ups; the overall political strategy of the Myriad Manifestations Sect had never gone entirely according to his wishes.

Wu An was stunned for a long time.

He suddenly understood that Lord Zhong Dao didn't actually need him to hunt down the Demonic Cultivators. Lord Zhong Dao needed Wu An to achieve a great merit.

Wu An needed an opportunity, a stage, and a victory to prove his worth.

Zhong Dao had given him an opportunity, built him a stage, and gifted him a victory!

"Lord Hall Master! Your subordinate... how could your subordinate possibly deserve this!" At this moment, Wu An felt a profound, undying loyalty to Zhong Dao.

Clang, clang, clang!

Three more bell chimes rang out.

Inside a cave, Qin De let out a miserable shriek and collapsed to his knees, clutching his head with both hands, his entire body convulsing violently.

Blood seeped from his seven orifices, and his skin began to crack. His soul, already suffering from compounding injuries, was on the verge of collapse.

"What a terrifying method..." he murmured, his voice so hoarse it was almost imperceptible.

"I'm going to die..." Qin De slumped to the ground, gasping heavily, his eyes full of despair.

A death where his soul was utterly destroyed meant he wouldn't even have the qualifications to become a ghost in the Underworld.

"No, I'm not resigned to this!"

He suddenly clenched his fists tightly, his nails biting deeply into his palms. Blood seeped from between his fingers and dripped onto the cold stone.

He had clearly already made it this far - he had escaped the Cloud Prison, he had obtained his freedom, and he still had the Sage's Great Thief Scripture and the Myriad Methods Fallen Demon Art!

"I've clearly made it this far..."

How could he die here?

How could he die in such a pathetic way?

Unlike Old Demon Withered Bone and the others, Qin De had never once thought of surrendering. Because he knew that once he surrendered, he would inevitably be subjected to severe torture and interrogation. By that time, he wouldn't be able to protect his demonic seed, nor even the Myriad Methods Fallen Demon Art.

It was highly likely that his memories would be wiped, and he would be imprisoned under even harsher conditions, relegated to being a Sect tool used to suppress the development of Confucian cultivators for the rest of his life.

This was absolutely not what Qin De wanted!

He gritted his teeth and used his last ounce of strength to sit up cross-legged. His hands formed seals as he struggled to activate the Myriad Methods Fallen Demon Art.

Right now, only this peerless cultivation method could bring him a sliver of hope.

A moment later, the deadly bell chime rang out again.

Pfft.

Qin De spat out a mouthful of blood.

"It's no use... I can't block it..."

His consciousness began to blur. The injuries on his soul had accumulated to the absolute limit and slowly started to shatter. Some edge fragments scattered into the air, turning into nothingness, unable to ever be reformed.

Despair enveloped Qin De.

He subconsciously mobilized whatever power he still had access to.

The Blood Mist Demonic Seed, his own Golden Core, and even Song Taosheng's Golden Core.

An inspiration struck like lightning, blasting a ray of light into his already chaotic Divine Sea.
