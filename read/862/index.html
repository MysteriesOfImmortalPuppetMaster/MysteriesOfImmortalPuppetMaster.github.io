Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 862: Ning Zhuo Is About to Die

In Ning Zhuo's divine sea, various thoughts arose and extinguished. His mind raced like lightning as he thought rapidly.

A Nascent Soul had appeared alone outside his cave dwelling, clamoring to find him. This was too bizarre! If it were a trap, what was the other party plotting? If it were an enemy, why not attack directly? If it were a misunderstanding, how could there be such a coincidence?

Ning Zhuo decided to act cautiously.

Guarding against a potential trap, he did not dispatch Gongsun Yan or Old Chef. Instead, he directly sent out a Mechanical Combat Puppet to probe the situation.

The doors of the cave dwelling opened, and the Mechanical Puppet strode out.

Seeing this, the Nascent Soul sensed Ning Zhuo's dense aura emanating from the Mechanical Puppet and couldn't help but be overjoyed.

It lunged forward.

It wasn't an attack; it was a pounce.

Opening its arms wide, it threw itself at the Mechanical Puppet, screaming shrilly, "Ning Zhuo! Ning Zhuo! I've finally found you!"

Under Ning Zhuo's control, the Mechanical Puppet subconsciously threw a punch to meet it.

The fist smashed into the Nascent Soul, sending it flying.

The Nascent Soul tumbled in the air a few times but struggled to fly right back, its eyes filled with urgency: "Quick! Hurry and refine me! I am yours! I belong to you!"

Ning Zhuo: "?!"

The Mechanical Puppet stood frozen in place, motionless.

With a smack, the Nascent Soul plastered itself atop the Mechanical Puppet's head, still crying out incessantly.

"Hurry and refine me! Let us fuse into one! I am yours! I belong to you! Quick, quick, quick! Once the main consciousness wakes up, it will be too late!"

Inside the cave dwelling, Ning Zhuo watched this scene through his Jade Token, completely dumbfounded.

The big-headed youth took a deep breath, forcing himself to calm down. Through the cave dwelling's Jade Token and the Mechanical Puppet, he carefully observed this Nascent Soul.

It was tiny and delicate, its pure white body covered in fine cracks. The cracks spread like spiderwebs or tree roots from the top of its head to the soles of its feet, dense and intricate. They made the entire Nascent Soul look like a piece of porcelain on the verge of completely shattering - a shocking sight.

The layer of purplish-black mist around it had mostly dissipated, leaving only a thin veil, like a tattered swaddling cloth wrapping its broken body.

Its aura was so faint it was barely perceptible; every breath seemed to exhaust its last ounce of strength.

"This Nascent Soul is heavily injured and on the verge of collapse!" Ning Zhuo concluded.

Through the Mechanical Puppet, he executed Sealing Spells. Sure enough, he easily restrained and controlled the Nascent Soul.

He then activated the cave dwelling's Jade Token, borrowing the power of the dwelling's Formation to further seal the Nascent Soul.

Throughout the entire process, the Nascent Soul remained motionless, allowing him to do as he pleased, even crying out, "Hurry up, do it faster!"

Ning Zhuo meticulously inspected it layer by layer, over and over again. Only when he was absolutely sure that everything was under his control did he have the Mechanical Puppet bring the Nascent Soul into the bluestone cave dwelling, finally entering the Cultivation Chamber to arrive before him.

Because the entire situation was far too bizarre, Ning Zhuo subconsciously chose to only inform Sun Lingtong, keeping the Old Chef and Gongsun Yan inside the cave dwelling in the dark.

"Refine me... I am yours..." Upon seeing Ning Zhuo's true body, the Nascent Soul was already at its last gasp.

Ning Zhuo truly didn't understand at all.

He questioned the Nascent Soul, but its replies were limited to a single phrase: "Refine me... I am yours..."

Ning Zhuo: "Who are you?"

Nascent Soul: "Refine me... I am yours..."

Ning Zhuo: "Why do you want me to refine you?"

Nascent Soul: "Refine me... I am yours..."

Ning Zhuo: "Who is the 'main consciousness' you mentioned? What will happen when he wakes up?"

Nascent Soul: "Refine me... I am yours..."

Ning Zhuo furrowed his brows tightly. The Nascent Soul's condition was so poor that it was delirious, only able to repeat this one sentence. It was severely injured and on the brink of collapse, yet it stubbornly insisted that he refine it. This was far too abnormal.

If the Nascent Soul were in slightly better condition, Ning Zhuo might have extended his divine sense or used other methods to probe it.

But right now, its condition was simply too terrible; Ning Zhuo didn't dare make even a single reckless move.

"I'll seal it away for now and discuss it at length when Boss returns." Just as this thought crossed Ning Zhuo's mind, he suddenly let out an "Ah!" and cried out in pain.

At that very moment, his Mechanism Ring violently contracted with unprecedented force!

In that instant, Ning Zhuo was in so much pain that he had the illusion the Mechanism Ring would snap his finger bone right off.

"The Mechanism Ring has never given such an intense warning before!" With just that one squeeze, Ning Zhuo completely recognized the gravity of the situation. A faint sense of horror caused his pupils to shrink instantly.

He knew he had to make a choice.

Time was likely running out.

This bizarre Nascent Soul seemed to contain a tremendous risk beyond his imagination. Or perhaps, the Nascent Soul would attract a massive danger that Ning Zhuo would be powerless to resist!

"I must handle this Nascent Soul correctly, and as quickly as possible!"

"What should I do?"

Ning Zhuo rubbed the Mechanism Ring on his hand and tentatively thought, "Should I let it go?"

The ring contracted slightly.

Ning Zhuo thought again, "Should I wake it up?"

The ring contracted slightly.

Ning Zhuo thought once more, "Should I help it, heal it?"

The ring contracted once again.

Ning Zhuo: "This..."

In his current situation, he was completely at a loss.

He couldn't be blamed. There was simply too much he didn't know. And unfortunately, this missing information was absolutely crucial.

The Nascent Soul he had just sealed was none other than Qin De's Demonic-Confucian Two-Phase Nascent Soul.

This Nascent Soul, which Qin De had formed after breaking his Golden Core, was truly too unique.

Usually, when a Cultivator successfully forms a Nascent Soul, they have complete mastery over it. But Qin De did not.

The reason for this was quite simple: the foundation of the Demonic-Confucian Two-Phase Nascent Soul consisted not only of Qin De's own Golden Core but also Song Taosheng's Golden Core and the Blood Mist Demonic Seed.

If the latter two had been fully refined by Qin De, they would have been his to control. However, while he was on the ninth floor of the Cloud Prison, he had absolutely no time to refine them.

He had only partially refined both of them.

This resulted in the final formation of the Nascent Soul. Although Qin De was the primary controller and the main consciousness, a secondary consciousness still emerged.

The origins of this secondary consciousness were the Blood Mist Demonic Seed and Song Taosheng.

Even though the previous owner of the Blood Mist Demonic Seed had lost their sanity, they insanely favored Ning Zhuo, feeling that the big-headed youth was the most suitable heir to inherit the Demonic Seed.

This mad pursuit, after being interrupted by Zhong Dao, had only intensified.

As for Song Taosheng, he, of course, also felt great fondness for Ning Zhuo! In fact, the entire group of Confucian cultivators highly appreciated Ning Zhuo.

Under these circumstances, when Qin De - the main consciousness - fell unconscious and lost control, the secondary consciousness seized control of the Nascent Soul's body. Decisively following his aura, it tracked down the bluestone cave dwelling where Ning Zhuo resided.

In truth, the place where Qin De had plummeted was the very peak where the bluestone cave dwelling was located!

As the Demonic-Confucian Nascent Soul followed the trail, sensing Ning Zhuo's aura growing denser, it became increasingly excited.

Upon reaching the entrance of the cave dwelling, it was finally blocked by the defensive formation. Lacking normal human intellect, it threw caution to the wind and began attacking the array. In the end, it was fortunate enough to draw Ning Zhuo out and come face-to-face with him.

Ning Zhuo was entirely unaware of these events; he couldn't possibly guess the origins of this Nascent Soul.

His breathing was now growing heavier as a formless sense of crisis continuously built up in his heart!

A flash of inspiration crossed his divine sea.

Ning Zhuo formed a thought: "What if I refine this Nascent Soul?"

The Mechanism Ring contracted slightly.

Ning Zhuo: "Refining it won't work either?!"

At the back mountain of the Myriad Manifestations Sect.

This was a valley spanning a hundred li in circumference.

Surrounded by mountains on all sides, the valley was shrouded in clouds and mist year-round, its spiritual energy dense and swirling.

A thousand years ago, this place had been an abandoned spiritual field belonging to the Myriad Manifestations Sect. Although its spiritual energy was abundant, the unique terrain caused its flow to be chaotic. Deemed unsuitable for cultivating spiritual medicines, it had lain desolate for years.

When Shao Qiannong first joined the Myriad Manifestations Sect, he visited all the peaks and immediately took a liking to this place.

He had said, "Though the spiritual energy here is chaotic, it has a pattern. Though the earth veins are mixed, they have a root. Order within chaos, purity within complexity - this is the perfect place to plant the Yi Forest."

The Myriad Manifestations Sect naturally agreed.

From then on, Layman Yilin built a hut and resided here, planting the Yi Forest, where he had remained for over a thousand years.

Having just barely stabilized his own injuries, Dong Chen hurried to this valley solely to seek an audience with Shao Qiannong.

At the end of the mountain path lay the mist-shrouded valley. The mist was faint but extremely heavy, pressing down on the valley like a thick cotton quilt. Within the mist, the vague silhouettes of vegetation flickered, indistinct and blurry.

At the mouth of the valley stood a bluestone carved with two ancient seal characters: Yi Forest. The writing was blurred, its edges creeping with moss; it was unknown how many years ago they were carved.

Dong Chen stopped and adjusted his robes.

Standing beside the bluestone, he cupped his fists. His voice was not loud, but it clearly transmitted into the woods: "Layman Yilin, Dong Chen requests an audience."

The woods were silent for a moment. Then, an ancient voice drifted from the depths of the mist, like dead leaves falling to the ground, like branches swaying: "Come in."

Dong Chen stepped past the bluestone, officially entering the valley.

The mist slowly parted around him, revealing a winding path. The path, paved with bluestone, was no more than three feet wide.

He stepped onto the path with light footsteps. The trees on either side flickered in and out of sight amid the mist.

There was the Heavenly Fiend Pine. Its trunk was as straight as a spear, piercing straight into the clouds. Its bark was as silver-white as snow, and its needles as jade-green as emeralds.

There was the Earth Mother Locust Tree. Its canopy hung low like a cover, and its roots coiled like dragons. Parts of the roots were exposed above ground, twisting and intertwining like a massive net. Its bark was a deep, earthy brown; its leaves were fleshy like palms, with veins as distinct as mountains and rivers.

There was the Startling Thunder Bamboo. Its segments were hollow, as thick as a child's arm, and it towered as high as a building. When the wind blew through the bamboos, it produced a rumbling sound, like distant thunder - muffled yet powerful.

There was the Wind Dance Willow. Its branches were as soft as silk, drooping to the ground and swaying with the wind. The willow leaves were as long and thin as eyebrows, and as tender green as spring water.

As Dong Chen walked past, the tree canopies parted on their own, the branches and leaves actively gave way, and the roots crawled aside to clear a path. Once he had passed, everything returned to its original state.

The woods were very quiet. There was no birdsong, no chirping of insects - only the rustling of the wind passing through the treetops.

This was precisely the Yi Forest.

In the very center of the Yi Forest lay a clearing. In the clearing stood a solitary thatched cottage.

The cottage was not large, consisting of only three rooms: a living quarter, a divination room, and a guest room. Its four walls were open, allowing wind and rain to enter freely. In front of the cottage was an old locust tree with a sturdy trunk that required three people to encircle. Its canopy acted like an umbrella, shading half the clearing. Beneath the tree sat a stone table and two stone stools, with a set of coarse pottery teaware resting on the table.

Shao Qiannong was sitting right beneath the old locust tree.

His face was gaunt, with slightly high cheekbones. His eyes were small and deeply set, and his pupils were an extremely pale grayish-brown, as if covered by a layer of morning mist.

His hair was graying and casually pinned up with a sharpened twig, with a few strands hanging loose over his forehead. Beneath his jaw grew three wisps of a sparse long beard, the color of withered grass, drooping down to his chest.

His hands rested on his knees. His ten fingers were long and slender, with distinct knuckles. His nails were trimmed extremely short, and his fingertips were covered in fine, dense scars. His palms were as rough as tree bark, covered in calluses.

His figure was lean as bamboo, his back slightly hunched, and he was not tall.

At this moment, he wore a coarse gray robe that had been washed until it was faded; the cuffs and collar were frayed. Around his waist was tied a grass rope, from which hung a small cloth pouch. Stitched from coarse hemp, the pouch was the size of a palm.

He looked like an old farmer, without the slightest hint of a cultivator's aura.

Yet Dong Chen knew very well that the man before him had a cultivation base that surpassed his own - a being at the Soul Formation-level, adept at the esoteric cultivation art of Divination!

Shao Qiannong shifted his gaze and looked at Dong Chen. His grayish-brown eyes were as calm as water, without the slightest ripple.

"Current Sect Master," his voice was flat. "Please, sit."

Dong Chen sat across from him. The stone stool was ice-cold, but he felt a sense of groundedness.

Dong Chen explained the purpose of his visit, concluding with: "Putting aside everything else for now, the intensity of this clash of destinies far exceeded our expectations. The most crucial part is that the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy had a corner overturned during the clash. I fear a portion of the calamitous fate it has been suppressing has fallen upon the head of our Myriad Manifestations Sect. I beseech Layman Yilin to perform a divination."

Shao Qiannong gave a slight nod. He slowly closed his eyes and leaned back against the old locust tree.

The wind blew through the treetops, rustling the leaves of the old locust tree.

A few withered leaves drifted down, landing on the stone table, beside the teacups, and upon Shao Qiannong's shoulder. He did not brush them away; he merely leaned quietly against the trunk, perfectly still, as if he himself had become a tree.

A moment later, he half-opened his eyes and informed Dong Chen of the preliminary result of his calculation: "Though this calamitous fate is small, the piece has already been played."

Dong Chen's heart tightened: "Where did it land?"

Shao Qiannong replied, "It fell upon a young cultivator."

"Who?"

"I have yet to calculate that."

Dong Chen gritted his teeth, his resolve firm: "Even if they are at the ends of the earth, I will mobilize the full might of the Myriad Manifestations Sect to eradicate this person!"

He then cupped his fists, pleading once more: "I beg the Layman to divine who this person is and their exact location."

Shao Qiannong nodded, closing his eyes once again and leaning against the old locust tree.

Ban Clan Estate, Ancestral Shrine Secret Chamber.

In the center of the secret chamber, the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain stood quietly.

Three Supreme Clan Elders sat in a circle around the mechanism.

Quite some time had passed since they last observed Ning Zhuo's destiny. The strength of that youth's destiny and the bizarreness of its fluctuations were truly something they had never seen in their lives. The reason they were activating the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain again today was simply because the cultivators present all felt an indescribable sense of unease.

The leading Supreme Clan Elder spoke up: "Let us begin."

The three of them struck out simultaneously, injecting their spiritual energy into the mechanism.

The bronze mechanism gave a violent shudder.

Sliders slid up and down, chains pulled left and right, and gears spun back and forth. The clicking sounds were as dense as falling rain; the entire mechanism seemed to awaken from a deep slumber. Within the secret chamber, an invisible, oppressive aura permeated the air.

One breath, ten breaths, thirty breaths.

The rotation of the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain grew faster and more intense. The sliders were almost derailing, the chains were pulled taut, and the gears emitted piercing sounds of friction.

"Ning Zhuo's destiny is growing stronger and stronger. With every observation, the difficulty skyrockets compared to the last."

"It's coming out."

Above the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain, a bizarre vision of destiny slowly manifested.

They saw that the Arm of Destiny was enveloped in swirling clouds and mist, slowly taking the shape of a coffin, intending to seal and bury all of Ning Zhuo's destiny within!

"H-How did this happen?"

"What on earth occurred?"

"Why has it come to this? The contrast before and after is far too extreme, isn't it?"

The three Supreme Clan Elders looked at each other in shock.

They never would have expected that this session of observing destiny would yield such a result.

Because this phenomenon directly indicated one thing - Ning Zhuo was about to die!
