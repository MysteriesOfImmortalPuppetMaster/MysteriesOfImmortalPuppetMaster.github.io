Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 938: The Assembly's Deliberation

Ning Zhuo continued his analysis: "The origins of the treasures used by Liu Jinke in his second battle are too obvious. The Flock-Startling Whistle, Thousand-Jin Drop, Shattering Thunder Bead, Fate-Severing Saber Talisman, and the Faith Moves Mountains Art - each and every one of them can be linked to the factions on Flowing Cloud Peak. We don't need to state it outright; we just need to let the public know that before Nanming Stronghold has even officially begun to scale the mountain, Flowing Cloud Peak is already afraid."

Sun Lingtong nodded repeatedly. The more he listened, the more he looked forward to the enemy's reaction.

Now that Ning Zhuo had the backing of Nanming Stronghold, his room to maneuver was vastly expanded.

At present, Liu Jinke had become his pawn. Ning Zhuo and the Nanming Stronghold faction on one side, and the many major factions of Flowing Cloud Peak on the other, were all making use of Liu Jinke, engaging in an indirect proxy contest.

Obviously, this was unfair to the various factions on Flowing Cloud Peak.

Because the innate talent of the Golden Liquid Reverted Core Constitution restricted the use of divine sense, the Flowing Cloud Peak side was heavily constrained in their attempts to provide assistance. The vast majority of their strength simply could not be brought to bear.

A battle often depends on favorable timing, geographical advantage, and harmony among the people.

By using the Naming Fire Furnace as a catalyst to cleverly establish Nanming Stronghold, Ning Zhuo could be considered to have gained a degree of favorable timing.

By utilizing Liu Jinke as a pawn, he had actually seized a significant "geographical advantage." Under these unfavorable conditions, the factions of Flowing Cloud Peak were forced to continuously provide - and even increase - their support for Liu Jinke.

Liu Jinke was the "letter of challenge" that Ning Zhuo had personally delivered!

As for harmony among the people, both sides were about evenly matched. Flowing Cloud Peak was filled with various factions that were constantly on guard against one another; it was a rare occurrence for them to join forces against Nanming Stronghold this time. However, the extent of their cooperation was limited.

The establishment of Nanming Stronghold was an opportunistic endeavor. There were many rifts among its members, and it was only maintained by their shared identity as creditors. Let alone the coexistence of humans and demon cultivators, some members even harbored deep-seated blood feuds against each other!

Despite Nanming Stronghold boasting five Nascent Soul-level entities and containing numerous Golden Core-level cultivators, it was actually strong on the outside but brittle on the inside. It was extremely weak, with scattered loyalties, making it fundamentally difficult for them to pull together in one direction.

"I have already fought Liu Jinke twice, beheading him in both consecutive battles," Ning Zhuo said, his gaze deep and profound. "The time is ripe. It is time to convene the members of our Nanming Stronghold once again."

Sun Lingtong departed, while Ning Zhuo broadly dispatched flying messages.

Not long after, the cultivators of Nanming Stronghold gathered in Ning Zhuo's cave manor.

Facing the crowd, Ning Zhuo didn't waste words.

He laid out the Flock-Startling Whistle, the Thousand-Jin Drop, the Shattering Thunder Bead, the Fate-Severing Saber Talisman, and a copied jade slip of the Faith Moves Mountains Art in front of everyone, one by one.

These items weren't just spoils of war; they were evidence!

The Flock-Startling Whistle belonged to the Bird and Beast Manor faction. The Thousand-Jin Drop carried the earth vein principles of the Earth Expansion Alliance. The Shattering Thunder Bead faintly displayed traces of the Thunder Cloud Society's imitation tribulation lightning. The Fate-Severing Saber Talisman closely resembled the blade-intent talisman patterns of the Water-Severing Blade Pavilion. As for the Faith Moves Mountains Art, it was inextricably linked to the Jinshi Alliance. As for the Nine-Orifice Blood Gold Fetus, any discerning eye could tell at a glance that an itinerant cultivator like Liu Jinke could never easily obtain it.

Liu Jinke's second battle was no longer his fight alone.

Since the factions of Flowing Cloud Peak had handed him the knife, Nanming Stronghold striking back would be completely justified.

"This certainly saves us some trouble." Red-Robed Guest stared at the spoils of war and grinned, his smile brimming with murderous intent. "They've delivered their weakness right to our doorstep. If we don't accept it, wouldn't we be letting down all their hard work?"

Chunyangzi's expression was righteous and solemn: "Retaliation is acceptable, but we must not act recklessly. Nanming Stronghold is newly established, and being within the main Sect gates of the Myriad Manifestations Sect, it attracts countless eyes. Therefore, our actions must heavily rely on proper justification. The factions of Flowing Cloud Peak funding Liu Jinke can serve as grounds for demanding an explanation, but it cannot be used as an excuse for wanton warfare."

Red-Robed Guest sneered: "The benevolence of a woman! The enemy's blade is already at our necks, and you still want to pause and ask if there's a name carved on the hilt."

Chunyangzi stared intently at him: "Without proper justification, it is merely a private brawl. With justification, it is holding them accountable. The difference between the two is immense."

"Justification?" Red-Robed Guest scoffed. "Isn't the justification sitting right here on the table? The Flock-Startling Whistle, the Thousand-Jin Drop, the Shattering Thunder Bead - which one isn't the fox's tail they left behind? I say the best course of action is to adjourn today and knock on their doors tomorrow. Whoever sent the treasures, we go to them for an explanation. If they don't give one, we fight!"

Chunyangzi and Red-Robed Guest locked eyes, neither willing to back down.

The others couldn't help but feel a headache coming on.

Before Nanming Stronghold even took external action, it seemed they were about to have an internal conflict.

In the midst of the escalating tension, Shen Xi suddenly spoke up, "If I recall correctly, Lord Chunyangzi sits in the first chair as the Chief Stronghold Master, right?"

He was clearly taking Chunyangzi's side.

At these words, Chunyangzi cast a glance at him and gave a slight nod, while Red-Robed Guest shot him a cold glare.

Shen Xi remained unmoved.

He was the Foundation Establishment figurehead of a super family; with such a powerful backing supporting him, he did not fear the malice of a Nascent Soul cultivator.

Red-Robed Guest suddenly stood up: "You want me to listen to this Chunyangzi? Don't even think about it! A bunch of old biddies hemming and hawing - how can we accomplish anything? Watch me go slaughter the enemy's prestige first! I'll show you all that Flowing Cloud Peak only looks strong; in reality, their internal friction is severe, and they aren't worth worrying about at all!"

After saying his piece, Red-Robed Guest flicked his sleeves and left, walking straight out of Ning Zhuo's cave manor.

The people in the hall looked at each other in dismay.

The discussion had barely just begun, and a Nascent Soul-level powerhouse had already stormed off in anger.

Many eyes turned to Chunyangzi, who remained expressionless, and then a portion of them subconsciously looked toward Ning Zhuo.

Ning Zhuo, surprisingly, wore a smile as he looked around. "Do any other fellow Daoists have different opinions?"

As soon as he spoke, Nine Fires Dragon Lord opened his mouth.

"The foundation of Nanming Stronghold has always been the Naming Fire Furnace."

"However the factions of Flowing Cloud Peak provoke us, we can settle the score with them slowly. But the final twenty percent of the furnace's damages cannot be delayed any longer. After all, every day we restore the furnace earlier is a day sooner that Nanming Stronghold gains the ability to repay its debts, and at the same time, it will stabilize people's hearts!"

Though Nine Fires Dragon Lord didn't sit in the first chair, his investment was exceptionally significant. He had even put in his father's relics, making him the person present who most desired to repair the Naming Fire Furnace.

"The Dragon Lord's words are mature and prudent!" Ning Zhuo looked at the others again, asking if there were any differing views.

Everyone began to speak their own minds, and the discussion hall gradually grew noisy.

Some advocated sending formal notices first, using the justification of the Righteous Path to demand accountability.

Some advocated an immediate challenge, beating back the factions behind Liu Jinke one by one to firmly establish their reputation.

Some advocated first investigating the earth veins, covert channels, and resources, arguing against acting rashly. Once the investigations were clear, they could see which territory suited them best and launch a justified retaliatory strike to seize it.

Some only focused on the Naming Fire Furnace, believing that until the furnace was restored, any talk of scaling the peak was empty.

Ning Zhuo sat in his seat, listening quietly.

This argument had long been within his expectations.

Nanming Stronghold wasn't a Sect, nor was it a well-drilled military unit. It was a temporary stronghold pieced together by debts, interests, ambitions, old grudges, and opportunities.

There was a deep, irreconcilable contradiction between Chunyangzi and Red-Robed Guest. Red-Robed Guest's radicalism and Chunyangzi's adherence to principles were both laced with personal enmity.

Nine Fires Dragon Lord wanted to repair the furnace, Tan Zhu wanted to strike covertly and attack from the flanks, Situ Xing had been continuously observing Ning Zhuo, Shen Xi wanted to investigate the formations, Lin Jinglong advocated for careful management, and Mu Yuehua just sat quietly the entire time. Her moon-white sleeves hung over her knees, her brows and eyes like cold radiance reflecting on water.

Tu Yuanzi also remained silent throughout.

The youth in yellow sat not far from Ning Zhuo, hands resting on his knees, listening very earnestly. He did not interject, nor did he rush to declare a stance. His clear eyes slowly swept across the faces of the crowd, secretly striving to understand the arguments of "humans."

By Cang Yazi's side, he had interacted with spirit pets - emotions were straightforward, and likes and dislikes were clear. But these cultivators before him had hidden meanings in their words, calculations in their anger, and barbs in their smiles; even their offers of help carried ulterior motives. For Tu Yuanzi, who hadn't taken human form for long, this kind of deliberation was perhaps more unfamiliar and profound than a magical battle.

The argument continued for a while, becoming increasingly difficult to rein in.

Nine Fires Dragon Lord stood up impatiently, his gaze settling on the big-headed youth: "Ning Zhuo, I will list out the materials needed for the furnace's repair. As for the factions of Flowing Cloud Peak, you all can take your time arguing about it."

He turned around and left.

Before leaving, he shot Tu Yuanzi a deeply meaningful look.

Tu Yuanzi was baffled, thinking to himself, "Why are you looking at me with such deep affection?"

Seeing that Tu Yuanzi was unmoved and made no move to defend a fellow Demon Cultivator, Nine Fires Dragon Lord gave a displeased, cold snort in his heart, but ultimately could only leave entirely.

The departure of the second Nascent Soul cultivator caused the remaining cultivators in the hall to feel even more unsettled.

Up until the meeting concluded, everyone failed to settle on a unified strategy.

It could even be said that they parted on bad terms.

But Ning Zhuo wasn't disappointed in the slightest.

This stronghold couldn't possibly achieve strict obedience to orders - at least, not right now.

The argument just now allowed him to see more clearly the desires, calculations, and interest alignments of each member.

As long as these actions weren't aimed at sabotaging one another, they could all become forces pressing down upon Flowing Cloud Peak.

By saying almost nothing, Ning Zhuo had already achieved his original goal.

Mu Yuehua turned back around.

Her moon-white dress cast a faint, clear glow under the lamplight. She sat quietly, like a sliver of moonlight lingering in the night. During the crowd's argument just now, she had barely spoken a word, only listening in silence.

Ning Zhuo looked at her. "Does Fellow Daoist Mu have more to say?"

Mu Yuehua walked right up to sit across from Ning Zhuo and said sincerely, "If Fellow Daoist Ning Zhuo needs anything, within the limits of my ability, I am willing to offer my full assistance."

Her tone was calm and cold, but her attitude was exceedingly resolute.

Ning Zhuo's expression grew solemn, and he offered a formal bow. "Fellow Daoist Mu's willingness to assist me - I, Ning Zhuo, will remember this."

Mu Yuehua shook her head gently. "I am not merely helping you."

"You are someone I envy, having accomplished things I could not. I am willing to help you continue walking down this path."

"I am helping myself."

Ning Zhuo sighed with admiration. "Having Fellow Daoist Mu's help is truly my honor!"

Flowing Cloud Peak, Impermanent Cloud Hollow.

This place was the territory of White Cloud Village.

Three layers of restrictions were laid down around the cloud hollow. The outer layer used white clouds to obscure vision, the middle layer used tea fragrance to bewilder the mind, and the inner layer used formation runes to lock in sound, sealing the entire cloud hollow airtight.

The fact that the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak sent representatives to gather in one place was an extremely rare sight in past years.

Flowing Cloud Peak was used to chaos.

The factions of the various peaks guarded against each other. It was common to greet each other with smiling faces on normal days, only to stab each other in the back. If it weren't for the pressure coming from Nanming Stronghold, and if it weren't for Ning Zhuo slaying Liu Jinke twice in a row while publicly exposing all of their methods, these people would absolutely never have sat down at the same cloud desk.

The cloud desk was circular, its surface made of condensed clouds turned to jade, with white mist flowing over it like a captive pool of living water.

Representing the Thunder Cloud Society was Lei Wangyue.

He had a burly stature, wore a short purple-black robe, and had a prominent brow ridge. There was an old scar on his face scorched by thunder-fire, and when he spoke, his voice was as raspy as the wind scraping against mountain rocks before a thunderstorm. After he sat down, fine sparks of lightning still danced at his cuffs, clearly showing that his mood was unsettled.

Zhao Ni of the Bird and Beast Manor leaned against his chair back, wearing a half-open animal skin vest, with a small gray-feathered hawk perched on his shoulder. This man looked like a wild mountain hunter, but his eyes were incredibly bright. When he swept his gaze over others, it was like a falcon skimming the ground, his rough knuckles constantly rubbing the bone whistle at his waist.

The representative from the Earth Expansion Alliance was named Qiu Lei. He had an earthy yellow complexion and a stout build, looking like a boulder that didn't care to speak. Yet the moment he sat down, the Earth Qi beneath the cloud desk was quietly suppressed, and even the drifting clouds grew a bit heavier.

The representative for the Water-Severing Blade Pavilion was Xu Duan, dressed in a cyan-gray blademaster's robe, with an unsheathed long saber resting horizontally across his lap. The edge wasn't shiny, yet it carried a chilling intent capable of drawing a blade to sever a river's flow. Since taking his seat, he hadn't said a single word, merely staring at the cloud shadows in the desk as if looking at an opponent he was about to cleave in two.

Ye Qingming of the Green Tea Society was also present.

She still wore a moon-white long dress with an aqua-green half-sleeve jacket thrown over it, and a simple, elegant plain silver hairpin in her hair. She poured herself a cup of clear tea, emitting a faint fragrance. Her gaze was gentle, but she took in every single change in the expressions of everyone present.

Representing the Jinshi Alliance was an old woman with a gold hairpin.

Her head of silver hair was tied up into a bun, pierced with only a single old gold hairpin. Her face was as wrinkled as stone grain, but her gaze was as hard as cold gold deep within a mineral vein. Since the Faith Moves Mountains Art had fallen into Liu Jinke's hands, she was an unavoidable part of this situation.

The one sent by Xuanhu Retreat was Wen Suzhen, one of their six wandering physicians.

He had a clean, fair face and a neatly trimmed short beard under his jaw. He pinched a silver needle in his hand, twirling it back and forth between his fingertips.

Jin Mantang of the Floating Life Society was also there.

His plump body was squeezed into his chair, the copper coin patterns on his golden robe flashing dazzlingly in the cloud light. The man who usually loved to smile the most had significantly less laughter tonight, only occasionally rubbing the ring on his hand, seemingly calculating exactly how much this round was going to lose him, and how much it might potentially earn him.

Old Man Candeng of the Setting Sun Society shrunk back into the shadows. With a slightly hunched back, a dilapidated small lamp hovered beside him. The dim yellow lamplight illuminated the crisscrossing ravines on his face.

His cloudy eyes narrowed as he let out a sneer. "Everyone, this is truly a rare occasion."

Old Man Candeng's voice was dry and hoarse: "Over the years on Flowing Cloud Peak, we've fought, cheated, killed, and sold out each other, but we've rarely sat down so completely to discuss matters. If word gets out, it'll sound like we've had our courage shattered by a mid-Foundation Establishment brat."

Lei Wangyue snorted coldly: "Old Ghost Candeng, save the sarcastic remarks. Your Setting Sun Society has been selling the most recording jade slips. A lot of the rumors that Ning Zhuo picked up probably leaked from your side, didn't they?"

Old Man Candeng chuckled darkly. "Selling intelligence is a skill in itself. Besides, without the Flock-Startling Whistle, Thousand-Jin Drop, Shattering Thunder Bead, and Fate-Severing Saber Talisman that all of you sent over, this old man couldn't have drummed up such a lively sale even if he wanted to."

Qiu Lei frowned. "Now is not the time to pass the buck."

"Then let's get down to business," Zhao Ni said, pressing down on the small hawk on his shoulder, his gaze cold and fierce. "Can we still invest in Liu Jinke?"

As those words fell, the cloud hollow fell completely silent.

Liu Jinke.

This name had now become a hot potato.

To support him would be to continue acknowledging that the factions of Flowing Cloud Peak were using him to deal with Ning Zhuo. Ning Zhuo had already spread the rumor that before Nanming Stronghold had even ascended the mountain, Flowing Cloud Peak was already terrified, scrambling to fund Liu Jinke. If they continued to pour treasures into Liu Jinke, it would only prove these rumors true.

But what if they didn't support him?

Liu Jinke had suffered two consecutive defeats. If he failed in the third battle, it would make it look as if they feared Ning Zhuo.

If these local tyrants took a step back before Nanming Stronghold even truly charged the peak, how could they manage people's loyalty in the future? How could they intimidate newcomers? How could they convince the itinerant cultivators in their respective territories to submit?

Right now, the various major factions of Flowing Cloud Peak felt trapped in a dilemma!

Ye Qingming gently blew away the foam on the surface of her tea and said softly, "I warned you all long ago: this Ning Zhuo is not simple."

Lei Wangyue sneered. "Of course he's not simple. How could anyone who can behead Liu Jinke twice be simple?"

Ye Qingming shook her head. "I am not talking about his combat strength."

The crowd fell silent once again.
