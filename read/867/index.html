Chapter 867: The Furnace Spirit Chooses a Master

Just thinking about the price the Ban Clan would have to pay to repair the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain made the Ban Clan's Grand Supreme Elder feel his scalp tingle and his heart nearly stop.

The other Supreme Elders conversed in low voices.

"Is a Royal Mandate Human Tribulation really this strong? It's simply unimaginable!"

"What should we do now?"

"Are we still going to help transcend this Royal Mandate Human Tribulation or not?"

"Sigh. If Ning Zhuo were outside the gates of the Myriad Manifestations Sect... we could just directly strike and eliminate him. But of course, he's inside."

The Ban Clan's Grand Supreme Elder slowly closed his eyes.

"Let it be," he said, his voice very soft, as if he had exhausted his last bit of strength. "We won't interfere anymore. Don't think about dealing with Ning Zhuo, don't even observe his luck. First, we will put all our effort into repairing the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain, and then do our best to help Ban Ji."

"A Royal Mandate Human Tribulation naturally requires someone with a Royal Mandate to transcend it."

A Supreme Elder asked, "Then what about the arrangements we made against Ning Zhuo in the Rising Cloud Minor Trial?"

The Grand Supreme Elder opened his eyes, his gaze resolute: "Repairing the Clan Destiny Pivot Chain is the top priority; terminate everything else!"

Danxia Peak.

Inside the alchemy room after the explosion, a burly man squatted in front of a ruined alchemy furnace.

He was strapping and well-built, with a back like a tiger and a waist like a bear. His square face was covered in a thick beard, in which fine iron shavings were faintly visible.

He wore a coarse, grease-stained tunic with the sleeves rolled up to his forearms, revealing two heavily muscled arms covered in burn scars. They were layered thick, a mix of old and new.

His fingers were thick and short, with protruding knuckles, and an indelible rust color embedded under his fingernails.

He was none other than the current Hall Master of the Myriad Manifestations Sect's Artifact Refining Hall, Tie Kuang.

At this moment, his hands caressed the surface of the furnace as his divine sense continuously scanned it.

Tie Kuang's brows furrowed tighter and tighter.

After a long while, he slowly stood up, his face full of solemnity.

"How is it?" the old alchemists gathered around, their voices anxious.

"The Nanming Fire Furnace... is a Spirit Treasure," he began, his voice low. "The artifact spirit's intelligence is quite extraordinary. It knows it is about to die and has fallen into a deep terror."

The old alchemists all stared at him.

Tie Kuang continued, "Conventional artifact refining methods can no longer restore it. It's not a lack of resources... but rather, the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit has closed itself off, rejecting all external treatment."

The old alchemists were stunned.

"What do you mean?" someone asked, their voice trembling.

Tie Kuang glanced back at the ruined furnace, as if seeing the tiny Vermilion Bird hidden deep within it. It was curled into a ball, its wings drooping, burying its head beneath its wings, revealing only the listless tuft of fine golden feathers on the top of its head.

Tie Kuang looked at the old alchemists and told them the truth: "Repairing ordinary magic treasures or alchemy furnaces wouldn't actually be difficult for me."

"But Spirit Treasures are special."

"Repairing a Spirit Treasure requires the artifact spirit's cooperation."

"And right now, the Vermilion Bird spirit is terrified. Not just an ordinary fright, but the kind of fear that seeps into the bones. It has been scared out of its wits."

Tie Kuang continued, "The Vermilion Bird spirit has completely lost faith in you. Any movement you make towards it is seen as a lethal attack. Your spiritual energy, your medicinal pills, your medicinal powders, your divine sense - it fears them all. It can't distinguish whether you are trying to save it or kill it."

He paused, his voice even lower. "It is also very wary of me. When I probed it just now, its fear reached its peak; it's simply too weak and doesn't even have the strength to struggle."

"Then..." an old alchemist's voice was dry, "what do we do?"

Tie Kuang was silent for a moment. "If only the cultivator who forged the Nanming Fire Furnace back then was still around. That was its old master, the only person it trusts. Only he could soothe it and get it to open its heart again to accept treatment."

"Old master..." an old alchemist muttered. "The creator of the Nanming Fire Furnace... That was one thousand three hundred years ago."

"Doesn't that mean..." another old alchemist's voice trembled, "it's hopeless?"

Tie Kuang gritted his teeth. "Perhaps there is one last method. We can only try it as a desperate last resort."

The old alchemists hurriedly pressed for details.

Tie Kuang explained, "Actually, the principle is simple. We need to find someone the Vermilion Bird spirit finds pleasing to the eye. Someone who can make it lower its guard and feel fondness. First, we soothe it, wait for it to calm down, rebuild its trust, and then slowly treat it."

"This..." The old alchemists looked at each other in dismay.

"The Vermilion Bird spirit already possesses complete intelligence; it has its own preferences and judgments," Tie Kuang said. "Maybe someone will just look pleasing to it, and it will be willing to get close to them. Maybe its intuition will tell it that someone won't hurt it. A person like this is, in fact, its medicine."

"But..." an old alchemist hesitated, "where do we find someone like that?"

Tie Kuang shook his head. "I don't know either. Maybe on Danxia Peak, maybe within the main sect gates, maybe in the Flying Cloud Kingdom, or maybe in some corner of the world. It's even possible that such a person doesn't exist under heaven."

"I am not it."

"Everything depends on the spirit itself."

Pausing for a moment, Tie Kuang added, "However, there are still some patterns to follow."

"First, a cultivator capable of regaining the Vermilion Bird spirit's trust likely won't have a high cultivation level. The higher the cultivation, the more threatened the artifact spirit will feel. How could they gain its trust then?"

"Second, since the Vermilion Bird spirit originated from an alchemy furnace, it should naturally feel closer to alchemy, or to Fire Element cultivators."

The old alchemists looked at each other again.

"Report it upward," the leading old alchemist let out a long sigh. "We cannot make the decision on this matter."

Tie Kuang nodded, giving a final warning: "This must be done quickly. The Vermilion Bird spirit won't hold on for much longer."

Green Grass Peak.

At the top of the peak was a wide, open stone platform. Twelve stone pillars stood around its edge, and at the top of each pillar burned a bronze lamp. The flames were a faint blue, casting a cold, desolate light over the entire platform.

Around the stone platform, a group of cultivators watched, the atmosphere lively.

This session of the Rising Cloud Minor Trial had already reached its final stage.

Only two people remained, and Ning Zhuo was one of them.

His opponent was an elderly man.

He was thin, with a slightly hunched back. His graying hair was casually tied up, with a few strands falling across his forehead.

His hands were as rough as tree bark, with large knuckles and an indelible soil color embedded under his fingernails.

However, his eyes were very bright - as bright as freshly dug ginger, carrying an earthy aura.

His original name wasn't widely known; at least, Ning Zhuo didn't know it. He only knew his title was Old Man Baicao.

The presiding cultivator announced the start.

Ning Zhuo walked over to his assigned alchemy platform and first opened the pill recipe.

The Fire Origin Pill. A mid-grade medicinal pill specifically refined for Fire Element cultivators. Taking it could increase Fire Element spiritual energy and nourish Fire Element meridians. The main ingredient was Crimson Flame Grass, and there were seven supplementary ingredients: Fire Lingzhi, Vermilion Fruit, Sun-Scorched Flower, Earthfire Root, Lava Moss, Phoenix Blood Extract, and Red Copper Ore Powder.

Ning Zhuo then flipped to the additional requirement - Water Element physique.

He immediately frowned.

Merely refining a Fire Origin Pill was not difficult at all.

During his lifetime, Jia Luanzhen had refined no fewer than a hundred furnaces of it.

The Crimson Flame Grass needed to be vigorously urged with a martial fire; the Fire Lingzhi had to be slowly stewed with a civil fire; the Vermilion Fruit had to be added later; the stamen of the Sun-Scorched Flower had to be removed first; the Earthfire Root needed to be sliced; the Lava Moss had to be ground into powder; the Phoenix Blood Extract needed to be dissolved in water; the Red Copper Ore Powder had to be sifted... All these memories and experiences now belonged to Ning Zhuo.

However, the content of the trial was to first modify the Fire Origin Pill recipe so that it could be consumed by someone with a Water Element physique, and only then proceed with the refinement.

Changing a Fire Element pill to a Water Element one was highly difficult. Water and fire conflicted, and the medicinal properties clashed. The slightest carelessness would result in a ruined batch.

Ning Zhuo read through the pill recipe from beginning to end once more.

He pondered in his heart, "The Crimson Flame Grass cannot be touched. It's the main ingredient. If I change this, it can't even be called a Fire Origin Pill anymore."

"The supplementary ingredients' ratios can be adjusted, replaced, added, or reduced."

"Among them, the Fire Lingzhi is the primary supplementary ingredient. Its medicinal property is extremely hot. If a Water Element cultivator were to take it, it would be like drinking boiling water. It must be replaced."

So, what should it be replaced with?

Ning Zhuo closed his eyes and fell deep into thought.

Jia Luanzhen's memories continuously surged in his mind...

"Among Water Element medicinal ingredients, the only thing that can replace the Fire Lingzhi is the Cold Pool Lotus Heart. Its medicinal nature is extremely cold but mild, entering the kidney meridian, which perfectly suits a Water Element physique. But suddenly adding a cold medicine into the Fire Origin Pill will disrupt the balance of the entire recipe. With the fiery nature of the Fire Lingzhi replaced by the cold nature of the Cold Pool Lotus Heart, the other supplementary ingredients must be adjusted accordingly."

Ning Zhuo picked up a brush and made marks on the pill recipe.

The Vermilion Fruit is hot in nature; its quantity must be reduced.

The Sun-Scorched Flower is fierce in nature; it must be removed.

The Earthfire Root is dry in nature; it can be replaced with Profound Ice Root.

The Lava Moss is warm in nature; it can be replaced with Water Mica.

The Phoenix Blood Extract is warm in nature; should I try replacing it with Ice Silkworm Silk first?

Stroke by stroke, Ning Zhuo made changes faster and faster. When he reached the seventh supplementary ingredient, he suddenly stopped his brush.

A ripple of intuition arose in his heart, making him feel that something was wrong.

"Something is not quite right..."

Ning Zhuo valued this feeling. He possessed the profound truths of Artifact Refining, and distinguishing and pondering the medicinal properties of herbs fell within its sphere of influence.

He began to rethink it, scrutinizing it strictly.

Time ticked by, and a thin layer of sweat gradually beaded on the large-headed youth's forehead.

By the time Ning Zhuo finished modifying the recipe, he looked up at the opposite side.

Old Man Baicao was already selecting his herbs.

The old cultivator moved unhurriedly, carrying a sense of calm composure. Whenever he picked up an herb, he would often first roll it between his fingertips, then bring his fingers or the herb itself to his nose to sniff. If that wasn't enough, he would even stick out his tongue to give it a lick.

His experience was too rich. The journey of cultivating this alchemy art practically spanned his entire life.

His tongue could accurately distinguish the age of the herbs, his nose could even sniff out their region of origin, and under his fingers, the moisture, hardness, and toughness of the herbs had nowhere to hide.

He stopped watching Old Man Baicao. Taking a deep breath, he couldn't help but feel a strong sense of urgency.

Fortunately, Jia Luanzhen's lifelong memories and experiences, as well as Ning Zhuo's own foundation in Artifact Refining, were very real.

Ning Zhuo finished selecting his herbs and began refining the pill.

The first furnace, he failed.

Although a pill was formed, its appearance was too poor, and its efficacy was less than ten percent. Ning Zhuo did not hesitate and directly threw it out.

For the second furnace, he immediately began making adjustments.

Quite a few pills were formed.

On the third furnace, he reached the final stage. Seeing that there wasn't enough time, he could only temporarily change his alchemy technique and use a fierce fire.

Ultimately, even fewer pills were formed, but their quality was a notch higher than the second batch.

However, when his medicinal pills were placed next to Old Man Baicao's results for comparison, anyone could tell that the latter's were far superior.

The watching cultivators murmured among themselves, finding Ning Zhuo's failure to be quite a novelty.

Ning Zhuo remained calm and took the initiative to cup his fists in congratulation to Old Man Baicao.

Old Man Baicao did not look down on Ning Zhuo because of his defeat; instead, he viewed him in an even better light. "Little Friend Ning Zhuo, there's no need to be disheartened."

"I myself am at the peak of the Golden Core stage, while you only have Foundation Establishment cultivation."

"In this alchemy session, my spiritual energy and alchemy spells far exceed yours. It is not surprising at all that I won."

"Little Friend Ning Zhuo, you have considerable potential in alchemy. In the future, you will definitely achieve greatness."

"It's just that alchemy has always been a slow, grinding effort. You cannot rush it, you must not rush it."

Ning Zhuo bowed with cupped hands. "Thank you for your guidance, Senior."

Old Man Baicao nodded with a smile, looking at Ning Zhuo with a strange light in his eyes. He had long heard of Ning Zhuo's reputation as a genius, but this was their first meeting. Finding Ning Zhuo completely free of arrogance and quite grounded secretly surprised him while also generating a lot of goodwill.

Ning Zhuo returned to his bluestone cave dwelling and reviewed this session of the Rising Cloud Minor Trial in his mind.

Although he had lost, he didn't take it to heart.

Closing his eyes, he recalled every detail of today's trial. Not just about himself, but also about his opponents.

Various alchemy techniques, herb identification methods, interactions between ingredients, and recipe modifications - all the related memories and experiences of others within Ning Zhuo's mind became vivid, dynamic, and solid through this firsthand experience.

"My alchemy skills have greatly improved!"

"Old Man Baicao is right. Where I truly lost was my insufficient cultivation. On the final, third furnace, I spent a lot of time resting and replenishing my spiritual energy. If I hadn't wasted that time, even if I still lost, the gap wouldn't have been this massive."

If his opponent had been a Foundation Establishment cultivator, Ning Zhuo would have had the confidence to win.

But Old Man Baicao was at the Golden Core stage, and at the peak of the Golden Core stage at that. He had cultivated for far longer than Ning Zhuo.

Therefore, it wasn't strange at all that Ning Zhuo lost.

The onlookers wouldn't feel it was unacceptable, either.

Ning Zhuo had already shown outstanding performance in other areas; expecting him to also be exceptional in alchemy would be too demanding of him.

What the audience found more confusing was why Ning Zhuo had suddenly changed direction to participate in an alchemy trial.

Unlike array formations and Artifact Refining, Ning Zhuo lacked the proper realm in alchemy. When facing a Golden Core-level alchemist, his disadvantage was glaringly obvious.

"In the future, I will definitely still lose more often than not."

Even so, as long as he could hone his alchemy skills, Ning Zhuo was happy to endure it.

He didn't lack the foundation now; he only lacked actual combat experience to verify those memories and experiences and make them his own. This in itself was a highly efficient form of digestion.

"Of course, it's still not as fast as the Unity of Knowledge and Action Technique."

This skill of alchemy definitely needed to be elevated. Ning Zhuo's long-term plan was clear: in the future, consuming medicinal pills would inevitably replace the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat to continue boosting his Physical Body cultivation.

He also wanted to keep a low profile and observe the aftermath of the mysterious cremated Nascent Soul.

Yet, on the second day, news regarding the Rising Cloud Minor Trial spread like wildfire through the main gate of the Myriad Manifestations Sect, stirring up a storm of public opinion rarely seen in past sessions!

Danxia Peak's Rising Cloud Minor Trial - The Furnace Spirit Chooses a Master.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect acted openly, directly providing a detailed explanation of the causes and consequences.

"Save the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit!"

"Whoever can gain its trust and save it will become the new master of the Nanming Fire Furnace."

"The Nanming Fire Furnace... That's a Spirit Treasure for alchemy."

Even Ning Zhuo couldn't help but feel his heart race.
