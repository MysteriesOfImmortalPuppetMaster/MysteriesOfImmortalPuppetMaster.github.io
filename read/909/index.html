Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 909: Widely Issuing Invitations

Immediately, Ban Jie gave a brief and concise overview of the current situation.

Ban Ji's yellowish-brown pupils contracted sharply, his face filled with astonishment.

He had painstakingly gone into secluded cultivation, devoting all his efforts to cultivating and exhausting his Hundred-Refined Golden Body Pills, all to enhance himself, raise his strength, and break through this Human Tribulation.

And now, to deal with Ning Zhuo, he would very likely have to face a massive Nascent Soul-level faction?!

How did things develop to this point?

"My secluded cultivation this time lasted for merely half a month, not three to five years!" For a moment, Ban Ji felt a sense of grievance and absurdity, as if he had nowhere to even voice his complaints.

A spirit treasure like the Naming Fire Furnace had actually fallen into Ning Zhuo's hands. And following this trend, its complete restoration was an absolute certainty.

This made Ban Ji find it even more unbelievable: "Am I the one with the Royal Mandate, or are you? Why do I feel like your luck is so much better than mine?!"

It took quite a while before Ban Ji accepted this reality. His mood barely settling down, his gaze turned gloomy: "Ning Zhuo wanting to rely on the Naming Fire Furnace to rally creditors and conquer Flowing Cloud Peak is far too reckless. He will definitely end up smashing his head and bleeding."

"When he weakens, that will be the day I strike him down."

Ban Ji naturally hoped for Ning Zhuo's failure.

Ban Jie held a different view in her heart. In her eyes, Ning Zhuo's method was extremely brilliant and very likely to succeed.

However, in front of Ban Ji, she naturally wouldn't say this aloud.

The next moment, she handed over the heavy treasure entrusted by the family to Ban Ji: "Your father requested this from the family for you."

Ban Ji was slightly surprised. Upon seeing this heavy treasure, he immediately revealed a shocked expression.

He was thoroughly familiar with the Ban clan's foundation, so he immediately recognized it and blurted out, "Ah, it's actually the Gold-Silk Silver-Shuttle Plate?"

The Gold-Silk Silver-Shuttle Plate was composed of two matching items: the first was a bronze plate, and the second was a silver shuttle.

The bronze plate was perfectly round, about one foot and six inches in diameter, and three and a half inches thick. Its base was cast from thousand-year ancient copper, appearing cyan with hints of crimson. Its surface was mottled and diverse, resembling the patterns of turquoise or the cracks on a turtle shell - this was the "copper coat" formed by the passage of time seeping into the bronze bones.

Ban Ji received it with both hands and lightly tapped the plate with his finger. The plate vibrated slightly, emitting a clear and melodious sound like a lithophone, the lingering echoes gracefully curling and refusing to dissipate for a long time.

The face of the plate was divided into outer and inner boundaries.

The outer boundary was about three inches wide, inlaid with the Twenty-Eight Mansions star map using a silver inlay technique. The star positions were outlined with pure silver threads, its silver light as clear and cool as autumn water, forming a charming contrast with the cyan-crimson copper hue. Every single star could be slightly rotated, producing a crisp sound like pearls falling onto a jade plate. These were used to set up the "formation layout" for weaving.

The inner boundary was the domain of weaving, about nine inches in diameter. At the bottom of this weaving domain, hexagonal tortoise-shell grids were cast. The grids were as neat as a honeycomb, and every corner was smoothly rounded so as not to damage any threads. Exactly in the center of every hexagonal grid lay a "Qi aperture," the very pivot where the spiritual charm of the bronze plate gathered.

Suspended within the weaving domain was a silver shuttle.

Its shape resembled a crescent moon or a willow leaf, a bit over four inches long, and about seven-tenths of an inch at its widest. The silver material of the shuttle body was as dense as steel, yet a layer of natural frost-flower patterns grew on its surface. Touching it felt as smooth as fat, and looking at it was like watching moonlight flowing.

Ban Jie spoke up at this time, "The family has already performed the rites to infuse your aura into it. You can control it freely right now."

Ban Ji attempted to activate it, channeling his spiritual energy, projecting his divine sense, and manipulating the silver shuttle.

The silver shuttle slowly moved, and golden radiance flowed from its tail. The golden light condensed, transforming into gold silk that stretched across the weaving domain.

As Ban Ji grew more proficient, the silver shuttle's speed increased, weaving between the Qi apertures like a boat gliding over water or a swallow darting through willows. Each time it passed a Qi aperture, that aperture would slowly sink, becoming unusable. Meanwhile, other Qi apertures would rise up, available for crossing. In this manner, the Qi apertures rose and fell in succession, and the golden threads became endless.

Suddenly, the silver shuttle trembled lightly. Ban Ji decisively stopped the shuttle to inspect it and immediately found the mistake. He corrected it at once and continued to drive the silver shuttle forward.

A moment later, the gold silk interlaced into a net and detached on its own. The silver shuttle hovered within the plate's inner boundary, while the golden net fell outside the plate and into Ban Ji's hands.

Ban Ji channeled his spiritual energy and casually tossed it.

The golden net expanded in the air, descended from the sky, and covered a boulder. Then, it contracted fiercely, directly shredding the boulder into countless fragments.

Ban Ji laughed heartily: "Wonderful, wonderful! A Dao Artifact is indeed profoundly mystical and utterly extraordinary!"

That's right, the Gold-Silk Silver-Shuttle Plate was a Dao Artifact!

Its function was to weave a golden silk dharma net. The tier of the dharma net could be a Magic Artifact or a magic treasure. The effects of the dharma net varied, depending on the Formation the cultivator employed while weaving it.

For instance, the Formation Ban Ji had used gave the dharma net offensive power. One could also use other Formations, allowing the dharma net to cover and lock down enemies, or even turn into an illusionary array to trap them.

Since it was a Dao Artifact, the Gold-Silk Silver-Shuttle Plate inherently possessed Dao Rhyme. Whenever a cultivator made a weaving mistake that violated the principles of weaving or Formations, it would trigger a vibration from the silver shuttle to alert the cultivator, enabling timely corrections.

There is a poem praising it:

Bones forged of bronze for years unknown, frost-silver shuttle pierces clouds and smoke.

Qi apertures guide the way leaving no room to stray, a single tremor of a wrong pass awakens the sleeping fool.

Golden threads weave a heavenly net, gathered in one palm, capturing myriad mysteries.

Do not claim this artifact lacks killing might; trapping dragons and locking phoenixes, it surpasses blades and whips!

Ban Ji put away the golden silk dharma net, unable to contain his joy. With shining eyes, he looked at the Gold-Silk Silver-Shuttle Plate again, his palms constantly rubbing it, finding it hard to put down.

Ban Jie watched with a smile, staying silent.

Ban Ji gradually stopped his hands, frowning slightly: "What's going on? Why did my father suddenly go and request the clan's foundation for me? And such a heavy artifact at that! Why would the family agree?"

Ban Jie said, "Your Human Tribulation is bizarre and eccentric; the difficulty of surviving it has skyrocketed, so the family naturally had to step in."

Ban Ji grew even more confused: "Although a Dao Artifact is strong, weaving them one by one, I can at most produce Magic Artifact-level golden silk dharma nets. What can that do to Ning Zhuo?"

Ban Jie chuckled and finally revealed the real reason: "The family gave you this Dao Artifact not so you could produce dharma nets. Just as you said, no matter how many Magic Artifact-level dharma nets you make, they won't help the current predicament."

"The family wants you to use this Dao Artifact to quickly master a weaving technique called the [Twisted Roots and Tangled Branches Weave]."

Ban Ji: "[Twisted Roots and Tangled Branches Weave]? Why?"

Ban Jie said, "Old Monster Can Lv has set up a Rising Cloud Minor Trial. Those who pass it can receive his teachings of the [Shattered Mechanism Defect-Mending Art]. As long as you master the [Twisted Roots and Tangled Branches Weave], there is a high chance you can pass the minor trial and obtain this crucial Mechanical Art!"

Ban Ji finally understood: "So this is the family's arrangement? Is the [Shattered Mechanism Defect-Mending Art] very helpful to me?"

Ban Jie nodded affirmatively.

The truth was: the Shattered Mechanism Defect-Mending Art was just as important as the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Tube], the Void Spider Silk, and the Blind Fool Divine Art. They could all systematically stimulate Ban Ji's Royal Mandate step by step!

Unfortunately, both the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Tube] and the Void Spider Silk had fallen into Ning Zhuo's hands. Ban Ji's acquisition of the Blind Fool Divine Art had only stimulated the Royal Mandate by a single step.

This time, with Ning Zhuo rallying creditors, establishing an alliance, and charging the peak, he was definitely tied up and lacked the bandwidth to clone himself.

The Ban clan was seizing this great opportunity by lending out the Dao Artifact to help Ban Ji secure the [Shattered Mechanism Defect-Mending Art], further stimulating his Royal Mandate by another step!

The Ban clan was busy repairing the clan's destiny pivot chain. With the Human Tribulation growing stronger and harder to cross, they had to make Ban Ji stronger.

"Good! Then I will diligently cultivate and practice hard. Using the Gold-Silk Silver-Shuttle Plate, I'll master the Twisted Roots and Tangled Branches Weave as quickly as possible and obtain the Shattered Mechanism Defect-Mending Art!" Ban Ji decided.

He thought for a moment and added: "Ning Zhuo's growth is a massive threat to me. But the one who triggered such a Human Tribulation was Pi Fujie. We absolutely cannot let that guy off easily!"

"Ban Jie, help me contact him. If Ning Zhuo is going to grow powerful, we must also form alliances and strategies to counter him."

Ban Ji had been meticulously groomed since childhood, so he possessed a certain degree of foresight and perspective. However, his political acumen wasn't profound enough to see that this move was doomed to fail and practically equal to useless effort.

Ban Jie did not try to persuade him. She knew that persuasion would be ineffective; only when facts were laid bare before his eyes would Ban Ji fully realize it.

She immediately nodded: "Alright, I'll get to it right away."

Holding the Dao Artifact, Ban Ji dove back into his cave dwelling and went into secluded bitter cultivation.

Ban Jie knew the outcome of contacting Pi Fujie and didn't want to waste too much effort, so she simply sent a flying letter.

Not long after, the flying letter landed in the hands of a young cultivator.

The person had a head full of gray hair, eye sockets sunken like a skull, and was draped in a wide, large dharma robe sewn from the skins of a hundred pythons. It was none other than Pi Fujie.

Pi Fujie swept it with his divine sense, coldly snorted, and casually tossed the flying letter aside.

"I originally formed a grudge with Ning Zhuo because of Shen Xi. How was I to know this guy was so strong?"

"Fortunately, I was smart enough to lure Ban Ji out to deal with Ning Zhuo. As a result, those two are now locking horns."

"Wonderful!"

"Ning Zhuo previously defeated Gu Qing, and his pillar of righteous Qi startled the entire sect, earning such high regard from Zhong Dao! Now he has the Naming Fire Furnace and even wants to form an alliance to attack the peak. Even if his alliance fails, trying to deal with him means making an enemy out of over a dozen cultivators! Only a fool would do such a thing."

"I need to distance myself from Ban Ji and figure out a way to establish relations with Ning Zhuo, turning an enemy into a friend."

"Ning Zhuo's side has already started widely issuing invitations. I have to get my hands on one and go congratulate him when the alliance is established!"

The Pi clan originated from the Demonic Path before washing their hands clean to become legitimate. Pi Fujie was even more capable of bending and stretching, utterly shameless.

In order to form the alliance, Ning Zhuo first spread rumors to test the waters.

The testing was further divided into two steps.

The first step was circulating the news of establishing an alliance. His primary focus was observing the Demon Subjugation Hall, but the latter showed no reaction.

The second step was to leak further information about the location. Ning Zhuo had booked out the Heavenly Treasure Pavilion. The reactions from all sides were positive.

Seeing this, Ning Zhuo formally issued invitations, inviting cultivators from all factions to attend the ceremony.

Cao Gui finished his day's busy work and returned to his residence.

He didn't take off his outer robe and rest for a bit like he usually did. Instead, he immediately sat by the table and took an invitation out from his robes.

This was exactly the viewing ceremony invitation Ning Zhuo had sent him.

Cao Gui unfolded the invitation and read the text.

Ning Zhuo bows his head and respectfully salutes you:

This untalented one, originally a rural youth, chanced upon a fortuitous encounter, inheriting the Naming Fire Furnace at the Danxia Minor Trial. To be entrusted by the artifact spirit was truly beyond my expectations; whenever I think of it, I cannot help but feel trepidation and sweat in shame.

Today, I secretly harbor a thought, and I dare to lay bare my heart.

I have observed Flowing Cloud Peak: its terrain is majestic and expansive, its spiritual veins stretch continuously, and it has always been an important domain of the sect.

Overestimating my own capabilities, I wish to establish a small stronghold on the peak, naming it 'Naming'. I dare not boast of establishing a great lineage, but truly only wish to gather three to five fellow Daoists to settle down with the furnace, nurture pills, plant bamboo, and read books...

I am but a latecomer to Foundation Establishment, shallow in seniority and young in age; I inherently shouldn't harbor such presumptuous thoughts. I only consider that matters of the world cannot be achieved by the wisdom of one person, nor lifted by the strength of a single individual...

If I am favored with your esteemed presence to attend the banquet and watch, or offer a word or two of guidance, then I shall be immensely fortunate, and the Naming Stronghold shall be immensely fortunate.

Cao Gui had actually read it many times already, yet he still sighed in admiration at this moment: "'Zhai' (Stronghold) sounds like 'Zhai' (Debt), and 'Naming' refers to the furnace. Naming Stronghold is indeed fitting."

"On the surface, a modest gentleman; in his bones, majestic nobility; in his soul, grand ambition and wild aspirations."

Cao Gui's fingers gently rubbed the edges of the invitation.

He recalled his encounters today.

The invitation was delivered to his hands in the morning. By noon, the Cao clan had sent someone to pass a message, telling him to keep up the good work. In the afternoon, his superior in the Commerce Hall had called him into the duty room, amiably inquired about his work, and praised his outstanding communication skills.

Cao Gui naturally knew that all these changes were due to this invitation. Or rather, they were because of Ning Zhuo.

"To be able to encounter Young Master Ning Zhuo is truly my honor," Cao Gui sighed softly.

Three invitations appeared simultaneously.

Lu Xuan sat on the left, his cyan robes washed until they were faded white. His hands rested folded on his lap, his back as straight as a pine. His gaze fell upon the invitation, his brows tightly furrowed.

Gu Huaijiu sat on the right. He half-leaned against the back of the chair, one hand resting on the armrest, the other pinching the edge of the invitation. His gaze was sharp yet somber, like an unsheathed sword.

Yun Niaoniao sat in the middle. Dressed in a plain white dress, a medicine pouch hung from her waist. The pouch was bulging, emitting a faint herbal fragrance. She held the invitation with both hands, subconsciously pinching it so hard that her fingertips turned slightly white.

Yun Niaoniao sighed softly, "I don't understand. Why does he want to establish an alliance? He clearly knows... Hall Master Zhong values him so highly. To lend out the White Rainbow Righteous Integrity Bone twice, what kind of immense regard is that? He..."

Gu Huaijiu said, "His ambitions do not lie there."

Yun Niaoniao's voice tightened, "But that is Flowing Cloud Peak, one of the eight great main peaks. Fellow Daoist Ning Zhuo only has Foundation Establishment cultivation."

"He has a Naming Fire Furnace," Lu Xuan suddenly interjected. "With the furnace in hand, creditors can become allies. This game of chess he is playing is not small."

Yun Niaoniao: "Then should we attend the viewing ceremony?"

The room fell into silence.

The three of them had all resolved to join the Demon Subjugation Hall. During the Demon Subjugation Hall's minor trial, they had formed a friendship with Ning Zhuo.

But now that Ning Zhuo's alliance establishment and peak charge were a foregone conclusion, it was obvious he had cast Hall Master Zhong Dao's high regard to the back of his mind. This forced the three to ponder over this crucial point. The feather-light invitation revealed an extraordinary weight.

Lu Xuan suddenly stood up and walked to the window.

Outside the window, the moonlight was like water. The silhouette of Flowing Cloud Peak loomed in the night, like a dormant giant beast.

"I won't go," he said. His voice wasn't loud, but it was resolute and decisive.

He had thought it through clearly: His friendship with Ning Zhuo was shallow. How could he risk a bleak future in the Demon Subjugation Hall because of this kid?

"Fellow Daoist Lu..." Yun Niaoniao had just opened her mouth.

"I will go," Gu Huaijiu suddenly said, his voice deep and hoarse.

He and Ning Zhuo had felt like old friends from their very first meeting, to the point where he had even entrusted Ning Zhuo with the crucial matter of resolving his military dissolution.

If such a friendship were to be explained, outsiders would find it hard to comprehend.

Gu Huaijiu stood up and tucked the invitation into his sleeve: "I bid you two farewell."

He turned and left without a hint of hesitation.

Lu Xuan: "I'm leaving too."

Leaving Yun Niaoniao all alone, holding the invitation tightly, plunged into deep hesitation.
