Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 860: Sect Calamity

The main gate of the Myriad Manifestations Sect.

Danxia Peak.

This was the most important alchemy chamber on Danxia Peak.

Built atop an Earth Fire spirit vein, it used Ten-Thousand-Year Profound Iron for its walls, Nine Nether Cold Jade for its doors, and the fire of the earth's core for its fuel.

In the center of the alchemy chamber stood a three-zhang-tall bronze alchemy furnace. The entire text of the True Explanation of the Alchemy Cauldron was engraved on its body. A lifelike Vermilion Bird coiled atop its lid, and its bottom connected to the Earth Fire spirit vein, burning day and night without pause.

Several old alchemists sat cross-legged around the furnace. They had completely white hair and beards, wore gray robes, and were surrounded by a rich alchemical aura.

They had a clear division of labor: some kept their divine sense lingering inside the furnace, some controlled the formation, some continuously poured their spiritual energy into the furnace, some controlled the scale of the earth fire, and others manipulated the Vermilion Bird fire.

They were refining the Nine Revolutions Soul Return Pill. The refinement had been ongoing for over eight years; they were just one month away from reaching exactly nine years.

And this would be the exact moment the pill formed and the furnace opened.

Everything was proceeding in an orderly manner.

But right at this moment.

Boom!

The alchemy furnace suddenly shook violently.

An inexplicable spatial tremor triggered the formation.

By sheer coincidence, the power of the earth vein became chaotic at the exact same time, causing the earth fire to slip out of control.

Instantly, the earth fire inside the furnace surged madly, the runes on its body flickered wildly, and the Vermilion Bird on the lid let out a miserable, piercing cry.

The old alchemists' expressions drastically changed in unison, and they hurriedly took action to suppress it.

"Steady! We must keep it steady!" someone growled.

BOOM-!

The alchemy furnace exploded.

Fragments scattered and flew everywhere, blasting the alchemy chamber beyond recognition. A terrifying, massive amount of earth fire gushed out from the bottom of the furnace, threatening to swallow everything.

Fortunately, at the critical juncture, the formation in the alchemy chamber played a crucial role, saving the lives of the alchemists.

When the blast wave subsided and the smoke and dust gradually cleared, the shaken old alchemists looked at the shattered remains before them, their faces completely ashen.

What pained them the most was that alchemy furnace.

Only a small half of the alchemy furnace remained after the explosion. The Vermilion Bird from the lid was on its last breath, collapsed on the ground. Countless motes of light continuously dissipated outward from its body.

"Oh no!"

"The Nanming Fire Furnace is severely damaged. The artifact spirit of the furnace is about to be wiped out!"

"Act quickly! We cannot let the artifact spirit die!"

The Nanming Fire Furnace was of the Spirit Treasure grade. It was one of the foundational assets of Danxia Peak and was priceless. In past years, it had always undertaken the heaviest, and often the most valuable, alchemy tasks for Danxia Peak.

Once the artifact spirit was completely annihilated, this heavy treasure would drop from the Spirit Treasure grade down to the magic treasure grade.

The bigger problem was that once it dropped, even if they used countless methods to remedy it in the future, they might not be able to elevate this magic treasure back into a Spirit Treasure.

Nurturing an artifact spirit was truly too difficult and was filled with tremendous unpredictability.

It could be said that the destruction of the entire alchemy chamber and the collapse of the formation that cost millions were not as great a loss as the death of the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit.

The old alchemists hurriedly used various methods to salvage it.

Large quantities of precious Medicinal Pills and medicinal powders were sprinkled onto the artifact spirit.

Under their frantic rescue efforts, the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit eventually shrank into a tiny bird, only the size of a baby's fist, resembling a newly hatched chick. Its entire body was the color of fire, with only a small tuft of golden feathers on its fluffy head.

One old alchemist struggled to crawl over and carefully cupped the artifact spirit in his palms. The artifact spirit trembled slightly and let out an incredibly faint and soft whimper, like a dying infant.

The rest of the old alchemists quickly gathered around.

They projected their divine sense, their observation movements incredibly cautious, terrified that this frail artifact spirit wouldn't be able to withstand it.

After the crowd inspected it, their expressions fluctuated with uncertainty.

Although they had saved the artifact spirit, it was only temporary.

"Quickly go invite Iron Madman, the Hall Master of the Artifact Refining Hall!"

"We must repair the furnace as soon as possible to stabilize the artifact spirit's existence."

The current artifact spirit was too weak, like a tiny flame in a fierce gale, on the verge of going out.

Myriad Beasts Peak, Spirit Beast Garden.

This was the most core area of Myriad Beasts Peak, raising various spirit beasts that the Myriad Manifestations Sect had collected over hundreds of years.

At this very moment, the Spirit Beast Garden was in utter chaos.

A Blue Luan soared into the sky, its wings flapping and stirring up howling gales.

A Black Tortoise frantically rammed into the garden wall, causing the entire structure to collapse.

A Crimson Flame Tiger was surrounded by surging flames, devouring everything in its vicinity.

The cultivators tried their hardest to remedy the situation, but they still couldn't stabilize it.

Heavy Formation Peak.

The runes of the Return to Sect Formation began to crack, and soon after, fissures spread out from the formation core.

The cracks grew more numerous and denser. The formation's light began to dim, and the power of its restrictions started to dissipate.

The array-guarding elder opened his eyes and sighed softly.

The Return to Sect Formation was one of the foundational assets of the Myriad Manifestations Sect, but after years of disrepair, its collapse was only a matter of time. He hadn't expected it to happen today.

The mighty cultivator who had initially laid down this formation had long passed away. Since then, no matter how large the Myriad Manifestations Sect had grown, no talent capable of setting up this formation had emerged.

Now, this unique masterwork of a formation was fading away.

Sword Resonance Peak, Hidden Sword Pavilion.

The renowned sword [Evil Wind] underwent an anomaly, broke free from its restraints, and shot into the sky.

This famous flying sword was the lifebound flying sword of the founding ancestor of Sword Resonance Peak. Forged from Ten-Thousand-Year Profound Iron, it had accompanied the founding ancestor throughout their life, slaying demons and eradicating evil, establishing illustrious merits.

Not only did the renowned sword [Evil Wind] leave, but by breaking through the formation and restrictions, it caused other famous swords to act up as well, resulting in six more swords flying out after it.

Everything happened too fast!

By the time the guarding cultivators reacted, it was already too late.

The guards on duty collapsed onto the ground in terror, their faces full of despair. This was an extremely severe dereliction of duty!

Hundred Herbs Peak.

Vermilion Fruit Medicine Garden.

A ten-thousand-year-old Vermilion Fruit Tree was currently undergoing tribulation.

Crack.

Lightning struck the fruit tree, causing its trunk to shake violently. It was immediately split in two, and raging tribulation fire began to burn on the tree's branches.

The three cultivators watching let out a synchronized sigh.

Aiding someone else during a lightning tribulation was a severe taboo. Unless one possessed an exquisite method, attempting to help would only result in both parties being implicated.

The ten-thousand-year-old Vermilion Fruit Tree had wanted to take human form and take a step further, but it ultimately failed on the verge of success, unable to block the final bolt of lightning.

When the tribulation fire dissipated, only the root portion of the entire fruit tree remained somewhat intact. More than half of its branches and trunk had been burned to a crisp. Not a single fruit was left.

……

Within the main gate of the Myriad Manifestations Sect, all sorts of terrible things happened at almost the exact same time. Various pieces of bad news poured in, ultimately gathering in Dong Chen's hands.

Seeing these losses, Dong Chen felt pained, but he wasn't surprised.

In the recent clash of Qi Luck, although the Myriad Manifestations Sect had won, it was a pyrrhic victory.

In this battle, the Myriad Manifestations Sect had not only utilized three fate-suppressing magic treasures to assist the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy, but Dong Chen had also summoned a large number of the Myriad Manifestations Sect's high-level cultivators.

These people were now mostly recuperating from their injuries.

Injuries to the physical body and soul could be healed quickly. However, the decline in their respective Qi Luck would require a long time to recover from.

The bad news Dong Chen received was clearly concentrated in the Eight Peaks and Sixteen Halls.

"Among the sixteen halls, the Demon Subjugation Hall's loss in Qi Luck is actually the smallest."

"Among the Eight Peaks, the one with the smallest loss of Qi Luck is Flowing Cloud Peak, followed by Purple Lightning Peak."

Flowing Cloud Peak had never had a true Peak Master and was more like a pile of loose sand. Purple Lightning Peak had been weak for a long time, retaining only the glamorous facade of the former Four Great Peaks. Moreover, this time, the only high-level member they dispatched was Vice Peak Master Meng Wuyan, who was also a Demon Cultivator.

It wasn't that the Myriad Manifestations Sect lacked Demon Cultivators, Soul Cultivators, or other such special cultivators, but they were fundamentally mostly of the Human Race.

The Human Race was the absolute mainstream in today's Cultivation World, bar none.

Dong Chen couldn't help but ponder in his heart.

After consecutively using three fate-suppressing heavy treasures, Dong Chen noticed the situation turning bad and decisively changed his strategy in secret. He had originally thought that relying solely on himself, Tuoba Huang, and one other - just the three of them - would be enough to hold the line.

As a result, the battle situation grew increasingly dire.

Dong Chen chose not to continue using fate-suppressing heavy treasures; instead, he gathered more and more high-level members.

He was the Peak Master of Heavy Formation Peak and also the current Sect Master of the Myriad Manifestations Sect. He couldn't just let the Qi Luck of a few peaks decline. If they were going to fight a major war, they had to treat everyone equally!

When the intensity of the Qi Luck struggle exceeded his original expectations, he decisively adjusted his strategy.

Being a good Sect Master was not that easy!

The current balance of power was something Dong Chen had painstakingly established after years of effort.

He didn't want this balance to be easily disrupted.

Once disrupted, it would inevitably cause turmoil, inevitably lead to a loss of his power, and cause him to lose control over the entire sect.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect was too big.

On the eve of his succession, Dong Chen had been summoned by the previous Sect Master.

Previous Sect Master: "Do you know why I ultimately chose you?"

Dong Chen: "This subordinate does not know."

Previous Sect Master: "It is because you can calm your mind, keep your composure, and control the overall situation. You must remember, one of the true opponents of the Myriad Manifestations Sect is ourselves."

Yes, after Dong Chen took charge of the sect, he agreed with this even more deeply.

Because the Myriad Manifestations Sect was too big.

Once control was lost, this behemoth would tear itself apart, biting and devouring each other while attempting to use the name of righteousness to restore unity. And in this process, countless opportunists would dream of satisfying their own selfish desires.

At this moment, Dong Chen was deep in thought.

"The path of Qi Luck possesses an obscure divine might."

"The Cloud Prison of the Demon Subjugation Hall being attacked appears to be a disaster zone on the surface, but in reality, its loss of Qi Luck was the smallest. Compared to its surroundings, it will instead be blessed with good luck and become stronger!"

"In addition, the other seven peaks and sixteen halls have all experienced terrible situations. On the surface, this will also greatly offset the negative impact of the Cloud Prison being attacked and Demonic Cultivators escaping."

"The Demon Subjugation Hall still requires more suppression."

Toward Zhong Dao, who held onto the philosophy of eradicating all evil, Dong Chen kept a respectful distance, feeling both love and hate for him.

Without a doubt, Zhong Dao was the sharpest battle blade among the higher-ups of the Myriad Manifestations Sect. But precisely because he was too sharp, he was prone to cutting his own people.

The fundamental founding philosophy of the Myriad Manifestations Sect was 'the sea embraces all rivers.'

Just look at each Flying Cloud Assembly, and see how many Heretical Cultivators and Demonic Cultivators voluntarily join. Even if these people don't put in effort or contribute, under this ebb and flow, the Myriad Manifestations Sect loses countless enemies.

If Zhong Dao's style of doing things were allowed to take the upper hand, what would those Heretical Cultivators and Demonic Cultivators outside the sect think? How would they view a Righteous Path powerhouse like the Myriad Manifestations Sect?

Relying solely on absorbing cultivators from the Righteous Path, the Myriad Manifestations Sect would absolutely never have reached its current scale.

Dong Chen was very clear about this.

Most of the higher-ups also acknowledged this.

Therefore, they invariably joined hands to suppress the Demon Subjugation Hall.

This was a high-level conflict caused by different political strategies.

Because Zhong Dao and Duanmu Zhang were very close and shared similar philosophies.

Thus, Qin De was ultimately imprisoned in the Cloud Prison. This not only suppressed the development of the Confucian cultivator faction but could also sow discord between the two of them. It could be called killing two birds with one stone.

Apart from this, Dong Chen was also very cautious when distributing operational funds to the Demon Subjugation Hall.

Wasn't the Cloud Prison under the jurisdiction of the Demon Subjugation Hall?

Then Dong Chen and the others would try their best not to kill Demonic Cultivators, but merely lock them up in the Cloud Prison under the pleasant guise of 'reformation.' On one hand, this would increase the financial burden on the Demon Subjugation Hall, and on the other hand, it would allow the Myriad Manifestations Sect to continue building its good reputation to the outside world.

When the rest of the Heretical Cultivators and Demonic Cultivators saw that the higher-ups were lenient towards evil, sparing their lives and imprisoning them in the sect's cells without ever adopting a 'kill on sight' attitude, they would be more inclined to join the Myriad Manifestations Sect when an opportunity like the Flying Cloud Assembly arose.

"But this time, Song Taosheng's assassination attempt on Qin De has changed the past situation."

"Although the Demonic Cultivators in the Cloud Prison only suffered heavy losses on the ninth floor, these individuals had always been the highest priority. The cost to imprison and restrain them normally was also the highest."

The Demon Subjugation Hall's burden had been greatly reduced.

"Because Zhong Dao intervened to help, Wu An will definitely receive a light punishment despite the severity of the situation. He is Zhong Dao's subordinate and has publicly recognized merits, making it difficult for us to make a move."

"Zhong Dao, oh Zhong Dao, did you know about Song Taosheng's assassination attempt?"

Profound light continuously flickered in Dong Chen's eyes.

On the mountainside, a young cultivator at the Foundation Establishment Stage was traveling alone, his footsteps hurried. He wore the standard Daoist robe of a Myriad Manifestations Sect outer disciple, and an identity token hung at his waist, proving that he was acting on the sect's orders to carry out a certain mission outside.

"You're the one."

Qin De's Nascent Soul silently floated down, landing on top of the young cultivator's head. It trembled slightly, turning into a beam of white light that entered the Upper Dantian between the young cultivator's brows.

The young cultivator merely felt a slight itch between his brows and didn't think much of it. With his mind occupied by his current mission, he hurriedly rushed toward the teleportation formation at the peak of the mountain.

The teleportation formation at the mountain peak was not large; it could only teleport twenty people out of the sect at a time.

A long queue had already formed in front of the teleportation formation.

The young cultivator arrived and stood at the very back of the line.

Qin De's Nascent Soul lodged within his divine sea, observing everything around through the young cultivator's eyes.

The young cultivator remained completely oblivious.

The line slowly advanced until it was finally the young cultivator's turn.

He handed over his token.

The guarding cultivator took it, conducted a strict verification, and then nodded to let him pass.

For the next dozen or so steps, the young cultivator endured rigorous inspections from formations, magic treasures, and other methods with every step.

During this period, Qin De's Nascent Soul shrank into a tight ball, doing its utmost to conceal itself.

He was not exposed.

The young cultivator stood within the teleportation formation. Alongside him were other cultivators, as well as a carriage hitched to a mount.

The teleportation was about to begin.
