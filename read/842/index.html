Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 842: Supreme Grade

Qin De pursued his victory: "You say, 'If desires are impermanent, the mind is impermanent.' I say, 'If principles change, the mind also changes.' Using your spear to attack your shield - what does Mr. Zhao think of this?"

Zhao Hansheng fell silent.

Behind him, the other Confucian cultivators looked at each other, seeing the shock on one another's faces.

Zhao Hansheng had originally assumed that since Qin De had hastily created the Sage's Great Theft Scripture while imprisoned for so many years without seeing the light of day, how much depth could it possibly have, even if he integrated demonic studies into it?

But now, having launched a probing attack, he was surprised to discover that Qin De's demonic philosophy was not only self-consistent but also possessed a unique grandeur, like a great cultivation nation with strategic depth.

Zhao Hansheng's first round of attacks couldn't even probe the opponent's foundation.

Even more troublesome was that while Qin De wasn't speaking Confucian principles, he used the rhetoric of Confucian principles at every turn. He was extremely proficient in traditional Confucianism, so in this second debate, he was actually the one who knew the enemy better than Zhao Hansheng did.

Having failed the first round of attacks, Zhao Hansheng gritted his teeth and shifted direction, like dispatching another great army to attack from the left flank.

"You said just now, 'Where desire moves, that is action.' I ask you: If action arises where desire moves, then are killing and arson also acceptable actions? If where desire moves, there is no distinction between good and evil, and no separation of right and wrong, then how are humans different from beasts?"

"Mr. Zhao's words are a great fallacy," Qin De said unhurriedly. "When did I ever say there is no distinction between good and evil? When did I ever say there is no separation of right and wrong?"

Qin De continued: "Action arises where desire moves - however, actions can be good or evil, and thoughts can also be good or evil. The thought of killing and arson is an evil thought; where an evil thought moves, there is an evil deed. This is not a lack of good and evil, but treating thought as action and the mind as traces. What do you mean, 'how are humans different from beasts'? The desires of beasts do not know good from evil; the desires of humans know good and evil and can act upon them. This is the difference between man and beast; what need is there to say more?"

Zhao Hansheng's brows locked tight. He wanted to refute, but he needed to think deeply.

Qin De gave him no time and spoke again: "Your School of Mind says, 'The point where a thought arises is action.' How is this different from my theory? I merely pushed your theory to the extreme. You take thought as action; I take desire as mind. You take 'extending innate conscience' as your tenet; I take 'extending the desire-mind' as my method. The same furnace but different fire, the same source but different streams - why call it the Demonic Path?"

Hearing this, everyone was shaken physically and mentally.

Good heavens! As he spoke, the Demonic Path and the School of Mind seemed to become one family!

Zhao Hansheng's mouth was filled with bitterness.

He was shocked to discover that although Qin De had just come into contact with the School of Mind, after the first round of debate, he had already grasped its essence.

Every counterattack from Qin De was using his own spear to attack his own shield.

This wasn't a wild punch killing an old master; this was a precision strike born of knowing both oneself and the enemy.

The strength Qin De displayed far exceeded Zhao Hansheng's expectations.

But at this moment, Zhao Hansheng had no way of retreat!

Behind him stood not only Gu Qing but also Chu Xuangui, Song Taosheng, Situ Gu, and others.

"I cannot retreat!"

"I must win!!"

Zhao Hansheng grit his teeth, urging his Confucian techniques. His thoughts moved like lightning as he racked his brain to struggle against Qin De.

Zhao Hansheng attempted to find flaws in Qin De, launching attacks time and again.

Every counterattack from Qin De was highly effective, allowing him to firmly occupy the upper hand in the debate.

Xiao Juxia did not have the opportunity to participate in the trial and was already situated in a cave dwelling he had rented, unable to approach the heavily guarded Cloud Prison. However, since he had already made contact with Qin De, he secretly circulated techniques at this moment, easily taking the entire debate into his view.

Xiao Juxia nodded slightly: "This Qin De is not bad. He is a good seedling for cultivating the Demonic Path!"

"So when I met Ning Zhuo, a thought arose, and I foresaw that helping this lad would be beneficial to my goals. In the end, it fell upon Qin De."

"It is laughable that Zhao Hansheng has consistently underestimated Qin De, not anticipating that his demonic studies come from the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art! Its demonic theory is profound to the extreme, far exceeding his imagination!"

If Zhao Hansheng chose to defend, he could still maintain a stalemate; at least the situation wouldn't look so ugly.

But his active offense, attempting to dismantle the demonic studies Xiao Juxia possessed, was completely overestimating himself!

He was already the weaker party with fewer forces, yet he insisted on attacking actively, and attacking a heavily fortified stronghold at that. Naturally, he would gain no advantage.

The more Zhao Hansheng debated, the paler his face became. Not only was cold sweat dripping from his forehead, but his body began to teeter.

In contrast, Qin De could be said to be brimming with talent; no wonder he could create the Sage's Great Theft Scripture. Aided by the debate, his thoughts became increasingly agile. He constantly utilized his knowledge, and the more he used it, the deeper his understanding of the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art became. The deeper his understanding, the more forceful his debate.

Qin De was even secretly holding back his power, trying to counterattack from different angles and letting go of the huge flaws Zhao Hansheng exposed.

He wanted to use this debate to understand the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art even more profoundly!

"The child is teachable!" Seeing this scene, Xiao Juxia stroked his beard, feeling quite gratified.

"No, I cannot lose!" Zhao Hansheng screamed in his heart, his nervousness causing him to clench his fists involuntarily.

"Desire cannot be indulged!" His eyes widened, staring at Qin De as if looking at a fierce flood or savage beast. "As it is said, when joy, anger, sorrow, and happiness have not yet been aroused, it is called the Mean; when they are aroused and all attain due measure, it is called Harmony..."

Gu Qing was also sweating all over from nervousness. Seeing this, his spirit lifted: "Teacher is counterattacking!"

Qin De listened quietly until the end, then replied nonchalantly: "The state where anger, sorrow, and happiness are not yet aroused is Nature. Nature is Principle, and Principle is Desire. Before arousal, Principle and Desire are one body. When aroused and attaining due measure, it is Principle; when aroused and not attaining due measure, it is Desire. You separate Principle and Desire into two, but do you not know that before arousal, there is originally no distinction?"

Gu Qing was stunned.

Zhao Hansheng counterattacked again later: "The human mind is perilous, the Dao mind is subtle; have absolute refinement and focus, and hold fast to the Mean..."

Gu Qing cried out in his heart: "Brilliant!"

Qin De remained composed: "The human mind and the Dao mind are not two minds. The human mind is Desire; the Dao mind is Principle. Principle and Desire share the same source; one mind with two uses. You forcefully divide them into two, splitting one body; I combine them into one, returning to the origin. Who is right and who is wrong is clear at a glance."

Zhao Hansheng was dumbstruck once again.

At this point, Gu Qing finally realized: No matter what his mentor said, Qin De could answer fluently, and every sentence made sense. It wasn't that his teacher's principles were wrong, but that Qin De's principles could always overpower them.

At a loss, Gu Qing couldn't help but look back at those beside him.

He saw Chu Xuangui with brows locked tight, fingers trembling slightly.

Song Taosheng closed his eyes as if he couldn't bear to watch.

And Situ Gu's face was ashen, his hands clenched into fists.

The expressions of the other Confucian cultivators were dismal.

They understood more clearly than Gu Qing that Zhao Hansheng's footing was already in chaos.

The latter switched from the School of Mind to traditional Confucian classics, and then from traditional schools back to the School of Mind. This is the biggest taboo in debate, like changing generals right before battle!

In contrast, Qin De clearly had many opportunities to pursue victory but didn't do so. Instead, he deliberately let them go time and again.

He was using Zhao Hansheng as a whetstone to help himself understand the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art more profoundly.

And in the eyes of the Confucian cultivators, Qin De seemed like a cat playing with a mouse, toying with Zhao Hansheng!

Even Zhong Dao, who was far away in the main hall secretly observing the debate, was expressionless at this moment.

"You haven't learned enough of your School of Mind," Qin De suddenly said.

Zhao Hansheng felt as if he had been struck by lightning.

Qin De looked at him, his eyes full of mockery: "Mr. Zhao, you consider yourself an inheritor of the School of Mind, traveling everywhere to give lectures and accept disciples to pass on the Dao. But your School of Mind is merely half-baked."

Zhao Hansheng's face turned completely pale.

"No, I cannot fail." Zhao Hansheng still wanted to put up a desperate fight, but his mental state had completely collapsed.

Just then, a muffled groan came from behind.

He whipped his head around, only to see Gu Qing with a pale face, collapsing onto the ground. The expressions of the other Confucian cultivators were also quite ugly.

"Action arises where desire moves..."

"Conscience originally has no good or evil; it is the user who determines it..."

"Mind is Desire... Desire is Mind..."

Qin De's concepts echoed repeatedly in Gu Qing's mind, conflicting violently with his past Confucian principles.

"Ahhh!" Gu Qing held his head, rolling on the ground and letting out howls of pain.

Zhao Hansheng was frantic. He quickly rushed to Gu Qing's side and supported his upper body: "Qing'er!"

Gu Qing raised his head, looking at Zhao Hansheng with blank eyes: "Teacher... I... I don't know... I don't know who is right and who is wrong..."

In this instant, Zhao Hansheng felt as if a knife were twisting in his heart, while simultaneously feeling as if he had fallen into an ice cave.

He knew that he had lost this debate!

And it was a crushing defeat.

At this point, Zhao Hansheng had no face to force himself to stay.

He supported Gu Qing, helping the latter barely stand up. Then, he turned to look at Qin De with a complex expression and sighed deeply: "I lost the debate today, not the School of Mind."

"Fellow Daoist Qin De is right. I have never mastered the School of Mind. With my half-baked level, suffering a crushing defeat is only proper!"

"This matter ends here for now."

"Three years!"

"Three years from now, I wish to return to the Myriad Manifestations Sect in the Flying Cloud Kingdom for a third debate with you. Is that acceptable?"

Qin De laughed loudly: "Why not!"

Zhao Hansheng took a deep breath, looking solemn. He performed an incomparably formal Daoist bow to Qin De, then walked with heavy steps, supporting Gu Qing as they left the Cloud Prison step by step.

He was a great scholar after all; even in a crushing defeat, he ultimately retained a shred of dignity.

Chu Xuangui and the others witnessed the entire debate with their own eyes. At this moment, they stood frozen like wooden statues, not moving a muscle.

Only when Zhao Hansheng's heavy steps turned the corner and left their line of sight did Chu Xuangui startle awake. He took a deep look at Qin De and was the first to leave.

The gazes of the other Confucian cultivators toward Qin De before they left were also extremely complicated.

Qin De ignored them and sat cross-legged directly in the corner. He was extremely excited, closing his eyes and devoting his whole being to secretly pondering the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art.

"This cultivation art is definitely a top-tier art!"

"No, with such capacity and profound demonic principles, could it be the legendary Supreme Grade cultivation art?"

The cultivation arts in this world were as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, difficult to calculate.

But generally, they were divided into three grades: high, mid, and low.

Low-grade cultivation arts were mostly introductory, incomplete, or flawed techniques. Those who cultivated these were mostly loose cultivators with bleak futures or bottom-tier cultivators.

Low-grade arts basically only cultivated one dantian and could not fully develop it. The cultivation efficiency was usually very low.

Their explanation, coverage, and excavation of the principles of the world were superficial, singular, or even erroneous.

The highest achievement of low-grade arts was limited to the Qi Refining Stage and Foundation Establishment Stage, at most Foundation Establishment perfection.

Mid-grade arts were the mainstream cultivation arts in the cultivation world, the foundation for small and medium-sized sects and cultivation clans. The system was complete and could cultivate the backbone forces.

Mid-grade arts often focused on cultivating only one dantian, while the other two dantians merely passively carried the cultivation base without active development. As a result, among the physical body, spiritual power, and spirit, only one would be excellent, while the other two would be ordinary, or even weakened due to the negative effects of the cultivation art.

Such arts could often relatively completely explain the profound principles of a certain element or type of power (such as divine sense or breath). Cultivators could follow the steps and reach a high realm.

To describe them in two words: systematic and singular.

Cultivating mid-grade arts, cultivators could reach the Golden Core Stage, but rarely could they impact the Nascent Soul level.

Among high-grade arts, many were the defining supreme techniques of large sects and super sects.

Generally, they focused on cultivating one dantian while concurrently cultivating the other two.

The principles they covered were either profound or numerous.

The art itself might focus on one element (like the Ten Thousand Woods Spring Thunder Attraction deriving the Lightning element from the Wood element), but its principles were profound enough to allow the cultivator to derive and comprehend other related principles on their own.

Or they might be like the Five Elements Qi Law, directly covering the Five Elements and explaining the fundamental principles of the operation of Heaven and Earth from multiple angles.

This type of art allowed cultivators to cultivate to the Soul Formation-level or Void Refinement level.

Above the three grades was the Supreme Grade!

These cultivation arts often existed in legends, and even super sects might not possess them.

Supreme Grade cultivation arts were supreme methods pointing directly to the Great Dao, with very full development of all three dantians.

The principles covered either pointed directly to the Origin or united ten thousand into one.

Cultivators relying on this could often cultivate to the Body Integration and Grand Ascension stages.

Beyond Grand Ascension, to undergo Tribulation Transcendence and ascend to become an Immortal, relying solely on cultivation arts was not enough; it depended on the cultivator themselves. For Immortals, the significance of cultivation arts was almost nonexistent.

Ning Zhuo's Three Sects' Supreme Methods had some semblance of a Supreme Grade art because it cultivated all three dantians simultaneously and they were completely compatible.

But it originated from the Master of the Three Sects. This mighty figure's cultivation was at the Void Refinement level, not yet at Body Integration or Grand Ascension.

Therefore, the Three Sects' Supreme Methods he created could at most be considered a high-grade art.

Qin De had only been in contact with and studying the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art for a day or two, yet he believed this art was very likely a Supreme Grade art.

He was not guessing blindly.

In fact, the quantity and quality of the classics he had studied and mastered were far greater than Ning Zhuo's.

It was precisely because he was proficient in Confucian classics and had widely read other cultivation arts and records within the Myriad Manifestations Sect that he was able to create the Sage's Great Theft Scripture.

Someone like Ning Zhuo, despite having such a profound realm in the Five Elements, was still some distance away from creating a technique at the level of the Sage's Great Theft Scripture.

Of course, as long as Ning Zhuo widely studied and referenced Five Elements-type cultivation arts, he could also create one.

It was just that up until now, he hadn't had the leisure or mood.

There was no need!

Ning Zhuo couldn't even keep up with cultivating the Three Sects' Supreme Methods, so why would he waste time and effort creating a Five Elements cultivation art that he couldn't cultivate himself?

The more Qin De ruminated and reviewed, the more he felt that the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art was extremely likely to be a Supreme Grade art!

"In past years, I also borrowed and read many high-grade arts. None were as extensive, profound, and full of subtle meaning as the Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art!"

As this thought lingered in Qin De's mind, Xiao Juxia's voice came: "You guessed correctly. The Myriad Methods Falling Demon Art is a Supreme Grade cultivation art."
