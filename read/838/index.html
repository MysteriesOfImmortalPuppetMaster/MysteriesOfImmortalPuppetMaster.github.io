Chapter 838: Master and Student Debate the Scriptures

This round of the trial was jointly organized by the Internal Affairs Hall and the Artifact Refining Hall. The rewards were generous, and the checkpoints were numerous.

The original intention was to repair various facilities at the Myriad Manifestations Sect's main mountain gate, while simultaneously using this opportunity to examine the artifact refining attainments of outsider cultivators and select the best for admission. It could be considered a move that killed two birds with one stone.

Xiao Juxia could even mass-produce book pages like the Inheritance Jade Pages, and the quality of his work only improved with time; his artifact refining attainments could be described as vigorous and powerful.

The banners planted all over the mountain by the Peak Master of Soaring Peak were characterized by their sheer quantity rather than high rank. For Xiao Juxia, repairing them was simply a piece of cake.

Two hours later, the trial concluded.

Xiao Juxia’s results placed him in the second tier; he had deliberately reserved his strength, avoiding any showiness.

The cultivator presiding over the trial announced the list of the top one hundred. These individuals could continue to participate in the trial for the next checkpoint.

Xiao Juxia was naturally among them. His expression was calm, but he harbored doubts in his heart: "Strange, why didn't Ning Zhuo come?"

"Could it be that my previous calculations were wrong?"

Zhao Hansheng formally reviewed the Sage and Great Thief Sutra.

The opening was a preface by Qin De himself -

In my youth, I read the books of sages and admired the way of the wise predecessors. At seven, I recited the Classic of Filial Piety; at ten, I understood the Analects; at fifteen, I mastered the Five Classics; at twenty, I had perused the Hundred Schools of Thought. Whenever I read 'The Great Way prevails, the world is for the public,' I never failed to close the book and sigh, my heart longing for it.

However, as I grew older and my experience deepened, I felt that the affairs of the world were full of suspicious matters.

The sages teach people to 'restrain oneself and restore the rites,' yet generations of emperors used the ritual system to bind the myriad people; the sages teach people to 'sacrifice life for righteousness,' yet the high officials in the temple use righteousness and reason to seek personal gain; the sages teach 'do not do unto others what you do not want done to yourself,' yet the disciples of the Confucian school use the name of orthodoxy to ostracize dissidents.

I was perplexed for twenty years, finding it hard to eat or sleep. One night, while reading Zhuangzi, I came across the sentence 'If the sage does not die, the great thieves will not stop.' It was like being struck by lightning; suddenly, everything became clear.

I realized: The Sage and the Great Thief are actually two sides of the same entity.

Thus, I went into seclusion for three years, examining all scriptures and histories, comprehending the profound mysteries, and writing this sutra. It is named the 'Sage and Great Thief Sutra' -not to slander the sages, but to clarify them; not to encourage thievery, but to prove the Dao.

Zhao Hansheng could not help but sneer: "This so-called 'suspicion' is not the suspicion of the sage, but the suspicion of the one reading the sage's books. Qin De uses the actions of emperors to doubt the teachings of sages, and uses the selfishness of high officials to doubt the righteousness of wise predecessors. This is covering the heart with traces and obscuring the root with the branches. How absurd!"

"And this brat has such a big tone, actually boasting that he can 'clarify the sages' and 'prove the Dao'? I'd like to see what kind of fallacies and heresies you have!"

The Sage and Great Thief Sutra consisted of two volumes in total.

The first major volume was titled "Sage and Thief Share the Same Source," and the second major volume was "There is Dao Even in Theft."

"The volume titles are somewhat remarkable." Zhao Hansheng swept through with his divine sense, getting a vague overview before delving into a detailed investigation.

Volume 1, Section 1: The Sage Steals Heaven and Earth

Qin De first cited the Book of Rites, The Operation of Ceremonies: "The Great Way prevails, the world is for the public. Men of talent and virtue are selected, sincerity is emphasized, and harmony is cultivated."

In Qin De’s view, however: The four words "The world is for the public" seem upright and bright, but actually hide a profound mystery. What is "public"? Shared by the multitude. However, the things of the world originally have no ownership. The sage uses the name of "public" to carry out the reality of "fixing" - fixing rules, fixing order, fixing titles. If this "fixing" is not theft, then what is it?

Heaven and earth give birth to ten thousand things, which originally have no owner or name. When the sage appears, he uses ritual law to steal the authority of heaven and earth and divide the order of human relations. Rituals are the order of heaven and earth, and also the thief of heaven and earth.

Qin De then cited the I Ching, The Great Treatise: "The great virtue of heaven and earth is to give life; the great treasure of the sage is his position."

Qin De’s understanding of this was: "Position" refers to authority and status. The sage steals "position" with "virtue," steals "authority" with "position," and steals the "world" with "authority." However, when the sage steals it, it is named "receiving the Mandate of Heaven"; when a commoner steals it, it is named "offending superiors and creating chaos."

It is the same theft; how is the sage different from the common thief?

Zhao Hansheng fell silent for a while, then used the principles of the School of Mind to refute this in his heart.

"The School of Mind states: 'Ritual is reason. Reason is nature. Nature is destiny.' Rituals and music systems originally flow from the human heart and heavenly principle; how can they be 'theft'?"

"For example, a farmer tilling the field, sowing seeds, and applying fertilizer causes the land to support the growth of ten thousand things. This is not stealing the power of the land, but following the nature of the land. The sage establishing rituals is just like this - following the heavenly principle of the human heart, enabling the myriad people to find their proper places. Qin De interpreting this as 'theft' is judging the heart of a sage with the heart of a thief."

"The School of Mind clarifies the principle of 'position': Position is the responsibility of the heavenly mandate, the duty of conscience. The sage occupying a position is not stealing it, but accepting the heavenly mandate to carry out the heavenly Dao. For example, a patriarch managing a family is not stealing the power of the family members, but fulfilling the responsibility of a patriarch. Qin De judges this with a thieving mind, seeing only the profit of authority and position, not the weight of responsibility."

"Qin De takes quotes out of context and interprets them as 'theft.' It is truly despicable!"

Zhao Hansheng continued his deep investigation.

He threw his entire mind into it, immersing himself, his expression constantly changing.

Sometimes angry, sometimes frowning, sometimes shouting "Absurd!", sometimes showing a solemn expression, painstakingly thinking of flaws.

After finishing it, Zhao Hansheng closed the scroll and sighed deeply.

In his heart, he had to admit that the heresy of this Sage and Great Thief Sutra had its own internal logic.

Qin De’s arguments cited classics and scriptures, and were clearly organized. From the Book of Rites and I Ching to Mencius and Xunzi, every citation was just right, and every deduction was logically tight.

"Although Qin De achieved logical self-consistency, his initial understanding was flawed from the very beginning."

"This Sage and Great Thief Sutra has only two volumes. It has a strong aura of being a draft, full of flaws. It will not be a problem for me to debate him down."

Know the enemy and know yourself, and you can fight a hundred battles with no danger of defeat.

Zhao Hansheng applied the School of Mind, studying deeply and thinking hard. He had already debated down the Sage and Great Thief Sutra in his heart, and thus his confidence swelled.

He swallowed a medicinal pill, allowing its power to slowly restore his condition, stroking his beard with a smile: "Once I debate down Qin De, Duanmu Zhang will step down and yield to the worthy as promised. I will surely be able to lead all the Confucian cultivators of the Myriad Manifestations Sect."

"With this foundation to help Qing-er, when we fight our way back into the Hua Zhang State, we will inevitably have even higher prestige. It makes taking him on this journey through the world worthwhile."

Gu Qing's learning and reputation had hit a bottleneck, which was why he was traveling the world.

Ning Zhuo was also traveling the world, and their situations were quite similar.

Zhao Hansheng suddenly had a thought. He wrote a flying letter and summoned Gu Qing.

"Qing-er, take a good look at this Sage and Great Thief Sutra." Zhao Hansheng intentionally tested Gu Qing.

Since losing to Ning Zhuo in the three Confucian trials, Gu Qing had been constantly reflecting on himself.

He was a genius famous throughout the country, born of a great clan, and carefully cultivated by a great scholar like Zhao Hansheng. Not only his strength but also his mental disposition was excellent.

Although he was dejected and depressed for a period at first, he soon regrouped and cheered up.

Recently, hearing that Ning Zhuo had swept through many minor formation trials, he lamented that Ning Zhuo actually had such strength, while simultaneously having his competitive spirit ignited again. His morale was boosted, and he cultivated bitterly every day.

In his heart, Gu Qing had already taken Ning Zhuo as his main opponent and treated him seriously. He often collected the latest intelligence on Ning Zhuo, using such an opponent to sharpen himself.

Suddenly summoned by his teacher to read some Sage and Great Thief Sutra, Gu Qing was initially puzzled.

But Zhao Hansheng did not explain much, simply telling him to read.

Gu Qing knew deeply that Zhao Hansheng would not harm him, so he probed with his divine sense and read carefully.

At first, he wore a look of curiosity.

But soon, his brows furrowed.

Gu Qing's brows knit tighter and tighter, and his face began to turn pale.

Not long after, the hand holding the cultivation method jade slip began to tremble slightly. His face became even paler, and fine cold sweat seeped from his forehead.

Finally, having finished reading the Sage and Great Thief Sutra, his whole body felt ice-cold, as if he had fallen into an ice cave.

"Teacher..." Gu Qing raised his head, his voice trembling, "This, this..."

His thoughts were wavering violently.

Those principles he had believed in since childhood, those classics he had studied bitterly day and night, that scholarship he took pride in - all were savagely impacted by Qin De’s work and were shaking violently.

Zhao Hansheng suddenly shouted: "Gu Qing!"

Gu Qing's whole body jolted.

Zhao Hansheng asked abruptly: "Do you have a conscience in your heart?"

Gu Qing was stunned.

Zhao Hansheng asked again: "When you see your father, do you know filial piety?"

Gu Qing nodded subconsciously.

"When you see your brother, do you know fraternal love?"

Gu Qing nodded again.

"When you see a child falling into a well, do you know compassion?"

Gu Qing nodded once more.

Zhao Hansheng said in a deep voice: "This is conscience! Conscience is in your heart, and no one can take it away! Qin De may speak extravagantly, but can he steal the filial piety in your heart? Can he steal the fraternal love in your heart? Can he steal the compassion in your heart?"

Gu Qing stood dazed, then immediately realized the crux of the matter, and his mind steadied.

He hurriedly performed a Confucian bow, his voice hoarse, his back soaked with sweat: "I ask Teacher to resolve my confusion."

Zhao Hansheng stroked his beard and spoke slowly.

Gu Qing listened carefully, his will no longer wavering.

After Zhao Hansheng’s explanation, Gu Qing felt as if he had been reborn. He could not help but kneel on the ground, "Thank you, heavy mentor, for your guidance!"

He instantly felt his attainments in the School of Mind soar.

Zhao Hansheng helped him up, speaking with deep meaning: "The Way of learning lies in clarifying the heart. If the heart is not clear, the more one reads, the deeper one gets lost. Qin De is a warning from the overturned cart ahead."

Gu Qing nodded heavily, looking up at Zhao Hansheng with eyes full of admiration.

Zhao Hansheng instructed: "Comprehend today's principles well. Tomorrow, go with your teacher to the Cloud Prison. In front of Qin De, we will debate down his Sage and Great Thief Sutra."

"Achieving this step will allow the Confucian cultivators of the Myriad Manifestations Sect to be used by us, master and student."

Gu Qing did not understand the details.

Zhao Hansheng gave a brief introduction.

Hearing this, Gu Qing rejoiced and directly congratulated him: "With you taking action, Teacher, success is guaranteed. Qin De’s heresies are nothing but crude opinions before you, Teacher!"

Zhao Hansheng stroked his beard: "Observe well. This experience is also an opportunity for you."

Debating scriptures was indeed an opportunity.

Because cultivation itself follows the principles of heaven and earth, correctly utilizing the resources between heaven and earth to achieve the goal of constant self-improvement.

Fire element cultivators follow the principles related to fire between heaven and earth, utilizing resources close to fire to constantly become stronger.

Demonic path cultivators follow the survival principle of survival of the fittest, often treating other cultivators as resources to strengthen themselves.

When it comes to Confucian cultivators, it is about abiding by Confucian rules and utilizing resources like the Literary Palace, Literary Heart, and Literary Qi to become stronger.

Throughout cultivation history, there have been countless debates on scriptures.

Some were between the Daoist and Buddhist schools, some between Daoist and Demonic sects, and some involving the Demonic, Buddhist, and Daoist schools. The development time of Confucianism is far less ancient than the other three, but there are also many key debates imprinted in the grand occasion of history.

Truth becomes clearer the more it is debated.

Speaking on a small scale, debating scriptures is a war of words. Speaking on a large scale, it is a contest between Dao paths.

The results of debating scriptures can sometimes be immense. In history, there have been cases where strong individuals on the opposing side were moved, their own Dao paths migrating violently. After being debated down, they believed the other side was correct, and thus directly changed their course, switching from Daoism to Buddhism, and so on.

Zhao Hansheng was thinking of Gu Qing, so he first showed the Sage and Great Thief Sutra to Gu Qing. When Gu Qing was lost and wavering, he gave a sharp warning and guided him patiently, allowing Gu Qing to reaffirm his Confucian Dao path. This allowed Gu Qing to suffer a setback in a short time, then climb back up, gaining tremendous growth.

Zhao Hansheng had absolute confidence in debating down Qin De, so he let Gu Qing observe, expecting his proud disciple to gain another improvement.

Green Stone Cave Dwelling.

Ning Zhuo had formally begun repairing the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat.

Ning Zhuo sat cross-legged.

In front of him, seven jade boxes were lined up, glowing with various lusters under the green lamp.

"Having the Commerce Hall is convenient. If I were to collect these precious materials personally, I'm afraid it would take years. But here, as long as there is enough money, they can be bought immediately."

Of course, Ning Zhuo was also clear that this was due to the Myriad Manifestations Sect being a super sect, recognized as number one in scale and strength in the Flying Cloud Kingdom.

Only with such a massive organization could various precious materials be collected continuously from all directions.

"Because the organization is huge and has many people, it can gather various cultivation resources."

"When cultivation resources are easily obtained, energy and time can be saved, allowing cultivators to cultivate faster and become stronger."

"Stronger cultivators are more capable of collecting more and better cultivation resources."

"Under such a positive cycle, the Myriad Manifestations Sect will only become more prosperous and developed!"

Ning Zhuo had been at the Myriad Manifestations Sect's main mountain gate for a few months now, and he increasingly felt that joining it was a correct decision.

"Of course, even with such a massive scale, the Myriad Manifestations Sect still has many resources and methods that cannot be easily obtained. One needs personal opportunities."

Ning Zhuo wanted methods for raising spirits, but the Myriad Manifestations Sect did not have them. At least from what he had seen so far, they didn't.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect was a renowned righteous sect; when it came to obtaining massive amounts of souls or soul path resources, it was still a notch below the White Paper Immortal City in the Ghostly Black Marshlands.

Therefore, Ning Zhuo's cultivation plan was to join the Myriad Manifestations Sect to satisfy his basic resource needs, and then focus on participating in the White Paper Immortal City to obtain massive amounts of souls from it. Ning Zhuo had a huge and intense need for this cultivation resource. Thus, the White Paper Immortal City was his true blessed land for cultivation.

Ning Zhuo took out the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat, gently holding it in his palm.

His hand brushed over the surface of the spirit boat, while his divine sense simultaneously probed the entire hull, going deep and coming out, cycling back and forth.

With a Renowned Artisan level artifact refining realm, and the artifact refining principles he had obtained from the Green Martial Young Master, analyzing materials and objects generated a tangible sense for him.

The depth of every crack on the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat, the degree of wear on every formation pattern, and the fatigue status of every inch of material were gradually collected by Ning Zhuo and memorized in his heart.

Among them, there were three places where the cracks were deepest.

One was near the bow, about three inches long, reaching deep into the core.

One was near the stern, short but wide, with many surrounding formation patterns broken.

One was in the center, seemingly inconspicuous, but actually hollow inside.

The rest of the tiny cracks were countless.

Ning Zhuo already had a plan in his heart, and at this moment, he directly opened the first jade box.
