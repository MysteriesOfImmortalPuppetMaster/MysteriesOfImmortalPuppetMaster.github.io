A Brief Response to Recent Controversies

Over the past couple of days, some discussions have arisen among readers regarding certain content in a particular work. I have also seen everyone's attention, defense, and emotions.

First of all, I would like to thank the readers.

For a work to be remembered and taken seriously by readers - to the extent that, even years later, they are still willing to analyze and speak up for it - is something incredibly precious to an author. For this work to have made it to where it is today is inseparable from the reading, discussion, support, and protection of every single reader. I see your sincerity, and I keep it in my heart.

The web literature industry is not easy. Anyone who has been writing long-term knows that this path is filled with pressure, loneliness, self-doubt, and many unspeakable persistence. Many authors started out as readers themselves, and they are inevitably influenced by the works they once loved. Certain works, characters, plot tropes, and modes of expression can leave deep marks in a person's creative memory.

This kind of influence, in itself, is nothing to be ashamed of.

The development of literature and storytelling inherently involves inheritance, echoes, inspiration, and recreation. What works an author was once moved by, and where they once drew strength from - these experiences are often a part of their creative life. Therefore, I do not believe that words like "drawing inspiration," "paying tribute," or "being inspired" should be stigmatized.

However, precisely because creators influence one another, we need to collectively cherish a clear, candid, and restrained creative habit.

If a work is indeed inspired by certain specific works - especially regarding core settings, special plot tropes, unique expressions, or certain highly recognizable sentences and imagery - it is actually a very good thing to clarify this in an appropriate place. For instance, openly writing down the source of inspiration in the author's notes, related work sections, postscripts, or any other suitable format, explaining which work or author provided the influence.

I do not think this diminishes the work at all. On the contrary, it reflects the author's candor and demonstrates respect for the origins of creativity.

Sunlight is the best disinfectant. Many misunderstandings, controversies, and emotions would often not escalate to a point where everyone is left embarrassed if they were handled in a more transparent and generous manner from the very beginning.

As for this particular discussion, I personally do not wish to see it expand further, nor do I want things to turn into an antagonism between authors. I completely understand and sincerely thank my reader friends for defending me - there will be extra chapter updates tomorrow!!

Getting angry harms your health. What truly deserves our long-term attention remains the works themselves, creativity itself, and how this industry can become healthier and more mature.

For writers, being able to walk this path means we all share a similar level of hardship. I, too, have been caught in the rain and felt the cold. Therefore, I am currently actively looking into ways to genuinely help my fellow peers and do a good job serving everyone.

Let's help each other and run toward a brighter tomorrow together!

If this discussion ultimately prompts more creators to value originality, respect sources, and express themselves candidly, then it will be more than just a controversy - it can also serve as a reminder pointing toward truth, goodness, and beauty.

Let us leave our passion for the places truly worth investing in: reading, creating, communicating, and those great stories that are still on the way.

Thank you, everyone!
