Chapter 910: A Congratulatory Gift for Ning Zhuo

The teahouse was named "Listening Pine," situated halfway up the mountain and facing the open sky on three sides. Pushing open the window, the distant mountains looked like dark green pigment.

Kong Ran had booked a private room here. By the time Liu Fushu and Bai Jiyun stepped through the morning light and pushed the door open, the room was already filled with the curling fragrance of tea.

Kong Ran sat in the host's seat. His apricot-yellow short jacket was particularly bright in the morning light, and the baby fat on his cheeks was slightly flushed from the rising steam of the tea.

"Brother Bai, Brother Liu, please take a seat," Kong Ran greeted.

Their relationship was much deeper now compared to the beginning of the Flying Cloud Assembly, so Kong Ran acted quite casually.

Liu Fushu and Bai Jiyun both responded with smiles and took their seats.

The three enjoyed their tea while admiring the scenery.

The morning light leaked through the carved window lattices, paving a layer of shattered gold on the tabletop. Outside the window, the distant mountains were shrouded in thin mist like an undried ink wash painting. Flowing clouds moved slowly around the mountainside, looking like a giant beast rolling over after a night's sleep.

The tea was freshly brewed "Jade Ravine Spring." The leaves unfurled like orchids in the celadon gaiwan, and the tea liquor was as clear as morning dew. The spout of the purple clay teapot was still steaming, and the curling white mist slowly rose in the morning light.

Kong Ran set down his teacup and took out the invitation Ning Zhuo had sent him. "You both received yours, right? I am definitely going."

He was the first to state his stance.

Liu Fushu nodded, "Brother Ning is filled with awe-inspiring righteous qi, which I deeply admire. If I didn't attend an invitation from someone like him, it would be truly discourteous."

Bai Jiyun let the tea liquor swirl on the tip of his tongue before swallowing it. "Naturally, I'm going too."

His temperament was lazy and unrestrained, completely different from Ning Zhuo's, making them quite incompatible.

"I love observing the beautiful sights of the world. In this Flying Cloud Assembly, an aggregation of heroes, all the various geniuses, hermits, ambitious lords, and experts are part of the scenery."

"To be honest, I am very much looking forward to the spectacle at Heavenly Treasure Pavilion tomorrow. Such a 'humanly beautiful sight' cannot be missed."

Kong Ran chuckled, "The senior Confucian cultivators of the Myriad Manifestations Sect all appreciate Ning Zhuo very much. Even though Ning Zhuo doesn't practice Confucian cultivation arts, he is very close with us Confucian cultivators and can be considered half a Confucian cultivator."

"He only has a Foundation Establishment cultivation base, yet he can stir up the winds and clouds. To be favored by Zhong Dao and Tuoba Huang, yet choose to fight for Flowing Cloud Peak himself... That's a completely different matter from the Flying Cloud Assembly!"

"With such a future and such methods, even if his attempt to take Flowing Cloud Peak fails this time, he is bound to achieve extraordinary things in the future."

"Forging more good karma with him can't go wrong."

"I invited you two worthy brothers today to discuss what congratulatory gifts would be best to send."

Ning Zhuo had booked the Heavenly Treasure Pavilion, prepared delicious delicacies, and widely invited fellow cultivators to attend the ceremony. According to etiquette, the cultivators coming to observe the ceremony needed to present some congratulatory gifts.

Liu Fushu smiled, "I have already prepared mine."

He took an item out of his storage bag and gently placed it on the table.

It was a writing brush, at the level of a Magic Artifact.

"I made this myself," Liu Fushu said.

At this moment, the morning light shone through the brush shaft. The shaft was like jade, with the texture of green bamboo clearly visible. Every grain extended along the direction of the shaft, even and fine.

Bai Jiyun nodded, "A good brush."

Although it was only a Magic Artifact, he could feel its overflowing Literary Qi.

This was a literary treasure!

Kong Ran looked at Bai Jiyun, "What about yours?"

Bai Jiyun shook his head, "I haven't thought of anything yet."

He had always been like this, naturally casual and scattered.

"If all else fails, I'll just compose a poem for him at the pavilion tomorrow."

"And you?" Bai Jiyun asked Kong Ran in return.

Kong Ran took out a stack of paper.

The paper was slightly yellow, its texture as delicate as fat, with faint flowing lights wandering across its surface. The four corners of the paper were outlined with cloud patterns using gold powder, the lines as fine as hair but clearly distinguishable. An indistinct scent of ink drifted from the paper.

Liu Fushu's pupils dilated slightly, "Is this official tribute paper?"

Bai Jiyun was also drawn to it, adding, "And it's special-grade official tribute paper at that!"

Kong Ran said, "This is what my father uses in his study; it's specially supplied from the palace."

He clapped his hands in praise, "I was right to invite you two worthy brothers. Since our gifts are like this, we might as well present them together tomorrow."

"Brother Bai can hold the brush and write a poem on the official paper to present to Fellow Daoist Ning Zhuo. What do you think?"

Liu Fushu's eyes lit up, "This is a wonderful suggestion."

Bai Jiyun, however, stroked the official paper and felt a wave of pressure. He laughed, "Good old Kong Ran. Since that's the case, I absolutely must think this through and compose a poem worthy of this brush and paper."

Liu Fushu had always liked Bai Jiyun's poetry and couldn't help but say happily, "With Brother's poetry, I can't wait for tomorrow to arrive!"

Che Zhuzi entered his treasure room.

The domed ceiling of the secret chamber was inlaid with three hundred and sixty star beads. The starlight overflowed, illuminating the regular octagonal chamber as bright as day, yet without the slightest dryness of firelight.

The floor was paved with flowing-light bricks. Blue spiritual light occasionally wandered between the gaps in the bricks like streams or slithering snakes, swimming from the east wall to the west wall, and back again, cycling endlessly.

Che Zhuzi stepped into the center of the treasure room. With a manipulation of his divine sense, the eight bronze wall panels surrounding him unfolded one after another, revealing the treasures hidden within.

"For Ning Zhuo to stir up the winds and clouds this time, actually wanting to gather forces to vie for Flowing Cloud Peak... The young people these days are truly audacious!"

"It doesn't matter whether he succeeds or fails this time. He possesses the Naming Fire Furnace and is highly regarded by people like Zhong Dao and Tuoba Huang. In the future, he will definitely be a powerful figure within the Sect."

"Most importantly... he is already a major client of mine!"

Che Zhuzi's most profound memory was when they were initially discussing business, and Ning Zhuo directly took out a Golden Core to pay him the deposit.

At the time, Ning Zhuo's expression was natural, not even batting an eyelid.

So wealthy and imposing!

The series of news that followed made Ning Zhuo's fame surpass itself time and time again, which also caused Che Zhuzi to constantly raise his evaluation of Ning Zhuo.

When Ning Zhuo's invitation reached his hands, Che Zhuzi immediately understood the soaring ambitions hidden behind it.

A Foundation Establishment cultivator who dared to plant his flag on Flowing Cloud Peak, dared to tie his creditors to the same war chariot, and dared to directly face the high regard of Zhong Dao from the Demon Subjugation Hall without subordinating himself.

This courage, this calculation, this methodology - Che Zhuzi had lived for many years and seen many people, but there weren't many he truly admired. Ning Zhuo definitely counted as one!

Therefore, he very naturally made a further judgment: maintaining and strengthening a relationship with Ning Zhuo was a worthy long-term investment that would definitely yield wonderful returns!

Thus, for this ceremony at Heavenly Treasure Pavilion, Che Zhuzi decided to give a heavy gift to show his sincerity!

He needed to pick a treasure from his storage room.

"So, which one should I give?"

Che Zhuzi slowly paced around the treasure room.

Inside a crystal cage, over ten mechanical spiders, precise and agile, secreted void spider silk to repair torn storage bags.

A Starlight Astrolabe. Three layers of bronze rings interlocked and slowly rotated. The star marks on the surface of the rings constantly flowed with radiant silver light, like a poured-out galaxy or the flow of time.

A Yellow Crystal Spider Nest. The nest looked as if it were cast from amber, its entire body a translucent orange-yellow. The body of the nest was divided into many small compartments, with several mechanical spiders of various sizes resting in each one. Some compartments were empty, with traces of spiders crawling on the walls. The spider nest emitted a hazy yellow light, the brightness changing slowly and rhythmically, as if breathing, as if sleeping.

A Heavy Mountain Shovel. The shovel head was as large as a door, pitch-black throughout, and gleaming with a cold metallic luster. The top, sides, and even the long handle of the shovel were densely covered with large and small steam vents. The edges of the vents were polished extremely smooth, reflecting spots of cold light under the starlight.

A clump of Plain White Wandering Cloud. The cloud hung suspended inside a glazed bell, snow-white and untainted by a speck of dust. At the top of the bell, a spinning wheel continuously whirled, sucking in cloud qi from the outside, shredding it into mist in a whirlwind, and pouring it into the bell.

There was also a Black Turtle Escaping Shell, with only about forty percent of its original shell patterns remaining. The patterns meandered like rivers or veins, with profound light faintly circulating within. The turtle shell was placed in a Wutu pond.

Che Zhuzi's gaze lingered on the Black Turtle Escaping Shell for a moment.

"Old friend," he sighed softly in his heart.

Soon after, his gaze skipped the turtle shell and rescanned the rest of the treasures.

This turtle shell involved an agreement between Che Zhuzi and an old friend; it could not be given away.

Che Zhuzi quickly passed over the Plain White Wandering Cloud. "Ning Zhuo is a mechanism cultivator. Even if he doesn't major in this Cultivation art, he definitely dabbles deeply in it. Giving him mechanical constructs will likely move him more."

Che Zhuzi then discarded the Heavy Mountain Shovel. "Ning Zhuo is a Young Master of a major clan. Even when he fought in the Profound Armor Cave before, he used Mystic Weapon Armor to remotely control close combat. Having him swing such a heavy weapon clearly doesn't fit the demeanor of a major clan, and I'm afraid it's not to his liking either."

"The Starlight Astrolabe can assist a cultivator in calculations. Mechanical spiders can weave silk for precise repairs..."

Che Zhuzi pondered for a long time before deciding to give up on both and ultimately settling on gifting the Yellow Crystal Spider Nest!

"Give a man a fish and you feed him for a day; teach a man to fish and you feed him for a lifetime. The Yellow Crystal Spider Nest can continuously produce mechanical spiders."

"I have the blueprints; I'll just build another one in the future."

"Only a gift like this for Ning Zhuo can truly be considered a heavy gift! It will definitely leave a deep impression on him!!"

Weapon Armor Peak.

Several cultivators, clad in dark cyan standard-issue magic robes with bronze bells hanging at their waists, stood solemnly and silently.

The leader stepped forward and took out an incense stick.

The body of the incense was slender like a sword, blue-gray in color.

He lit it.

Blue smoke curled upwards, straight as a sword, rising about ten feet before slowly dispersing.

The cultivator held the incense with both hands, raised it above his head, touched his forehead to the ground, and slowly chanted a prayer.

"Disciples of the Profound Armor Cave, with the bronze incense and a sincere heart, humbly beseech the Divine Lord Qingwu."

"The Lord's divine might pierces the sun and moon and soars above the nine heavens. Iron steeds and golden halberds shake the Eight Desolations and intimidate the four poles. You uphold the severe slaughter of weapons and grasp the solidity of armor. With a single breath of anger, the colors of heaven and earth change; with a single roar, mountains and rivers collapse. We disciples look up to your divine might like ants clinging to a mountain, like sunflowers turning towards the sun."

His voice was solemn, overflowing with admiration.

"Today, there is a cultivator named Ning Zhuo, who has established the 'Naming Stronghold' at Flowing Cloud Peak and delivered an invitation. We disciples are slow-witted and do not know whether this is auspicious or ominous, nor do we know how to proceed. We specifically burn this missive to reach the heavenly ears."

After finishing his prayer, he inserted the incense stick into the censer and lit the talisman paper filled with intelligence over the candle flame.

The talisman paper ignited immediately upon touching the fire, its edges curling up. The ashes fluttered like black butterflies, circling a few times before slowly falling.

The room was perfectly silent.

The incense smoke continued to curl upward, straight as a sword.

Suddenly, the smoke swirled and condensed into the illusory upper body of Lord Qingwu.

Lord Qingwu chuckled, "I am already aware. That boy Ning Zhuo has vast ambitions, far from ordinary. Since that's the case, have someone bring a Golden Core-level Mystic Weapon Armor as a congratulatory gift tomorrow."

The cultivators all bowed and said in unison, "We respectfully obey the Divine Lord's command."

Ning Zhuo's invitation was like tossing a stone into a lake, stirring up ripples. He was no longer comparable to when he first entered the Myriad Manifestations Sect. His name had spread throughout the main sect, and although he only had a Foundation Establishment cultivation base, everyone was optimistic about his future prospects.

Ning Zhuo's mind at this moment, however, was focused on the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Tube].

The last time he fought Situ Xing, it had allowed him to use combat as practice, slightly testing his conventional combat strength and tactics, and he discovered many areas where he could improve.

One of these areas was his mechanical birds.

Thunder Silence Birds and Golden Swallows were the conventional mechanical constructs Ning Zhuo had stockpiled the most, but the former was too classic and stereotypical, making it easy to counter. The design of the Mechanical Golden Swallow was not excellent; it was merely the result of a minor practice aimed at matching it with the Black Iron Han Armor - Golden Swallow Model.

Inside the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Tube], there were five types of mechanical birds. All of them had undergone layers of testing by past generations. Ning Zhuo could now take them and use them immediately, which was extremely convenient.

Ever since the end of the last battle, Ning Zhuo decided to unearth the inheritance potential of the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Tube] and materialize it into combat power.

This would be of massive help to him in dealing with the heavy challenges of the future.

"Who knows, someone might publicly challenge me tomorrow at Heavenly Treasure Pavilion," Ning Zhuo estimated.

The five types of mechanical birds in the inheritance of the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Tube] were: Spear-Necked Crane, Bamboo-Hugging Pheasant, Headwind Kite, Golden-Silk Caged Sparrow, and Sleeve-Piercing Harrier.

"The Spear-Necked Crane is capable of both long-range and close-range combat, but an individual unit is easily countered. Only by forming a flock can they become a main force."

"The Bamboo-Hugging Pheasant only has low-altitude flying capabilities, but it can drill into the ground, making it the most advantageous weapon for breaching defenses and breaking armor."

"The Headwind Kite is good at prolonged battles and can ideally sustain endless flight. Its weaknesses are also obvious: large size, slow movement speed, and since it soars at high altitudes, it requires an escort."

"The design of the Golden-Silk Caged Sparrow is particularly unique. Its entire body is woven from twined bamboo silk. The whole body is just a ball of silk threads that can stretch, expand, and contract, without any skeletal structure in the traditional sense. Grappling and locking down are its specialties. The shortcoming is also obvious: it has almost no lethality. It can only trap the enemy, not kill them."

"Finally, the Sleeve-Piercing Harrier. Small in size, fast, and possesses the most precise attack. However, the poisonous stinger can only be used once, and it requires applying poison. If it is discovered in actual combat, the enemy can easily set up relevant defenses."

Ning Zhuo carefully compared their strengths and weaknesses, keeping them in mind.

He did not choose any one of them for large-scale production.

Firstly, tomorrow was the alliance-building ceremony at Heavenly Treasure Pavilion, so there wasn't enough time. Secondly, Ning Zhuo had a better solution, and this solution primarily depended on another key figure.

Old Chef came over at this time to report, "Young Master, the person you invited has arrived."

Ning Zhuo immediately drew his gaze away, revealing a joyful expression. "Good."

He immediately put away the Jade Slip and set off for the reception hall, meeting the cultivator he had specially invited.

The person had calm eyes and three wisps of a long beard. He had a lean figure and wore a neat blue gown.

His aura leaked out, shockingly reaching the Golden Core level.
