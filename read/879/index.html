Chapter 879: Strategizing for Flowing Cloud

Ning Zhuo’s nature was cautious and steady. Hearing Meng Yaoyin say this, he immediately wanted to give up on the Nanming Fire Furnace.

However, Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin shook her head and said, "Tribulations and fortunes are perilous and full of unforeseen events. Using the opportunity of the Nanming Fire Furnace to trigger it will actually allow you to better control the situation."

Ning Zhuo lowered his head in thought. After a few breaths, he nodded. "Mother, you are right."

If this tribulation destiny opened now, he would at least be prepared. If it suddenly triggered during a critical emergency in the future, it would only add frost to the snow, leaving Ning Zhuo with no spare energy to handle it. The result would be disastrous.

It was a variable.

Or rather, a hidden danger.

Triggering it early was much more conducive for a cultivator to control the situation.

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin continued, "Calculating based on this tribulation destiny, Little Zhuo, the most suitable faction for you to join is Flowing Cloud Peak."

"Flowing Cloud Peak?" Ning Zhuo was surprised.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect had eight main peaks: Heavy Formation Peak, Myriad Beast Peak, Danxia Peak, Purple Thunder Peak, Sword Resonance Peak, Hundred Herbs Peak, Soaring Peak, and Flowing Cloud Peak. The problem was that the first seven peaks all had masters. The eighth peak, Flowing Cloud Peak, had been masterless for a long time.

The eighth peak was extremely chaotic, with various organizations and small factions mixed and entangled together. The Peak Lord was merely a temporary substitute, held by the most outstanding cultivator among them, and was not truly recognized by the Sect.

When Ning Zhuo considered which faction to join, the first one he had eliminated was the eighth peak, Flowing Cloud Peak.

Yet Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin’s suggestion pointed directly to this peak.

Ning Zhuo pressed further, "The eighth peak is not a unified faction. During the Flying Cloud Assembly, a large number of small organizations emerge and ascend this peak. They frantically compete with the old factions on the peak, ultimately deciding a relative victor, and the leader of this faction temporarily assumes the position of Peak Lord. But doing so lacks proper justification and legitimacy. The so-called 'Peak Lord position' has never been truly recognized by the Sect."

"No, Mother, you know about this situation. Your meaning is..."

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin shook her head, "This is not my meaning, but what I deduced based on the tribulation destiny. You can join any of those factions and participate. The more active you are, the more you can dissolve this tribulation destiny. If you can find a leader and assist him in becoming the temporary Peak Lord of the eighth peak, that works too. The greater the role you play, the faster your tribulation destiny will be consumed."

Tribulations and fortunes were interdependent. The faster Ning Zhuo consumed it, as long as he could withstand the heavy trials, the greater the benefits he would gain and the more progress he would make.

Ning Zhuo nodded silently, keeping this suggestion firmly in mind.

Having a tribulation destiny attached to him was always a hidden danger; he had to find a way to resolve it.

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin said, "My suggestion to you is this: First, struggle on Flowing Cloud Peak and try your best to consume this tribulation destiny. Then, in the final stages of the Flying Cloud Assembly, choose the faction or clique you truly want and join them, so as to coordinate with your layout and plans in the White Paper Immortal City."

"Finally, there is one more thing."

Ning Zhuo's heart trembled. "Finally?"

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin reached out, rubbed Ning Zhuo's head, and comforted him before saying, "This matter is also a regret from your mother's past life. If you can complete it, it will be of great help to you in joining the Myriad Manifestations Sect."

Speaking up to here, Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin paused. "Actually... let's forget it for now."

Ning Zhuo asked, "Mother, what is it? I will do everything in my power to help you make up for this regret!"

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin looked deeply at him. "Somewhere in the Flying Cloud Kingdom, I have a hidden manor, which also contains many of my relics. Once you go there, you will learn of this matter. Make your decision then."

Ning Zhuo then asked for the location of the hidden manor.

But Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin shook her head. "When the time comes, you will naturally know. Learning of it now will only bring harm and no benefit to you."

Ning Zhuo hated riddlers, but the riddler right now was his mother, so he could only pout. "Alright."

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin tilted her head slightly, her gaze seeming to pierce through the formation to look into the distance. "I am going to enter my slumber."

Ning Zhuo's eyes reddened, and he softly called out, "Mother!"

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin flicked his forehead. "Do you think divination doesn't consume energy and spirit? Having digested Wu Hen's Dao principles, I need deep closed-door cultivation even more."

"This is all for the sake of a longer companionship in the future!"

"Mother..." Ning Zhuo was persuaded. His nose felt sour, as he still harbored far too much reluctance to part. Such a reunion, to him, was far too brief.

Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin, however, slowly closed her eyes, leaving behind only one sentence: "Little Zhuo, you have grown up. You must walk your own path. Remember, your path must be chosen by you alone."

The next moment, she made no further sound, falling into a deathly silence, just like before.

"Mother!" Ning Zhuo called out, receiving no response.

He lowered his head and wiped his eyes with the back of his hand. Only then, with a face full of solemnity and seriousness, did he place Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin's mechanical body back into his Storage Belt.

The big-headed youth subconsciously touched his Mechanism Ring, but the latter remained motionless.

It took a long time for Ning Zhuo to calm his emotions and reflect on this mother-son reunion.

The help Meng Yaoyin had brought him was simply too great!

She hadn't just provided him with assistance regarding the Nanming Fire Furnace; she had also expounded on the profound mysteries of luck. Although it was just fragmented knowledge, it had already broadened Ning Zhuo's horizons immensely.

Since Mother is proficient in the cultivation art of divination, when she wakes up next time, I will ask her for guidance.

Ning Zhuo couldn't help but have this thought again.

But immediately after, he shook his head and sighed.

He already knew that heavy wealth weighed down the body; a person's fate compass had a limit to its bearing capacity.

I possess too many heavy treasures right now. My fate compass's bearing capacity is limited, and going too far is as bad as falling short.

Moreover, this cultivation art of divination is quite special. For most cultivators, obtaining it involves an element of Heaven's Will.

Heaven obviously does not want me and Boss to obtain this skill.

If I forcefully beg Mother to teach me, I fear it will incur Heaven's disgust.

Ning Zhuo then thought about this issue from another angle and felt a bit more relieved: Mother loves and protects me, and she knows my eager-to-learn nature. If I were suited to master the art of divination, she definitely would have taken the initiative to teach me long ago.

I'm really looking forward to seeing myself capable of divination. I wonder what it would feel like...

Sigh, I won't think about this for now.

Ning Zhuo continued to review his plans.

This time, Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin hadn't just broadened his horizons; she had also guided his grand strategy for future cultivation.

Ning Zhuo understood that he had a portion of tribulation destiny upon him, giving him a more accurate understanding of himself.

He also understood that during this Flying Cloud Assembly, he needed to join Flowing Cloud Peak first, strive to dissolve his tribulation destiny, and then make a final decision based on the actual situation.

Regarding the immediate contest for the Nanming Fire Furnace, starting from his own destiny, he now had a more correct strategy.

I have to say, the cultivation art of divination is truly useful.

Ah, I really want to learn it.

Suppressing this thought, Ning Zhuo began to consider his own faction: From the perspective that an individual's fate compass and destiny have limits on the treasures they can bear, the management of a clan faction is highly necessary.

Ning Zhuo thought of the Ning Clan branch he had split off - no, founded. Previously, he had established the branch mostly to capitalize on the situation and go with the flow.

Looking at it now, managing it well would be very beneficial to his own cultivation.

Many of the treasures and precious scriptures I have right now can be given to my family members so they can use their respective fate compasses and destinies to share and bear the burden.

This is exactly what the Myriad Manifestations Sect does. Past sect masters of the Myriad Manifestations Sect rarely mastered divination techniques.

But among the guest elders or regular elders they recruited and invited, several possessed powerful divination skills. At critical moments, the Myriad Manifestations Sect could rely on these talents to help them perform crucial divinations.

To a certain extent, the relationship between Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin and Ning Zhuo was also like this.

If the clan is managed well, even if I can never learn the arts of destiny and divination in the future, I can still recruit others or nurture our own people within the clan.

Thinking of this, Ning Zhuo suddenly felt his perspective broaden.

"It's getting late. This night was truly lively." Ning Zhuo calculated the time and first released Sun Lingtong, Qing Chi, Old Chef, and Gongsun Yan from the Ten Thousand-Li Traversing Dragon.

He dismissed the other three and kept Sun Lingtong alone, informing the latter of what had happened.

Sun Lingtong had complicated feelings about Ning Zhuo's mother. After hearing Ning Zhuo's account, he gained a bit more reverence for Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin.

He was happy for Ning Zhuo and expressed his willingness to help him actively search for top-tier materials to repair Buddhist Doctor Meng Yaoyin's mechanical body.

Ning Zhuo said to Sun Lingtong, "Since my mother has made arrangements regarding the Nanming Fire Furnace, Boss, you won't need to participate tomorrow."

Sun Lingtong nodded: "Since your mother specifically advised against it, I definitely won't join in the fun. If it arouses suspicion and leads to an investigation, that would be terrible."

The two brothers quickly reached an agreement.

The night sky swiftly brightened, and morning arrived.

On Danxia Peak, there were still many people watching.

The dozens of cultivators who still retained their qualifications gathered together under the watchful eyes of the crowd.

The previous grand formation had already changed. There was no longer a closed formation space; instead, it was exposed to heaven and earth, in full view of the public.

This is the Illusory Eaves Open Courtyard! Ning Zhuo's heart slightly trembled.

This was a type of formation-arranging technique, not something an ordinary cultivator could achieve.

Conventional formation spaces were sealed and self-contained. But the Illusory Eaves Open Courtyard technique represented the connection between the formation space and the external space.

Just like the scene before them: in the very center was a pillar of light. The pillar was hazy and dreamlike, radiating flowing pink colors. From the top and bottom of the light pillar, pink walls of light extended in all directions.

The two walls of light formed a simple dome and floor. The further the walls of light extended outward, the dimmer their radiance became and the paler their colors, until they finally dissipated into nothingness.

The edges of the light walls seemed to naturally blend into the normal environment.

To achieve such a natural effect, as if everything was perfectly logical and seamless, was incredibly difficult. This was the defining characteristic of the Illusory Eaves Open Courtyard technique.

Led by the duty cultivators, all those participating in the minor trial gathered around the pink light pillar.

There was Mu Yuehua, wearing a moon-white long dress, as cold and clear as frost.

There was Shen Hongyao, in a fiery red dress, her arms crossed over her chest and her almond eyes slightly narrowed.

There was Jiang Ping, wearing a rosy-cloud-colored Daoist robe, his expression calm.

There was Cang Yazi, completely expressionless.

There was Nine Fire Dragon Lord, with long fiery red hair and a red-gold robe, standing prominently in the center of the crowd.

There was Old Man Baicao, standing at the edge of the crowd with a gentle gaze.

There was the Red Robed Guest, garbed in scarlet, surrounded by a bloody aura.

There was Old Man Taoli, sitting cross-legged in a corner with his eyes closed to rest, like a sculpture.

There was also Chunyangzi, standing with his hands behind his back, exuding the demeanor of a major sect master.

As well as Qin Ke, Gu Lianjie, Zhu Fenxiang, Situ Xing, and others.

Ning Zhuo was also among them, appearing utterly ordinary. He thought to himself, Just as Mother predicted, upon reaching the third round, the rules have drastically changed.

The duty cultivator slowly spoke, announcing the rules for the new minor trial.

Everyone would take turns stepping forward. Under the witness of everyone present, they would use their own methods to attract the attention of the Artifact Spirit, Vermilion Bird. Whoever gained the Artifact Spirit's acknowledgment would be the winner.

If multiple people gained its acknowledgment, a next round would be held.

If only one person did, they would naturally be the winner.

If no one did, the minor trial would be declared over. The Artifact Spirit, Vermilion Bird, would simply have failed to find a fated person capable of saving it.

To demonstrate fairness, the order in which the cultivators stepped forward would be decided by drawing lots.

The duty cultivator first displayed a purple sandalwood box, showing it to all the cultivators present for strict inspection to ensure there were no tricks.

Then, in front of everyone, he displayed dozens of jade tallies. These jade tallies were all marked with different numbers.

He placed all these jade tallies into the purple sandalwood box.

The cultivators stepped forward one by one, reached into the box, and drew out a jade tally.

Ning Zhuo was in the middle of the line. When it was his turn, he reached into the box, his fingertips brushing against a jade tally. He gently took it out and looked down - "Five."

Fifth place.

This was considered very far ahead.

Ning Zhuo put away the jade tally and retreated to his original position. After waiting for everyone to finish drawing lots, the order was revealed.

The first was a male cultivator with a pale face and an icy temperament.

The second was Mu Yuehua.

The third was Chunyangzi.

The fourth was Shen Hongyao.

The fifth was Ning Zhuo.

Under the watchful eyes of the crowd, the cold-faced male cultivator approached the pink light pillar first.

He stood there, hands hanging naturally by his sides, and closed his eyes like a meditating monk.

Then, a chilling aura surged from his body.

The chilling aura poured out endlessly, seeping from his pores and growing denser, forming a cluster of pale blue radiance that covered his entire body.

Innate Talent - Cold Marrow Dawn!

Cold implies freezing, female, and Yin; preserving it allows one to return to their true nature.

Marrow is the essence within the bones.

Dawn refers to the morning light, the color of the sun. It stands for the morning dawn, the rising sun, the beginning of light.

The three words combined formed "Cold Marrow Dawn."

This innate talent was born in the bone marrow, emitted through the skin and muscles, and completed in the divine sense. Its nature was Yin yet contained Yang, its essence was cold yet hid warmth, its manifestation was still yet harbored movement.

Simply summarizing its power and utility: It allowed mastery over both cold and warmth, enabling the dual cultivation of two methods.

A cultivator only has three dantians, so the cultivation methods they can practice are limited. Techniques are a different matter.

Ning Zhuo concurrently cultivating the Three Sects' Supreme Methods was quite a rare exception.

A cultivator possessing the "Cold Marrow Dawn" Innate Talent could freely choose a Fire Element and a Water Element cultivation method to practice simultaneously. As long as these two cultivation methods did not occupy the same dantian, there was no need for their contents to echo or coordinate with each other. Even if they were vastly different, this innate talent could mediate and harmonize them, allowing for smooth dual cultivation.

Not only Fire Element and Water Element, but the cultivator could also simultaneously practice opposing cultivation methods of Yin and Yang attributes.

Opposing cultivation methods, under the "Cold Marrow Dawn" innate talent, would achieve perfect balance and harmony.
