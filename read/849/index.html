Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 849: Mask

Xiao Juxia had not the slightest thought of helping Qin De.

On one hand, he had deep confidence in the Demonic Path's Qi Luck.

In the experience of coercing others to nurture a peerless Cultivation Art, Xiao Juxia himself was a direct participant and had seen cases similar to Qin De's.

"Even if I and the Sage's Great Thief Sutra are exposed and made known to Wang Yu, it would only mean that exposing these secrets is more beneficial to the Demonic Path!"

"Perhaps, it might even turn Wang Yu into one of our own."

"After all, this is a peerless Cultivation Art!"

Wang Yu pitied Qin De's talent and fed him Medicinal Pills twice. The Medicinal Pills did not need to be of such high quality. Just from this detail alone, Xiao Juxia had already deduced some of Wang Yu's thoughts and the underlying tone of his character.

On the other hand, Xiao Juxia had always treated Qin De as a chess piece.

He and Qin De were neither kin nor friends. The reason he was able to discover him was by following divinations, or rather, the boost of the Demonic Path's Qi Luck.

Since he could discover Qin De, he could naturally discover others as well.

Wang Yu cherished Qin De's talent, but to Xiao Juxia, it wasn't something worth treasuring so much. After all, if this one failed, the Demonic Path's Qi Luck would boost him and allow him to discover the next one.

Therefore, in Xiao Juxia's mind, Qin De was just a chess piece.

Since he was a chess piece, he was meant to be used, risked, and even sacrificed.

"Fake, fake, this one is also fake!"

Sun Lingtong pointed repeatedly with his finger. Anyone he pointed at slowly retreated and disappeared into the mist.

In the end, only one Cultivator revealing their true appearance remained.

"Hee hee hee," Sun Lingtong laughed.

His results were too outstanding. Participating in this Cloud-Rousing Minor Trial to distinguish true from false was simply effortless for him.

Unsurprisingly, he won first place this time.

Granny Thousand Faces, who hosted this Cloud-Rousing Minor Trial, proactively revealed herself and asked Sun Lingtong amicably, "Little Daoist friend, which family are you from? Exactly how did you see through the true and false in here?"

By using his Innate Talent Spirit Eye, Sun Lingtong could even see through the River Luo Water Formation, let alone these disguise and camouflage techniques.

Sun Lingtong also did not understand his own Innate Talent very well. Early on, when he and Ning Zhuo were in Fire Persimmon Immortal City, they had secretly investigated it but found no relevant information at all.

The two brothers believed this should be some brand new Innate Talent unknown to the world.

Of course, Sun Lingtong would not answer truthfully at this moment. His playful mood arose, and he chuckled, "I guessed them all."

Granny Thousand Faces chuckled, finding it amusing.

On the surface, Sun Lingtong cast a technique as a cover, but secretly used his Innate Talent. Granny Thousand Faces noticed a clue, which was why she asked.

Sun Lingtong didn't continue the disguise to say what technique he cast, nor did he reveal the truth.

Granny Thousand Faces took out a Jade Token from her sleeve and handed it to Sun Lingtong. "This is an identity token for Thousand Faces Peak. In the future, if you join the Myriad Manifestations Sect and need any help, you can come find this old woman."

Sun Lingtong took the Jade Token, flipped it over to look at it, and suddenly asked, "Can this be given to someone else? Or... sold for Spirit Stones?"

Granny Thousand Faces' expression instantly darkened. "This identity token only recognizes you."

Sun Lingtong curled his lip, stuffed the Jade Token into his robes, and muttered, "Then you might as well give me a more practical reward. I heard that many trials reward magic treasures, or even high-grade Cultivation Arts."

Granny Thousand Faces was taken aback. The attendant beside her said bluntly, "Little Daoist friend, our first-place reward is the identity token of Thousand Faces Peak. This was announced to the public long ago. Since you came to participate, how could you not know?"

Sun Lingtong chuckled and told the truth, "I didn't have time to check. I just thought this trial was quite interesting."

The attendant was stunned, and then immediately revealed a look of anger.

Granny Thousand Faces, on the other hand, was rendered speechless.

She presided over Thousand Faces Peak, and what she excelled at most was the art of disguise and camouflage. She carefully selected the disciples she recruited, while constantly improving the foundation of her establishment.

Only through constant improvement and perfection could the art of disguise and camouflage maintain its effectiveness and power. Otherwise, if it were not updated in time, it would instead become a fatal flaw.

Whenever the Flying Cloud Assembly was held, Thousand Faces Peak would host the Cloud-Rousing Minor Trial, displaying various disguise and camouflage techniques to conduct small-scale tests, allowing the Cultivators participating in the trial to distinguish true from false.

Then, through these practical results, they would accumulate experience to improve their own disguise and camouflage techniques.

Sun Lingtong easily discerned the real from the fake, which deeply shook Granny Thousand Faces. That was why she asked Sun Lingtong exactly what method he had used.

Sun Lingtong did not give the true answer and even disdained the reward. Little did he know that Thousand Faces Peak held a special status within the Myriad Manifestations Sect and played an irreplaceable role. Whether spying for intelligence, infiltrating enemy camps, or changing one's identity to retreat into seclusion, it had an irreplaceable value.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect consisted of eight peaks and sixteen halls, but besides the eight main peaks, there were also other peaks with special statuses. Thousand Faces Peak was needed by everyone; from elders down to handymen, they might all have needs in this regard, so its network of connections was quite extensive.

Therefore, hearing Sun Lingtong act so casually, coming to participate in the trial without inquiring clearly, the attendant felt slighted and naturally grew angry.

Granny Thousand Faces, however, thought of something else and gave a bitter smile. "My Thousand Faces Peak cannot compare to those great mountains. As a lone old woman running it by myself, where would I get so many assets?"

Sun Lingtong only replied with an "Oh."

Granny Thousand Faces pondered for a moment, and still took out three items from her sleeve, handing them to Sun Lingtong.

"Never mind, never mind. This old woman will pay out of her own pocket to add a little something extra for you."

The attendant was immediately shocked; such treatment for Sun Lingtong was unprecedented.

The first item was a mask as thin as a cicada's wings. The mask was pale gold, cold to the touch, and weightless in the hand.

"This is a carapace mask refined from the molted shell of a thousand-year-old golden cicada," Granny Thousand Faces pointed and slowly introduced.

"Wear it, and you can change your appearance, figure, and aura. No one below the Nascent Soul stage can see through it. However, it can only be used three times. After three uses, the material will be exhausted, and it will lose its function."

She then pointed to the second item.

It was still a mask, but made of shadow puppet material. There were a total of six human-shaped shadow puppets in the guise of boys. Each shadow puppet was as thin as paper, yet incredibly lifelike.

"These are Boy Shadow Puppets. When you activate them with your spiritual energy, the shadow puppets can transform into your true appearance, mimic your spiritual energy aura, and perform a series of actions. They are perfectly suited for confusing enemies and escaping perilous situations."

She then introduced the third item.

It was still a mask.

A mask crafted from White Paper with simple facial features drawn on its surface, looking like a child's doodle.

"This is the 'Papercraft Substitute Mask'," Granny Thousand Faces said. "Made using papercraft techniques, wearing it can block one fatal attack in a critical moment."

The attendant was greatly surprised; he had never seen Granny Thousand Faces treat someone so generously.

Sun Lingtong felt pleasantly surprised. He quickly took the three treasures, gleefully stuffed them into his storage bracelet, and bowed to Granny Thousand Faces: "Thank you, Senior! Thank you, Senior!"

"Once I join the Myriad Manifestations Sect, I will definitely get closer to you, Granny."

Hearing the response she wanted, Granny Thousand Faces immediately revealed a kind smile and said "Good" three times in a row.

Sun Lingtong first left Thousand Faces Peak, carefully inspected the three newly acquired treasures to ensure there were no hidden tricks, and then returned to the Bluestone Cave Dwelling.

Ever since the River Luo Book page was successfully plucked, Sun Lingtong had begun participating in the Cloud-Rousing Minor Trials everywhere, playing to his heart's content.

As the sky grew dark, Sun Lingtong returned to the Bluestone Cave Dwelling.

From Old Chef, Sun Lingtong learned that Ning Zhuo was doing his daily cultivation routine, so he did not disturb him.

He asked Old Chef again, "Where is Qing Chi?"

Qing Chi was inside the Formation Space, practicing talisman crafting.

In front of her lay a long red sandalwood desk, neatly stacked with various colored Talisman Papers, Cinnabar, talisman brushes, and inkstones. In the corner were piled several boxes of blank jade talismans, procured from the Commerce Hall.

Qing Chi sat cross-legged in front of the desk, her expression focused.

Holding a purple bamboo talisman brush in her hand, the tip soaked with gold-red talisman ink, she suspended her wrist above a piece of Talisman Paper.

The Talisman Paper was high-grade "Cloud-Patterned Paper," its surface as smooth as jade with faint cloud patterns flowing across it. This paper was not cheap; a single sheet cost ten low-grade Spirit Stones.

Qing Chi took a deep breath, and the brush tip descended.

The brush moved like dragons and snakes.

The cyan-red talisman ink meandered across the paper, every stroke and line incomparably precise.

This was the talisman of the Yin Fire Talisman; it had a total of thirty-seven strokes, each with a fixed length, angle, and depth.

Fine beads of sweat seeped from Qing Chi's forehead.

Her hand was very steady, while her mind was taut as a bowstring.

The path of talisman crafting dictates that a miss is as good as a mile. If a stroke was a fraction too long, the talisman's power would become unbalanced; if it was a fraction too short, the power would be insufficient. If the angle deviated by one degree, the Rune would fail; if it deviated by two degrees, the Rune would explode.

Thirty-seven strokes; every single stroke was a test.

The twelfth stroke, smooth.

The twenty-third stroke, smooth.

The thirty-first stroke - Qing Chi's hand trembled slightly.

She hastily steadied it, but that tremor had already left an extremely fine, quivering trace on the Talisman Paper.

Her heart sank, yet she didn't dare stop the brush.

Continue.

The thirty-seventh stroke, finishing the brushwork.

The Talisman Paper lit up slightly, then dimmed. The cyan-red talisman ink gradually seeped into the paper, merging with the cloud patterns. At first glance, it looked as if nothing had happened.

But Qing Chi knew that this talisman was half-ruined; its quality was very low.

That trembling mark had ruined the integrity of the Rune. This Yin Fire Talisman could, at most, exert thirty percent of the power of a normal talisman.

She sighed softly, pushed the Talisman Paper aside, and picked up a fresh, blank Talisman Paper.

While dipping her brush in ink again, she reviewed the mistake she just made and how to adjust it, doing her best to ensure she wouldn't make the same error this time.

The purple bamboo talisman brush gently rolled in the inkstone, allowing the talisman ink to fully soak the bristles. Her gaze was focused and resolute, as if the previous failure had never occurred.

Although Qing Chi had lost her memories, her temperament remained unchanged; she still possessed an indomitable character.

Talisman crafting was just like this. The process of learning and practicing was extremely tedious. A Cultivator had to endure, increasing their proficiency time after time, making tiny improvements time after time, accumulating them like drops of water forming a river, and then, at some ordinary moment, the quantitative changes would spark a qualitative transformation.

The brush tip glided along, flowing like drifting clouds and running water.

Thirty-seven strokes, completed in one smooth motion.

The moment she lifted her brush, the Talisman Paper suddenly lit up. A cyan-red glow seeped from the Rune, illuminating the entire room. Within the glow, the phantom of a jumping flame could faintly be seen, and a cold, Yin aura blew against her face.

Success!

A glint of joy flashed in Qing Chi's eyes. She hurriedly picked up the Talisman Paper and scrutinized it carefully.

The Rune was complete and smooth, without a trace of trembling. The talisman ink seeped in evenly, without a single clump. The talisman's light was bright and steady, without a hint of fluctuation.

A high-grade Yin Fire Talisman.

She smiled faintly and carefully placed the Talisman Paper into a wooden box to the side. Inside the wooden box, there were already twenty-seven completed talismans lying there - three high-grade, eight mid-grade, fifteen low-grade - and dozens of ruined talismans stacked on the other side.

Qing Chi had been continuously crafting talismans for several hours.

She didn't feel the slightest bit bored, especially when she thought that these would be given to Ning Zhuo. Just imagining her Young Master Ning Zhuo using the talismans she made with her own hands made her feel a surge of excitement and genuine happiness.

She was about to continue crafting when a message arrived from outside the formation, causing her to pause slightly. "What is he looking for me for?"

After thinking for a moment, she opened the Formation.

Sun Lingtong stepped into the Formation Space. "Oh my, Qing Chi, your talisman-crafting skills are improving very quickly."

Sun Lingtong acted overly familiar. After all, during the White Paper Immortal City battle, he had hidden inside the Ten Thousand-Li Traversing Dragon and witnessed the interactions between Qing Chi and Ning Zhuo.

Qing Chi, however, felt that Sun Lingtong was merely Ning Zhuo's attendant, just a stranger.

"Is something the matter?" she asked, her gaze falling back onto the Talisman Paper.

Sun Lingtong took the golden cicada carapace mask out of his robes and placed it on the desk.

"Here, for you!" Sun Lingtong chuckled.

It was exactly the item Granny Thousand Faces had just given him. Sun Lingtong turned around and gifted one to Qing Chi.

Sun Lingtong introduced its functions.

Qing Chi shook her head and refused to accept it.

Sun Lingtong froze. "Why?"

Qing Chi said, "I am not familiar with you. Are you asking me for a favor?"

Sun Lingtong rolled his eyes, chuckled, and said, "Alright, alright, I was tricking you. Of course I'm not the one giving it - Ning Zhuo asked me to pass it to you."

Qing Chi paused. "Young Master?"

Sun Lingtong nodded repeatedly. "How would I dare to tell such a lie?"

Qing Chi's expression immediately changed, and she looked down at the golden cicada mask. Putting down her talisman brush, she reached out and solemnly held the mask in her hands, bringing it close to inspect it carefully.

Young Master is thinking of me. Qing Chi felt deeply moved in her heart. Her eyes reddened slightly, and the corners of her mouth curled up into a smile.

Seeing that Qing Chi was in a great mood, Sun Lingtong brought up his own request, "I see you practicing talisman crafting all day long. Don't you find it dull and tedious?"

"I found a fun trial outside. The rewards are novel, but I am short on hands."

"Let's work together, pass the trial, and snatch the reward."

Qing Chi looked up at Sun Lingtong, suppressed her smile, and slowly shook her head. "Young Master asked me to practice crafting talismans, so I should prioritize that. Whatever this trial is, I'm not going."

Sun Lingtong tried to persuade her a bit more, but Qing Chi's attitude was firm.

Sun Lingtong curled his lip and had no choice but to seek out Ning Zhuo.

Ning Zhuo had already finished his cultivation routine for the day.

His Upper Dantian, Middle Dantian, Lower Dantian, Physical Body, and Soul all received a slight enhancement. Among them, because the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat had been fully repaired, his Physical Body cultivation efficiency had once again returned to what it was before, barely able to keep up with the progress of his Soul.

Ning Zhuo's focus was still on studying the Demonic Contamination Blood Meridian Art.

Ever since his formation path realm soared to the Master level, his progress in comprehending this Cultivation Art had become much faster than before.

"But it's still not enough."

"I still need a realm in the Demonic Path, especially in the aspect of blood and qi."
