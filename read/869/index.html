Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 869: Gathering of the Worthies

The door to this Cultivation Chamber had been tightly shut for over twenty years.

In the exact center of the chamber sat a massive pill furnace. It was blue-gray, with the full text of the Baopu True Incantation engraved on its body, and a blue ox coiled upon its lid.

Inside the pill furnace, air currents surged, emitting a blue-gray light that flickered between bright and dim.

Tao Liweng was inside this very furnace, sitting cross-legged with his eyes tightly shut, cultivating unceasingly.

The vast majority of cultivators used pill furnaces to refine medicine. His lineage, however, used them for their own cultivation.

Tao Liweng had entered the furnace over twenty years ago and had never come out since.

In this state of cultivation, he knew nothing of the rising and setting of the sun and moon, nor the turning of the four seasons. He only knew the heat of the fire within the furnace and the quality of his Golden Core.

This was the Embracing the Core to Attain Immortality Art!

There were rumors about this cultivation method - that it was born from a demonic art.

Demonic Cultivators excelled at refining humans into pills. Past sages of this lineage transitioned from the Demonic Path to the Righteous Path by treating themselves as human pills to be refined. They would willingly enter a pill furnace and use countless medicinal ingredients to concoct their own bodies.

Tao Liweng's eyelids twitched slightly. Mobilizing his divine sense, a narrow slit opened in the storage bag at his waist, and a wave of medicinal ingredients flew out.

Cast into the flames, the medicinal ingredients rapidly melted, transforming into medicinal juices and qi. First, an ethereal mist enveloped Tao Liweng's entire body, and then the medicinal juice seeped into his dantian.

Deep within his dantian, his Golden Core - flawlessly round and slowly rotating - continuously absorbed the medicinal properties, steadily growing stronger.

"Ten more years."

"In ten years, my Golden Core will reach its peak, possessing the foundational requirements to break the core and form the Nascent Soul."

"Hm?"

Right at that moment, a telepathic message of divine sense pierced through the pill furnace and reached into Tao Liweng's divine sea.

"Didn't I say not to disturb my cultivation casually unless it was a major event!" Tao Liweng was initially furious, but his anger swiftly turned into pleasant surprise.

"Wait, it's actually the Nanming Fire Furnace?!"

"How could Danxia Peak make such a massive blunder?"

"Hehehe, to think that I would actually have the chance to become the master of the Nanming Fire Furnace!"

"If I acquire this furnace, repair it, and use it for my own cultivation, I won't need ten years of secluded cultivation at all."

"The time would be drastically shortened, perhaps to less than three years!"

Reaching this thought, Tao Liweng was suddenly startled and couldn't help but let out a long sigh. "Alas..."

"My mind and qi are disturbed; my state of mind has changed."

"I must exit the furnace this time."

As it turned out, practicing the Embracing the Core to Attain Immortality Art required not only an excellent pill furnace and a sufficient amount of medicinal ingredients, but also a matching state of mind.

Now that his state of mind had been greatly stirred by the news of the Nanming Fire Furnace, it could no longer sustain his secluded cultivation.

He had no choice but to emerge!

"Nanming Fire Furnace, here I come."

Ancient Tomb Black Market.

There was no sunlight here; it was perpetually shrouded in dim yellow light. The illumination came from Spirit Light Stones hanging over the stalls on both sides. Their grade was low, casting a murky, drowsy glow that made the entire street look like a slumbering giant python winding into the depths of the darkness.

The air was a mix of various spiritual materials - herbs, ores, beast bones, and medicinal liquids - along with an indescribable scent of decay. This was the accumulation of countless years.

Both sides of the street were crammed with stalls. Some were as crude as a torn piece of cloth spread on the ground; others were as exquisite as carved beams and painted pillars. Some consisted simply of a single woven mat, with the stall owner sitting cross-legged upon it, displaying a few items in front of them with a "take it or leave it" attitude.

Yunyouzi's stall belonged to the most inconspicuous category on this street.

A torn cloth, covered in dust, made it impossible to tell its original color. There were a few scorch marks at the corners, as if it had been singed by flames. Neatly arranged in rows on the cloth were medicine bottles. The bottles were also old, with fine cracks on their surfaces, but they had been wiped completely clean, gleaming with a warm luster under the dim yellow light.

The medicine bottles were affixed with labels written in Yunyouzi's own handwriting. The script was messy but legible - Healing Powder, Antidote Powder, Qi Recovery Powder, Cultivation Boosting Powder, Mind Calming Powder. Each one was among the most commonly used consumables in the Cultivation World.

On the far right of the medicine bottles sat a small, palm-sized pill furnace.

The pill furnace was tiny, small enough to be held in the palm of a hand. Its body was a dark bronze color with a rough surface devoid of any patterns, even showing a few uneven casting marks that made it look like a discarded defective product. However, the inner walls of the furnace chamber were as smooth as a mirror, with a faint fiery glow circulating within, indicating that despite its crudeness, it had been used for a very, very long time.

Yunyouzi sat cross-legged behind the torn cloth, resting with his eyes closed.

He looked to be in his forties, with a lean face, slightly high cheekbones, and slightly deep-set eyes, exuding a weathered sense of having endured year-round exposure to the elements. His grayish-white Daoist robe had been washed until it faded, bearing several mended spots at the cuffs. The stitches were fine and neat, looking as though he had sewn them himself, stitch by stitch. His hair was casually tied up with a wooden hairpin, and a few loose strands fell across his forehead, swaying gently in the faint yin wind of the black market.

"Yunyouzi."

A voice called out from the front of the stall.

Yunyouzi opened his eyes, casting a lazy glance at the newcomer.

"It's you," Yunyouzi said upon recognizing him, his tone flat. He reached out, picked up a bottle of Healing Powder from the torn cloth, and casually tossed it over. "The usual?"

The middle-aged cultivator caught the medicine bottle but didn't reply immediately. Instead, he weighed it in his hand, uncorked it, and took a sniff before nodding. "The usual."

He took out a few Spirit Stones from his sleeve, placed them on the torn cloth, and sat down cross-legged across from Yunyouzi.

"How many years of friendship has it been?" the middle-aged cultivator suddenly sighed, staring intently at Yunyouzi. "We've known each other for over thirty years now, haven't we?"

"Thirty-seven years," Yunyouzi stated the exact number. His tone remained flat, but the corners of his mouth twitched up slightly.

"Thirty-seven years," the middle-aged cultivator repeated, shaking his head emotionally. "Thirty-seven years ago, you were the most promising young alchemist in the Alchemy Hall. Back then, great hopes were pinned on you. You were seen as the future Vice Hall Master, perhaps even the Hall Master. But now? You've set up a stall in the black market, selling pills that cultivators dismiss as ordinary if they don't know any better."

Yunyouzi didn't respond.

From beneath the torn cloth, he pulled out a crude ceramic teacup and a waterskin, poured half a cup of water, and took a slow, leisurely sip. The water was cold, but he didn't care; he drank it with ease, as if savoring celestial nectar.

The middle-aged cultivator looked at his undisciplined demeanor, his brows furrowing slightly before relaxing.

"Do you really not feel a hint of regret?" he pressed.

Yunyouzi chuckled. "Not at all."

"When it comes to alchemy, the most important thing is following one's heart. If the heart isn't free, the pills refined are dead. I am someone who cannot stand being bound. The Alchemy Hall has too many rules, too many reprimands from the elders, and too many comparisons among fellow disciples. I stayed there for ten years, and the longer I stayed, the more suffocated I felt."

"So I left. I traveled everywhere, viewing mountains and rivers, and refined a furnace of loose pills whenever I met someone in need. If I didn't have spiritual materials, I used the most common herbs. If I didn't have an alchemic fire, I used the most basic mortal fire. My pill furnace was small, so I refined them slowly, one furnace at a time."

He raised his head, his gaze piercing past the middle-aged cultivator's shoulder to look at the dim, murky darkness at the end of the black market, as if looking at a place very, very far away.

"The heavens and earth are boundless, and life is long. There's no need to rush."

The middle-aged cultivator fell silent for a moment.

He had known Yunyouzi for thirty-seven years and deeply understood his nature - undisciplined, free-spirited, and willing to go with the flow. He was like a cloud, drifting wherever the wind blew. If you asked him to follow the rules in the Alchemy Hall, he couldn't do it. But setting up a stall in the black market to sell loose pills? He enjoyed it immensely.

Yet, precisely because he knew Yunyouzi's talent, he felt it was a pity.

"I came to you because I have some important intelligence to share specifically with you." The middle-aged cultivator then recounted the events concerning Danxia Peak and the Nanming Fire Furnace.

"Danxia Peak is currently a mess internally. The old alchemists are being held accountable for the furnace explosions, and their positions are unstable. The Alchemy Hall is also restless. All factions are fighting for the ownership of the Nanming Fire Furnace. But no matter who ultimately wins, they won't be able to do without a specific person - someone who can make the Vermilion Bird artifact spirit open its heart again."

"That person could be you."

The middle-aged cultivator stared scorchingly at Yunyouzi.

Yunyouzi looked down at the dilapidated pill furnace on his stall. His gaze flickered, and he murmured softly, "Nanming Fire Furnace?"

Flying Cloud Kingdom, Yuehua Sect.

Yuehua Peak, Alchemy Chamber.

The alchemy chamber wasn't large, spanning no more than three zhang in radius. Its four walls were built with moon-white jade, the jade warm and translucent, bathing the entire room in a soft halo. A skylight was opened in the vaulted ceiling, facing the night sky. A full moon hung at its zenith, and its silvery-white moonlight poured down through the window like a silent waterfall, illuminating the silver-white pill furnace in the center of the chamber.

The pill furnace was about half a person tall, with three legs and two handles. Engraved on its body was an image of the Moon Palace's Cassia Tree - the tree was gnarled and vigorous, with lush foliage and clear textures on its trunk, so vivid one could almost smell the faint fragrance of cassia blossoms. Beneath the tree lay a jade rabbit, ears perked as it gazed at the moon, incredibly lifelike. The furnace lid depicted another scene: a jade rabbit coiled upon it, its front paws holding a small lunar disc. In the center of this disc was an embedded night pearl, which was currently flickering with a soft light in tandem with the rising and falling heat within the furnace.

In front of the furnace sat a slender figure, cross-legged.

Mu Yuehua.

She looked to be only around twenty years old, with an elegant, clear visage and skin like congealed fat. Between her brows lay a transcendent elegance that didn't seem to belong to the mortal world. Her black hair was loosely pinned up with a jade hairpin, and a few strands fell by her ears, slightly curled from the steam radiating from the pill furnace. Wearing a moon-white long dress, its hem spread across the floor like a blooming epiphyllum.

At this moment, her eyes were closed in concentration. Her hands slowly formed incantation seals in front of her. Her ten fingers, slender and jade-like, were wreathed in a faint silvery-white glow. That light shared the same origin and nature as the moonlight outside the window, as if she were the incarnation of the moon in the mortal realm; every movement tugged at the purest Lunar Power between heaven and earth.

Inside the pill furnace, a flame burned silently.

This was the Lunar True Fire.

Silvery-white like frost and piercingly cold like ice, it made not a single sound as it burned. There was only an extremely faint tremor, as if the moonlight itself was breathing.

Within the furnace chamber, a medicinal pill was taking shape.

The pill's body was translucent, with faint patterns of cassia blossoms flowing inside. A medicinal aroma escaped from the gaps in the furnace lid - thread by thread, clear and elegant. It wasn't the pungent smell of ordinary pills, but rather like the scent of cassia blossoms under the moonlight, subtle yet refreshing to the soul.

This was the Yuehua Pill.

This pill was an exclusive recipe of the Yuehua Sect, crafted specifically for female cultivators. It could assist them in breaking through bottlenecks on full moon nights, utilizing Lunar Power to cleanse their meridians and purify their spirit roots.

Refining a single furnace of Yuehua Pills required forty-nine days without interruption. During this time, the alchemist had to maintain a state of mind as tranquil as a mirror, free of any ripples. Even the slightest error in any step could result in the pill's destruction at best, or a furnace explosion and a backlash against the alchemist at worst.

This furnace of pills held extraordinary significance.

It was the final test in the Yuehua Sect's internal competition to determine the current generation's Fairy Yuehua.

Possessing Immortal Talent, Mu Yuehua was the strongest candidate and the most highly anticipated junior of the sect.

Yet at this moment, ripples were forming in the lake of her heart.

She had fallen victim to a secret plot.

The method of the plot was incredibly cunning - it simply involved conveying a piece of information.

Information regarding the Myriad Manifestations Sect's Nanming Fire Furnace.

The turbulent waves in Mu Yuehua's heart were difficult to calm.

"I cultivate Lunar spiritual energy, which is extreme Yin. Because of my innate talent, my Yin Qi is overly abundant, while my Yang Qi is waning. If I'm even slightly careless, my Yin and Yang will lose balance, and my mind will become unstable."

"Before Foundation Establishment Stage, there was still plenty of room to adjust and correct this."

"But now, I am at the Foundation Establishment Stage, facing the critical juncture of forming my Golden Core."

"If I genuinely follow the step-by-step process and form a Lunar Yin Golden Core, it will only add coldness to coldness, absolute Yin upon Yin. It'll be a case of going too far. Even if its power is outstanding and uniquely miraculous, it will be extremely difficult to shatter the core and form a Nascent Soul. Even if I manage to form a Nascent Soul, the calamities will be severe, making it difficult to mature into a Primordial Spirit and reach the Soul Formation Stage."

Mu Yuehua was deeply aware of the sect's precedents. She knew that if she followed the path of her predecessors, her journey would start out auspicious but end in misfortune. She would shine brilliantly at first, only for her cultivation difficulty to surge dramatically later, halting her progress completely and leading to a very bleak future.

"Although I have reached Foundation Establishment Stage, I still haven't formed a Golden Core yet."

Foundation Establishment can still be recultivated, but the Golden Core leaves no room for regret!

Mu Yuehua still had one final chance to make an adjustment.

It wasn't that Mu Yuehua hadn't tried to find alternatives; but either her attempts had failed, or the things she found fell short of her standards.

Mu Yuehua had gradually come to realize: the sect had long been acting in the shadows, snuffing out any other future possibilities for her and leaving only the path of the Lunar Yin Golden Core.

The Yuehua Pill was the signature product of the Yuehua Sect. If the sect wanted to secure its foothold and flourish in the Flying Cloud Kingdom, it needed generations of Fairy Yuehua to practice the corresponding cultivation art and refine Yuehua Pills in large quantities.

This was where the sect's interests lay!

"The Nanming Vermilion Bird Fire is a Yang Fire. Even more exceptionally, unlike the Solar True Fire, it possesses a celestial phenomenon aspect and can also be called a Star Fire."

Mu Yuehua cultivated a Lunar art; if she relied on the Solar True Fire, it would be as incompatible as water and fire. But just as the stars and moon shine together, the Nanming Vermilion Bird Fire could reflect upon her Lunar spiritual energy. Consequently, this would achieve a state of Yin and Yang producing each other, and water and fire supplementing one another.

Ultimately, this would alter the nature and quality of Mu Yuehua's Golden Core!

Mu Yuehua knew full well that her choice at this very moment involved more than just a single furnace of pills or the battle for the title of this generation's Fairy Yuehua; it was a critical decision for her lifelong Dao Path!

She felt as if she were standing at a crossroads, with the scenery of the two diverging paths vastly different.

Mu Yuehua's slender, jade-like fingers trembled slightly, and the Lunar True Fire within the furnace fluctuated and danced in response. The Lunar spiritual energy at her fingertips wound around like threads of silver silk, perfectly mirroring her complex thoughts and hesitation.

"Then..."

Fairy Yuehua took a deep breath and slowly exhaled.

"Then I will leave."

The Lunar True Fire in the pill furnace gradually extinguished. The Yuehua Pill that was on the verge of taking shape dropped to the bottom of the furnace and shattered. Instantly, its medicinal aroma scattered in all directions, seeping into the soul.

A furnace of Yuehua Pills, over a month of arduous effort, was ruined in an instant.

Seeing this, Mu Yuehua instead smiled in relief.

She had figured it out. The moment the lake of her heart refused to calm, she had already made her choice.

She slowly stood up, calmly withdrew her gaze, pushed open the door of the alchemy chamber, and walked out.

Outside, the moonlight flowed like water, bathing the entirety of Yuehua Peak.

She did not linger at Yuehua Peak.
