Chapter 852: Song Taosheng Enters the Cloud Prison

Wang Shu, Directorate of the Imperial College, stood in a corner holding a scroll of white silk. The white silk was woven from the highest grade "Cloud-Patterned Brocade"; its texture was exquisite, and it was as smooth and bright as a mirror.

The silk was already filled with a sacrificial text.

The sacrificial text consisted of exactly three thousand words. Not a single character could be wrong, and not a single stroke could be careless. With every stroke, a wisp of Literary Qi had to be infused. With every completed character, it had to be nourished by mind and soul. The slightest error would render the entire text useless, requiring it to be rewritten from scratch.

Wang Shu was making a final inspection.

Not long after, Chancellor Han Song of the Jixia Academy arrived alongside the Monarch of Huazhang.

Zheng Jing and Wang Shu paid their respects in unison.

"Dispense with the formalities." The Monarch of Huazhang wore a solemn expression, his attention primarily focused on this national sacrifice.

He walked up to the Sacrificial Altar, carefully inspecting the surroundings and the arrangements.

Han Song extended his divine sense to contact the Golden Chapter Jade Tome.

Once the Monarch of Huazhang finished his inspection, Han Song opened his eyes and immediately reported: "My Lord, over the past two days of destiny resonance, the Golden Chapter Jade Tome has locked onto the source of the turbulence. At the source, destiny is rising, showing a clear upward trend."

"You mandated two days, saving us a day. Truly wise!"

The Monarch of Huazhang chuckled. "This is the protection of our nation's destiny. My beloved ministers, follow me to the altar."

The Monarch was usually calm and composed, but whenever there was a crucial operation, he acted with lightning speed and absolute resolve.

The four of them ascended the Sacrificial Altar simultaneously.

The Golden Chapter Jade Tome lay quietly on the three-tiered white jade Sacrificial Altar. Its sixty-two Jade Tablets were closed together, as if waiting for this national sacrifice to begin.

On the surface of the yellow silk draped over the altar, the sun, moon, stars, mountains, rivers, plants, and the names of ancient sages faintly flickered and appeared incredibly lifelike amidst nine-colored light.

In a celadon incense burner, three sticks of sandalwood incense had already been placed, waiting to be lit.

Han Song, Zheng Jing, and Wang Shu sat on three sides of the altar, closing their eyes to regulate their breathing and gather their strength.

The Monarch of Huazhang went to the highest tier of the heaven-offering altar and sat cross-legged before the desk, directly facing the incense burner and the Golden Chapter Jade Tome behind it.

The palace doors were tightly shut.

Zhou Zheng stood on the outside of the palace doors, personally standing guard. He was as motionless as a statue.

The rest of the guards were mostly hidden in various locations; it appeared relaxed on the outside but was highly secure on the inside.

All was deathly silent. Time slowly trickled by.

Myriad Manifestations Sect main gate.

Song Taosheng walked expressionlessly toward the gates of the Cloud Prison.

The mountain breeze caused his Confucian robe to flutter slightly, much like the hidden ripples in his heart.

He recalled the intelligence regarding the Cloud Prison in his mind.

"The Cloud Prison belongs to the Demon Subjugation Hall. Built against the cliff, it consists of nine overlapping layers and is perennially hidden within the sea of clouds. Every layer is constructed from a special stone material called 'Cloud Fiend Rock,' which is incomparably hard and can resonate with the clouds and mist."

"Based on the Cloud Fiend Rock and the nine-layer structure of the Cloud Prison, the Myriad Manifestations Sect arrayed the outermost defensive formation of the prison, named the Nine Heavens Cloud Fiend Formation. This formation draws upon the power of the fiendish clouds of the nine heavens, condensing into a tangible cloud-fiend barrier three zhang thick and utterly indestructible. Even an all-out strike from a Soul Formation cultivator would struggle to shake it in the slightest."

"There is no hope for a forceful assault, not even a tiny bit!"

Song Taosheng stopped as he reached the main gates.

He was not alone; behind him followed five cultivators, all dressed as servants.

Two cultivators guarded the entrance of the Cloud Prison. One of them stepped forward. "It's you."

The guard recognized Song Taosheng.

Not long ago, Song Taosheng had followed Zhao Hansheng and the others into the Cloud Prison and personally witnessed the entire scripture debate.

Song Taosheng took out an official document and handed it over.

The guard took the document, glanced over it once, and then took out a jade slip talisman for a simple comparison. After confirming there were no errors, he immediately let them pass.

It was only after everyone had officially stepped into the Cloud Prison and passed the first layer that they finally breathed a sigh of relief.

They had succeeded!

Song Taosheng timely recalled the plan Kong Zhaoming had given him-

"With the Nine Heavens Cloud Fiend Formation in place, there is absolutely no possibility of a forceful assault to kill someone. But precisely because the defenses are overly formidable, and because the Cloud Prison hasn't had a single accident in hundreds of years, the guarding cultivators are generally lax and their vigilance is weak."

"The most direct method is to enter the Cloud Prison directly and visit the prisoner!"

Visiting a prisoner wasn't something that could be done casually; it required advance reporting.

Song Taosheng was a member of the Confucian cultivator group. He first approached Duanmu Zhang and informed him: he had decided to follow Zhao Hansheng to the Huazhang Kingdom. However, before leaving, he was unwilling to let things go and wanted to confront Qin De once more.

As the saying goes, 'Know yourself and know your enemy, and you will never be defeated in a hundred battles.' He wanted to probe for as much intelligence as possible so that when he reunited with Zhao Hansheng, he could present it to him as a meeting gift.

After hearing this, Duanmu Zhang immediately agreed. He offered a few words of encouragement to Song Taosheng, telling him to go to the Huazhang Kingdom, follow Zhao Hansheng closely, and properly study the Philosophy of the Mind. Even if Zhao Hansheng failed in the future, Song Taosheng would still retain a glimmer of hope.

Song Taosheng sighed in his heart but feigned agreement.

Duanmu Zhang then reported to his superiors, requesting that Song Taosheng act as a representative to visit Qin De.

The Cloud Prison was a restricted area, and the approval process was lengthy. However, Duanmu Zhang and the current Hall Master of the Demon Subjugation Hall, Zhong Dao, were good friends, a friendship that was widely known.

The cultivator in charge of approvals, considering his superior, always processed Duanmu Zhang's requests the fastest.

If Wang Yu hadn't come, the approving cultivator might still have had some misgivings. But Wang Yu had already finished his soul-searching and left directly without leaving any specific instructions.

From the perspective of the Demon Subjugation Hall, Wang Yu representing the upper echelons of the Myriad Manifestations Sect had personally taken action to search his soul and investigate. He had confirmed there were no errors or mistakes, meaning everything was already set in stone.

The approving cultivator, considering this, didn't hesitate and issued the approval document.

After obtaining this approval, Duanmu Zhang had someone deliver it to Song Taosheng.

One of the five death warriors arranged by Kong Zhaoming immediately went all out to tamper with the document.

Originally, the approval only listed Song Taosheng. If Song Taosheng brought five others along to visit, all five would have to undergo investigation.

After the document was tampered with, the five names were added. They were betting on the laxity of the guarding cultivators and their failure to conduct a strict inspection!

Sure enough, the guarding cultivator merely glanced over it once, used a jade slip talisman for a simple check, and let them through.

In reality, according to the rules and regulations, they should have visually inspected it at least three times, utilized six jade slip talismans for a comprehensive cross-reference and review, and only permitted entry after confirming there were absolutely no issues.

There was another layer of calculation here.

Previously, Zhao Hansheng had brought Gu Qing, Chu Xuangui, Song Taosheng, Situ Gu, and others to question Qin De together. This served as a precedent.

It had only been a few days, and with the precedent still fresh in their minds, the guarding cultivators didn't find it strange.

Once Song Taosheng and his group successfully infiltrated the Cloud Prison, the Nine Heavens Cloud Fiend Formation no longer posed an obstacle.

Cloud Canopy Peak. The Summit.

In the main hall, Dong Chen and the other three positioned themselves in the four cardinal directions, having long been waiting in strict formation.

Under their assistance, the violent shaking of the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy had begun to ease. Suddenly, with an explosive sound, the nine-layered umbrella surface abruptly stalled, then began to tremble violently.

The entire Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy shook intensely like a tree caught in a gale.

Sitting cross-legged on the eastern side of the Sacrificial Altar, Dong Chen's body swayed from the sudden tremor. His expression instantly turned grave as he reacted immediately. His hands formed hand seals at astonishing speed, and streaks of golden spiritual energy shot out from his fingertips, desperately plunging into the cloud canopy.

"It's starting!" he shouted in a low voice.

The other three suddenly jolted awake.

Wei Ji sat upright in the south, his hands pressing down in the air as streaks of azure spiritual energy surged from his body. But he was ultimately only a Vice Peak Master; only a small portion of his spiritual energy was absorbed by the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy. The majority was scattered time and time again by the violent tremors.

He had to condense it again and again. In just a few breaths, his face had already turned slightly pale.

Tuoba Huang sat in the west. Just as the fiery aura around him surged forth, he suddenly let out a muffled groan as a mist of blood exploded from his shoulder. Ignoring the injury entirely, he let his crimson spiritual energy soar into the sky, pouring it into the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy.

Wang Yu sat cross-legged in the north, waving his fly whisk repeatedly. A few threads of it were blasted apart, fluttering around him.

The four of them increased their output, successfully stabilizing the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy.

Song Taosheng and his group made their way down to the lower levels of the Cloud Prison.

With every layer they passed, the guiding cultivator would hand over their duties, switching to a different person to continue leading them.

Song Taosheng remained expressionless, but relevant information echoed in his heart.

"The outer layer of the Cloud Prison is the Nine Heavens Cloud Fiend Formation. Once inside, there are three more Formations. They are the Cosmic Starlight Formation, the Eight Trigrams Bewildering Formation, and the Vajra Mountain-Suppressing Formation."

"These three formations are interlocked and operate day and night. The moment we make a move, we will be exposed first and will immediately plunge into the Formation Space. The three layers of might will stack upon us consecutively; it is no trivial matter!"

"Therefore, we must make covert arrangements along the way, scattering talismans and other formation-breaking artifacts. The earlier and more thorough these preparations are, the more time we can buy for ourselves once we strike."

Thinking of this, Song Taosheng began to strike up a conversation with the guiding guard.

"It seems there are far fewer guards in the Cloud Prison compared to the last time I came," Song Taosheng said suddenly.

The guiding cultivator sighed, "Ugh, it's all because of the renovations."

He then began to pour out his grievances.

As it turned out, whenever the Flying Cloud Assembly took place, the Cloud Prison underwent a major overhaul.

Previously, when Xiao Juxia came to participate in the Rising Cloud Minor Trial, there was a checkpoint where they entered the outskirts of the Cloud Prison to repair some Magic Artifacts and magic treasures.

After these repairs, they naturally had to be recalibrated, causing everyone up and down the Cloud Prison to be run ragged and thoroughly exhausted.

One must know that the Cloud Prison was a restricted area. Not only was it heavily layered with formations, but it also utilized a massive number of magic treasures and Magic Artifacts. All of these continuously suffered wear and tear over years and months of operation.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect used the Flying Cloud Assembly as an excuse to have a large number of cultivators repair these components under the guise of trials. Not only did this save manpower, but the materials were also paid for out of the participating cultivators' own pockets. More importantly, with a vast number of cultivators participating in the trials, the repair time was naturally shortened - it was simply killing multiple birds with one stone.

The external components were repaired by outsiders, while the internal components were handled by the Demon Subjugation Hall themselves. If they wanted to seek help from other peaks or halls within the Myriad Manifestations Sect, they would have to pay additional fees.

Once all sorts of components were repaired, they had to be recalibrated. Setting aside other things, the various formations had to be as harmonious and unified as possible. Otherwise, if they caused internal friction while operating, it would be disastrous.

At the very least, it would increase the maintenance cost of the formations. In worse scenarios, it could create gaps between the formations, forming defensive loopholes that would give ill-intentioned individuals an opportunity to exploit.

Operating multiple formations together required a formidable realm in the Dao of Formations to achieve relatively perfect compatibility.

In truth, the cost of maintaining formations was immense.

Even the Demon Subjugation Hall, as a powerful and authoritative department within the Myriad Manifestations Sect, had to carefully calculate these economic costs.

The Cloud Prison's largest defensive formation, the Nine Heavens Cloud Fiend Formation, extensively utilized Cloud Fiend Rock. By relying on this to connect with the outside Cloud Sea, it could borrow the momentum of Heaven and Earth to assist in forming the array. In this way, the formation's expenses were drastically reduced.

Even so, during peaceful times, every budget allocation for the Cloud Prison gave Zhong Dao a headache.

Song Taosheng was a Confucian cultivator; he was well-read and eloquent. Being an elder, he was also familiar with human nature. By taking the initiative to converse now, he made the guarding cultivator feel as though he was bathing in a spring breeze, as if he had met a kindred spirit, and his footsteps unconsciously slowed down.

The five death warriors behind him followed closely, having already begun taking action covertly.

They had a clear division of labor: some disguised their movements, some reconnoitered, and others secretly scattered formation-breaking and restriction-breaking talismans, setting up formation-breaking items along the way.

Cloud Canopy Peak.

The trembling of the Heaven-Bearing Cloud Canopy grew even more violent.

The rotation of its nine-layered umbrella surface had gone completely out of control - sometimes turning clockwise, sometimes counter-clockwise, sometimes almost coming to a halt, and sometimes spinning madly.

With every change in rotational direction, thick arcs of seven-colored light would shoot out from the edges of the umbrella surface, lashing out at Dong Chen and the other three, leaving behind bloody welts.

Dong Chen's face was deathly pale. Beads of sweat dripped down from his forehead onto his body, instantly mingling with his blood.

Wei Ji still sat upright, but his coarse hemp Daoist robe had been dyed red with blood in many places.

Tuoba Huang's face was flushed red, veins bulging on his forehead. His hands were tightly clenched, his knuckles pale white, as a trail of blood leaked from the corner of his mouth.

Wang Yu's breathing was ragged and chaotic. He could only wield his fly whisk with his left hand now. His right arm hung powerlessly by his side - it had been struck by an arc of seven-colored light, the bones completely shattered.

Dong Chen's expression was resolute. "Hold on! The destiny resonance is becoming increasingly intense. I'm on the verge of discovering the crux of this calamitous shift in destiny! Whatever it is, it lies right here within the main gates of our Myriad Manifestations Sect."

The spirits of the other three rallied. They all knew that once this crucial point was discovered, they would be able to turn the tables.

This was the Myriad Manifestations Sect's headquarters, after all! Once they unleashed their full power, they would undoubtedly sweep the courtyard and root out the evil, rendering themselves completely unstoppable.

Huazhang Kingdom Capital.

In the great hall, a burst of spiritual intuition flickered in the Monarch of Huazhang's heart, causing his eyes to snap open. "The time has come. Begin the national sacrifice!"

With a shift of his mind, he spat a ball of fire from his mouth.

The flame flew out and ignited the three sticks of sandalwood incense in the celadon burner. Blue smoke curled upwards, straight as a line, shooting directly toward the ceiling of the hall. The fragrance was clear and distant; catching its scent calmed the mind and soul, yet faintly carried an undertone of chilling, murderous intent.

The sun, moon, stars, mountains, rivers, plants, and names of ancient sages upon the three-tiered yellow silk began to glow brightly.

Han Song stood on the left side of the Sacrificial Altar, holding a scroll of white silk nine feet long. Every character of the three-thousand-word sacrificial text on the silk also began to shimmer faintly, as if resonating with the altar.

Han Song took a step forward and began to recite the sacrificial text:

"In the 1,237th year of the Huazhang Kingdom Calendar, the cyclical year of Jiazi, the month of mid-spring, on the first day of Dingmao. The minister and Monarch of Huazhang, Kong You, respectfully leads Zhou Zheng, Director of the Imperial Ancestral Temple; Han Song, Chancellor of the Jixia Academy; Zheng Jing, Erudite of the Imperial Academy; and Wang Shu, Directorate of the Imperial College. We dare to announce to the Sovereign Heavens and Supreme Earth, the sages of past dynasties, the First Teacher Confucius, Patriarch Cang, and the divine spirits of all preceding Confucians. We beseech the vast and righteous destiny of our Confucian sect to eternally protect Huazhang..."

His voice echoed through the hall, solemn and majestic. As Han Song's spiritual energy was continuously consumed, every character of the sacrificial text drifted out from the scroll and landed within the Golden Chapter Jade Tome.

Han Song continued his recitation: "Writing is the warp and weft of Heaven and Earth, the glorious chapter of the sun and moon, the guiding principle of all creation, and the standard of human relations. Since Patriarch Cang created writing, millet rained from the heavens, ghosts cried in the night, civilization dawned, and ten thousand generations shall forever rely upon it..."
