Mysteries Of Immortal Puppet Master

Chapter 963: Tan Zhu vs. Yue Qingshan

Soon, it was time for the third match.

Miasma Poison Martial Arena.

The daylight was pressed extremely low. Cloudless and windless, there was only a gloomy, deep valley, the bottom of which was covered with blackish-brown sludge, and on the surface of the mud were grayish-green puddles.

Bubbles would occasionally bulge from the puddles; when they burst, a fishy and bitter smoke would rise, flowing slowly close to the ground. The surrounding mountain walls presented a sickly bruised-black color, and the surface of the rocks was hung with wet traces of moss.

Occasionally, a few dead trees grew diagonally among the cliffs, their branches twisted and bark peeling, roots drilling out from the stone crevices like gaunt fingers gripping the mountain wall.

Whenever the yin wind blew through the valley, the dead branches would rub against each other, emitting a fine, piercing sound that made one's teeth ache.

This was a martial arena specially set up by the Myriad Manifestations Sect for poison cultivators, miasma cultivators, corpse cultivators, and shadowy marsh cultivators.

According to the arrangements of Ning Zhuo and Chunyangzi, Nanming Stronghold dispatched Tan Zhu for the third match, leaving the White Cloud Village and Earth Expansion Alliance faction to choose how to respond. Because Nanming Stronghold had the initiative, Tan Zhu possessed the right to select the venue.

He did not hesitate and directly chose this place.

Poison smoke would not be easily dispersed by mountain winds here, and miasma would not be quickly purified by Formations here. The sludge at the valley bottom could hide poison, the grayish-green puddles could nurture poison, and the bruised-black mountain walls could gather poison gas in reverse.

If an ordinary Righteous Path cultivator entered, even without exchanging blows, just standing there for a moment would make them feel their spiritual energy circulating sluggishly and their chest feeling heavy with stagnant blood and qi.

Cultivators from all three factions had already taken their seats.

In the Nanming Stronghold seating area, Chunyangzi sat properly in the main seat, his expression calm, the injury on the webbing of his right hand already covered by his cuff.

Ning Zhuo sat slightly behind, a white-robed youth with clear, bright eyes.

The Red-Robed Guest leaned back in his chair, his gaze gloomy and cold.

Tu Yuanzi looked at the sludge at the valley bottom, a look of exploration on his face.

The Nine Fires Dragon Lord was not present.

Poison Annihilation Wanderer Tan Zhu appeared in the martial arena, standing atop a giant black boulder at the valley bottom.

He wore a grayish-black deep robe with a half-sleeve overcoat of the same color. His robes hung down heavily, looking as if they had been soaked in cold rain for many years. His collar stood up half an inch, covering his neck. His skin was abnormally pale, his eyebrows thin and faint, the bridge of his nose high and straight, and his breathing was extremely light and slow.

Miasma approached him from all directions, flowing slowly around him.

His expression was gloomy and obscure; he did not look like a Nascent Soul cultivator about to engage in a spell battle at all. Instead, he resembled a chronically ill old man bored out of his mind in a damp, dark valley.

The combatant from the Earth Expansion Alliance also took the field.

Yue Qingshan!

His figure was hunched, his back slightly bent, and his arms were extremely long. The wrinkles on his face were deeply etched, with an old scar at the corner of his left eye. His skin was a sallow, earthy yellow, and his eyebrows were sparse.

He wore a deep yellow long robe without any magnificent decorations on its surface; only a ring of dark mud patterns was sewn along the hem.

Beneath Yue Qingshan's feet, he was supported by a massive earth cloud.

The cloud was earthy-yellow, thick, and turbid, yet its edges continuously flowed. Mud seemed to churn within the heart of the cloud, and strands of yellowish-brown dust threads hung down from its base. When the dust threads fell into midair, they condensed into fine sand before dispersing on their own.

For this battle, he had received this sponsorship.

Ning Zhuo's pupils shrank slightly; with just a single glance, he immediately understood in his heart: "This cloud contains an incredibly heavy earth and cloud attribute!"

This type of earth cloud could float in the air, carry people, gather dust into sand, condense earth into stone, transform into mud rain, and drop rolling boulders. It was clearly a cloud, yet it possessed heavy earth properties. Suspended high above, it carried the momentum of a falling landslide.

Old Man Wandering Cloud's wide sleeves moved lightly, white clouds subtly drifting within them. Watching this scene, he sighed in his heart: For this battle, White Cloud Village had mobilized its foundation and lent it directly to Yue Qingshan. It couldn't be helped; the pressure Tan Zhu brought to White Cloud Village and the Earth Expansion Alliance was truly too great. Since the agreement for the Five Victories Martial Demonstration began, the two factions, and even many of the major powers across Flowing Cloud Peak, had been doing everything possible to seek a possibility of defeating Tan Zhu.

Yue Qingshan stepped on the earth cloud, suspended thirty zhang above Tan Zhu. The earth cloud slowly rotated, yellow mud churning within it, the friction of sand and stone sounding as fine and dense as rain.

Deep in the valley, Tan Zhu seemed to merge into one with the black boulder beneath his feet, surrounded by heavy mist.

The jade chime rang out.

The third battle, begins!

No pleasantries.

No probing.

Yue Qingshan pressed both hands downward.

The earth cloud abruptly expanded, its base splitting open with countless cracks. The next moment, rolling mud and sand poured down from the cloud like a yellowish-brown heavenly river hanging upside down in the valley!

Within the mud and sand were countless crushed stones; as the stones plummeted, they crashed into each other with sounds as dense as drumbeats. Moreover, giant boulders condensed within the cloud, carrying a heavy earthen glow as they crashed down from on high.

Boom!

The first giant boulder smashed into the valley bottom, blasting black mud over ten zhang high. The muddy water splashed onto the protective light barrier around the martial arena, immediately emitting sizzling, corrosive sounds.

Then came the second, the third, the fourth.

Rolling boulders fell like a shower of stars.

Mud rain poured down like a sky-obscuring waterfall!

For a time, the entire Miasma Poison Martial Arena was enveloped by an earthy-yellow storm. The sludge at the valley bottom was smashed and rolled about everywhere, and the grayish-green puddles exploded into clouds of poison mist.

The mountain walls trembled, dead trees snapped, and stone fragments flew like arrows.

The light barrier of the protective Formation in front of the spectator seats rippled continuously. Some cultivators, just from watching, turned slightly pale and subconsciously leaned back.

In the Nanming Stronghold seating area, no one spoke. Many cultivators were awed and couldn't help but grow nervous.

Ning Zhuo stared intently at the arena.

Tan Zhu did not dodge in the slightest.

He was surprisingly still standing in his original spot!

The earth cloud was in the sky, the mud and stones were above. He stood atop the black boulder at the valley bottom, his gaunt figure almost obscured by the sky-filling mud and sand. As the rolling boulders plummeted, his robes fluttered lightly, swaying subtly like a wisp of shadow in the earthy-yellow storm.

Only when the first wave of mud and stone pressed within three zhang of the top of his head did he slowly raise his right hand.

His hand was pale and gaunt, his knuckles slightly protruding, palm facing upward.

A wisp of purplish-black poison smoke rose soundlessly.

The poison smoke was initially just a thin line, but the moment it touched the mud rain, it corroded it, transforming it into even more poison smoke.

Because of this, in the spectators' field of vision, the poison smoke rapidly expanded into an extremely deep and dark smoke screen.

The smoke screen was neither high nor wide, but it was incredibly dense, like a thin layer of ink-colored curtain splashed across the dark night.

As mud, sand, and crushed stones fell into it, their sounds became muffled.

When the rolling boulders smashed into it, they were supported by the poison mist, their speed dropping sharply. Large black spots surfaced on them, and starting from the outside, they were rapidly whittled away inch by inch, as if gnawed by something.

Yue Qingshan's pupils shrank slightly.

His move wasn't just using an earth cloud to smash someone. In fact, mixed within the earth cloud were Earth Vein Sand, Spirit-Locking Mud, and Sunken Mountain Stone (all stuffed into the yellow cloud during pre-battle preparations). As the three fell together, they could not only crush the body with their weight but also use their earth properties to block the opponent's spiritual energy.

If ordinary poison smoke encountered such a crashing strike from an earth cloud, it would only be scattered by the mud rain or even completely sealed by the earth properties.

Yet, Tan Zhu's poison smoke not only didn't scatter but actually drilled into the gaps between the mud, sand, and stones.

The poison smoke acted like a silent colony of ants, gnawing away all the earth properties in the earth cloud's falling debris.

The mud and sand no longer clumped together, the rolling boulders lost their solidity, and the Spirit-Locking Mud lost its spirit-locking effect.

The offensive that originally fell like a landslide crumbled from the inside out just three feet in front of Tan Zhu, turning into bursts of blackened dust!

The dust fell at his feet, not even staining the hem of his robes.

Tan Zhu looked up, but his gaze went into a momentary daze.

The sky-filling mud and stone, the rumbling in the valley, the cold and damp smell of mud.

These things suddenly brought him back to a long, long time ago.

At that time, he was still just a child in a mountain village; he wasn't called Zhong Limei, nor did he know what Danxia Peak was.

The rain had poured for many days.

At night, an extremely heavy, muffled thud first came from outside. His father woke up with a start from the bed, threw on a coat, and rushed out to look. His mother lit the oil lamp, and just as the flame brightened, a layer of mud dust shook loose from the roof beam.

Then came the landslide.

The mudslide poured down from the bruised-black mountain behind the village. The sound was not like thunder, because thunder would pass, while the landslide felt as if the entire heaven and earth were completely collapsing down.

The houses were the first to be knocked askew.

Walls cracked open, window paper shattered, and cold rain mixed with muddy water surged fiercely into the house.

His father rushed back in, his face covered in mud, hoarsely shouting: "Get under the beam!"

The house struggled to resist the impact.

It could only be considered temporarily safe.

The snapped main beam wedged diagonally into the corner of the house, unexpectedly propping up a narrow gap. His father and mother pushed their several children inside. The space was too small to fit everyone, and the wooden beam was still creaking and groaning. Every sound it made was like the God of Death panting with a bowed head.

The muddy water had already surged up to their knees.

Outside, there were people crying and shouting, livestock neighing, and the loud crashes of other houses in the village being swallowed up.

Zhong Limei was stuffed to the very back by his mother, his chest practically pressed against the splintered wood. His siblings were also pushed to his side; several small faces were squeezed together in the muddy water and darkness, so terrified they couldn't even cry.

The main beam continued to sink.

His father used his shoulder to bear the weight of the pillar, the veins on his neck bulging. His mother, however, suddenly grew ruthless in the darkness, raising her foot and kicking the outermost child towards the narrow gap outside the beam.

"Get out!" Her voice was frantic and hoarse.

It wasn't that she didn't want her children.

It was that she knew the space under the beam could no longer hold up.

One child was kicked out of the gap, rolling into the muddy water. The second was also kicked out. When it was Zhong Limei's turn, his mother's foot kicked his waist with astonishing force!

Zhong Limei tumbled out from under the beam, crashing into a broken cabinet door, splitting his forehead open.

He turned his head and saw his mother still trying to push out his youngest sister.

At that very moment, the main beam completely snapped.

Boom!

The roof collapsed, yellow mud, stones, and broken wood pressing down all at once.

Darkness swallowed everything.

His father's muffled groan, his mother's shouts, and his siblings' cries were all buried by the mud and stone. Zhong Limei was also buried alive.

The space was as narrow as an unsealed coffin.

His chest was pressed so hard it could barely rise and fall, and blood flowed down the corner of his forehead into his eyes.

In the darkness, he woke up and passed out, passed out and woke up.

In the end, he could no longer hear his family's voices.

He could only hear the sound of muddy water continuously dripping and the slow cracking of wooden beams.

Until a hand broke through the mud layer and dug him out of the darkness!

That hand carried the fragrance of Medicinal Pills.

Severely injured and on the verge of death, he was rescued and brought back to the Myriad Manifestations Sect, lying in bed for many days before surviving.

The owner of that hand later became his master.

...

In the Miasma Poison Martial Arena, the first wave of the earth cloud offensive had already been completely dissolved by the poison smoke.

Tan Zhu stood on the black boulder, the purplish-black smoke screen slowly retracting into his palm. He didn't look at the spectator seats, nor did he even look at Yue Qingshan; he just lowered his head and coughed.

Yue Qingshan's expression was heavy.

Tan Zhu's quasi-Divine Art!

"This is definitely no ordinary poison art; it's indeed troublesome!"

The poison smoke Tan Zhu spawned was not ostentatious, yet it could corrode spiritual energy, ruin earth properties, and annihilate spiritual intuition. The mud, sand, and rolling boulders dropped by the earth cloud were all easily whittled into dust by it.

In the Earth Expansion Alliance seating area, everyone wore a grave expression.

The White Cloud Village seating area was dead silent.

"I still have follow-up moves." Yue Qingshan took a deep breath, steadying his state of mind.

He poured in spiritual energy, and the earth cloud beneath his feet churned and rapidly expanded, with long silhouettes bulging from within the cloud's body.

Those silhouettes swam within the cloud, resembling long snakes and also mountain ranges. Soon, the edges of the earth cloud split open, and nine mountain stone long snakes poked their heads out of the cloud.

Each long snake was condensed from yellow mud, crushed stones, and ore sand fibers. Their bodies were as thick as large trees, and their scales were pieces of stone shells. When the snakes opened and closed their mouths, what they flicked out were not forked tongues, but shovel-like shapes made of long, narrow stone pieces.

Nine snakes coiling in the sky!

The earth cloud floated above, and the nine mountain stone long snakes hung their bodies down, slithering towards Tan Zhu from all directions.

They did not attack straightforwardly; instead, they coiled, crisscrossed, and tightened in midair, like a living stone formation.

"Here it comes." The cultivators of the Earth Expansion Alliance showed subtle changes in their expressions, all carrying looks of anticipation.

This method was specifically prepared by them for Tan Zhu!

The snake bodies focused on entanglement, the snake tongues focused on eradication, and the stone scales could wear down the poison smoke. Faint poison-purifying runes lit up on the belly of each long snake.

This time, Yue Qingshan had clearly changed his tactics; he no longer relied on large-scale mud and stone to suppress the arena, but instead used the earth cloud to shape-shift, utilizing the mountain stone long snakes to search for gaps in Tan Zhu's poison smoke.

The nine snakes pounced down together!

The stone scales rubbed against each other, emitting a heart-palpitating rustling sound. The snakes opened their mouths wide, and their stone tongues thrust forward rapidly, like nine bruised-black long blades piercing towards Tan Zhu from midair.

Tan Zhu still stood firmly in his original spot.

His gaze was like smoke and clouds; what the mountain stone long snakes brought him was not pressure, but rather another memory from the past.

Not long after he joined the Sect, he studied pill refining at Danxia Peak and received his new name: Zhong Limei.

Zhong Limei was not a genius.

He was slow at identifying herbs, slow at controlling fire, and even slow at memorizing pill recipes. Medicinal properties that others could memorize in three days took him seven. The heat level that others could discern with a single glance, he had to stare at the furnace fire until his eyes ached, accumulating a lot of experience before he could gradually and barely distinguish it.

Geniuses were as common as clouds on Danxia Peak; standing among them, he felt like the mud beneath those clouds.

He was as steady as a mountain stone.

Every day before dawn, he would go to the alchemy room to sweep the furnace ash. He copied notes on medicinal properties neatly, page by page. Old pill furnaces that others no longer used, he would take and use. Defective medicinal ingredients that others despised, he would collect bit by bit.

Once, he was in charge of processing a batch of snake-type materials.

Those were supplementary ingredients for refining the "Meridian-Restoring Spirit-Continuing Pill," which was related to an extremely important furnace of precious pills. The materials included snake gallbladders, snake tendons, snake skin, snake saliva, and a section of an extremely precious azure-patterned snake bone.

Snake-type materials were most afraid of being over-processed; if the fire was too heavy, the fishy poison would enter the pill, and if the fire was too light, the spiritual energy would not emerge.

Zhong Limei was careful to the extreme.

However, his Innate Talent was limited, and his judgment of the snake saliva's stickiness was a breath too slow. After the snake saliva entered the furnace, it clashed with the bitterness of the snake gallbladder, and the pill liquid suddenly recoiled. The furnace fire should have been suppressed, but because he was afraid of ruining the azure-patterned snake bone, he hesitated.

Just this one breath.

The pill furnace exploded!

The furnace lid flew up, a wave of fire smashed into the roof beam, and the fishy, medicinal snake smoke blanketed the alchemy room. Shards of the furnace grazed his arm, causing blood to flow endlessly. He fell into a brief coma.

When he woke up, his first glance was towards the pill furnace.

The pill furnace was cracked!

"It's over, how much does the pill furnace cost! I could never afford to pay for it." His face was pale, his expression filled with despair.

He couldn't afford it.

He really couldn't afford it.

His senior brother rushed in and saw the exploded pill furnace, saw Zhong Limei's robes scorched black and half his eyebrows burned away.

His senior brother immediately rushed to Zhong Limei's side: "Can your eyes still see? Can your hands still move?"

Zhong Limei was stunned.

His arm was still bleeding, and his lips trembled as he wailed, "Senior Brother, the furnace..."

His senior brother snorted coldly in anger: "To hell with the furnace! A broken pill furnace can be repaired, but if a person is broken, then it's a disaster!"

After checking him over, the senior brother breathed a sigh of relief: "You're lucky, it's only a light injury."

Zhong Limei's eyes were entirely bloodshot as he stared blankly at the shattered pill furnace: "Senior Brother, the furnace."

His senior brother sighed and patted him on the back: "This is my responsibility, assigning you to process the medicinal herbs this time. Stop worrying, Little Zhong, I will take responsibility for this matter!"

Later, his senior brother bore all the losses for the pill furnace on his behalf.

Not only did he sell off his protective Magic Artifact, but he also took on two years of hard labor for the Sect.
