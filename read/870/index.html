Chapter 870: Spiritual Energy Differences

Myriad Manifestations Sect, North Frost Mountain Gate.

Baimang Valley.

The valley was shrouded in clouds and mist all year round. Ancient trees towered into the sky, with vines hanging down like curtains. A stream babbled down the mountain ravine, its crisp sound interweaving with the chirping of birds and the roars of beasts in the forest to form a primal cacophony.

This place was filled with a pure naturalness, a vast and desolate wildness. Just like its master, Cang Yazi - uncouth, indifferent to his appearance, seeking only freedom and ease.

Cang Yazi sat on a rock.

He looked to be in his fifties. His long, grayish-white hair fell loosely, held up by a beast bone hairpin. He had a rugged face with high cheekbones and deep-set eyes; though his eyes were small, they radiated a piercing light.

He wore a grayish-brown Daoist robe, washed until it was faded. There were several mended patches on the cuffs and collar, the stitches large and clumsy, as if he had sewn them himself, stitch by stitch. Tied around his waist was an animal skin belt, from which hung seven or eight small cloth bags containing his commonly used spiritual materials, Medicinal Pills, and Magic Artifacts.

At this moment, a giant beast was prostrating at his feet.

It was Cang Yazi's most loyal spirit beast companion, cultivated for over a hundred years. It was an Earth Dragon named "Huang Yue."

The Earth Dragon possessed a certain amount of dragon bloodline and resembled a giant monitor lizard.

It was three zhang long, its entire body covered in earth-brown scales. The scales bore rock-like patterns, rough and heavy. Its four limbs were thick as pillars, the tips of its claws sharp as hooks. Its tail was thick and long; a single whip of it possessed the power to shatter gold and crack stone.

Its head was triangular, with deep-set eye sockets. Its vertical pupils were amber-colored, currently half-open and half-closed, yet unable to conceal the sharpness within.

Today was the Earth Dragon "Huang Yue's" big day.

Cang Yazi took out a Medicinal Pill the size of a fist, looking as if it were cast from metal.

Transformation Pill!

Cang Yazi tossed it directly to the Earth Dragon. "Eat it."

The Earth Dragon Huang Yue opened his large mouth and swallowed it whole without the slightest hesitation.

He lay quietly on the ground, his vertical pupils half-open and half-closed. His tongue occasionally flicked out to lick his lips, as if savoring the taste of the pill.

Cang Yazi remained seated where he was, staring intently at his beloved pet.

This was the calm before the storm.

Suddenly, the Earth Dragon's body violently stiffened.

His vertical pupils suddenly dilated, then violently contracted. A low, guttural roar welled up from deep within his throat. The roar carried an unsuppressible, heart-rending agony!

The Earth Dragon's body began to tremble violently.

The trembling spread from a specific area to his entire body, becoming increasingly intense and uncontrollable, as if a pair of invisible hands were kneading his body - rubbing, squeezing, and stretching him like dough.

Cang Yazi's face was filled with a grave expression.

The Earth Dragon thrashed his body madly. His tail whipped wildly against the ground, splitting rocks or carving deep ravines into the mountain walls.

Large patches of scales fell off, and his skin tore open piece by piece, revealing bright red flesh and blood.

Patterns began to appear on the flesh.

These were the traces washed out by the medicinal efficacy of the Transformation Pill as it surged through the Earth Dragon's entire body.

The Earth Dragon's spiritual energy, vital essence blood, and mental energy all followed these traces, converging into them.

The flesh rapidly collapsed and deformed. His bones emitted cracking sounds as they were continuously destroyed, like rotten branches being stepped on and crushed one by one.

Cang Yazi's breathing also became somewhat hurried.

The Earth Dragon Huang Yue's roars grew sharper and more mournful, grating like a blunt knife scraping against an iron plate - so piercing that it made one's scalp tingle.

The most dangerous moment of the transformation had arrived!

In an instant, it was as if a violent, silent explosion detonated deep within the Earth Dragon's soul.

Huang Yue's body suddenly arched up like a fully drawn bow. His head threw backward, mouth wide open to reveal two rows of sharp teeth. A large volume of fresh blood surged from his throat like a fountain.

His body shattered from the very depths, immediately followed by reconstruction.

What shattered were beast bones; what reformed were human bones. What shattered were beast meridians; what reformed were human meridians. What shattered was a beast heart; what reformed was a human heart.

A pale yellow halo first rippled outward from the depths of his soul, just like a stone cast into water.

Ripple after ripple was generated, enveloping his entire body, bathing his whole body and mind in the yellow light.

The halo rapidly intensified and condensed, transforming into a massive orb of light.

The yellow orb of light ultimately took on an oval shape, lying motionless on the ground.

Cang Yazi could hardly conceal his nervousness; his hands subconsciously clenched into fists.

The next moment, a tiny beam of light pierced out of the yellow orb. Then, more than a dozen beams successively shot outwards from within, breaking through the orb's surface and radiating in all directions.

Looking from the outside, Cang Yazi could vaguely see that within the yellow orb of light, the Earth Dragon's battered body was melting like water. His four limbs began to elongate, thin out, and straighten.

The thick beast claws retracted. His five toes pulled together, merged, and deformed, gradually turning into human soles.

His tail shortened; the tail vertebrae fused into the spine segment by segment, eventually disappearing completely.

His torso also shortened. The chest cavity contracted, and the abdominal cavity tightened as a waist gradually revealed the curves of a human.

The slowest and most agonizing change was the head.

The Earth Dragon's triangular head needed to become a human's round head. The skull had to be reshaped, the jawbone rearranged, and the teeth had to shift from sharp beast fangs to flat human teeth. The most agonizing part was the brain - changing from a beast brain to a human brain meant countless nerves, blood vessels, and the most complex structures needed to be entirely rebuilt.

The Earth Dragon's skull emitted "clack, clack" sounds, as if being kneaded by a pair of invisible hands, slowly but firmly altering its shape. His facial muscles twitched, his eyelids fluttered continuously, his nostrils flared and contracted desperately, and his mouth constantly opened and closed.

Hundreds of tiny light beams dispersed one after another. The yellow orb of light was reduced to a mere membrane, looking like a sheet of yellow paper.

A human hand tore through the yellow paper, and then a naked body stood upright. The youth "Huang Yue" revealed himself before Cang Yazi's eyes.

Huang Yue opened his mouth, speaking in a human voice to Cang Yazi: "M-Master."

Cang Yazi's eyes had already grown red, and he couldn't help but burst into loud laughter. He jumped down from the rock, strode quickly in front of Huang Yue, and took out a set of animal skin clothes to drape over him.

"The transformation was a success! Huang Yue, from now on, you can cultivate using a human body. Your cultivation will advance a thousand miles a day. You have now broken free from the constraints of the spirit beast race and completely smashed your upper limit."

Cang Yazi was proficient in Beast Taming, one of the hundred arts of Cultivation.

The Beast Taming school he pursued was the Transformation school.

The characteristic of this school was to convert tamed beasts into human form for cultivation. Then, during battle, they would revert to their original forms, thus possessing both strength and intellect.

Beast form and human form each had their own merits. The former had rough skin and thick flesh, boasting swift and fierce strength. The latter frequently generated wisdom and possessed extraordinary comprehension.

Once tamed beasts transformed into human form, they could cultivate the vast sea of Cultivation Arts in the Cultivation World. At the same time, their wisdom would constantly emerge, and spiritual energy would fill them in, breaking through the limits of beast kind. After all, humans were the most spiritual of all things. In this era, the human form was naturally favored and beloved by Heaven and Earth.

Generally speaking, if a tamed beast could cultivate a human-shaped Nascent Soul, it could transform its body into the appearance of a child. This was what it meant to transform.

But this kind of transformation was often incomplete.

On one hand, it was because of the Cultivation Arts. The vast majority of a tamed beast's methods were Demon Cultivator arts. These methods could be traced back to the era of the Blood Slaughter Dynasty. Demon Cultivator methods typically reinforced and strengthened a demonic beast's innate advantages - such as fangs and claws, wings, venomous stingers, and so forth.

Even if they cultivated Human methods, those were not tailor-made for tamed beasts. Therefore, even if they broke their core and formed a Nascent Soul, the 'infant' would not be entirely human-shaped; it would still retain some beast-like characteristics to varying degrees.

On the other hand, the sheer difficulty was incredibly high.

Transformation was akin to changing the very form of life. Humans and beasts were two entirely different species from the start. To completely shift from one end of the spectrum to the other meant that the greater the degree of transformation, the more terrifying the difficulty became.

Huang Yue himself was only at the Golden Core level; he had not yet reached the stage of breaking the core to form a Nascent Soul.

Beasts had limited spiritual energy and low comprehension. Even with superior Cultivation Arts, it was difficult for them to grasp the profound meanings within. Thus, their active cultivation progressed at an agonizingly slow pace.

For Huang Yue to wait until he broke his core to form a Nascent Soul, it would take at least several hundred more years.

Cang Yazi's school of Beast Taming aimed precisely at making tamed beasts like Huang Yue transform ahead of time. It allowed them to possess a human body before even reaching the Nascent Soul stage.

Once they possessed a human body, under the resonance between Heaven and Man, their spiritual energy would naturally elevate, and their comprehension would follow suit.

A human body could adapt to the vast majority of Cultivation Arts. And these cultivation methods were not ancient, archaic ones; they were the refined fruits of countless predecessors' explorations, trials, and improvements.

Of course, if the tamed beast was already practicing a certain method, they might need to dual-cultivate, convert, or modify the method.

These were but minor flaws.

The flaws did not obscure the jade's brilliance.

The benefits brought by possessing a human body - especially obtaining it early - were immensely huge.

The form of the Nascent Soul condensed upon breaking the core would be far more humanoid than if cultivated in beast form. The transformations in later stages would also be much more thorough.

Cang Yazi's divine sense enveloped Huang Yue, gaining a thorough understanding of his physical condition.

"Your meridians are snapped in seven places, many of your internal organs have shifted, and thirty percent of your divine sense has dissipated."

"You need to recuperate for a while. During this period, it's best to stay in bed. You cannot fight, nor can you cultivate."

Huang Yue nodded. "U-Understood, Master."

He had learned it in advance, but speaking the human language with a physical body for the first time still required a period of familiarization.

Cang Yazi brought Huang Yue into the cave dwelling. After settling the latter down, he fell deep into thought.

The Earth Dragon Huang Yue had breached this massive hurdle of transformation and would enjoy endless fortune hereafter. However, Cang Yazi still had a few other spirit beasts by his side that had not yet transformed.

For instance, the Blue-Eyed Water Ape, the Frost-Horned Heavenly Deer, and the Red-Crowned Ink-Feathered Crane.

These spirit beasts had accompanied Cang Yazi for many years. They were his tamed beasts, as well as his loyal companions.

From Cang Yazi's perspective, he did not want any of his tamed beasts to fail their transformation and lose their lives.

Yet, unfortunately, to give these tamed beasts a brighter future, an early transformation was unavoidable.

"If I can obtain the Nanming Fire Furnace and the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit, the various pills I refine will undoubtedly be far more outstanding!"

Cang Yazi majored in Beast Taming and minored in Pill Refining. He was most adept at refining five types of Medicinal Pills: Transformation Pills, Beast King Pills, Beast Yuan Pills, Beast Spirit Pills, and Beast Rations.

Transformation Pills allowed spirit beasts to transform ahead of time.

Beast King Pills helped spirit beasts possess kingly aura, allowing them to become the king of their packs.

Beast Yuan Pills could increase a spirit beast's cultivation.

Beast Spirit Pills, meanwhile, elevated a spirit beast's wisdom.

Beast Rations were the most complex; they were spirit beast food crafted with different nutritional combinations tailored to varying beast species and growth stages.

"If an artifact can generate spiritual energy, it is bound to be even more precious."

"If I can possess the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit, I'll trade for a Spirit-Endowing Secret Art. At the very least, it can help the Red-Crowned Ink-Feathered Crane ascend its spirit."

"After all, both the Vermilion Bird and the flying crane belong to the avian category."

Cang Yazi's Beast Taming arts were formidable. From his professional point of view, if he could control the Nanming Fire Furnace himself, the future transformation of the Red-Crowned Ink-Feathered Crane would definitely be the least difficult and most secure.

Myriad Manifestations Sect, Jade Blood Mountain Gate.

Blood Pill Cave Dwelling.

A blood-red pill furnace sat firmly in the center of the cave dwelling. The furnace body was about the height of a person, featuring three legs and two ears. Its entire form was a deep, dark red - the red of coagulated blood, dull, heavy, and carrying a nauseatingly sweet metallic scent.

Engraved on the furnace body was an illustration of a sea of blood. In the image, a sea of blood surged and churned, corpses and bones floating and sinking within. Countless twisted faces struggled, wailed, and drowned in the bloody waves. These faces belonged to men and women, young and old; some were ferocious, some terrified, some despairing, and some mad - each face represented a life sacrificed when the pill furnace was forged.

Hanging from the left and right furnace ears were human-bone bells. As the temperature of the pill fire changed, they would emit different sounds - sometimes like an infant crying, sometimes like a woman weeping, sometimes like an old man sighing, and sometimes like a strong warrior roaring. The sounds were ever-changing and never repeated.

Three completely crimson venomous snakes coiled and wrapped around the three legs of the furnace.

Each one was as thick as an adult's arm, their heads raised, tongues flicking in and out, and eyes as scarlet as blood.

Whenever their master, the Red Robed Guest, refined pills, the three blood-venom snakes would spit out blood-venom flames to fuel the pill fire.

The Red Robed Guest was an itinerant cultivator who practiced the Blood Demon Great Art. He fed on blood and sought the Dao through slaughter. Unfortunately, his innate talent was limited. Although cultivating demonic arts was swift and rapid, he was stuck at a bottleneck, unable to advance an inch, and had fallen into agonizing contemplation.

The turning point was an alchemy scripture named the Blood Pill Scripture.

This was a secret alchemy manual left behind by ancient Demonic Cultivators. It recorded methods of refining various blood pills using human blood, bones, and souls as ingredients.

Especially the Blood Demon Pill within it - crafted from human blood, bones, and souls - swallowing it would allow one to temporarily enter a Blood Demon state, skyrocketing their combat strength by ten times. However, the price was that after consumption, they would fall into a state of weakness for three days and three nights, effectively becoming a cripple.

The Red Robed Guest reached out, his palm caressing the surface of the Blood Sea Venom Snake Furnace, but in his heart, he was thinking of the Nanming Fire Furnace.

The Blood Sea Venom Snake Furnace suited him better; it was something he had forced people to forge by referencing the contents of the Blood Pill Scripture.

"But if I possess the Nanming Fire Furnace, I can use it to immediately ascend to the upper echelons of the sect."

"Instead of being stuck here, effectively in exile!"

"By utilizing the sect, I can openly and legitimately kill people and refine pills!!"

Myriad Manifestations Sect Main Mountain Gate, Bluestone Cave Dwelling.

Cultivation Chamber.

Sun Lingtong: "Little Zhuo, have you really decided to go all out and fight for that Nanming Fire Furnace?"

Ning Zhuo nodded. "Boss, my alchemy skills are still very weak."

"To elevate this craft, I have to refine pills with my own hands and constantly try, over and over again."

"This is different from my situation with artifact refining and formation setting. The latter two both have the reinforcement of my realms. But with alchemy, I have to improve step by step on my own."

Ning Zhuo shifted the topic. "But if I have the Nanming Fire Furnace and the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit, then my alchemy aspect will immediately receive a massive boost."

"This boost is near perfect!"

"Over a thousand years ago, the Nanming Fire Furnace was already born. Afterward, it has always been in the hands of Danxia Peak. The tasks of refining Medicinal Pills that this furnace has undertaken can be said to be as vast as the open sea."

"Even without me, relying solely on the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit itself, it can autonomously refine pills and medicine."

"In the future, when my alchemy skills improve, the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit will help me control the heat, inspect the medicinal herbs, and so on."

"Even right now, with the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit teaching me hand in hand, my alchemy craft can experience rapid improvement."

Sun Lingtong nodded; he was persuaded by Ning Zhuo's reasoning.

"The Nanming Fire Furnace is worth your all-out effort to obtain."

"I've calculated it, Little Zhuo. Actually, you have quite a few chips in your hands."

"First, you have Jia Luanzhen's lifelong experience and memories. Over these past few days, your alchemy skills have steadily climbed higher, giving you a solid foundation."

"Second, you understand spiritual energy quite well. You have the Cremation Prajna Spirit Release Sutra, this Buddhist Sect secret method uses flames to burn all things and extract spiritual energy. Perhaps you can burn other objects to nourish the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit's spiritual energy, thereby coaxing it to submit to you."

"Third, your Five Elements realm is very high. Both the Vermilion Bird Artifact Spirit and the Nanming Fire Furnace are strongly related to the Fire Element. You also have the Fire Element Heart Temple augmenting your own body."

"Your chances of snatching the Nanming Fire Furnace are very high!"
