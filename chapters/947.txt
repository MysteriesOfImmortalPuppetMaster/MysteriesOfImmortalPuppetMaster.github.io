Chapter 947: Blood Ember Fire Seed!

Within the stone chalice, the flame was no larger than a bean. Its color was deep crimson, yet the core of the flame was as dark and heavy as iron.

It burned quietly, lacking the dancing frivolity of ordinary spiritual fires. Instead, it contained an aura of the vicissitudes of time.

A Fire Seed!

Ning Zhuo knew very well how precious it was.

"This fire is named Blood Ember," the Red-Robed Guest said in a calm and unhurried tone. "Years ago, I obtained it from an ancient sacrificial altar. That altar was half-sunken into the earth, surrounded by a hundred li of scorched earth. The spiritual energy there was dry and desolate, devoid of any plant life. Only this single flame in the chalice remained unextinguished through ten thousand years."

Ning Zhuo focused his gaze.

He saw that on the outside of the flame, crimson ash as fine as dust slowly swirled. The ash did not scatter; it merely rose and fell with the intent of the fire, occasionally gathering and separating, like the lingering traces of some ancient sacrificial law.

The Red-Robed Guest stated indifferently, "This fire cannot be subdued by ordinary refinement methods. Pouring spiritual energy into it is merely adding firewood; forcibly restraining it will only cause the fire to backlash. The only thing that can truly move it is a segment of a prayer left on the altar."

At this point, the corners of his lips lifted slightly. His expression remained lazy, but his gaze secretly landed on Ning Zhuo's face.

"I can lend you the prayer to read. If you can chant it and draw a response from this fire, I will temporarily lend it to you. If you cannot, it still counts as me giving you a chance. Whether this succeeds or fails, it will count as me repaying your favor!"

Ning Zhuo naturally understood.

This was a repayment for healing his injuries, and also a test.

The Red-Robed Guest didn't directly shove the treasure to him; instead, he placed a door in front of him. The door was already cracked open, but whether it could be pushed open entirely depended on Ning Zhuo himself.

Ning Zhuo cupped his hands. "Many thanks to Senior for bestowing this opportunity."

The Red-Robed Guest took out a broken stone fragment. Its edges were charred black, and its surface was carved with many distorted ancient characters, resembling bird claws scratching through fire or beast fangs gnawing on stone.

"This is a rather fragmented prayer."

Ning Zhuo did not recognize these words, but he knew how to use them.

He lightly touched it with his divine sense, and the scene before his eyes immediately changed.

He felt as if he were standing on a crimson-black land.

The heavenly dome hung low, the ancient sun was like copper, and at the end of the wasteland stood a ruined altar.

Around the altar, ancient people draped in beast skins and painted with crimson patterns prostrated on the ground. Heavy stone drums beat one after another, the sound waves causing the earth to undulate. In the center of the altar, a blood-colored stone chalice hung high. A flame slowly rose from the chalice, illuminating a figure of blood and fire so massive it defied description.

It did not have clear facial features, but rather embodied an ancient divine might.

The moment Ning Zhuo's divine sense made contact, the spiritual energy and blood in his chest were tugged. His blood flow abruptly accelerated, and his spiritual energy was also drawn by the intent of the fire, racing through his meridians cycle after cycle. With every cycle, he gained an inexplicable fraction of comprehension regarding the ancient characters on the stone fragment.

Though he clearly did not recognize the words, he could understand their meaning.

Though he had never learned the pronunciation, he could articulate the sounds.

Ning Zhuo's lips parted slightly. His voice was initially too low to hear, but it gradually became clear: "He-Wu Crimson Chalice, burning blood for illumination."

The flame in the stone chalice suddenly trembled.

The Red-Robed Guest had been half-leaning on his fire couch, his demeanor relaxed. Upon hearing this line, his eyelids lifted imperceptibly, and his knuckles gently paused on his knee.

This single line had taken him three full days to comprehend back then, yet Ning Zhuo was able to utter the true sound with just a touch.

Ning Zhuo did not notice the Red-Robed Guest's subtle reaction. At this moment, his divine sense had completely sunk into the depths of the sacrificial text. In the illusion before him, the stone drums on the wasteland grew louder and louder, and the firelight on the altar illuminated his mind's lake with a brilliant crimson glow.

He continued to chant, "Luo-Fan Luo-Fan, the maw of fire holding blood."

The tiny flame in the stone chalice stretched half an inch longer, and the crimson ash at its tail swirled even faster.

"Crimson veins cycle thrice, ash fangs bloom sevenfold."

Chanting to this point, Ning Zhuo felt as if his own spiritual energy and blood were gently gathered by an invisible hand. It was not forcefully seizing them, but rather guiding them to circulate along some ancient sacrificial orbit.

With one cycle of his bloodline, the fire's intent brightened by a fraction. After three cycles, the flame seemed to recognize his aura and actively leaned toward him.

The Red-Robed Guest's face remained placid, but deep within his gaze, a sense of gravity gradually settled.

When his own divine sense communicated with this fragmented prayer, for many syllables, he only knew the pronunciation but not the meaning.

Yet the lines Ning Zhuo chanted right now - despite their ancient, unadorned, and tongue-twisting syllables - landed perfectly on the Fire Seed's spiritual energy mechanism. It was like a key sliding into an old lock, turning and unlocking it notch by notch.

This kid... The Red-Robed Guest was secretly shocked. His comprehension of the Blood Path and the Fire Path is definitely not shallow!

Ning Zhuo continued to speak, "Blood walks the path of fire, embers fall upon the Dharma court."

As this line fell, a thin ray of light suddenly flew from the flame in the stone chalice and landed on Ning Zhuo's palm.

The flesh and skin of his palm were not incinerated. Instead, an extremely faint crimson-ash pattern surfaced. The pattern resembled the circular grooves of an altar, or the rim of the fire chalice, slowly contracting into a small mark.

Ning Zhuo's body shuddered slightly.

In an instant, he sensed the two powers of the Blood Ember Fire Seed.

First, Blood Ignition.

This fire did not consume wood or stone, nor did it crave spiritual charcoal. What truly allowed it to grow was the vitality, spiritual energy, and spirituality within blood. The more vigorous the blood, the more vigorously it burned; the stronger the nourishment, the easier it was for the fire to follow the traces and invade.

If used against Liu Jinke, his golden blood is his foundation. It will inevitably become the brightest fuse for this Fire Seed. This thought surfaced in Ning Zhuo's mind.

Second, Blood Ember Disruption.

After the fire burned, the fine ash would enter the blood and disrupt its circulation. Meridians, spiritual energy, Medicinal Pills, and bloodlines - all would be slightly impeded by the crimson ash.

It did not seek to incinerate the opponent in an instant; rather, it caused the enemy's internal circulation to lose order bit by bit. As this accumulated, quantitative changes would inevitably lead to a qualitative shift, achieving a severe internal disruption that would result in a total collapse.

Ning Zhuo's mind trembled slightly as he chanted the final two lines in a low voice:

"He-Xi unextinguished, the burning gate forever awake."

"An inch of blood opens the chalice, an inch of fire obeys the command."

Boom.

The flame in the stone chalice abruptly shot up three inches.

It was as if all the fire intent within the Cave Manor bowed its head simultaneously. Even the Red-Robed Guest's own core fire aura lightly sank in this instant.

The Blood Ember Fire Seed circled around Ning Zhuo's palm once before finally landing within that crimson-ash mark. It remained hidden and dormant, leaving only a trace of warmth lurking beneath his skin.

Ning Zhuo slowly opened his eyes.

His face was slightly pale, and a layer of fine sweat covered his forehead. His spiritual energy, vital blood, and divine sense had all been severely depleted.

"A pity," Ning Zhuo let out a soft breath. "The prayer is missing too much; I can only reach this step. This junior has barely achieved a medium-level refinement. I can temporarily borrow the fire, but it's far from true mastery."

The Red-Robed Guest quietly breathed a sigh of relief.

If Ning Zhuo had managed a deep refinement of the Blood Ember Fire Seed on his very first attempt at comprehension, then as a Nascent Soul Demonic Cultivator who had held this fire for years, while he might not completely lose face, he would definitely feel an unpleasant taste in his heart.

Outwardly, he remained indifferent. He merely flicked his sleeve to store the stone chalice away. His tone held a hint more profound meaning than before. "You're not satisfied with a medium-level refinement? This fire has followed me for many years. Many Golden Core cultivators have tried, yet they couldn't even articulate the first line of the prayer properly. You drew the fire into your palm in less than an incense stick's time, and you call that 'barely'?"

Ning Zhuo smiled bitterly. "This junior is not being modest. I have attempted the Demonic Path before, and it truly doesn't suit me very well. If we're strictly talking about innate talent for the Demonic Path, my potential is genuinely limited."

The Red-Robed Guest snorted coldly, finally revealing a hint of displeasure. "Saying that in front of me is being overly humble. If your talent is considered 'limited,' then those cultivators out there who call themselves prodigies of the Demonic Path should probably go into seclusion for thirty years to reevaluate their lives."

Ning Zhuo was momentarily speechless and could only cup his hands in respect.

The Red-Robed Guest looked at him. A flash of appreciation passed through the depths of his eyes, but he quickly returned to the customary arrogant composure of a Nascent Soul senior.

"I will lend you this fire," he said slowly. "But remember, the Blood Ember is no ordinary Fire Seed. It can counter Liu Jinke, but it can also harm you. Before using it, you must clear the path with the prayer, restrain it with spiritual energy, and use your vital blood as a guide. If the sacrificial mark on your palm burns hot three times, you must withdraw your hand immediately. Do not be greedy for success."

The Red-Robed Guest added, "You are the core of our Nanming Stronghold. You must achieve glory once more in the third battle! If Liu Jinke has truly cultivated some Golden Blood Pellet Bead, this fire will perfectly counter him. The more abundant his golden blood, the more paths the fire will have to spread. But do not think that you are guaranteed victory just because of a single Fire Seed. Since the people behind him dared to push him out again, they definitely have new methods."

Ning Zhuo nodded solemnly. "This junior understands."

Looking at Ning Zhuo, the Red-Robed Guest suddenly smiled. "As for the matter of the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud-Ascending Altar, I agree to it."

Ning Zhuo looked up.

The Red-Robed Guest leaned back, fully resting on the fire couch, his red robes spreading out. "You're right. If Chunyangzi were to monopolize that place, he would only become more of an eyesore to me in the future. It's better if I also contribute my strength to incorporate that treasured land into the Nanming Stronghold. When the time comes and he wants to use it, I can make sure he doesn't use it too comfortably."

He paused. His tone was flat but left no room for doubt. "I will have priority mining and refining rights for the Crimson Glow Residual Fire and Cloud Flame Cinnabar. Since there is a fire vein there, I won't make a trip for nothing."

Ning Zhuo said respectfully, "Understood. Senior's assistance today will be engraved in Ning Zhuo's memory."

The Red-Robed Guest waved his hand, his expression returning to its somewhat lazy state. "Go. Don't speak too many pretty words here. If you completely finish off Liu Jinke in the third battle, it won't be too late to come back and talk to me about gratitude."

Ning Zhuo gave a wry smile, cupped his hands in a bow, and respectfully took his leave.

Ning Zhuo returned to his own Cave Manor.

Entering the martial arena formation, Ning Zhuo stood alone and took out several discarded mechanical components and half-damaged talisman boards.

He reached out his hand and looked at his palm.

That crimson-ash sacrificial mark on his palm lay sunken beneath the flesh, almost imperceptible at first glance. Only when he slightly circulated his spiritual energy did the pattern surface with a dark red luster, like an ancient lamp half-buried in dirt and ash.

The Blood Ember Fire Seed had not truly entered his qi sea. Instead, it temporarily resided within the sacrificial mark, faintly pulling at his blood qi.

Ning Zhuo softly chanted the fragmented prayer. The ancient, tongue-twisting syllables rolled from his lips, and the sacrificial mark on his palm lit up with a faint crimson glow.

Immediately after, a wisp of small flame stood upright from his fingertip.

The Blood Ember Fire Seed was not dancing and spirited like ordinary flames. Its flame was thin and condensed, dark crimson like the light piercing through a blood jade.

Ning Zhuo guided it toward the broken wing bone of a mechanical bird. He watched as the flame gently licked along the old formation patterns on the inside of the wing bone. The impurities remaining in the patterns were immediately burned into fine ash, sinking deeper along the bamboo grain.

"It is indeed suitable for Artifact Refining as well." Ning Zhuo's eyes lit up slightly.

The Blood Ember's burning could turn impurities into fine embers. These fine embers contained Ning Zhuo's vital blood. Once branded into the texture of the artifact embryo, it was effectively another form of blood refining.

Ning Zhuo could distinctly feel that his connection with this broken mechanical bird wing bone had deepened significantly.

He then fetched a damaged talisman board. Using a drop of his own blood spiritual energy as a guide, he directed the Blood Ember flame to slowly glide over the board's fracture. Wherever the firelight passed, the fracture wasn't simply burned flat; rather, it was as if it had been re-combed by fine needles. The chaotic spiritual pathways gradually became distinct, and many originally blocked fine lines were flushed open by the Blood Ember, revealing places where they could be reconnected.

Ning Zhuo praised inwardly, When used for repairs, this fire allows for incredibly delicate work.

Ning Zhuo then had the Old Chef provide some "ingredients" - a live wild mountain boar.

The wild boar was not a demonic beast, but its spiritual energy and blood were much more vigorous than ordinary beasts. Its black bristles were like needles, and its tusks jutted outward. Its four hooves had been bound with thick hemp rope by the Old Chef. At this moment, as it was tossed into the martial arena formation, it still struggled madly, slamming against the ground with loud thuds.

Ning Zhuo flicked his finger.

A speck of dark crimson spark flew out, landing lightly on a wound on the boar's front leg where the rope had chafed the skin.

Sizzle.

The sound was exceedingly light, like a hot needle dropping into thin snow.

The wild boar continued to struggle, completely oblivious.

Ning Zhuo waited patiently and observed.

A moment later, a thin crimson line surfaced beneath the boar's skin.

The crimson line traveled along the bloodlines, spreading from the front leg all the way to its shoulders and back, branching out into several fine tributaries. It was like an extremely thin, exceptionally bright web of fire slowly unfurling beneath the skin.

The mountain boar's massive body violently arched. All four hooves pawed at the ground simultaneously as mournful, shrill howls erupted from its throat.

The fire web continued to expand, causing the boar's blood to boil and its muscles to spasm. Its originally furious struggling became erratic.

It wanted to charge, but its front and hind legs were turning to jelly.

It wanted to roar, but its breath caught halfway up its throat.

It wanted to roll over and break free, but the muscles in its back spasmed continuously, leaving it only able to shudder in place with completely uncoordinated movements.

Ning Zhuo poured his divine sense into the mountain boar's body.

He discovered that the boar's bloodstream, which had originally surged like a river, was now filled with countless tiny crimson ashes sinking into it.

These crimson ashes were extremely light, traveling with the blood. Like fine sand falling into gears, they caused the bloodlines to stutter, sometimes even surging chaotically out of order.

About a dozen breaths later, dark red fire patterns simultaneously lit up on the boar's underbelly, neck, and back.

Its cries were no longer high-pitched; instead, they became hoarse and intermittent. Hot mist sprayed from its nostrils, mixed with extremely fine crimson ash. When the ash landed on the ground, it actually burned pinprick-sized black dots into the bluestone.

Ning Zhuo's mind stirred. With a flick of his hand, the sacrificial mark on his palm glowed faintly. Restrained by the prayer, the Blood Ember Fire Seed instantly retracted its flames.

The wild boar collapsed flat on the ground, panting heavily, its entire body still twitching.

There were no large scorch marks on its body surface, and its fur was even kept intact, but the spiritual energy and blood within its body had devolved into total chaos.

Ning Zhuo was deep in thought.

"Ordinary fire methods inflict external damage, destroying skin and flesh, and incinerating muscles and bones."

"The Blood Ember is different."

"It follows the blood, ignites the blood, and ultimately disrupts the bloodline circulation."

If the enemy's spiritual energy and blood were not vigorous, its power might not be obvious. But if the enemy had tremendous spiritual energy and blood, with formidable regenerative abilities, and their blood itself was the foundation of their strength, then this Fire Seed would have the best possible fuel.

"Liu Jinke's Golden Liquid Restoring Core Physique is exactly like this."

Golden blood is his source; golden blood is his life.

If the Blood Ember Fire Seed could seize an opportunity to enter his body, continuously burning his blood and incessantly burying a layer of fine ash within the blood circulation he relied on most, then over time, it would inevitably make him increasingly sluggish and hindered. Ultimately, his combat strength would suffer a severe decline.

"However, this fire is ultimately difficult to control, and my time is limited."

The sacrificial mark on Ning Zhuo's palm began to burn hot for the first time.

He immediately withdrew the Fire Seed.

Wisps of the crimson line flowed backward out of the boar's body, regathering into that small flame on his fingertip. Having lost the Fire Seed's pull, the wild boar remained paralyzed on the ground. Its spiritual energy and blood surged chaotically on their own, unable to calm down for a long time.

Ning Zhuo tried taking out an ordinary healing Medicinal Pill and feeding it into the wild boar's mouth.

Ning Zhuo observed for a moment, his eyes shining brighter. "It can even interfere with healing from Medicinal Pills. This shows that it's difficult for medicinal pills to take effect against the Blood Ember."

Finally, Ning Zhuo looked at the wild boar once more.

The wild boar wasn't dead, but it seemed to be suffering from a severe illness, lying on the ground bereft of its former ferocity. The spiritual energy and blood within its body still required time to sort themselves out naturally. Even with the Medicinal Pill's efficacy, it would be difficult for it to recover immediately.

"The Blood Ember Fire Seed doesn't seek a one-hit kill, but rather causes internal strife within the enemy. It requires time to increasingly display its miraculous effects."

Ning Zhuo sat quietly for a long while. Only when the sacrificial mark on his palm thoroughly quieted down did he slowly open his eyes.

"This is a trump card."

"But it cannot be revealed early, nor can it be used excessively."

"The timing of its use in actual combat is extremely crucial."
