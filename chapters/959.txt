Chapter 959: Generating, Nurturing, Transforming, and Herding

"Could it be that he was knocked to his senses by Yun Wuji yesterday, so he's coming to learn cloud methods at the last minute?"

"Heh, what use is last-minute learning?"

"He's different! He's a genius. Is what he learns at the last minute something you and I can even compare to?"

After a brief silence, the crowd became noisy again, their topics all revolving around Ning Zhuo.

By now, Ning Zhuo had become a celebrity at the main gate of the Myriad Manifestations Sect; he would be recognized wherever he went.

Ning Zhuo's expression was calm as he scanned his surroundings.

Here stood a white jade cloud terrace. The terrace was broad, with cloud-patterned stone pillars standing at its four corners. Translucent cloud curtains draped down from the pillars. Behind the cloud curtains, several disciples of the Cloud Nursery Courtyard were busy organizing cloud materials, arranging formation plates, and inspecting the cloud storehouses.

A middle-aged female cultivator appeared to host this minor trial.

A white jade cloud shuttle was pinned at her temples. Her clothes were plain and clean, and a thread of wet cloud was wrapped around the edge of her sleeve. That wet cloud did not dissipate or scatter; it swam gently with the movement of her wrist, like a tamed white silk ribbon.

Her brows and eyes were cold and clear, and her lip color was very pale. Just standing there, she exuded a detached aura, like one who had resided long in cloud fields and rarely bothered with mortal dust.

This was precisely the deacon of the Cloud Nursery Courtyard, Yun Sanggu.

Yun Sanggu scanned the crowd. Her gaze paused on Ning Zhuo for a breath, a hint of surprise in the depths of her eyes, but she did not ask any questions, merely raising her sleeve and giving it a sweep.

The cloud curtains around the white jade cloud terrace slowly rose, revealing twenty small formation nodes.

Yun Sanggu said, "The Rising Cloud Minor Trial will follow the old rules today. This trial does not ask about one's background, does not compare combat prowess, and does not measure reputation. We only look at three things: whether you can generate clouds, whether you can nurture clouds, and whether you can herd clouds."

Her voice was not loud, but it spread clearly across the entire cloud terrace.

"Clouds are not dead objects. Clouds feel hunger and fullness, cold and warmth, fear, and a sense of belonging. If you all only wish to rely on spiritual energy to forcefully compel them, you need not enter the arena today. Those who injure the clouds will have points deducted. Those who ruin the clouds will be disqualified. If anyone intentionally damages cloud materials, they will be dealt with according to Sect rules."

The Cultivators in the arena all felt their hearts slightly chill.

The minor trial quickly began.

The first test: Gathering Water into Cloud.

Twenty light cyan jade plates rose from the center of the cloud terrace. In each jade plate were placed Clear Dew Cloud Sand and Morning Mist Water Droplets. The examinees needed to gather a stable mass of cloud qi from the surrounding water vapor and maintain it for a hundred breaths without it dissipating.

This test seemed simple, but it was the greatest test of one's foundation.

One Cultivator, eager to show off, clapped his palms together. Spiritual energy surged out, and white vapor immediately churned. However, that mass of white vapor floated without condensing, as if it had been forcefully kneaded together. Just past thirty breaths, its edges dispersed, the cloud sand fell back into the jade plate, and the morning mist water droplets shattered into dampness.

Light chuckles rang out from the side.

That Cultivator's face flushed red, and he hurriedly cast his technique again, resulting in the cloud qi becoming even more chaotic.

Ning Zhuo chose to observe first.

He stood before the jade plate, looking down at the Clear Dew Cloud Sand within. The sand grains were crystal clear, each containing water. The morning mist water droplets hung over the sand's surface, vibrating slightly as if disturbed by a mountain breeze.

Ning Zhuo extended his right hand, palm facing down, without touching the jade plate. He merely allowed a wisp of Water Element spiritual energy to slowly drift down.

Within his Kidney Temple, the profound Divine Intent gently stirred in response.

The water vapor in the jade plate was not pulled; rather, as if hearing an intimate call, a thread of thin mist first rose from between the grains of Clear Dew Cloud Sand. The thin mist swirled, supporting the morning mist water droplets. The droplets did not break; instead, they gradually melted away, blending into the mist.

Ning Zhuo's palm slowly lifted.

A fist-sized white cloud floated up from the jade plate.

It was not large, nor was it dazzling, but it was very stable. Within a hundred breaths, the edges of the cloud did not collapse, the core did not become turbid, and the bottom did not sink. It hung quietly beneath Ning Zhuo's palm, like a mass of soft white cotton supported by a watery intent.

The second test: Cloud Womb Warming and Nurturing.

The cloud materials were swapped for Cloud Womb Cotton and Warm Glow Powder. The examinees needed to nurture a newborn cloud womb until it wouldn't disperse, become turbid, or collapse.

Upon seeing the Warm Glow Powder, many subconsciously sprinkled too much. Nourished by the powder, the cloud wombs immediately swelled, their edges flushing red, looking exceptionally beautiful. However, turbid spots soon appeared in the cores of the clouds, like drops of foul ink falling into clear water.

Ning Zhuo first divided the Warm Glow Powder into seven small portions. He only added a tiny bit each time, waiting for the cloud womb to exhale and inhale for three breaths before adding the second bit. His movements weren't fast, but they were extremely precise.

The cloud womb was initially fragile; if fed too hastily, it would falsely bloat, and if supplemented too fiercely, it would become turbid. With the patience of an alchemist, Ning Zhuo fed the Warm Glow Powder into the cloud womb little by little. That mass of infant cloud slowly unfurled, its edges soft and moist, its core clear and bright, its body perfectly stable.

Yun Sanggu saw the method behind it and thought to herself, "He probably hasn't learned the Cloud Nursery Arts. But he understands heat control, and he understands materials."

The third test: Awakening the Cloud and Opening the Apertures.

The cloud materials were the Sleeping Cloud Core and the Awakening Mist Incense. The Sleeping Cloud Core slumbered extremely deeply. If the incense fire was too intense, it would startle the cloud; if the incense fire was too faint, it wouldn't be able to awaken the cloud's breath.

Ning Zhuo picked up the Awakening Mist Incense. He did not light it immediately; instead, he used his divine sense to observe the Sleeping Cloud Core.

The Luo Shu gently rotated in the depths of his sea of consciousness.

Countless minute changes arrayed themselves in Ning Zhuo's mind. The density of the incense aroma, the exhalations of the cloud core, the flow rate of the surrounding water vapor, the faint glow of the jade plate's array runes - all were rapidly incorporated into his deductions.

A moment later, Ning Zhuo lit the Awakening Mist Incense.

The incense smoke did not rush straight at the cloud core. Instead, using his fingertips, he guided it into an extremely fine arc that circled halfway around the cloud core before gently pressing against it from the side. The Sleeping Cloud Core trembled slightly, like someone turning over in their sleep. After ten more breaths, a barely visible wisp of white vapor was exhaled from the core.

As soon as that white vapor appeared, an old deacon standing beside Yun Sanggu let out a soft exclamation of surprise.

Yun Sanggu said, "Record this attempt of his as 'Excellent'."

The fourth test: Feeding and Growing the Cloud.

The cloud materials were Dew Rice, Glow Powder, and Light Breeze Salt. The goal was to increase the size of the cloud mass by more than thirty percent without it becoming turbid or bloated.

This test was the most bustling.

Some examinees threw all the cloud food in at once, causing the cloud mass to instantly inflate to the size of a washbasin, drawing gasps from the onlookers. However, no sooner had the cloud mass swelled than it stratified inside and out, its edges tearing open, like a mass of falsely bloated white vapor.

Yun Sanggu said expressionlessly, "Deduct half a point."

That Cultivator's face instantly fell.

The cloud mass that Ning Zhuo was feeding grew very slowly.

He first added the Dew Rice to nourish its roots, then used the Glow Powder to add color, and finally used the Light Breeze Salt to stretch out the cloud's shape. The habit of precise measurement brought about by his study of Mechanism showed its brilliance at this moment. Every pinch of cloud food that fell was accurate to the slightest fraction.

His cloud mass grew from the size of a fist to the size of a large bowl, its growth just surpassing thirty percent. The cloud's core was clean, its edges weightless, and its glow color was not overly vibrant, yet it possessed a layer of gentle, warm light.

Yun Sanggu thought to herself, "Is he using a Mechanist's techniques? The amount of each portion is gauged far too accurately."

The fifth test: Dividing the Cloud and Fixing the Quantity.

Soft Blade Cloud Silk and Form-Fixing Cloud Needles were presented. The examinees had to divide a single cloud mass into three portions of similar size without disrupting the cloud's nature.

The sixth test: Merging Clouds into One.

Cloud-Merging Glue and Harmonious Qi Mist Dew were brought up.

This test was the exact opposite of the previous one. It required merging three cloud masses of different sizes back into a single entity without allowing any stratified layers or turbid spots to appear.

After six consecutive tests, Ning Zhuo undoubtedly seized the first-place position for the time being.

"He scored 'Excellent' in all the first six tests?"

"Isn't Ning Zhuo a Mechanist? How is he nurturing cloud materials so well?"

Ning Zhuo felt moved in his heart: "Cloud materials are not pill materials. After pill materials enter the furnace, they are ultimately mostly refined into pills. Cloud materials, however, must retain their vitality and be allowed to breathe naturally. Refining pills focuses on 'refining,' while nurturing clouds focuses on 'nurturing.' Yet, the two are not entirely without common ground. Heat control, compatibility, impurities, recovery, and sealing - all of these can reflect and correspond to one another."

"It is precisely because clouds are alive that Yun Wuji was able to achieve ultimate victory."

Ning Zhuo gained an even deeper layer of understanding regarding his previous battle.

The seventh test: Dyeing the Cloud's Color and Shape.

Glow Dyeing Powder, Ink Mist Liquid, and Frost White Cloud Fluff were laid out on the table.

Examinees needed to dye the cloud qi into a designated color while ensuring its aura remained unchanged.

The eighth test: Transforming Cold and Warmth Natures.

Cold Cloud Shavings, Warm Glow Sand, and Climate-Regulating Water Droplets were brought to the stage.

Ning Zhuo reached out and touched the Cold Cloud Shavings, his fingertips instantly feeling a slight chill. The Warm Glow Sand was the opposite; before it was even added to the cloud, it emanated a feeling of warmth.

The harmony of water and fire, the regulation of cold and warmth - this was not a simple matter of direct confrontation. If a cold cloud was forcefully turned warm, the cloud womb would tear; if a warm cloud was forcefully turned cold, the cloud core would freeze rigid. Ning Zhuo used the Climate-Regulating Water Droplets as an intermediary, first softening the edges of the cold cloud, then feeding in the Warm Glow Sand, and finally using Water Element spiritual energy to stabilize the cloud's roots.

The cold cloud turned warm, then returned to a neutral state.

As the cloud mass shifted back and forth, its coloration changed subtly, yet the cloud womb did not suffer the slightest tear.

His realm in the Five Elements was displayed to the fullest at this moment!

Even Yun Sanggu felt a shock in her heart. "This person truly deserves to be the genius of this generation, capable of shaking the main gate of the Sect with his renown!"

The ninth test: Balancing Moisture and Dryness.

Wet Cloud Fluff, Dry Wind Powder, and Vein-Moistening Dew were placed in front of Ning Zhuo.

This test required the cloud materials to be adapted for three purposes: load-bearing, obscuring, and rain-making.

Ning Zhuo first adjusted the cloud to be moist, making it steady and heavy so it could bear the weight of wooden puppets. Following that, he added a tiny bit of Dry Wind Powder to thin it out, scattering it into an obscuring cloud curtain. Finally, he used the Vein-Moistening Dew to draw the water vapor back, causing the cloud curtain to condense into fine rain.

The transition between the three uses was unhurried and orderly.

Though clearly a novice, he possessed the demeanor of a Grandmaster.

The tenth test: Changing Forms Between Cloud and Rain.

The Rain Mother Cloud Mass and the Mist-Condensing Cold Sand were brought up.

Ning Zhuo looked at the Rain Mother Cloud Mass, a hint of seriousness finally showing in his eyes.

The continuous transformation between the three states of cloud, rain, and mist was not fundamentally a magic trick, but rather an understanding of the changes in cloud methods. Clouds gather and are light; rain condenses and is heavy; mist disperses and is ethereal. The three were different, yet all originated from water vapor.

Ning Zhuo gently raised his hands.

The Rain Mother Cloud Mass first transformed into a patch of thin mist. The mist permeated the air in front of him, obscuring half of his white robes. Subsequently, the Mist-Condensing Cold Sand entered the mist, causing it to condense into a string of tiny raindrops that hung in mid-air without falling. Finally, Ning Zhuo swirled his fingertips, and the raindrops scattered back into water vapor, once again supporting a mass of white cloud.

Visibly moved, Yun Sanggu issued a command with her divine sense: "Record this - Excellent among Excellents!"

As the tenth test concluded, a flying letter flew over, heading straight for Ning Zhuo.

Ning Zhuo caught the letter and glanced it over.

The sender was none other than Chunyangzi, writing to request Ning Zhuo's attendance at a meeting so they could discuss together who to dispatch for the second match of the five-win martial contest.

Ning Zhuo smiled faintly in his heart and immediately wrote a reply on the spot: "Senior Chunyangzi, as the Grand Chieftain, it is naturally up to you to oversee the overall situation. All decisions regarding this five-win martial contest wager should be determined by you, Senior. This junior's cultivation is still shallow, and all I can do is make up for my own shortcomings. I have the heart but lack the strength to assist Senior in this matter. I ask Senior to decide all matters of deploying troops and formulating the lineup on your own."

The Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascending Altar was merely one of Ning Zhuo's options, whereas Chunyangzi was the person most desperate for this treasured feng shui land.

Right now, the latter was under immense political pressure because the vanguard he had pushed forward, the Nine Fire Dragon Lord, had been defeated. Thus, writing this letter was, to some extent, an attempt to use Ning Zhuo to share this pressure.

Ning Zhuo saw right through this and didn't take the bait. Instead, he proactively stepped aside, leaving the stage open for Chunyangzi to perform.

"Since you want this treasured feng shui land so badly, you ought to be the one putting in the effort."

Ning Zhuo continued with the minor trial.

The eleventh test: Forbidding the Restlessness of Thunder Clouds.

An Infant Thunder Cloud was brought onto the cloud terrace.

It was a fist-sized mass of grayish-white cloud material, with tiny sparks of lightning flashing intermittently in its core. As soon as it neared the formation node, it crackled and popped, the minor explosions causing the cloud barriers to tremble slightly.

The faces of quite a few examinees grew tight.

Someone stepped forward and stuck a Thunder-Pacifying Talisman Paper onto the cloud. The moment the Talisman Paper touched the edge of the cloud, lightning flashed, charring the Talisman Paper black. The cloud mass expanded violently, startling the person into retreating several steps.

Ning Zhuo's heart stirred. He directly took out his Spirit Communication Mirror and waved it.

The Infant Thunder Cloud crackled and popped; there was no effect.

"The Spirit Communication Mirror can commune with objects possessing spiritual energy, but what the thunder cloud possesses is a lightning nature, not spiritual energy." After this probing attempt, Ning Zhuo gave up on that approach completely.

He first observed the rhythm of the Infant Thunder Cloud's lightning, then flicked a wisp of water vapor from his fingertips.

The water vapor formed three tiny eddies around the Infant Thunder Cloud. With a flowing path for its lightning nature, the cloud's restlessness slightly eased. Only then did Ning Zhuo gently sprinkle in Static-Calming Jade Powder, before finally sticking the Thunder-Pacifying Talisman Paper onto the most stable part of the cloud's core.

He passed the test with ease.

The twelfth test: Clarifying Turbid Clouds.

The thirteenth test: Discerning the Nature of Cloud Materials.

The fourteenth test: Compounding Cloud Food.

Dew Rice, Glow Powder, Wind Salt, Moon Frost Powder, and Thunder Dew Drops covered the table. Different cloud materials required different cloud foods. A wrong combination would, at best, cause discomfort to the cloud materials, and at worst, damage the cloud womb.

Cold clouds avoided heavy glows; thunder clouds rejected chaotic dew; dry clouds required vein-moistening; and wet clouds needed light breeze. Every type of cloud food in his hands was like an auxiliary herb in an alchemy recipe - sovereign, minister, assistant, and courier, each taking its proper place.

In one batch of food for a wet cloud, he even put in half a pinch less of Dew Rice and added a thread more of Wind Salt.

The result was miraculously excellent!

Yun Sanggu couldn't help but ask, "Why less Dew Rice for the wet cloud?"

Ning Zhuo replied, "This wet cloud is moist on the outside but heavy on the inside. If we heavily apply Dew Rice, it will cause the cloud core to sink. Adding Light Breeze Salt allows it to remain moist without stagnating."

For the first time, Yun Sanggu revealed a smile.

The fifteenth test, the sixteenth test...

The seventeenth test: Herding with Cloud Reins.

A Cloud-Herding Rope, Cloud-Guiding Incense, and Sleeve-Returning Cloud Silk were placed in front of Ning Zhuo.

Ten small clouds had different natures. Some were light, some heavy; some loved to fly high, while others drifted close to the ground.

The ten small clouds were scattered and disorderly at first, but under Ning Zhuo's guidance, they quickly arranged themselves into a formation.

They circled the cloud terrace once, suddenly gathering and suddenly dispersing, suddenly high and suddenly low. Finally, one by one, they returned to the edge of Ning Zhuo's sleeves, then were spat back out, suspending themselves in the air once again.

Mechanism had performed a great merit!

The eighteenth test: Returning the Cloud to the Sleeve.

The Sleeve-Returning Cloud Silk and Cloud-Guiding Incense were brought back to the stage. The requirement was to gather the cloud qi scattered in the surroundings back into the sleeves.

This made Ning Zhuo associate it with Yun Wuji's performance during battle.

With the blessings of his Water Element Kidney Temple, the Luo Shu, and other methods, Ning Zhuo didn't miss a single wisp, slowly gathering all the clouds into his sleeves.

His performance could be called perfect.

The nineteenth test: Sealing in the Cloud Storehouse.

A Cloud-Sealing Box, a Slumbering Cloud Talisman, and a Breath-Fixing Cloud Rope were presented.

When sealing a living cloud, the greatest fear was sealing it to death. If the cloud materials lost the ability to respire within the cloud storehouse, they would become dead clouds upon retrieval. But if sealed too loosely, they would escape.

Ning Zhuo accurately grasped the perfect balance of this process.

The twentieth test: Raising Clouds into a Garden.

This was the final test, as well as a comprehensive major test, possessing the highest level of difficulty.

Fewer than one-third of the examinees still remained in the arena.

Most people had either failed and left the stage, or given up voluntarily due to their scores being too low. Those still able to stand in the center of the cloud terrace were all people with some genuine skill.

Yet, everyone's gaze involuntarily fell upon Ning Zhuo.

Ning Zhuo, however, lightly closed his eyes, rapidly reviewing the contents of the previous tests in his mind.

Generating clouds was about lifting the cloud's roots out from the water.

Nurturing clouds was about accommodating the hunger, fullness, cold, and warmth of the cloud materials.

Transforming clouds was about understanding the shifts between cloud, rain, mist, cold, warmth, moisture, and dryness.

Herding clouds was about giving the clouds a place to go and a place to return.

"I have grasped it all," he sighed softly in his heart.
