Chapter 971: The Surging Undercurrents

Rumors and gossip surrounding Nanming Stronghold, the Heaven-Draping Single Line Hook, Ning Zhuo, and the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect had already caused a massive uproar!

"Nanming Stronghold is in danger. If they can bring out a Dao Artifact, who knows what other trump cards they have for the fifth match?"

"Ning Zhuo is taking things too seriously. The Red-Robed Guest is a Demonic Cultivator; his soul fell into enemy hands, and even if it's ransomed back, what good would it do? He shouldn't have gotten involved in the first place; this is a contest between Nascent Soul cultivators. Now look at him, he's been put on a pedestal and can't back down, so he can only fight the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect!"

"You can't say it like that. The Red-Robed Guest lent him a Fire Seed, and Young Master Ning Zhuo publicly acknowledged this debt, even announcing in front of everyone that he would seal away the corpse and relics. Such a sense of responsibility is truly rare."

"Yeah, even in a prestigious major sect like the Myriad Manifestations Sect, how many people could go to such lengths?"

"What's the use of taking responsibility? The Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect have killed Nascent Soul cultivators, while Ning Zhuo is only at the mid Foundation Establishment stage. If they really clash, he probably won't even be able to preserve his own corpse."

"Doesn't Nanming Stronghold still have Tu Yuanzi? He's always been mysterious, and rumor has it that he is Ning Zhuo's family servant. It's time for him to step up!"

"That's right, Tu Yuanzi hasn't made a move yet. The Red-Robed Guest is dead, the Nine Fires Dragon Lord was defeated, and Chun Yangzi has already fought. Although Tan Zhu is very strong, he's just one breath away from death. In the end, it might all come down to Tu Yuanzi."

The rumors rolled on, spreading wider and wider, almost stealing the show and overshadowing the various Cloud-Rousing Minor Trials.

A large group of cultivators who were striving to get ahead and participate in the Cloud-Rousing Minor Trials felt rather peculiar. Their biggest takeaway was - this year's Flying Cloud Assembly is truly different from previous ones!

The rumors reached the Alchemy Hall.

Shen Hongyao unconsciously clenched her fists and thought to herself, "Ning Zhuo!"

She and Ning Zhuo weren't actually close, but time and again she had seen in him the kind of person she wanted to become.

Therefore, learning that Ning Zhuo had fallen into such a passive situation, a deep sense of concern welled up in her heart.

Ban Jie was holding a more detailed intelligence report, currently reading it over.

Her expression was rather complex.

"Royal Mandate's Human Tribulation seems to be losing control again, setting himself up to get killed."

"Eh?"

"This feeling is somewhat familiar."

She was on Ban Ji's side and had an irreconcilable conflict with Ning Zhuo. But right now, her feelings were difficult to describe.

There was a fear of Ning Zhuo, but also an indescribable trace of admiration.

"He is only at the mid Foundation Establishment stage, yet he actually announced publicly that he wants to rescue the Red-Robed Guest's demonic soul from the hands of the numerous major powers of Flowing Cloud Peak."

"The key is that he and the Red-Robed Guest haven't even known each other for long!"

Three Lights Dao Heaven Cave Manor.

At the peak, cloud qi slowly flowed around, and the Three Heavenly Lights Refining Formation operated quietly. Sunlight, starlight, and cloud brilliance alternately flowed within the formation lines, reflecting off the stone walls of the cave manor and making them look as lustrous as jade.

Deep within the cave manor, the aroma of wine drifted in the air.

Ning Zhuo summoned Tu Yuanzi over. On the table were only two wine cups, a few plates of spirit fruits, and a newly opened jar of Ice Jade Wine.

Tu Yuanzi took the appearance of a youth in yellow clothing. He sat upright with his hands resting on his knees.

When he entered the cave manor, his eyes still carried a bit of solemnity. Naturally, it was because he had heard too many rumors recently, making his heart feel as heavy as a mountain boulder.

After taking a seat, however, he found that the big-headed youth looked calm and his movements were unhurried.

Ning Zhuo was pouring the wine.

The ice-blue wine flowed from the slender spout of the jade pot into the cups, making a soft splashing sound. The radiance of the Three Heavenly Lights Refining Formation fell upon his cuffs, light and shadow slowly shifting.

Tu Yuanzi stared at him for quite a while before he couldn't help but say, "Dao Friend Ning Zhuo, you don't seem very anxious."

Ning Zhuo pushed a cup of wine in front of him and smiled. "Will being anxious make the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect retreat?"

Tu Yuanzi thought about it seriously, then shook his head. "No."

"Then drink." Ning Zhuo raised his wine cup. "You've had this wine before, but you still need to drink it slowly. Last time you drank too fast and ended up sleeping for a whole night."

A look of embarrassment appeared on Tu Yuanzi's face. He reached out and cupped the wine glass, sniffed it first, and then took a careful sip.

As the Ice Jade Wine slid down his throat, his brows relaxed slightly.

"It's still delicious," Tu Yuanzi said softly.

Ning Zhuo also took a sip. The crisp and cold sensation of the wine washed down his throat, as if it could suppress the churning rumors and noises outside.

Tu Yuanzi set his cup down, still unable to hold back his true thoughts. "Before I came, I thought you were under a lot of pressure. I didn't expect you to be so relaxed."

Ning Zhuo replied, "Of course there's pressure."

"But why?" Tu Yuanzi wore a look of confusion.

"Because before I publicly announced this matter, I had already anticipated the current situation." Ning Zhuo bent a finger and lightly tapped the rim of his wine cup. With a ding, a clear and melodious sound rang out.

He continued, "The various powers of Flowing Cloud Peak won't let this opportunity slip by. The Red-Robed Guest's soul is in their hands, and they will inevitably perform a soul-search. The moment I spoke up, it became highly likely they would use this matter to pressure me."

The confusion in Tu Yuanzi's eyes deepened. "Then why did you say it?"

Ning Zhuo looked at him.

Tu Yuanzi's gaze was crystal clear, and his confusion was straightforward.

If it were someone else, those words might have harbored probing, blame, or calculation. But Tu Yuanzi simply couldn't understand, so he asked.

Ning Zhuo felt relaxed, his smile becoming a bit more genuine. "If you were me, what do you think should be done?"

Tu Yuanzi thought for a moment, his brows slowly furrowing. "The Red-Robed Guest is already dead. Even though his corpse is preserved, his Nascent Soul is gone, leaving only his demonic soul. Even if you rescue him, his cultivation is completely lost."

He tried hard to imitate a Human cultivator's analysis of pros and cons, speaking slowly. "If you had kept his corpse and relics to make up for Nanming Stronghold's losses, I could understand. If you return them, I can also understand. You have your stance and principles, unmoved by the temptation of profit; such vision is similar to my master's."

Speaking up to here, Tu Yuanzi paused, imitating Ning Zhuo by rubbing his finger against the outside of his wine cup.

"But you still want to rescue his soul."

"That requires throwing in even more things."

"If I were you, unless the Red-Robed Guest was my family, I would absolutely never do this!"

Tu Yuanzi looked up at Ning Zhuo, speaking with utter seriousness. "Yet you barely know the Red-Robed Guest. Even though he lent you the Blood Ember Fire Seed, it was you who helped him first."

The cave manor fell quiet.

The light of the formation lines flowed over the stone walls like water, illuminating their shadows and making them sway slightly.

Ning Zhuo looked down at the wine in his cup.

The clear, ice-blue liquid reflected his bright eyes.

"Tu Yuanzi, this is my Righteous Path," Ning Zhuo said slowly. "Nanming Stronghold was established because of me. I must have this kind of responsibility and act in the best interests of Nanming Stronghold."

Tu Yuanzi became even more bewildered.

He blinked his eyes. "Is Nanming Stronghold really that important?"

Ning Zhuo put down his cup, his gaze turning distant, as if piercing through the stone walls to look outside the cave manor.

Outside the cave manor, the clouds and mist were vast, and distant peaks rose and fell. The atmosphere of the Myriad Manifestations Sect's main mountain gate was incredibly expansive and profound. Yet on Flowing Cloud Peak, countless mountain summits, factions, and cultivators were fighting both openly and in secret.

Nanming Stronghold was still very weak right now.

So weak that it was like a newly lit lamp.

A strong gust of wind could blow it out.

But he had lit this lamp with his own hands.

"It is important," Ning Zhuo replied. "Nanming Stronghold is a part of my Dao Path; naturally, it's important!"

Hearing the words "Dao Path," Tu Yuanzi's expression instantly became much more solemn.

He still couldn't fully comprehend the intersecting interests within and outside of Nanming Stronghold, nor could he understand the various entanglements between the reputation, promises, leverage, and the great cause of the Righteous Path among Human cultivators.

But he understood Dao Paths.

Cang Yazi had taught him that a Dao Path was the journey of a cultivator's entire life; their life, desires, obsessions, and future were all encompassed within it.

Tu Yuanzi nodded slowly. "I see. If it concerns your Dao Path, then it is very important."

Ning Zhuo smiled and poured him more wine. "Thank you for understanding me!"

Tu Yuanzi raised his cup and took a sip. As the warmth of the wine spread through his chest and abdomen, he straightened his back. "Then you inviting me to drink this time, is it because you want me to participate in the Five-Victory Martial Contest?"

His face grew serious.

"I heard people outside saying that the Nine Fires Dragon Lord was defeated, the Red-Robed Guest is dead, Chun Yangzi has fought, and Tan Zhu is hanging on by a thread. Now everyone is talking about me."

Ning Zhuo shook his head. "I invited you for drinks this time mainly to advise you absolutely not to participate in this Five-Victory Martial Contest."

Tu Yuanzi was stunned.

His hand holding the wine cup froze in mid-air.

The wine rippled slightly, reflecting the astonishment in his eyes.

"Why?" he blurted out.

Ning Zhuo said, "You are my friend. I know the truth that you only have a Golden Core cultivation base; how could I push you into a pit of fire?"

Tu Yuanzi paused for a moment. "But I am a demon. If I reveal my true form, my combat strength can rival that of Nascent Soul cultivators."

Ning Zhuo gazed into Tu Yuanzi's eyes. "The Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect are my tribulation, and naturally I must transcend it myself. This matter has nothing to do with you. Do not, under any circumstances, let others egg you on to take this fifth match."

Tu Yuanzi felt Ning Zhuo's genuine sincerity. His throat moved slightly as words choked in his chest; for a moment, he couldn't express them clearly or speak them aloud.

Ning Zhuo, however, broke into a smile. He raised his wine cup and lightly tapped the rim against Tu Yuanzi's. "Drink."

Ding.

The crisp sound of clinking cups echoed through the cave manor.

Tu Yuanzi looked down at the wine in his cup, his eyes seemingly glistening a bit. He hurriedly lifted his cup, tilted his head, and drained it. His movements were much more hurried than before. A little bit of wine slid down the corner of his lips, which he hastily wiped away with his sleeve.

"Dao Friend Ning Zhuo." Tu Yuanzi put down his cup, his voice a bit lower than usual. "You are a good person."

Ning Zhuo chuckled. "Then thank you for the compliment, Dao Friend Tu Yuanzi."

Tu Yuanzi added, "But are you really planning to fight against the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect?"

Ning Zhuo replied, "I've already asked someone to gather intelligence. As the saying goes, 'Know yourself and know your enemy, and you will never be defeated in a hundred battles.' I cannot make this decision yet."

His expression remained calm and composed. "There's no rush."

"Dao Friend Ning Zhuo, you must think twice." Tu Yuanzi's voice grew tight. "They invited the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect, but don't forget, there is also that Dao Artifact, the Heaven-Draping Single Line Hook. It's truly terrifying!"

Tu Yuanzi had also watched the battle personally and witnessed the Red-Robed Guest's death. The Heaven-Draping Single Line Hook had left a deeply terrifying impression on him.

The tavern.

Chen San sat by the window on the second floor, with a pot of spirit tea and two plates of cloud cakes placed in front of him.

He didn't pick up his chopsticks, only using the corner of his eye to watch the lobby downstairs.

A grey-clothed patron near the counter slapped the table and said, "Everyone, think about it. Who was the Red-Robed Guest? A Nascent Soul Demonic Cultivator, covered in blood debts. But he fought for Nanming Stronghold and died in the Five-Victory Martial Contest. Young Master Ning Zhuo is willing to seal away even a person like his corpse and relics; what kind of magnanimity is that!"

A short and fat cultivator nearby chimed in, "That's exactly it! Young Master Ning Zhuo is only at the mid Foundation Establishment stage, yet he dared to acknowledge this debt under the watchful eyes of the public. Many Golden Core and Nascent Soul cultivators don't have this kind of responsibility."

Some in the tavern nodded, while others scoffed. "Can responsibility block the Heaven-Draping Single Line Hook?"

The grey-clothed patron immediately slapped the table again. "Whether it can be blocked or not is a matter of magical combat. Whether one dares to acknowledge it is a matter of the heart. Nanming Stronghold has just been established; it relies on Young Master Ning Zhuo, and it relies on this backbone!"

Chen San lowered his head to drink tea. Some of the people downstairs were paid off by him, and the relevant talking points were also personally polished by him.

Chen San rubbed the edge of his teacup with his thumb, listening to the growing discussions downstairs, thinking to himself, "The rumor mill is progressing quite well. However, regarding the investigation the Young Master entrusted to me, I have absolutely no clues."

Chen San felt deeply troubled. Ning Zhuo had previously asked him to investigate Tan Zhu and others; this level of task was truly too high for him.

But Chen San did not make any excuses. He would only do his utmost, giving his all to complete the mission Ning Zhuo assigned him!

The tea's aroma curled upwards, gradually taking on a cloying sweetness.

A feeling of weakness spread from within Chen San's body.

Something's wrong! His throat tightened and the base of his tongue went numb. He tried to mobilize his spiritual energy, but it scattered uncontrollably, completely chaotic.

Shocked, a cold sweat broke out on Chen San's back. He tried to stand up, but his knee bumped into the table leg, producing a dull thump.

The waiter pushed the door open. "Guest, is there something you need?"

Chen San opened his mouth. "I..."

His tongue was stiff, and what came out was merely a muffled sound. The stairs, tables, chairs, and patrons in front of his eyes were all gently swaying. The scent of the tea grew stronger, and even the cloud light outside the window was tainted with a thin layer of green.

The waiter's expression changed. He reached out to press on the table and suddenly shouted, "Guest, you haven't paid for the tea, the pastries, or the private seat yet!"

Chen San's eyes widened.

The waiter's voice grew louder. "Hah, trying to dine and dash at our Cloud Guest Tavern?"

That shout immediately drew many gazes.

Chen San's temples throbbed with anxiety.

"Still pretending to be drunk?" The waiter sneered. "I've seen this plenty of times! Someone, take him down!"

Two burly guards stepped forward, hoisting Chen San up from the left and right.

Chen San struggled with all his might, but the guards were extremely experienced. With a pinch under his ribs, Chen San was completely subdued.

Chen San was dragged down the stairs.

The soles of his boots scraped against the wooden steps, making dragging sounds. The cloud light outside the window was bright, while the scents of tea, wine, and meat inside the tavern mixed together, making his divine sea muddy.

He forced his eyes open, trying to memorize every feature of the waiter's and the two guards' faces. But soon after, he slipped into unconsciousness, the mocking voices of the bystanders echoing in his ears growing fainter and fainter.

When Chen San woke up, his nose was filled with the smell of wood ash.

He found himself lying on the cold ground, his cheek pressed against rough wooden floorboards, a wad of rag stuffed in his mouth. His wrists, ankles, and waist were tightly bound by ropes plastered with spell-restricting talismans. The talisman paper emitted a dim, earthy-yellow light, suppressing the tiny bit of spiritual energy he had just gathered back into the depths of his dantian.

This was a woodshed.

Chopped spirit wood was piled in the corner, sawdust scattered across the floor. There was light shining through the crack under the door, but it was very dim, like the fading light of dusk.

Chen San took a few breaths, letting out low muffled sounds from his throat.

No one responded.

He tried to twist his wrists, and the ropes immediately tightened. The light of the talisman pierced his flesh, hurting him so much that the veins on his forehead popped and layers of cold sweat broke out on his back.

I'm finished! he couldn't help but feel a sense of despair.

No, I can't give up just like this!

Biting down on the rag, a ruthless look surfaced in his eyes.

He had climbed out of the slave miner pits, crawled his way up from the pile of handymen in the Qingfu Sect, suffered false accusations, eaten whips, and seen too many people in high places casually crush those at the bottom.

I finally managed to latch onto the Young Master and have been entrusted with heavy responsibilities. I cannot die here!

Footsteps suddenly sounded outside the woodshed.

Very light.

Chen San immediately closed his eyes, pretending to still be unconscious.

The door lock did not clatter.

Under the door crack, a line of shadow seeped in. The shadow slithered along the ground, went around the firewood pile, and came to Chen San's side, suddenly standing up and transforming into a grey-robed cultivator.

This person's face was unfamiliar. He wore a wide-brimmed bamboo hat that cast a shadow over his eyes, revealing only his slightly pale jaw.

Chen San kept his eyelids tightly shut, motionless.

The grey-robed cultivator crouched down and reached out to remove the rag from his mouth. "Black Harrier Chen San, if you're awake, stop pretending. Your breathing has faltered three times."

Chen San's lips were dry, his voice hoarse. "Who are you?"

The grey-robed cultivator brought two fingers together and tapped on the rope's talisman.

The talisman paper silently split open.

The rope subsequently loosened, falling to the ground like a dead snake.

Chen San slowly stood up, scrutinizing the other party. "Who are you, sir? Why are you saving me?"

The grey-robed cultivator gave a low chuckle. "What kind of thing are you to be worth me saving? It's just that my master couldn't bear to see your Young Master Ning Zhuo fall into a predicament, so he specifically sent me to help."

Chen San remained expressionless. "I don't know what you're talking about."

The grey-robed cultivator shook his head, staring fixedly at Chen San. "My master has a grudge against the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect. If Ning Zhuo is going to fight them, my master is willing to fully sponsor him!"

The grey-robed cultivator pulled a plain white storage bag from his sleeve and tossed it into Chen San's arms. "This is a down payment. The items inside can be considered a gift to your Young Master. Among them is various confidential intelligence regarding Tan Zhu and the Heaven-Draping Single Line Hook. I hope your Young Master can receive our sincerity."

Chen San caught the storage bag, his hands dropping slightly from the weight.

It was not light.

The grey-robed cultivator transformed into a pool of shadow and quickly disappeared.

Chen San's emotions were complex. It made no sense for the other party to want to sponsor Young Master Ning Zhuo but also scheme to harm him beforehand. Therefore, the items in the storage bag were highly likely genuine.

"To think that the investigation task the Young Master assigned me would be accomplished in this manner! Truly... sigh."

Chen San gritted his teeth, carrying mixed emotions, and immediately left the area.
