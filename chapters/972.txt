Chapter 972: Intelligence

The bamboo shadows danced, falling upon the snow-white window paper like light ink washed by clear water, every stroke exuding a pure tranquility.

Sun Lingtong was temporarily staying here to rest.

He sat cross-legged on a low couch. Spread out before him were several ancient Jade Slips, two tightly sealed soul bottles, and a battered wooden box scavenged from a second-hand stall.

The lacquer on the surface of the wooden box was peeling, and its edges were worn smooth, resembling an antique that had been handled by countless hands over the years.

The box was completely empty.

Sun Lingtong extended two slender fingers, tapping his knuckles lightly on the bottom of the wooden box.

Tap.

Tap, tap.

At the third tap, a hidden compartment at the bottom of the box sprang open silently, revealing a piece of black Talisman Paper as thin as a cicada's wing.

The Talisman Paper was so thin it was almost transparent. Under the lamplight, it glowed with a deep, dark luster, like a dormant black butterfly whose dream had been disturbed.

Sun Lingtong slightly raised an eyebrow, the corners of his lips curling into a smile.

"Oh, to think there was such a hidden trick left behind."

Pinching the Talisman Paper between his fingertips, he held it up to the lamplight. There was no writing on the talisman; instead, a few damp, dark patterns slithered slowly across it like water snakes.

Sun Lingtong flicked his finger.

A wisp of azure light struck down like a needle tip, landing squarely in the center of the slithering dark patterns.

The dark patterns abruptly froze.

Leaning in for a closer look, his smile deepened. "A Breath-Tracking Talisman. They certainly took painstaking effort to hide this."

This wooden box came from an inconspicuous junk stall in the outer city's black market.

Over the past few days, he had been helping Ning Zhuo gather past secrets and rumors regarding Tan Zhu, the Heaven-Trailing Single Line Hook, and the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect. Some merchants demanded exorbitant prices, while others tried to pass off forgeries as the real deal.

While Sun Lingtong sought intelligence, he also noticed many gazes falling upon him.

Sun Lingtong was not in a hurry to destroy the Breath-Tracking Talisman.

Taking out a fine bamboo needle, he gently tapped the tip against the edge of the Talisman Paper. With a slow, lifting motion, he extracted a nearly transparent wisp of gray qi from deep within the talisman's patterns.

Sun Lingtong closed his eyes, his divine sense extending out like tentacles to lightly touch that wisp of gray qi.

Immediately, he activated a spell to trace its source.

"So it's coming from here."

He stuck the Breath-Tracking Talisman back into the wooden box's hidden compartment and returned the box to its original place, leaving it as if it had never been touched.

Standing up, he casually snatched a frosted spirit fruit from the table and took a bite. As the sweet and tart juice exploded on his tongue, his figure was already slipping out the window.

Outside, the night wind was slightly cool.

In the distance, the various peaks of the Myriad Manifestations Sect were hidden amidst the clouds and mist. The Formation light at the peaks flickered unsteadily, like the occasionally fluttering eyelids of a sleeping giant beast.

Threading his way through the night, Sun Lingtong alighted upon the mid-levels of a certain mountain.

There was a market here.

Even during the night, it bustled with noise. Some discussed the victories and defeats of the Cloud-Rousing Minor Trial, others debated the gains and losses of the Five-Victory Martial Contest. Some spread the gruesome tale of the Red Robed Guest's soul being hooked away, while others whispered about the fierce reputation of the Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect.

As Sun Lingtong passed by, he listened to a few sentences, and the corners of his lips turned down.

"Sigh, Little Zhuo has really pushed himself right into the heart of the storm this time."

His footsteps were brisk, merging into the night market crowd like a swimming fish.

When he walked past a stall lit by a dim yellow lamp, his face quietly changed.

The handsome youth transformed into a sallow-faced, timid-eyed servant boy. His clothes were drab and dusty, and a worn cloth pouch hung at his waist. Even his gait had changed - he hunched his shoulders and stooped his back, ready to apologize profusely at the slightest bump from a passerby.

Heading south all the way, the street gradually became deserted.

The lights grew sparse, pedestrians were few, and the houses on both sides transitioned from bustling shops to abandoned courtyards. The walls were covered with withered vines, rustling in the night wind.

Sun Lingtong stopped next to an abandoned altar.

The altar was dilapidated on the outside, with thick moss accumulating in the crevices of the stone steps. The offering table was broken at one corner, fragments of an incense burner were scattered nearby, and cobwebs hung from the eaves. However, in his eyes, a layer of Formation light faintly seeped out from the bottom of the altar, flowing between the stone bricks like ripples on water.

He formed hand seals, casting a detection spell for a moment.

Not long after, he circled to the back of the altar, crouched down, reached deep into the moss, and fumbled for a moment. His fingertips brushed against a gray stone button. It was ice-cold but carried a faint fluctuation of spiritual energy, like a sleeping eyeball.

With a light twist.

The floor tiles underfoot silently cracked open, revealing a gap.

Cold, damp air rushed up into his face from below, mixed with the rot of moldy wood and the burnt bitterness of inferior spirit incense.

Sun Lingtong wrinkled his nose and leaped down.

Below was a hidden talisman-making room.

Sun Lingtong continuously formed hand seals, opening secret compartments. He found quite a few precious materials and saw many Jade Slips.

The Jade Slips were entirely filled with intelligence.

Obviously, this place was an underground stronghold for some faction.

A moment later, Sun Lingtong snickered secretly, completely satisfied, and returned fully loaded.

Three Lights Dao Heaven Cave Manor.

Deep in the cave manor, Ning Zhuo sat alone in a quiet room.

Outside, cloud qi surged like the sea, slowly swirling around the peak. The Three Heavens Light Refining Formation operated silently, as sunlight, starlight, and cloud brilliance - three alternating colors - flowed freely, casting ever-changing light and shadow upon the cold stone walls.

On the desk, several Jade Slips were lined up in a row.

They mainly originated from Chen San, with some from Sun Lingtong.

A spirit lamp sat at the corner of the desk, its flame small and tranquil, casting a warm, jade-like luster over the smooth surfaces of the Jade Slips.

Ning Zhuo successively infused his spiritual energy and divine sense, reading through all of them.

Tan Zhu!

The intelligence did not conclusively prove Tan Zhu's origins.

There were three main theories.

First, he was a boy from an isolated village in the North. On the day his village was destroyed, he was abducted by a wandering Demonic Cultivator, thus stepping onto the demonic path.

Second, he was a corpse child who crawled out of the Mass Grave Marsh, adopted by an itinerant Soul Cultivator living in seclusion deep within, from whom he learned a set of secretive corpse poison arts.

Third, he was originally a concubine-born child of a minor Righteous Path clan. His clan was exterminated due to a dispute over property inheritance. Bearing a blood feud, he fell into the demonic path.

The three records conflicted with one another, like different illusions reflected across three mirrors.

Seeing this, Ning Zhuo knew that this intelligence was very likely a fog of multiple origins and backgrounds deliberately created by Tan Zhu to hide his true source.

The remaining intelligence recorded Tan Zhu's deeds, which were almost perfectly consistent.

In his early years, Tan Zhu wandered through several northwestern provinces. He often wore a green bamboo conical hat and a long gray linen coat, with a string of black wooden plaques hanging at his waist. Every time he killed a person, a shallow mark would be added to those black wooden plaques.

Outside rumors claimed his killing intent was extremely heavy and that he slaughtered people like flies. However, by flipping through those old cases, one could discover that those whose lives he took were often secretly involved in some hidden transaction - be it selling living souls, refining forbidden pills, or raising evil creatures.

The old case of the Yin Spring Ferry:

Three Golden Core Demonic Cultivators died suddenly overnight at the ferry crossing. Their corpses were uniformly found kneeling on the riverbank, hands reaching into the water, as if fishing for something invisible.

The intelligence recorded another layer to the truth: Tan Zhu had sneaked into the ferry crossing seven days prior, disguised as an old boatman. He punted back and forth every day. After waiting for the three Demonic Cultivators, he lured them to the middle of the river and poisoned them to death.

The old case of the Black Water Marsh:

A Nascent Soul itinerant cultivator raised a poisonous flood dragon in the marsh, feeding it living cultivators, making its scales harder and its toxicity more violent day by day.

Tan Zhu's cultivation level at that time was far inferior to this person's, yet he lurked by the Black Water Marsh for a full three months. During this period, he disguised himself as a fisherman, a hunchbacked herb farmer, and a peddler selling fishbone lamps, thoroughly investigating the situation.

Three months later, on the night the poisonous flood dragon shed its scales, a massive swath of white fog suddenly rose in the marsh. It was thick as cow's milk, obscuring the sky and hiding the moon.

The Nascent Soul itinerant cultivator chased it into the fog. By the time he finally rushed out of the fog Formation, the poisonous flood dragon's reverse scale had already been cut off, and its soul had been completely harvested.

The old incident of Jackdaw Ridge:

There used to be a small gathering of evil cultivators on Jackdaw Ridge, specializing in trading souls, severed limbs, and Yin-attribute precious materials, gathering a group of cultivators who walked in the dark.

Tan Zhu disappeared there for a full four years, silently, as if he had melted into the wind and snow on the ridge.

On a snowy night four years later, Jackdaw Ridge suddenly fell into chaos. A crazed internal struggle broke out within the small gathering. The seven leaders accused one another of being traitors. Some cut off their own tongues in their sleep, some burned all the secret account books to ashes, and one even jumped off the cliff clutching their most precious soul bottle.

Tan Zhu only showed himself on the final night of that riot. He pushed open the doors of the empty discussion hall and took away a black wooden box from the dusty offering table.

At the end of the intelligence, a line of small text was written: That black wooden box might contain what Tan Zhu truly wanted - something related to his lifespan, related to his impending death, and very likely related to his joining the Naming Stronghold to fight for the Naming Fire Furnace.

"Tan Zhu's actions are mostly setting up conspiracies and traps. His recent performance, however, is charging at the forefront, which is completely contrary to his usual style," Ning Zhuo reviewed in his heart.

The lamplight reflected on his profile. His brows and eyes were as calm as water, though deep in his gaze, undercurrents seemed to surge.

The truth behind these old cases was not included in ordinary intelligence, allowing Ning Zhuo to once again verify his previous conjecture: Tan Zhu "unintentionally" excavating the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascending Altar, and beheading Yue Qingshan in the Five-Victory Martial Contest, must have been done deliberately.

"But what exactly is the specific plot? It is still far from known."

Ning Zhuo sighed, continuing to use his divine sense to read the Jade Slips.

The Heaven-Trailing Single Line Hook.

In the Jade Slip, the relevant circumstances and appearance of this Dao Artifact were recorded.

A slender white line floated in midair. The line was so thin it looked like moonlight drawn into a thread, dropping from the void. At its end hung a small black-and-white hook. The curve of the hook's body was so perfect it didn't seem like a mortal object, and a point of deep blue cold light constantly flowed at the tip, like a solitary star suspended in the cold night.

In front of Ning Zhuo's eyes, the scene of the Red Robed Guest's death couldn't help but surface once more.

In broad daylight, the sea of blood had surged. The Red Robed Guest, with overwhelming demonic might, almost pressed the entire battlefield into his own rhythm.

Then, that single thread dropped from the sky, light as a spider's silk, cold as a moon blade, yet the moment it touched the blood shadow's palm, it erupted with unimaginable power.

A figure like the Red Robed Guest was actually forcibly dragged from the peak of his winning momentum into an abyss of eternal doom!

The Jade Slip detailed that the origins of the Heaven-Trailing Single Line Hook were extremely ancient, having profound ties to the "Fishing Art" lineage among the myriad cultivation arts.

The Fishing Art was initially just a technique for retrieving objects - using bait to lure, using a line to pull, using a pole to unload momentum, and using a hook to secure the target. However, the path of Cultivation is long, and this art gradually evolved into more profound methods. Now it could fish for objects, fish for souls, fish for lives, and even fish for karma!

This reminded Ning Zhuo of Luo Si's Netherworld Ferrying Line and the Three Lifetimes World-Hanging Hook. The Three Lifetimes World-Hanging Hook could forcefully tear spiritual energy, vitality, and spirit out of an enemy cultivator's body. The Netherworld Ferrying Line borrowed the momentum of the water-yin soul stream to pull the target like a marionette.

The Heaven-Trailing Single Line Hook took it to another level entirely!

It had already entered the ranks of Dao Artifacts.

The difference between Dao Artifacts and magic treasures lay in the principles contained within them. To some extent, they could be seen as magic treasures that had mastered Divine Arts!

What it fished for was not ordinary vitality or souls, but the absolute most fragile turning point on the boundary between life and death.

This was the principle it embodied.

Once triggered, it would follow this principle forward, ultimately obtaining the result at the other end.

The triggering condition had already been clarified: the wielder themselves must be in a desperate situation, their life hanging by a thread.

At this time, a karmic encounter would be generated between the wielder and the enemy, forming an extremely narrow "life gap," and only then could the Heaven-Trailing Single Line Hook fall.

The Heaven-Trailing Single Line Hook was not a killing weapon that could be used casually; every time it fell, it required a precise alignment of fate.

The first half of the intelligence slightly reduced Ning Zhuo's pressure. But the content of the second half cast a shadow over his heart.

"Heavy Array Peak?" Ning Zhuo's pupils shrank imperceptibly.

The intelligence pointed out that this Dao Artifact had all kinds of hidden connections with a certain high-ranking member of Heavy Array Peak.

Ning Zhuo's expression remained calm as he rapidly accepted this new situation.

He had understood a truth very early on: the higher-ups of any faction were bound to have infighting!

The Ning Clan had infighting, Fire Persimmon Immortal City had infighting, and White Paper Immortal City was even more of a whirlpool of intertwined interests.

The Myriad Manifestations Sect had numerous peaks standing tall, its factions were interlocked, its inheritances were as vast as the sea, and its interests were unfathomable. For a giant Cultivation mountain, the grander its surface appearance, the more turbulent its internal undercurrents. Disciples fought, elders fought, peak lineages fought - even the right to use the sect-guarding spirit treasures could be fought over until rivers of blood flowed.

Power was the only indivisible thing in this world! Reason, reputation, affection, Sect rules - all grandiose rhetoric and solemn plaques were nothing but convenient tools to be wielded in the face of power.

After seeing the words Heavy Array Peak, Ning Zhuo's state of mind became like a reef after the tide receded, increasingly calm and clear.

As the main peak with the most power, exerting influence was highly possible and extremely natural.

Furthermore, the source of this intelligence was suspicious, making it difficult to distinguish truth from falsehood.

"But if Heavy Array Peak is behind this, I must have been swept up into the infighting of the Myriad Manifestations Sect's higher-ups."

Ning Zhuo smiled slightly.

Wealth and honor are sought amidst danger!

Since he wanted to climb to high places, he would have to face the wind and snow of those high peaks sooner or later.

Wherever there are people, there is a jianghu. Since it cannot be avoided, he would face it head-on.

The Naming Stronghold was already his way of entering the board himself. This was the path he chose, and he wouldn't turn back.

"It's just that right now, I am not qualified to sit calmly behind the scenes and act as a chess player unhurriedly placing pieces. I have too few chess pieces in hand, my foundation is too thin, and although my reputation has initially shown itself, it's like morning dew - unable to withstand the wind and waves."

"What I can do is charge at the forefront and lead the attack!"

Ning Zhuo continued checking the intelligence.

The Three Valiants of the Death-Facing Sect.

Relevant images surfaced in Ning Zhuo's divine sea.

Three figures stood shoulder to shoulder.

Triplets from the same womb.

The physical statures of the three were almost identical. Their shoulder width, arm length, and facial contours all seemed as if they were carved by the exact same knife. However, upon closer look, they possessed distinctly different temperaments and characteristics.

The person on the left had a dull-white lamp-shaped mole between his eyebrows. His eye sockets were deeply sunken like ancient wells, and a cold luster gleamed in his pupils.

This person was named Xiang Hundeng. He mainly cultivated the divine sea Upper Dantian, and his cultivation art was the Life-Illuminating Nether Lamp Visualization. His divine soul power was peerless and tyrannical. He excelled at illuminating souls, confusing thoughts, and suppressing spirits. He usually held a life-illuminating white lamp. Wherever the lamplight illuminated, the enemy cultivator's soul would emerge within the lamp shadow, their thoughts would become sluggish as if trapped in a quagmire, and their divine sense would be pulled out of their body wisp by wisp.

The person in the middle had light gray water vapor lingering around his cuffs. His breathing was long and profound, and the aura of his spiritual energy was like a deep abyss or an ocean.
