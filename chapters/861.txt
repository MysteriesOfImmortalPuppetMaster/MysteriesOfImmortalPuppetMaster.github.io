Chapter 861: A Nascent Soul Attacks the Door

"Wait a moment, there is still one last round."

The teleportation was suddenly halted!

The cultivators on duty initiated the final round of inspections.

Once the inspection passed, they finally let out a breath of turbid qi. Through divine sense transmissions, they complained to each other about the drastically increased workload.

But there was nothing they could do.

Recently, disasters had occurred frequently within the Myriad Manifestations Sect. The higher-ups had issued strict orders for them to work diligently and ensure not a single mistake was made.

Therefore, many steps in the teleportation process that had previously been omitted were now restored, and an extra final round of inspection was added.

The teleportation formation slowly started up.

Radiance rapidly condensed, about to transform into a pillar of light that would shoot up into the sky.

I am about to be free! Qin De's Nascent Soul could hardly contain its excitement.

Boom!

The teleportation formation violently shuddered.

The formation runes began to crack. Fissures spread outward from the formation core like a spiderweb, like twisted and tangled tree roots.

"Oh no!" The guarding disciple's expression drastically changed. "The teleportation formation is collapsing!"

The nearly twenty people inside the formation were terrified. Some screamed, some struggled, and some tried to rush out of the formation. However, since the teleportation had already been activated, they were firmly locked in place by the formation's power, completely unable to move.

Qin De's Nascent Soul: ?!

The cracks on the runes multiplied and grew denser. The formation's light flickered wildly, alternating between bright and dim, like the final struggles of a dying man.

BOOM - !

With an earth-shattering roar, the teleportation formation exploded.

Fragments scattered and flew in all directions, swallowing everything nearby. Everyone and everything inside the formation were swept into the void by a terrifying force, disappearing into the chaotic currents of shattered space.

"How could this happen?!" A cultivator on duty clutched his head, dumbfounded and in complete disbelief.

"This shouldn't be. Ever since the higher-ups issued the strict orders, we inspected the teleportation formation no less than ten times."

"Right, this shouldn't be our fault."

"You are all correct. I've already discovered the reason for the formation's collapse."

"What is it?"

"Hurry and tell us."

The crowd of cultivators hurriedly urged.

They were not in a hurry to save anyone - or rather, it was already too late to save anyone.

Teleportation formations rarely malfunctioned, but when they did, it was no small matter. It was essentially irredeemable.

The cultivator's answer would largely determine the severity of the Sect punishment the cultivators on duty would face.

"It's the void."

"A void power is lurking near our Sect. When the teleportation formation opened the space, it caused ripples that happened to hit and trigger this power. This, in turn, damaged the teleportation formation and caused its direct collapse."

"The void?! I know what it is! It's the Cloud Prison of the Demon Subjugation Hall. A Demonic Cultivator previously succeeded in a Blood Sacrifice there, summoning the Lord of the Infernal Rift. His power still has remnants that haven't been completely cleansed."

The on-duty cultivators discussed animatedly, all leaning towards this answer. This way, the primary responsibility wouldn't fall on them, but on the Demon Subjugation Hall.

As for the series of personnel and material losses caused by the teleportation formation explosion, they would be included in the subsequent tally and report.

Within the void, there was no up or down, no left or right, no near or far, and no time. There was only endless darkness, endless cold, and endless nothingness.

Chaotic currents of shattered space - these were the dregs left behind after space fractured, the ruins left after laws collapsed, and one of the most terrifying nightmares for cultivators in the world.

Here, Golden Core cultivators were like ants, Nascent Soul cultivators were like worthless grass, and even Soul Formation cultivators had to tread lightly.

The young cultivator that Qin De's Nascent Soul was inhabiting was completely torn, slashed, and reduced to fleshy debris almost the instant he fell into the chaotic currents.

Qin De's Nascent Soul was forced to "abandon ship"!

No... I cannot die here... He pushed the power of his Dual-Aspect Nascent Soul to the absolute limit. The Confucian-aspect Nascent Soul emitted a gentle white light, doing its best to suppress and stabilize the surrounding space. Meanwhile, the purplish-black demonic qi did its best to corrode, causing the smallest spatial fragments to melt away prematurely so they wouldn't harm the Nascent Soul body.

However, the chaotic currents of shattered space were still too fierce.

Normal Nascent Soul cultivators had no way to resist it, and even Soul Formation-level experts would keep their distance. Let alone a Nascent Soul like Qin De's, which was hastily achieved and not yet fully under his control.

Qin De's Nascent Soul tumbled, tore, and was crushed within the chaotic currents, being shattered time and time again, only to re-condense time and time again.

His Nascent Soul was formed in a unique manner, and its foundation was profound; it had utilized the Golden Cores of two Confucian cultivators, and more importantly, a Demon Seed. Causing a Demon Seed to perish was no simple matter.

"No, I cannot die!"

"I am not resigned to this!!"

Perhaps it was sheer luck, but amidst his tumultuous drifting, Qin De's Nascent Soul fortunately crashed out of the void, returning to the reality of the Living Realm.

Qin De's Nascent Soul plummeted onto an unnamed mountain peak. He was covered in cracks, his light was dim, and his aura was so weak it was barely detectable. He lay motionless among a pile of dead leaves, like a broken porcelain doll.

In the short span of a dozen breaths, he had reached the absolute limit of total collapse.

For the time being, he could only lie in the dead leaves, letting the morning wind blow over him, the dew soak him, and the sunlight shine upon him.

"Myriad Manifestations Sect..." Qin De murmured, his voice so soft it was almost inaudible.

He sensed it. This was a mountain peak belonging to the Myriad Manifestations Sect. He had fled for so long, struggled for so long, and fought for so long - yet in the end, he had still returned to the Myriad Manifestations Sect.

But this wasn't surprising.

The teleportation formation had been inside the Myriad Manifestations Sect to begin with. If he had truly been thrown into the endless sea of clouds outside the main Sect gates, it would have been an absolute dead end.

"At least I still have a glimmer of hope."

Qin De's consciousness gradually blurred.

The Confucian-Demonic dual-aspect Nascent Soul curled up in the dead leaves and closed its eyes.

Morning light scattered down, shining on his dilapidated body. Those cracks shimmered faintly in the sunlight, like shattered glazed glass, like fractured jade, and like a dream about to dissipate.

Outside the gates of the Myriad Manifestations Sect.

"Why hasn't Qin De appeared yet?" The place where Xiao Juxia was lying in ambush was precisely the other end of the small-scale teleportation formation.

Xiao Juxia didn't even need to ask around; the cultivators on this end of the teleportation formation had already realized something was wrong. Especially the cultivators waiting there specifically for reception duties; they had started asking questions one after another.

From their conversations, Xiao Juxia quickly learned the truth of the matter.

"What? The teleportation formation suddenly collapsed, and everything and everyone inside were swept into the chaotic currents of shattered space?!"

"Was this accident caused by the Void Demon God again?"

"Not good, not good at all!"

Xiao Juxia's heart trembled, and an intense premonition of misfortune instantly arose within him. He hastily pinched his fingers to divine the situation, discovering that Qin De was still located inside the main gates of the Myriad Manifestations Sect.

He also divined that if he didn't quickly go and rendezvous with Qin De right now, he would miss his final opportunity.

"But how can I enter the Myriad Manifestations Sect's main gate now?"

The teleportation formation in front of him could no longer be used.

The nearest functioning teleportation formation was still ten thousand miles away.

But what made Xiao Juxia feel even more despair was his divination: since this small teleportation town had experienced a collapse resulting in severe loss of personnel and resources...

...the Myriad Manifestations Sect had already reacted. They temporarily shut down all teleportation formations and dispatched the appropriate cultivators to delve into the nearby void to actively inspect and clear out any threats.

In other words, unless Xiao Juxia forged a new path, he would be completely unable to enter the Myriad Manifestations Sect's main gates.

"And the reality is, if I teleport in independently, I will be immediately detected and hunted down by the Myriad Manifestations Sect."

Since the Myriad Manifestations Sect had discovered the issue with the teleportation formation, they would inevitably mobilize the entire Sect's power to conduct a thorough investigation.

Acting right under their noses during the most stringent phase of the investigation - if Xiao Juxia were to forcefully barge in like that, it would simply be the pinnacle of stupidity!

Xiao Juxia forcefully suppressed the impatience in his heart, leaving him with no choice but to endure and wait.

He had only just begun to wait, but the Monarch of Huazhang's wait had already ended.

The entanglement of destiny between them had completely vanished, leaving the Monarch of Huazhang entirely unable to sense the outcome of his final strike.

He looked at the royal seal in his hand. Due to that final strike, the jade light that originally flowed across the seal had now grown dim.

The Monarch of Huazhang let out a soft sigh and summoned the Director of the Imperial Ancestral Temple, Zhou Zheng, from outside the hall doors to come in and clean up the mess.

Huazhang Kingdom would undoubtedly conduct a vigorous investigation into this clash of destiny. However, to uncover the true results, they would inevitably have to wait for a long time.

Bluestone Cave Dwelling.

Cultivation Chamber.

Ning Zhuo sat cross-legged on a futon, a bronze alchemy furnace placed in front of him.

The furnace wasn't large, only about two feet tall, with three legs and two handles. He had asked a subordinate to buy it from the Commerce Hall. Although its appearance was old, it won out in having a solid foundation.

Of course, the price was not cheap either.

The price of an alchemy furnace was never cheap.

Beside the furnace, dozens of medicinal ingredients were neatly stacked - Century Spirit Reishi, Snow Mountain Poria, Crimson Flame Grass, Cold Pool Lotus Hearts, Golden Thread Vines...

These were all ingredients for the Foundation Consolidation and Essence Cultivation Pill.

This pill's grade was not high, only considered mid-grade, but it was the most commonly used supplementary medicinal pill for Foundation Establishment cultivators. It used the Reishi as the sovereign, Poria as the minister, Crimson Flame Grass as the assistant, and Cold Pool Lotus Heart as the courier, harmonizing yin and yang to cultivate and restore vital qi. The pill recipe was simple, the ingredients were common, and the refining techniques were not complex - making it the perfect choice for a beginner to start with.

Ning Zhuo ignited the furnace fire. Pressing his hands against both sides of the furnace, he slowly injected his spiritual energy.

Flames rose up, licking the bottom of the furnace.

The temperature inside began to climb. Ning Zhuo's divine sense probed into the furnace, sensing the subtle changes in the flames.

His technique was not proficient; it could even be called clumsy.

He threw in the first ingredient - the Century Spirit Reishi. He roasted it slowly over a gentle fire. Inside the furnace, fine sap slowly seeped from the surface of the Reishi, emitting a crisp, clear medicinal fragrance.

"The heat was too much." Ning Zhuo frowned and hurriedly withdrew a fraction of his spiritual energy. The edges of the Reishi were slightly scorched. Although it wasn't ruined, its quality had already dropped a tier.

His expression remained unchanged as he threw in the second ingredient - Snow Mountain Poria.

Poria favored the cold and similarly required slow simmering over a gentle fire; it could not be rushed with an intense flame.

Ning Zhuo was much more careful this time. Drawing on the experience from his recent practice, he used his divine sense to monitor the Poria's changes, controlling the heat just right.

But when he reached the third ingredient, the Crimson Flame Grass, another problem occurred.

Crimson Flame Grass was fierce in nature and needed to be rapidly stimulated with a vigorous fire to ignite its medicinal properties.

Ning Zhuo channeled his spiritual energy, and the flames in the furnace suddenly surged. The Crimson Flame Grass tumbled in the furnace. Its medicinal properties were activated, but they leaked outward, wasting a significant amount of medicinal power.

The fourth ingredient, the fifth ingredient, the sixth ingredient...

Tossing in each medicinal ingredient was a test. Ning Zhuo's technique went from rusty to proficient, from clumsy to fluid; his progress was visible to the naked eye.

His control over the flames became increasingly precise, and his timing for adding ingredients grew more and more opportune.

This was not innate talent.

It was memory.

Jia Luanzhen's memories - that Demonic Cultivator alchemist had hundreds of years of pill-refining experience. His understanding of medicinal ingredients, mastery over heat control, and comprehension of pill recipes were all deeply branded into Ning Zhuo's Divine Soul.

Ning Zhuo wasn't learning how to refine pills; he was reviewing. Those experiences already belonged to him; they simply needed to be reawakened.

Then, through practice, he would make the final calibrations and fine-adjustments.

An hour later, all the ingredients were in the furnace. Ning Zhuo's hands formed hand seals as he pushed the flames, beginning the final pill condensation.

Inside the alchemy furnace, the medicinal powers of dozens of ingredients intertwined, slowly fusing under the tempering of the flames.

The medicinal fragrance grew thicker, the furnace began to tremble slightly, and the runes on the furnace lid started to glow.

Ning Zhuo held his breath in absolute concentration, keeping his divine sense constantly locked inside the furnace to sense every change in the pills.

Finally - 

Pop!

The alchemy furnace jolted, and the lid flew up. Three round medicinal pills flew out of the furnace, landing in the palm of Ning Zhuo's hand.

They were pale yellow all over, with fine, dense patterns on their surfaces, emitting a mild medicinal fragrance. While their quality wasn't considered top-tier, they were still mid-grade. For someone refining this type of pill for the first time, this was already an excellent result.

Ning Zhuo looked at the pills in his palm, a smile rising at the corners of his mouth. "Success."

He stored the medicinal pills in a jade bottle. Just as he was about to continue refining the second batch, the Jade Token at his waist suddenly began to vibrate.

The Jade Token was the size of a palm and entirely verdant green. It was the control key for the Bluestone Cave Dwelling, as well as the proof of lease. Ning Zhuo extended his divine sense into it, and the scene outside the cave dwelling immediately surfaced before his eyes.

He was stunned.

Outside the cave dwelling, a fist-sized Nascent Soul was attacking the dwelling's defensive formation.

Ning Zhuo simply couldn't believe his eyes: "A Nascent Soul? This looks like... a Nascent Soul, right?"

Ning Zhuo found it bizarre and unimaginable.

First of all, infighting was strictly forbidden within the Myriad Manifestations Sect. Even if there was a life-and-death feud, both parties had to enter the Martial Arts Hall to resolve it.

Therefore, Ning Zhuo absolutely never expected his Bluestone Cave Dwelling to be directly attacked.

This was too brazen!

Moreover, violating this sect rule of the Myriad Manifestations Sect would undoubtedly invite extremely severe punishment.

Yet right now, there really was an enemy attacking his Bluestone Cave Dwelling, tossing the Myriad Manifestations Sect's rules entirely out of their mind.

Not only that, but this enemy was actually a Nascent Soul!

How could a Nascent Soul cultivator fight alone in their Nascent Soul form?

Under normal circumstances, once a cultivator formed a Nascent Soul, they would absolutely never use it directly for attacking.

The Nascent Soul was the foundation of a cultivator, the crystallization of their life's cultivation - fragile and precious. If a cultivator's Nascent Soul left the body, it was either to escape for their life, to command a magic treasure, or to evade a Heavenly Tribulation. In short, it would absolutely never be used for solitary combat.

This was far too abnormal.

Could it be a trap? In an instant, alarm bells rang loudly in Ning Zhuo's heart.

Then, he heard the Nascent Soul shouting.

"Ning Zhuo, come out!"

"I know you're in there, stop hiding."

"Hurry up and come out, I'm looking for you!"

Ning Zhuo's face was full of confusion.

Why did this Nascent Soul act as if they were very familiar with him?

And why was it in such a rush to see him? What was it for?

A normal visit would have sufficed. Why was it in such a rush that it started attacking my cave dwelling's formation?

What in the world is going on!
