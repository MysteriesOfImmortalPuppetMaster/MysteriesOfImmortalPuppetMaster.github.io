Chapter 839: Ning Zhuo Repairs the Boat

In a jade box lay a pool of transparent water. Its color was clear and bright, rippling slightly within the container. Even through the jade, one could feel a gentle, moist aura.

This was Heavenly Pool Nurturing Water.

Ning Zhuo was already familiar with these precious materials. At this moment, he dipped his fingertip directly into it, gently dotting the surface of the spirit boat. Then, using his divine sense and spiritual energy, he guided it to seep toward the core.

The spirit boat lit up slightly, almost like an illusion.

The Heavenly Pool Nurturing Water flowed slowly along the minute cracks inside the spirit boat. Wherever it passed, the dried-out areas gradually regained moisture.

Ning Zhuo waited quietly until the liquid was completely absorbed before dipping for the second portion.

He repeated this process. After more than a dozen times, the cracks in the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat were soaked with the spiritual water, and the core was plump and moist, emitting a lustrous watery sheen.

Ning Zhuo opened the second jade box.

Coiled inside was a flesh-pink vine, about as thick as a pinky finger and three feet long. The surface of the vine had fine, dense spiral patterns, with spiritual light faintly flowing between them.

This was the Umbilical Spirit Vine. It grew in deep mountains and great marshes abundant with spiritual qi. Shaped like an umbilical cord, it was hollow and channeled spirit.

Ning Zhuo reached out and took the spirit vine.

The vine was soft and elastic, like the skin of an infant.

Ning Zhuo mobilized his Fire Seed and cast a spell, quickly refining this spirit vine into silk. Each thread of vine silk was as fine as hair and incredibly tough.

Ning Zhuo mobilized his divine sense and spiritual energy again, his fingers simultaneously forming hand seals to assist the guidance.

Threads of vine silk, seemingly there yet not, slowly wove their way along the array patterns inside the spirit boat.

Ning Zhuo held his breath and focused his mind, exerting his full effort. In his Upper Dantian, various mirror thoughts dissolved one after another like snow meeting the sun. At the same time, his divine sense was being consumed intensely.

The requirements for this step were very high.

Wherever the spirit silk passed, it had to fit perfectly with the original array patterns, without deviating by even a hair's breadth. A slight mistake would not only fail to repair it but would damage the existing array patterns.

Ning Zhuo's hand was as steady as a rock.

With the attainment of a Renowned Artisan, he had long passed the stage where practice merely made perfect.

His basic skills in artifact refining were already solid, and he had absorbed numerous demonic souls. Artifact refining was one of the four great arts of cultivation; every cultivator inevitably came into contact with it and learned a little.

His divine sense extended along the spirit silk, sensing every node and every turn.

As the spirit silk wove through, it automatically filled any breaks it encountered; it also automatically nourished any worn areas.

After a full stick of incense had burned, Ning Zhuo successfully threaded all the vine silk through the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat without missing a single crucial spot!

The hull of the spirit boat trembled slightly, giving the impression of a dying patient who had caught their breath at the most critical moment, their breathing stabilizing once more.

Sweat beaded on Ning Zhuo's forehead, but he had no time to wipe it.

Controlling objects with divine sense, he opened the third jade box.

Inside the jade box was a Nine Orifice Spirit Stone. The stone resembled an infant's head, greenish-gray throughout, with nine holes distributed on its surface like a human face.

The big-headed youth took out the Nine Orifice Spirit Stone and used a furnace and Fire Seed to quickly refine it into a spirit pearl the size of a pigeon egg. The spirit pearl still possessed all nine orifices, no different from the original.

This furnace and Fire Seed were naturally rented from the Artifact Refining Hall, costing a significant fee.

Ning Zhuo placed the spirit pearl into the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat and poured his spiritual energy into it.

The Nine Orifice Spirit Pearl began to slowly rotate. Faint spiritual light flickered occasionally from the nine orifices. Like breathing, the light brightened slightly upon inhalation and dimmed upon exhalation.

The Nine Orifice Spirit Pearl began to harmonize the circulation of spiritual qi within the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat.

Ning Zhuo did not stop. He took out the Earth Milk Essence from the fourth jade box.

The essence was milky white, thick as honey, emitting a faint, warm, milky fragrance that seemed to drift in and out of existence.

Ning Zhuo gently scooped it up with a jade spoon, pouring it spoonful by spoonful onto the surface of the spirit boat.

With every spoonful poured, he used artifact refining techniques to refine it slightly.

As the milky liquid landed on the hull, due to the refining methods, it was instantly absorbed without leaving a trace. Wherever it passed, those tiny cracks disappeared at a speed visible to the naked eye, as if wounds were healing.

One spoon, two spoons, three spoons...

After eighty-three spoons, the tiny cracks on the surface of the spirit boat had completely vanished.

Just doing this was absolutely not enough. The disappearance of the cracks was only temporary; the effect of the Earth Milk Essence would only last for a period of time. It required further refining to consolidate permanently.

Ning Zhuo opened the fifth jade box and took out the Sun Essence Moon Splendor Pearl inside.

This pearl was as large as a pigeon egg. The Yang side was golden-red like fire, and the Yin side was silvery-white like the moon, the two colors rotating endlessly. When Ning Zhuo held the pearl in his palm, he could sense the alternating rhythm of warmth and coolness - the Yang side warm as the morning sun, the Yin side cool as the night moon, the rhythm of rotation seemingly reflecting the phenomena of heaven and earth.

Ning Zhuo suspended the spirit pearl three inches above the spirit boat and activated it with spiritual energy. The spirit pearl rotated slowly, sprinkling down golden-red light from the Yang side and silvery-white radiance from the Yin side.

Bathed in the radiance, the spirit boat itself began to shine brightly.

The golden and silver lights rotated endlessly, becoming increasingly brilliant.

Sun essence warms the spirit (Shen), moon splendor nourishes the essence (Jing). Spirit and essence embraced and guarded each other, reaching a state of great stability.

An hour later, the aura of the spirit boat was distinct from before.

It was no longer a withered, deathly aura, but a vibrant one, like a fetus sleeping in its mother's womb.

Ning Zhuo opened the sixth jade box. Inside lay a dark green stone, cool and moist to the touch. Holding the stone in hand, Ning Zhuo's mind naturally quieted down, and distracting thoughts gradually faded.

Ning Zhuo cast an artifact refining spell, burning the Calming Stone like a candle and placing it beside the spirit boat.

The Calming Stone slowly melted in the flame, and the intent of stillness contained within it spread rapidly, enveloping the spirit boat.

This act was equivalent to a ballast stone; it could prevent the nature of the materials from becoming volatile, greatly increasing the probability of final success.

The entire spirit boat seemed to sink into a dreamless, sound sleep like an infant.

In this sound sleep, those minute damages that had not yet fully healed grew and closed up quietly.

Ning Zhuo also rested briefly, swallowing a medicinal pill to restore his condition, which had fallen to less than half.

From the last jade box, he took out the White Tiger Bone Powder.

The bone powder was a fine, lustrous white dust, faintly shimmering with a golden sheen.

Ning Zhuo took a jade bowl, poured in a little spiritual spring water, and then gently sprinkled the White Tiger Bone Powder into it. The bone powder dissolved upon contact with water, turning into a lustrous white liquid that radiated vigorous vitality.

With the guidance of Ning Zhuo's divine sense, the lustrous white bone water was drawn into a thread, falling straight from the bowl deep into the spirit boat.

There was a crack there, near the prow, about three inches long and reaching deep into the core. The previous nourishing and warming had healed most of it, but that single point at the deepest part remained unhealed.

The lustrous white bone water reached the deepest part, causing the last, most stubborn sliver of the rift to begin closing.

When eighty percent of the white bone water in the jade bowl was consumed, the spirit boat suddenly trembled.

A warm, moist radiance slowly lit up from the core of the spirit boat. The light was soft and warm, like the first rays of dawn, like a waking infant.

Seeing this scene, a surge of joy welled up in Ning Zhuo's heart.

The final step was achieved.

With this, Ning Zhuo completed the repair.

Ning Zhuo removed the Nine Orifice Spirit Pearl and extinguished the fire on the Calming Stone. The spirit pearl had shrunk incredibly from regulating the spiritual qi, now only the size of a baby's pinky finger. The Calming Stone was also mostly consumed, with less than twenty percent remaining.

Ning Zhuo gathered these remaining precious materials and looked at the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat again.

The spirit boat floated in mid-air, glowing white throughout, with light flowing across it. Those cracks that once covered it densely had now disappeared; the array patterns that were once worn were now clear and new.

Ning Zhuo reached out and gently stroked the hull.

It felt warm and moist to the touch, full of vitality.

This artifact refining was accomplished entirely by him alone.

This brought Ning Zhuo a greater sense of achievement.

Although Gongsun Yan obeyed his orders, he was completely unfamiliar with the Natural Heaven and Earth Embryonic Breath Grand Method. And the primary condition for repairing the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat was that the cultivator must be proficient in the Natural Heaven and Earth Embryonic Breath Grand Method.

The main function of this magic treasure was to perform the Natural Heaven and Earth Embryonic Breath Grand Method to help others increase their physical foundation.

Without understanding the Natural Heaven and Earth Embryonic Breath Grand Method, even if one's artifact refining skills were high, it would be easy to botch the job.

After checking that there were no errors, Ning Zhuo put away the Embryonic Breath Spirit Boat, relaxed, and immediately felt physically and mentally exhausted.

He naturally understood the principle that haste makes waste, so he immediately chose to rest.

Once his essence, energy, and spirit were fully restored, continuing his cultivation would yield twice the result with half the effort.

-----

Xiao Juxia and one hundred other cultivators arrived at the Cloud Prison together.

The cultivator in charge of the trial announced the content of this checkpoint, causing all the cultivators to prick up their ears and listen attentively.

The Cloud Prison was a place controlled by the Demon Subjugation Hall, holding numerous cultivators - both demonic heretics and righteous cultivators who had committed errors.

The entire Cloud Prison utilized the method of "group artifacts," constructed from a massive amount of magic artifacts, magic treasures, and core spiritual treasures. It was one of the heavy grounds of the Myriad Manifestations Sect.

The trial content for Xiao Juxia and the others was to inspect and repair the magic artifacts, and even magic treasures, here.

Of course, this was limited only to the periphery.

The truly important components were naturally not something they could access.

In the crowd, Xiao Juxia's expression was calm, but doubts arose in his heart again: "Why hasn't Ning Zhuo arrived yet?"

He knew the rules of this artifact refining trial well - Ning Zhuo possessed a higher-grade identity token, allowing him to skip the preliminary trial checkpoints and participate directly in the subsequent ones.

At the same time, under the guidance of cultivators from the Demon Subjugation Hall, Zhao Hansheng, Gu Qing, and others arrived at the Cloud Prison. They crossed paths with Xiao Juxia's group and then stepped into the depths of the Cloud Prison.

Xiao Juxia's heart shook slightly as his spiritual intuition was triggered.

"So my calculation lies with them!"

"But what does this have to do with Ning Zhuo?"

"Zhao Hansheng and Gu Qing have had no interaction with Ning Zhuo recently."

The trial officially began on this side. Xiao Juxia and the others scattered like stars, entering various parts of the Cloud Prison to repair magic artifacts and magic treasures.

While refining artifacts, Xiao Juxia secretly activated a technique to spy on the movements of Zhao Hansheng's group.

This was a deeply hidden prison cell, surrounded by Demon-Suppressing Black Stone, with sealing runes carved all over the walls. Deep in the cell, a Confucian cultivator sat. His limbs and neck were all bound by chains as he lay on the ground.

His hair was disheveled, and his appearance was haggard. Hearing the commotion, he suddenly opened his eyes.

In an instant, his eyes shot forth divine light, revealing a madness of falling into the demonic path amidst sharpness.

It was precisely Qin De.

Qin De stared dead at Zhao Hansheng's group: "Two Confucian cultivators?"

He sneered: "Why don't I see that old cultivator Duanmu Zhang? Is it because he couldn't out-debate me and has no face to see me again?"

Zhao Hansheng took a step forward, walking to the cell door: "Qin De, I have reviewed your Sage's Great Thief Sutra in its entirety; it is full of omissions and errors. Do you dare to debate the scriptures with me?"

"Debate scriptures?" Qin De was stunned.

Xiao Juxia was also slightly stunned.

He didn't know the intelligence regarding Qin De and was wondering why Zhao Hansheng and Gu Qing, this pair of teacher and student, would suddenly come to the depths of the Cloud Prison specifically to find a heavy prisoner for a debate.

The debate had already begun.

Qin De struck first: "'Until the sage is dead, great thieves will not cease.' This is the word of Zhuangzi. Do you Confucian cultivators dare to face it directly?"

Zhao Hansheng replied calmly: "Zhuangzi's words are not to slander the sage, but to warn the world. The sage governs the world with benevolence and righteousness; thus, benevolence and righteousness can become resources for thieves. However, the sage was not born to provide resources for thieves, but established for the sake of the world. To give up eating because of choking - is that not absurd?"

Qin De sneered: "Clever words and ingratiating looks! I ask you, is it a fact that emperors throughout history have used ritual law to bind the myriad people?"

Zhao Hansheng said: "Ritual law binds the myriad people, but also nurtures the myriad people. Without ritual, there is chaos; without law, there is violence. The sage established rituals and composed music not to bind the people, but to facilitate the people's lives and perfect the people's virtue."

-----

At the beginning of the debate between the two sides, the speed of speech was extremely fast, like master swordsmen engaging in rapid offense and defense, whipping up flashes of blades and shadows of swords.

Xiao Juxia was located very far away, but his divine sense could penetrate layers of obstacles, hearing every word from both sides clearly.

He listened for a moment and came to some understanding: "Although this Sage's Great Thief Sutra is biased and has many flaws, it manages to be logically self-consistent. Not bad, not bad."

"Did this child create it himself? He indeed has some talent."

He began to appreciate Qin De.

Inside his sleeve, he pinched his fingers to calculate again: "So that's it."

"It is Qin De's Sage's Great Thief Sutra that has hindered the development of the Confucian cultivators in this sect, so they need to move this stumbling block."

"According to the content of the Sage's Great Thief Sutra, Duanmu Zhang and the others couldn't move it, so they had to find outside help."

"No wonder they treated Zhao Hansheng and Gu Qing with such courtesy."

"Oh, on the other hand, 'Heart Learning' is indeed formidable..."

By the time Xiao Juxia listened to this point, Qin De was already completely unable to hold his ground, facing almost total defeat.

Heart Learning created a unique mechanism, opening up a brand-new world. Qin De had lived in prison for a long time; encountering such new learning for the first time, he suffered a severe impact and could only retreat again and again in the debate, suffering defeat after defeat.

Those arguments he thought were seamless were dismantled one by one by Zhao Hansheng. The logical loops he believed to be unassailable were also destroyed beyond recognition.

In contrast, Zhao Hansheng had obtained the Sage's Great Thief Sutra in advance and had already conducted a round of debate, having the most sufficient pre-battle preparation.

One side came prepared carrying the new Confucian learning, while the other worked behind closed doors, kept in the dark, and responded in haste.

In the final moment, Qin De gritted his teeth: "'The mind is principle'? Laughable! If the mind is principle, whence come selfish desires? Whence come evil thoughts?"

Zhao Hansheng smiled calmly: "The original substance of the mind is merely Heaven's Principle; originally, there is nothing that is not proper. But covered by selfish desires, there is ungood. For example, a bright mirror is originally bright in itself; covered by dust, it reflects things unclearly. But the dust is not the mirror, and selfish desires are not the mind. Remove the dust and the mirror is bright; remove desire and the mind is bright. This is the work of extending innate knowledge (liangzhi)."

Qin De froze, opened his mouth, and wanted to refute, but could not find the words.

Gu Qing witnessed this with his own eyes from the side. He was already sweating profusely from listening, and seeing Qin De remain silent for a long time, joy rose in his heart: "Won, Teacher has actually won."

"This Qin De is indeed remarkable. No wonder he could create the heretical Sage's Great Thief Sutra; he actually debated with Teacher to such an extent. If it were me, I definitely wouldn't have lasted more than thirty percent of the time."

However, just at this moment, Qin De suddenly spoke, asking a question: "Innate knowledge (liangzhi) knows right and knows wrong. But isn't that 'knower of right and wrong' also something that can be stolen?"
