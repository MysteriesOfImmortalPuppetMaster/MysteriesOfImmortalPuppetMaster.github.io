Chapter 853: Assassination!

A dragon’s roar echoed in everyone’s hearts.

Immediately after, a nine-colored pillar of light erupted from the Monarch of Huazhang. The pillar was not wide, and it shot straight into the high heavens; however, under the concealment of the palace, it was not visible from the outside.

The Monarch of Huazhang formed hand seals, chanting the Heart Seal and performing Body Seals simultaneously.

The Golden Chapter Jade Records suddenly shuddered.

It flipped open its cover, emitting a glow of gold and jade that surged forth to meet the Monarch’s pillar of light, quickly merging into one.

From this merger, a profound and indescribable power of destiny arose. It pierced through the clouds and the vast sky, rolling toward a destination far, far away.

-----

Cloud Canopy Peak.

"Let me see what you really are!" Dong Chen’s specialized vision gradually shifted from a blur to clarity.

Just as he was about to see the truth...

BOOM!

It felt as if he had heard a massive explosion.

A majestic and gargantuan force, like a mountain flying through the air, slammed violently into the Heaven-Sustaining Cloud Canopy.

The Heaven-Sustaining Cloud Canopy rocked violently; the magnitude of the tremor was terrifying.

The seven-colored clouds atop the umbrella’s surface instantly became chaotic. The nine colors - crimson, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, purple, gold, and silver - flickered manically, flashing in and out of existence.

The nine main cloud ribbons suddenly snapped taut, lashing out wildly. They whipped the air with a series of sharp cracks, leaving golden trails of light in the sky with every strike.

As their destinies were linked, Wang Yu and the other two ministers spat out mouthfuls of blood simultaneously.

Although they had sensed a heavy strike coming, they hadn't expected the force to be this ferocious.

Fortunately, Dong Chen had already made preparations. At the critical moment, Ling Juejian, the Peak Master of Sword-Chime Peak, teleported over. He immediately surrounded the Heaven-Sustaining Cloud Canopy, contributing his own strength.

Only then did the Heaven-Sustaining Cloud Canopy stabilize its footing, though it remained at a disadvantage. The situation was critical.

Dong Chen’s vision went dark. For a short time, it would be difficult for him to sense the key secret again; he had to deal with the immediate situation first.

-----

Song Taosheng and the others had already descended to the seventh floor.

The guard leading them was replaced by a middle-aged cultivator with a cold expression and few words.

"This floor holds serious criminals of the Golden Core stage," he introduced briefly, providing only surface-level information.

Song Taosheng nodded, his gaze sweeping over the surroundings surreptitiously.

The layout here was even more complex, featuring many branching paths. Cultivator guards were stationed at every interval. Song Taosheng and his group felt the pressure - the density of guards here far exceeded the six floors above.

Under these circumstances, it was nearly impossible to secretly set up traps along the way. The five death-sworn soldiers had no choice but to temporarily give up on that plan.

After walking further, the guard suddenly stopped.

He turned to Song Taosheng and the others: "The path ahead is called the Thousand-Blade Corridor. It is three hundred feet long, and the walls on both sides are embedded with thirty-six thousand rotating blade wheels. The blades rotate once every thirty breaths. When they spin, the entire corridor is engulfed in blade light. We only need to rush through during the intervals when the blades stop."

Song Taosheng’s heart tightened as he raised a question: "Why didn't I encounter this the last time I came?"

He was worried that their identities and motives had been exposed, and that the guard was lying to lure them into a trap.

The guard replied, "When you came last time, this section hadn't been calibrated yet."

"The Cloud Prison doesn't just have guards and formations; there are mechanical traps at every vital point. These traps must be recalibrated after every major overhaul. The path I’m taking you on has the fewest traps - only three. Rest assured, as long as you do as I say, nothing will go wrong."

Song Taosheng nodded slightly. "Fine."

He realized that if the other party truly intended to trick them, there would be no need to explain the trap beforehand.

He and the five death-sworn soldiers stood silently at the entrance of the corridor, waiting patiently.

The walls vibrated slightly, and then dense cracks appeared across the surface. In the next moment, countless blade wheels spun out at high speed!

Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh...

In an instant, the blades whipped up a whistling wind, and deadly blade light filled the entire corridor. The light reflected off the faces of Song Taosheng’s group, turning them pale white.

Even though these cultivators were prepared to die, their hearts trembled at the sight of this terrifying blade light. Any expert could see that the power was horrific; even a Peak Golden Core combatant would struggle to last three breaths within it.

The light dissipated, and the blade wheels snapped back into the cracks. The fissures in the walls sealed up quickly, looking as if they had never existed.

"Run," the guard prompted.

Before the words had even fully left his mouth, he took off. Song Taosheng and the others followed close behind.

Before long, the group passed through the corridor unscathed. After the Thousand-Blade Corridor, they reached the Rolling Stone Passage.

Several burly men with fierce expressions and bulging muscles - all Golden Core-level body cultivators - were moving rolling stones. The stones were only the size of a millstone, but they were covered in talismans.

The guard explained: "These profound iron rolling stones each weigh ten thousand catties. Right now, they are compressed by the talismans. Once the traps are activated, they will return to their normal size. The walls, floor, and ceiling are all lined with talismans used for reversing the five senses, spatial shifting, suppression, slowing, and soul-snatching. We will wait for the stones to pass before we move."

In this manner, the group crossed the Rolling Stone Passage.

Song Taosheng secretly let out a sigh of relief. He hadn't expected the journey alone to be so fraught with obstacles.

"But fortunately, everything is still going smoothly!"

-----

Huazhang Kingdom. Ancestral Temple.

Han Song had finished his recitation. He found that he could not sit down; he could only stand in place, holding the white silk in his hands. The others were in the same state.

Han Song was inwardly terrified: The opponent is truly powerful! Our side struck with the National Luck, yet they have such resistance. Under the entanglement of destiny, every cultivator present is affected. We must go all out to grind them down!

Zheng Jing held his Jade Ruyi, waving it continuously. The jade beads atop the scepter lit up periodically, firing beams of green light that converged into the nine-colored pillar.

The nine-colored pillar had two sources: the National Treasure Golden Chapter Jade Records and the Monarch of Huazhang himself.

The Monarch’s face was turning pale, and his hands were trembling slightly. His speed in forming seals had slowed significantly.

Wang Shu gave a low shout, pushing his spiritual energy to the limit, causing the green smoke in the incense burner to billow. The smoke formed the shape of a dragon, coiling around the Monarch.

Han Song began to recite the sacrificial text again. His literary qi was being consumed at a staggering rate, and blood began to seep from the corners of his mouth.

Under their combined efforts, Huazhang’s National Luck expanded once more, forming an even greater power that struck out through the void!

-----

Cloud Canopy Peak.

Lu Zhanshu of Soaring Peak had also joined the effort. Dong Chen and the others were straining with all their might.

Suddenly, the pressure surged.

The seven-colored radiance on the Heaven-Sustaining Cloud Canopy flickered into darkness, then suddenly flared with blinding brightness. With each change, terrifying waves of heat and cold surged out alternately, scorching the surrounding cultivators before freezing them to the bone in the next heartbeat.

Snap.

A soft sound.

Dong Chen’s pupils shrank violently. He saw one of the main cloud ribbons of the Heaven-Sustaining Cloud Canopy snap, dissolving into specks of golden light. The main pendant at the end of the ribbon fell, slamming onto the altar with a metallic clang.

The vibration of the eighty-one small pendants reached an unprecedented level at that moment. The resulting cacophony slammed into the cultivators present, causing them to bleed from their seven orifices.

Dong Chen was shocked: We’ve gathered so many high-level members, yet we’re still being pummeled. Just who is the opponent? How can they draw forth such a gargantuan power of destiny?

Left with no choice, Dong Chen knew he had to mobilize other luck-stabilizing treasures. He couldn't pull any more high-level members away from their posts.

Luck-based duels were extremely dangerous. Given the current dire situation, a defeat would inevitably lead to a backlash - not only would their bodies and souls be injured, but their personal luck would plummet. The high-level members of the Myriad Manifestations Sect were tied too closely to the sect; if they fell, the sect’s luck would suffer horrific damage.

Fortunately, the Myriad Manifestations Sect had no shortage of luck-stabilizing treasures. There were three others from which he could draw power!

Raise the stakes!

Dong Chen was decisive. Since he had to act, he directly mobilized two of them.

In this invisible gamble within the unseen realms, the Myriad Manifestations Sect finally gained the upper hand for the first time!

-----

Huazhang Kingdom Ancestral Temple.

The entire main hall suddenly shuddered violently.

Han Song’s pupils constricted. To his horror, he saw the white silk containing the sacrificial text ignite spontaneously. The characters of the text were being rapidly destroyed by the flames.

Han Song rushed to save the situation, pushing his spiritual energy and Confucian techniques to the limit. He fought until his literary qi was overdrawn and his mind exhausted, but he could only slow down the terrible process.

Crack, crack...

A series of sharp snaps rang out as cracks appeared on Zheng Jing’s Jade Ruyi, spreading rapidly. The glow on the jade beads dimmed, and they were no longer able to bolster the nine-colored pillar.

The body of the incense burner had become scorching hot. One of the three incense sticks snapped in half, and the dragon formed by the smoke became intermittent and broken.

The Monarch of Huazhang’s face was as white as paper. His hands shook so much he could barely hold his seals, and his eyes were bloodshot. For the moment, he could only barely hold on.

The Golden Chapter Jade Records shuddered violently. Its sixty-two jade pages flipped manically, the text on every page glowing as if it were burning!

"The existence that threatens our kingdom's future already possesses such gargantuan destiny in the present?!" The Monarch’s heart was in turmoil. "Should I have conducted a public National Ritual?"

A sense of regret rose within him.

The situation had completely exceeded his expectations. This was a National Ritual - even if it wasn't public, it mobilized the National Luck of the mighty Huazhang Kingdom. And yet, the opponent was suppressing that luck!

Looking across the world, most cultivation nations couldn't achieve this. Huazhang was a powerful nation and a sacred land for Confucian cultivators.

During the clash, the Monarch sensed something with certainty: the opponent was not a nation!

-----

Cloud Prison. Ninth Floor.

Song Taosheng and the others stood before a cell door, looking at Qin De inside.

Qin De sat cross-legged, leaning against the corner of the wall. His hair was disheveled, and his eyes were tightly shut.

The leading guard stepped back a few paces, giving them space while he watched from a distance.

Song Taosheng stared at Qin De and slowly let out a breath. "Qin De, I’ve come to kill you."

His voice was incredibly calm.

The distant guard froze, doubting his own ears. What did Song Taosheng just say?

Qin De snapped his eyes open, only to see Song Taosheng take out a magic treasure.

BOOM!

In the next moment, an explosion occurred. The terrifying force was highly concentrated. Though the cell door remained and absorbed most of the power, a fraction leaked inside like a series of heavy hammers. It pulverized Qin De’s body, crushing his bones into dust until he was nothing more than a puddle of meat!

"Success!" Song Taosheng whispered, unable to suppress his excitement.

His painstaking plan, his assassination attempt carried out with a death-wish, had succeeded!

Song Taosheng and the others didn't doubt the fact that Qin De had been crushed without any resistance. This was the Cloud Prison; all prisoners were heavily suppressed and restricted.

"Go!"

"Go, quickly!!" The five death-sworn soldiers surrounded Song Taosheng. One of them grabbed him and turned to run.

Before the mission, the five had been instructed: if the assassination went smoothly and the target was killed in one strike, they were to do everything in their power to bring Song Taosheng back. A living Song Taosheng was far more valuable than a dead one!

By this time, the guard finally reacted.

"Song Taosheng! Where do you think you’re going?!"

"Assassins! Assassins!"

"Enemy attack! Sound the alarm! Activate the formations fully!"

Of the five death-sworn soldiers, three pounced forward like hungry tigers. There were only two guards present; they were outnumbered and forced to retreat as they fought.

Song Taosheng was flanked by the remaining two soldiers as they made their emergency escape. After running only a hundred paces, more guards intercepted them. Song Taosheng’s side was immediately at a numerical disadvantage.

"It’s time!"

"Witness my Heavenly Demon Disintegration!"

One soldier resolutely activated a forbidden technique, blowing himself up and killing over a dozen guards on the spot.

The other two soldiers cast techniques and threw out specialized magic treasures, merging all the corpses of the fallen cultivators into a single Flesh-and-Blood Monster.

The monster rampaged through the area. Several guards who couldn't escape in time were sucked into it, their souls suppressed as they became hostages within the beast.

As the number of hostages grew, the guards became hesitant, fearing that a full-scale attack would kill their own comrades.

"Why hasn't the formation activated yet?!" someone shouted.

"They scattered a large number of high-grade formation-breaking talismans and tools along the way! If we force the formation to activate, it will cause a massive internal collapse!"

"We have to wait!"

"Dammit!"

Despite having more people, the guards were forced to retreat continuously. Song Taosheng and his men used the Flesh-and-Blood Monster to charge ahead, forcing a path open. Just as they were about to break out of the ninth floor, a Golden Core cultivator blocked their path.

He blew a breath of air, whipping up a fierce cold current that instantly froze the Flesh-and-Blood Monster.

A Peak Golden Core combatant!

"You guys go first!" A second death-sworn soldier stepped out from the group, activating a forbidden technique to entangle the powerful expert.

The ruthless fear the mad, and the mad fear those who don't care for their lives. Faced with an opponent who completely disregarded his own life in a frantic assault, the guard expert could only retreat.

Song Taosheng’s group broke out of the ninth floor and entered the eighth.

Boom!

The entire space suddenly shuddered.

The scene before them began to twist, blur, and shatter, like a painting being torn to shreds.

Three breaths later, Song Taosheng and his group found themselves standing in a completely foreign place.

Above them was an endless starry sky. Countless stars twinkled, some the size of a fist, some as large as a head, and others as big as millstones. These stars rotated slowly, casting down a blue-silver light that illuminated everything around them.
