Chapter 841: The Second Scripture Debate

Qin De was a grave criminal; naturally, Wang Yu could kill him.

At the very least, once Qin De died, it would be considered a victory for the School of the Mind over the Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief, even if the success wasn't total. This outcome would still serve to curb the Confucian cultivator community to a certain extent.

Qin De certainly had nothing to do with the payment for the medicinal pills, but as long as he died, wouldn't fabricating "evidence" be a simple matter for Wang Yu?

Zhong Dao had seen through this, which was why he had mocked Wang Yu earlier, saying "death provides verification."

Wang Yu reiterated his point: "Therefore, this matter concerns my Myriad Manifestations Sect. However, whether it concerns the Demon Subjugation Hall is for you, Hall Master, to decide with a single word."

Without Zhong Dao's approval, Wang Yu would not make a move against Qin De.

Neither Wang Yu nor Dong Chen and the others would do something like sneaking in and slaughtering Qin De, as Xiao Juxia might have done.

That was not the way of the Righteous Path.

Once such a thing was done, the hidden costs would be enormous!

According to the rules, the higher-ups of the Myriad Manifestations Sect had to first obtain Zhong Dao's support.

Before coming here, Wang Yu had already decided on his strategy. However, facing the sect's greater good that Wang Yu presented, Zhong Dao remained firm in his stance.

"The matter of Qin De has always been adjudicated by sect rules. When the judgment was made years ago, everyone conferred and discussed the outcome - although he created the Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief and committed acts of theft, the comprehensive evaluation was that his crimes did not warrant death, resulting in life imprisonment."

"Back then, you all wanted to use Qin De to constrain the development of Confucian cultivators, so there was a bias. I did not object because, according to the sect rules at the time, such a verdict was indeed permissible."

"Qin De has been detained until now and has never accumulated further crimes. What reason do you have to execute him? Just for the sake of the sect, you want to corrupt the sect's laws?"

"Do you not know that destroying the rules is the greatest loss of all!"

Wang Yu sighed softly, his whisk sweeping from his left shoulder to his right. "Since that is the case, this Wang will disturb you no longer."

"However, before I leave, I have one last thing to say - Hall Master Zhong, you are upright and hateful of evil, and I respect you for that. I also know that you and Duanmu Zhang are of compatible temperaments and are close friends. I only hope that you place the sect's overall situation above personal friendship."

After saying this, his figure vanished from the spot like dispersing rosy clouds.

Zhong Dao let out a cold snort, and under the lamplight, buried his head in the case files once more.

At the break of dawn.

Duanmu Zhang pushed open the door.

The door was made of pine, ordinary and unremarkable. As he pushed it open, it let out a soft creak, which sounded exceptionally clear in the quiet dawn.

Duanmu Zhang stood on the threshold, not stepping out immediately.

The mountain breeze brushed against his face, carrying the unique dampness and chill of the early morning. He took a deep breath; the air was cool, traveling from his nasal cavity straight into his lungs, seeming to disperse the helplessness and weariness that had accumulated deep in his heart for years.

Duanmu Zhang mastered the Five Classics at seventy, became a Great Confucian at one hundred yet continued to study behind closed doors, became famous throughout the Huazhang Kingdom at two hundred, and was invited to join the Myriad Manifestations Sect at three hundred. In his life, he had weathered countless storms and had long since reached the state where he would not blink even if Mount Tai collapsed before him.

But today, at this moment, his mind was unsettled.

Simply because he knew that today was a monumental day for the Confucian cultivator community!

Duanmu Zhang gazed into the distance; the horizon was a dark curtain.

Buried there was his past.

Years ago, he received orders to leave the country to propagate Confucianism and develop Confucian cultivators, arriving in the Flying Cloud Kingdom.

Originally, he intended to enter officialdom in the Flying Cloud Kingdom, but he was persuaded by the previous Sect Master of the Myriad Manifestations Sect and ultimately joined the sect to develop the Confucian path.

At first, relying on his own scholarship, Duanmu Zhang unfolded grand plans and advanced by leaps and bounds.

However, the good times did not last. The previous Sect Master died under a Heavenly Tribulation, and the Azure Duckweed Kingdom, along with many other small cultivation nations, underwent complete reformation because of Confucian cultivators. This caused a massive sensation and a monstrous wave of public opinion worldwide.

The attitude and policies of the Myriad Manifestations Sect's high-level leadership toward Confucian cultivators changed drastically as a result.

Originally, even so, they wouldn't have been suppressed so ruthlessly.

But alas, Duanmu Zhang's luck was poor. Within the Confucian cultivator community, a great traitor like Qin De emerged.

The Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief he created was truly deviant and rebellious, practically digging up the roots of the faith. Seeing the opportunity, the Myriad Manifestations Sect's higher-ups immediately took action, preserving Qin De's life and imprisoning him.

From then on, Qin De and the Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief became a mountain suppressing the development of the Confucian cultivator community, preventing them from advancing an inch for many years!

As Duanmu Zhang was lost in his memories, a faint fish-belly white floated up on the horizon.

That white was extremely faint, extremely soft, as if someone had gently brushed the finest brush across rice paper. Beneath the white was a deep ink blue - the fading colors of the night.

Then, very quickly, that touch of white began to change.

First, a faint crimson rose at the edge of the white.

Beneath the red was orange.

That orange was warm and full, like a ripe persimmon, or like honey fresh from the hive.

Then came the gold.

Once it arrived, the crimson and orange became its accompaniment. The golden light burst forth from the gaps in the cloud layers, ray by ray, like the sharp swords of a celestial god, shattering the remaining night into pieces.

Finally, the sun poked its head out.

Just a dot, just a sliver. But it was this dot and this sliver that brought the entire world to life. The mountains were no longer dark silhouettes but had layers, textures, and life. The trees were no longer pitch-black outlines but had color - ink-green pines, verdant bamboo, and tender yellow new leaves.

Duanmu Zhang watched all this quietly, his eyes slightly reddening.

He suddenly realized he hadn't watched a sunrise like this in many years.

"So today, all of this will be resolved," Duanmu Zhang thought to himself.

Behind him, on the desk in the room, lay a flying sword message.

The message came from Zhao Hansheng last night. It was short, containing only one sentence: "Brother Duanmu, rest assured. Today, we shall accomplish complete success in one battle, utterly defeating Qin De and sweeping away the obstacles!"

Duanmu Zhang knew of yesterday's debate situation and Qin De's final sophistry, so he had abundant confidence in Zhao Hansheng.

Zhao Hansheng rode the clouds, gazing toward the Cloud Prison.

This time, the group was even larger.

Not only Gu Qing, but also Chu Xuangui, Song Taosheng, Situ Gu, and others were present.

The sun had already risen, its golden light illuminating the nearby peaks. The clouds and mist surged beneath their feet like a sea or a tide. The distant mountains layered upon one another, stretching all the way to the horizon.

"What a magnificent atmosphere!" Zhao Hansheng sighed, filled with confidence for the upcoming debate victory.

In fact, on his way back halfway through the journey, he had already figured it out - that was merely Qin De's sophistry, his final reluctance, a deathbed struggle!

Qin De had said at the end: "Conscience knows right and knows wrong; however, the one who knows right and wrong - is that not the knowledge that theft is possible?"

He had proposed a concept that sounded terrifying - "Conscience is the Thieving Heart"!

Zhao Hansheng's elaboration was: When you want to steal something, the function of Conscience is to know right and wrong. It knows that "stealing" is "wrong," and thus it completes its task. It makes you feel shame and stops you from stealing.

Qin De, however, expressed: "Look, your Conscience knows 'stealing' is wrong. But the reason it knows 'stealing' is wrong is because it first knows that 'stealing' is a thing that 'can be done.' If it didn't know the existence of the act of 'stealing' at all, how could it judge whether this act is right or wrong?"

Since it knows stealing is a thing that can be done, that is "Knowing Theft is Possible," which means "Conscience is the Thieving Heart."

Zhao Hansheng couldn't refute it at the time.

Zhao Hansheng focused on the result: The result of Conscience is to stop people from doing evil.

Qin De focused on the premise: For Conscience to stop evil, it must first "recognize" evil.

Now, Zhao Hansheng had thought it through clearly. Qin De had dug a very deep pit on the logical level: "Knowing Good" and "Knowing Evil" were actually two sides of the same ability. While Conscience tells you "what is good," it inevitably tells you "what is evil," and this "recognition of evil" was the subtle "Thieving Heart" Qin De spoke of.

Zhao Hansheng stood with his hands behind his back on the flying cloud, calm and composed.

"Conscience knows right and knows wrong; this is knowing the distinction between good and evil, not knowing that theft is permissible to do. It is like a bright mirror reflecting objects, reflecting beauty and ugliness. Yet, the mirror does not feel joy for beauty, nor anger for ugliness. Conscience is the same; it reflects good and evil, but Conscience itself does not drift along with good and evil."

"Qin De equating 'Knowing Good and Evil' with 'Knowing Theft is Possible' is merely stealing concepts and swapping definitions."

"Sigh, I was actually close to figuring it out back then; why was I so anxious and impatient?"

"Today, I will utterly crush Qin De!"

Carrying matchless confidence, Zhao Hansheng and the others arrived at the Cloud Prison.

Because they had sent a flying message in advance, the cultivator on duty was already waiting at the main gate and immediately welcomed the group of Confucian cultivators into the prison cells.

Deep within the prison, Zhao Hansheng saw Qin De once again.

Today's Qin De, though still disheveled with messy hair, seemed radiant with spirit, his eyes shining with a strange light.

It felt as if there had been a transformation.

Zhao Hansheng frowned slightly but didn't think much of it.

The debate began.

Zhao Hansheng struck first: "Three days ago, you said 'Conscience knowing right and wrong implies knowing theft is possible.' Today I tell you, this is swapping concepts. Conscience knowing good and evil is like a bright mirror reflecting objects. The mirror reflects beauty and ugliness, but the mirror does not become ugly; Conscience knows evil, but Conscience does not become evil. Knowing that theft can be done but not doing it, versus knowing that theft can be done equating to a Thieving Heart - how can these be confused as one?"

Qin De smiled.

For some reason, seeing this smile, Zhao Hansheng felt an inexplicable chill in his heart.

"Mr. Zhao speaks correctly," Qin De said unhurriedly. "The mirror reflects beauty and ugliness; the mirror does not become ugly. However, I ask you, sir - the mirror can reflect beauty and ugliness, and the mirror can also reflect ugliness and beauty. The reflection of the mirror has no inherent good or evil. But when a person holds the mirror to reflect things, they can choose to reflect beauty, or they can choose to reflect ugliness. This is not the fault of the mirror, but the fault of the person."

Zhao Hansheng frowned. "What exactly are you trying to say?"

A strange light flashed in Qin De's eyes. "I want to say - Conscience is the same. Conscience has no inherent good or evil. Yet when people use Conscience, they can do good, or they can do evil. The Sage uses Conscience to do good, thus becoming a Sage; the Great Bandit uses Conscience to do evil, thus becoming a Bandit. Conscience is neither Sage nor Bandit; the user is the person."

Everyone's hearts trembled in unison.

This was because Qin De's words were completely different from the first debate. This was not the logic of Confucianism, but - the logic of the Demonic Path!

Qin De continued: "The Heart is Principle (Li), but the Heart also harbors Desire (Yu). Principle and Desire are originally one body; why force them apart? The Sages say 'Eliminate Desire to Preserve Principle,' but if Desire can be eliminated, how can Principle survive? It is like water and fire; if water extinguishes fire, water remains; if fire evaporates water, fire remains. But within water there is fire, and within fire there is water; when have they ever been severed?"

Zhao Hansheng: ?!

Wearing shackles and dragging chains, Qin De walked step by step toward Zhao Hansheng, his voice growing louder and louder: "Mr. Zhao, you attack me with the School of the Mind, so I answer you with the School of the Mind. You say the Heart is Principle, I say the Heart is Desire. You say Extend Innate Knowledge, I say Extend Desirous Heart. You say the Unity of Knowledge and Action, I say the Unity of Desire and Action - where desire moves, that is action; why wait for Principle?"

What Qin De spoke was not Confucian principle, yet every sentence used the rhetoric of Confucian principle. He cited the framework of Confucianism but filled it with the content of the Demonic Path. The same house, with a changed owner, became unrecognizable.

Zhao Hansheng was caught off guard!

He thought he had seen through Qin De's reality, but after just one night, how had this fellow changed so drastically?!

For a moment, Zhao Hansheng fell into silence.

Gu Qing also felt it was completely inconceivable. He had been well-groomed and educated; frowning deeply, he pointed at Qin De, his tone angry: "This is no longer Confucianism!"

Qin De raised his head, his eyes flashing with a look of hatred: "You all criticize my Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief as a heretical demon path! Fine, then let it be a demonic scripture, so what?"

Chu Xuangui, Song Taosheng, Situ Gu, and the others looked at each other in dismay.

Qin De's change was truly too great.

In the past, Qin De had always regarded himself as a Confucian cultivator and felt that the Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief was a Confucian classic. But now, he had discarded his identity as a Confucian cultivator and admitted to studying Demonic Philosophy.

Qin De laughed loudly, beginning to bluff: "Zhao Hansheng, did you really think victory was in your grasp?"

"During the first debate, I was merely letting you take the initiative."

"The Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief you saw was merely a version from long ago. Within this prison cell, contemplating day and night, you have yet to witness my true strength."

"You knew of the Scripture of the Sage and the Great Thief known to outsiders beforehand and prepared more thoroughly than I. Therefore, the first time, I showed weakness to the enemy to understand the principles of your School of the Mind."

Qin De paused, sighing with emotion: "The School of the Mind is indeed excellent, opening a new path, truly breathtaking!"

He pointed at Zhao Hansheng: "You attacked me with the School of the Mind, so I learned where the School of the Mind can be broken. You taught me with Conscience, so I learned where Conscience can be stolen. Mr. Zhao, thank you."

Zhao Hansheng felt as if he had been struck by lightning, his body and mind shaken violently.

He was a Great Confucian; gritting his teeth, his gaze became sharp as a knife. "Good, then let the two of us debate once more. I would like to properly experience your Demonic Philosophy!"

Zhao Hansheng attacked first: "Qin De, you just said 'Heart is Desire.' Then I ask you: Does Desire have constancy?"

"Today you desire this, tomorrow you desire that; if Desire has no constancy, then the Heart has no constancy. If the Heart has no constancy, can there be a consistent Dao? Without the Dao, there is chaos; with chaos, one cannot establish oneself in the world. Taking Desire as the Heart, how do you establish yourself?"

"Mr. Zhao asks well," Qin De said unhurriedly. "Although specific desires have no constancy, the inconsistency of desire is precisely the constancy of the Heart."

Zhao Hansheng frowned.

Qin De continued: "The human heart is like flowing water, ceasing neither day nor night. Yesterday's desire is not today's; today's desire is not tomorrow's. However, although the flowing water changes, its nature remains water; although desires change, their nature remains desire. Water has no constant shape but has a constant nature; desire has no constant thought but has the constant presence of desire. This is the constancy of Desire; how can you say there is no Dao?"

Zhao Hansheng was struck dumb.

Qin De spoke again: "You take Principle (Li) as constant. I ask you: Does Principle change? Confucius spoke of 'Benevolence' (Ren), Mencius spoke of 'Righteousness' (Yi), the Cheng-Zhu school spoke of 'Principle' (Li), and your School of the Mind speaks of 'Heart' (Xin). Within the same Confucian gate, Principle is ever-changing, so how much more so for Desire? If Principle can change but you say Desire cannot be accepted for changing, is that not a double standard?"

Zhao Hansheng's expression changed slightly.
