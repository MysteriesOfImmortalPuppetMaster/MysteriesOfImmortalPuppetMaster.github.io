Chapter 951: Chaotic Sounds

In the stands, the spectators were whispering among themselves, many of them enjoying the spectacle and not minding if things blew out of proportion.

"This is the third battle."

"How many factions from Flowing Cloud Peak are standing behind Liu Jinke? If he loses this battle too, he won't be the only one losing face."

"Is it just losing face? Precious materials, Medicinal Pills, Magic Artifacts, Divine Summoning... With so many resources piled onto him, if he still can't suppress a Mid Foundation Establishment Stage Cultivator, how will the major factions on Flowing Cloud Peak ever have a voice in the main Sect again?"

"Ning Zhuo," Liu Jinke spoke, his voice heavy.

"Today, I will let you know that a Golden Core is ultimately a Golden Core, and Foundation Establishment is ultimately just Foundation Establishment! Your mechanical birds, profound armors, fire Techniques, lute sounds... none of them can stop me from winning this battle."

As he spoke, he raised his right hand. A tuning fork flew out from his Storage Belt, landing in his palm and spinning rapidly.

The tines of the Chaotic String Fractured Sound Fork scraped through the air, emitting streams of ear-piercing, shattering noises.

The sound was thin and shrill, like someone snapping a taut string from on high. It made many Cultivators outside the arena frown and hastily circulate their spiritual energy to protect their ears.

"This time, you should be the one to lose your head!" Liu Jinke roared deeply, his imposing aura continuously climbing.

Ning Zhuo wasn't angered. Instead, he raised his hands and bowed formally to Liu Jinke.

The youth's white robes fluttered in the wind and clouds of the martial arena. The sound patterns on his Great Thousand Mechanism Melody Garment glowed with a faint Pure Light, making his features appear even more serene.

"Senior Brother has suffered many lessons yet is still willing to come back again; I, Ning Zhuo, am full of admiration."

He said this mildly, sounding like genuine praise. Liu Jinke immediately furrowed his brow.

Ning Zhuo continued, "I also know that Senior Brother and I had never met before. In the previous two battles, there was no life-and-death enmity between us; you were simply entrusted by others and pushed to the front. The reason I held back twice was not out of contempt for Senior Brother, but because I did not want this conflict to escalate further."

The voices in the stands suddenly lowered, clearly indicating that many people were pricking up their ears to listen.

Ning Zhuo did not raise his voice, but the Formation automatically projected his words extremely far.

The independent cultivators on the cloud bridge leaned forward, the Cultivators by the windows of Heavenly Treasure Pavilion set down their wine cups, and even the spectators on several flying boats hovering in the distance ceased their discussions.

"The various factions on Flowing Cloud Peak have operated for many years and naturally have deep foundations. My Nanming Stronghold was newly established; initiating a peak assault to found the stronghold was merely to seek a foothold within the rules of the Myriad Manifestations Sect. If, because of me alone, the flames of war are fanned larger and larger, making everyone feel endangered and causing endless internal friction on the peak, the ones suffering losses won't just be Nanming Stronghold or a single area on Flowing Cloud Peak. It will be the very vitality of our Myriad Manifestations Sect."

Ning Zhuo's gaze swept over the seating areas of the Thunder Cloud Association, Green Tea Society, Bird and Beast Manor, and Floating Life Association.

As these words fell, the expressions of many people in the Flowing Cloud Peak seating area instantly changed.

The purple thunder iron baoding balls in Lei Wangyue's hand were squeezed with a crisp crack.

Ye Qingming's teacup lid paused in mid-air; the light green tea in her cup reflected her eyes, revealing her icy gaze.

Jin Mantang's mouth twitched, and he almost cursed out loud.

What a Ning Zhuo!

These words were spoken too beautifully, and yet too ruthlessly.

It was clearly Ning Zhuo, a newcomer from the outside, who used the minor trial of the Naming Fire Furnace to forcefully pull up Nanming Stronghold, aiming to disrupt the old power dynamic of Flowing Cloud Peak and pry away their territories, spirit veins, businesses, and years of operations.

But after he put it that way, it sounded as though the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak were disregarding the Sect's grand scheme, ganging up to oppress a newcomer, engaging in habitual infighting, and harming public interests for private gain.

What was even more troublesome was that many people were more than willing to listen to this narrative.

Throughout the Sect, the one thing they never lacked was people looking for a good show.

The grander Ning Zhuo sounded, the more amused they felt. If Ning Zhuo lost, the masses would say: It's perfectly normal for a Mid Foundation Establishment Stage Cultivator to be defeated by a Golden Core cultivator backed and funded by numerous factions.

If Ning Zhuo won, then it wouldn't just be Liu Jinke who lost; it would be a massive, humiliating defeat for the various factions of Flowing Cloud Peak at the hands of Ning Zhuo.

The old woman with the golden hairpin tightened her withered fingers around her cane, letting out a cold snort from her throat, "This youth is so wicked he's oozing pus."

Wen Suzhen did not refute her. He looked at Ning Zhuo in the arena, the medicine bottle in his sleeve clicking softly as he flicked it with his fingertips.

The golden eagle behind Zhao Ni ruffled its feathers, seemingly affected by the shifting qi dynamics in the arena. Zhao Ni cursed under his breath, "He's got a big head, and it's filled entirely with schemes."

The gazes of the many Cultivators of Flowing Cloud Peak unanimously fell on Liu Jinke.

Hurry up and refute him!

Rebut him with facts and logic!

But Liu Jinke came from a rogue cultivator background. Spending half his life engaging in duels, snatching resources, cultivating, and fleeing for his life, how could he possibly have the eloquence to sway public opinion in front of thousands?

He only felt that every word Ning Zhuo spoke clogged his chest, as if he were once again placed on a chessboard, having someone point at his nose and say, "You are nothing but a tool."

He felt belittled, feeling as though under Ning Zhuo's rhetoric, he had become utterly insignificant!

Thus, the raging fire in his heart overwhelmed everything else.

"Cut the crap!" Liu Jinke roared furiously, launching himself off the ground and charging at Ning Zhuo, his feet stomping spiderweb-like cracks into the cloud-stone floor.

Seeing this scene, the Cultivators of Flowing Cloud Peak almost blacked out from exasperation.

Lei Wangyue closed his eyes, then opened them again, his face ashen.

Ye Qingming placed her teacup back on the table without a word. Wen Suzhen only sighed inwardly: Liu Jinke has already fallen into Ning Zhuo's calculations.

But the battle would not stop just because of their frustration.

Liu Jinke transformed into a streak of golden light, fiercely pouncing toward Ning Zhuo.

This time, Ning Zhuo did not choose to use Wood Element Techniques to stall.

The second battle had already proven: ordinary vines and forest-wood Techniques were largely meaningless against the current Liu Jinke. This Golden Core cultivator closed the distance too quickly, and his golden blood could break through any restraints. If the opening was wasted on Wood Element stalling tactics, Liu Jinke would instantly seize control of the tempo.

Ning Zhuo flicked his sleeves, and a flock of mechanical birds surged out like a dark cloud, rapidly spreading across the midair.

Neck-Spear Cranes, Sleeve-Thorn Harriers, Joint-Clasping Pheasants, Headwind Kites, and Gold-Thread Caged Sparrows!

The five types of mechanical flying birds divided into a three-layered formation and swooped down on Liu Jinke.

For a time, bamboo frames hummed softly, feathers sliced through the wind, and gears and springs bounced continuously within their bellies. The dense sounds wove together to form a massive curtain of mechanical rain.

The new birds that Qing Huangzi had rushed to construct day and night had all been brought along by Ning Zhuo.

Their forms were more refined, their frames more resilient, and the talisman circuits within their bellies were much smoother than in the previous two battles. As soon as they appeared, they made the Cultivators who understood mechanisms twitch their eyelids in shock.

"Is Ning Zhuo planning a war of attrition?" Shen Xi watched the arena, twirling a jade pendant in his palm. "His mechanical flying birds are far more numerous than last time, and of better quality too."

Lin Jinglong narrowed his eyes. "If we're just looking at his inventory, he certainly shouldn't fear any losses."

In the seating area of Flowing Cloud Peak, many who knew the inside story favored Liu Jinke even more.

"A war of attrition? Haha, we aren't afraid of that in the slightest!"

"No matter how many mechanical birds he has, they're just external objects. Liu Jinke currently possesses a body full of Golden Blood Core Beads; his endurance proudly looks down on any Golden Core in the world."

Liu Jinke raised his hand.

Golden blood erupted from his palm, instantly transforming into thousands of fine needles.

The golden needles pierced the air, shooting diagonally like a torrential downpour.

Every single needle carried a blood-gold tail light, so dense that they nearly turned the air into a sieve.

The Neck-Spear Cranes were the first to charge into the rain of needles. Several long-necked mechanisms were pierced through on the spot. Crane necks snapped, bamboo frames and iron axles exploded, and parts plummeted to the ground.

The Sleeve-Thorn Harriers swooped low to the ground, but their paths were sealed by the golden needles, forcing them to flip and evade.

Before the Joint-Clasping Pheasants could even burrow underground, they were forced into continuous hops by the needle rain, the sound of popping springs inside their bellies a chaotic mess.

Clang, clang, clang, clang!

The golden needles struck the mechanical birds with sounds like driving rain beating against copper, destroying them at a visible rate.

In the stands, quite a few Cultivators erupted into cheers. The vast majority of people were just there to enjoy the spectacle and weren't taking either Ning Zhuo's or Liu Jinke's side.

Liu Jinke gaining the upper hand right at the start allowed many Cultivators on Flowing Cloud Peak's side to secretly breathe a sigh of relief. Some had eyes shining with excitement, silently cheering him on: "Just keep blasting and smashing through like this, Liu Jinke!"

Ning Zhuo raised his hand, and several mechanical birds furiously charged, crashing into Liu Jinke.

Liu Jinke did not dodge. Covered in golden armor, he let the attacks hit his body amidst a chaotic clanging. The golden light on his surface sank slightly, and the mechanical flying birds were shattered into fine powder.

The Golden Blood Core Beads gave a gentle spin.

The spiritual depletion from transforming his golden blood into needles moments ago was rapidly replenished.

Ning Zhuo's expression remained unchanged. He felt no heartache over the destroyed mechanical birds. The mechanical bird flock was meant to be part of a war of attrition. Qing Huangzi's day-and-night refinement was precisely to give him enough capital to trade for time, intelligence, and openings on the battlefield.

Seeing this, the people of Flowing Cloud Peak grew even more confident.

The beaded bracelet in Jin Mantang's hand resumed its spinning, his face radiating the gleam of a merchant calculating accounts. "If they keep wearing each other down like this, Ning Zhuo's mechanical birds will be the first to give out."

Wen Suzhen stared at the faintly circulating qi dynamics around Liu Jinke's chest and abdomen. "The Golden Blood Core Beads after attaining quasi-Divine Art status grant endurance far beyond the common sense of a Golden Core. In terms of a war of attrition alone, Liu Jinke is now approaching the quasi-Nascent Soul level!"

But contrary to their expectations, Liu Jinke did not choose to slowly wear him down.

He flipped his right hand, and the Chaotic String Fractured Sound Fork burst with brilliant light.

This trident-like weapon had a bizarre appearance. Entirely dark gold, its shaft wasn't perfectly straight, but bore slight contortions, resembling a segment of golden bone that had been twisted and then forcibly set.

The outer edges of the tines were covered in dense sound runes. The patterns looked like snapped strings, cracked porcelain, or shattered gold. Usually utterly silent, they now layered with light when provoked by the golden blood, projecting a trace of restless, fierce crimson radiance from within the dark gold luster.

Liu Jinke charged into the flock of birds, sweeping his fork horizontally.

Zheng-

A fractured sound exploded.

The noise was so ear-piercing that it didn't sound like weapons clashing at all. Instead, it sounded like the strings of dozens of guqins snapping simultaneously, the shattered soundwaves rippling outward through the air layer by layer.

The formation of the mechanical bird flock instantly fell into disarray. Several Sleeve-Thorn Harriers, initially guided by Ning Zhuo's sound commands to cut diagonally at Liu Jinke's flank, had their feathers shaken so violently by the fractured sound that they crashed into one another.

A Headwind Kite hovered high in the midair; the runes on its wings flickered twice before it completely deviated from its original trajectory.

"He's using the Chaotic String Fractured Sound Fork this early?" Ye Qingming lowered her eyelashes, the tea vapor masking her expression.

Xu Duanchao stood completely still holding his saber, a cold glint flashing in his eyes. "He is afraid."

Liu Jinke himself might not want to admit it, or might not have even realized it, but the seasoned Cultivators watching the battle saw it clearly.

He was far too wary of Ning Zhuo.

This apprehension was hidden beneath his rage. The shadows left by the first two battles were too deep - so deep that despite clearly holding a massive advantage, he still refused to let Ning Zhuo comfortably establish his battle lines, desperately needing to prove himself!

Ning Zhuo, however, seemed utterly oblivious to Liu Jinke's impatience.

He lightly plucked his fingers, first casting the Empty Valley Echoing Art.

An incredibly thin sound command shot out from his sleeve, landing first inside the belly of the foremost Neck-Spear Crane. The Neck-Spear Crane's bamboo frame vibrated, emitting a clear, resounding echo from its sound holes. The mechanical birds of the same type behind it responded in turn, the echo transmitting layer by layer through bamboo frames, springs, and hollow cavities, like a wisp of mountain wind sweeping through an empty valley.

Though it had an effect, it was ultimately not polished enough.

A Joint-Clasping Pheasant on the left was half a beat too slow, its landing point skewing half a foot off during its low-altitude charge. Two Sleeve-Thorn Harriers in the rear received the command too hastily, flipping their wings prematurely and exposing a momentary opening. The Headwind Kites and Neck-Spear Cranes were of different models, so their resonant beats didn't match; upon forcefully receiving the echo, one wobbled in the air and nearly crashed into a volley of golden needles.

Liu Jinke immediately seized this flaw.

Blood-transforming golden needles fired once more, instantly pinning down and grounding three swaths of mechanical bird flocks.

The cloud-stone floor sparked wildly from the golden needle impacts, and bamboo frame fragments rolled across the ground in a cacophony of clatter.

Ning Zhuo executed the Bamboo Aperture Hidden Sound Art.

His hands constantly formed incantation seals, swiftly burying sound commands within the bellies of many mechanical birds.

As one Sleeve-Thorn Harrier was cornered by the Chaotic String Fractured Sound Fork, the bamboo aperture inside its belly suddenly released a short, soft chirp. The hidden sound was triggered, causing the Sleeve-Thorn Harrier to dodge with a diagonal swoop. An iron plate on its tail feather slashed back across Liu Jinke's shoulder, drawing a trail of golden blood.

A gasp had only just erupted from the stands when an error occurred on the other side.

A Neck-Spear Crane accidentally triggered a hidden sound. It wasn't supposed to retreat, yet it abruptly folded backward, crashing right under a Headwind Kite's wing. The two birds tangled in midair. Liu Jinke swept his fork, the fracturing sound bursting through the air like shattering jade, blasting both mechanisms apart simultaneously.

Many people began to snicker, seeing that Ning Zhuo was just experimenting with his mechanism arts. It was painfully obvious that he was completely unpracticed.

Wen Suzhen saw through it: "These are mechanism arts from the inheritance of the [Empty Valley Syllable Azure Mechanism Cylinder]. The time since Ning Zhuo obtained the inheritance is simply too short."

The golden-hairpinned old woman struck the ground with her cane. "To be able to employ these two arts in such a short amount of time is astonishing enough. But this is the battlefield of the third bout, and he is actually acting this arrogant!"

The old woman felt a surge of silent irritation. Ning Zhuo underestimating Liu Jinke was indirectly showing contempt for all the major factions of Flowing Cloud Peak!

On the martial arena, Liu Jinke had already seized a massive advantage.

The mechanical bird flock had its formation thrown into chaos by the Chaotic String Fractured Sound Fork and golden needles, suffering huge losses. Although Ning Zhuo's newly learned mechanism arts showed flashes of brilliance, they had yet to become a true pillar of support.

Ning Zhuo sat upright on the Forest-Piercing Azure Python Carriage, remaining perfectly calm and composed.

He took out a zither.

Resting the instrument across his knees, its mirror-smooth lacquered surface reflected the chaotic lights and shadows of the martial arena.

Ning Zhuo raised his fingers and struck the strings. The first note was as clear as a frozen spring, the second as coarse as tearing silk, and the third surprisingly forcefully wedged a bizarre, erratic off-beat between the first two notes.

Zheng!

Zheng-zheng!

Zheng-pa, qiang-si-qiang, zheng!

Hearing this, many Cultivators outside the arena wore looks of utter bewilderment.

The zither notes grew abruptly frenetic, like a white crane flapping its wings and charging into stormy clouds, or a coarse copper gong being struck by a heavy mallet deep within a valley.

Within the proper tones hid sheer noise, and within the noise was utter chaos.

This was the musical expertise left behind by the demonic soul of the Fractured Palace Wandering Entertainer. After Ning Zhuo absorbed it, he forcefully blended it into his mechanism deployment.

The Chaotic String Fractured Sound Fork detonated with its splitting sound once more.

Ning Zhuo's zither melody surged forth to meet it.

The two sounds tore at each other in midair. One side was snapping strings and shattering gold, while the other was a frenzied zither fracturing a valley.

The originally disrupted flock of mechanical birds surprisingly found their rhythm anew amid this ear-piercing cacophony. They no longer sought elegant unison; instead, like bamboo leaves in a chaotic gale, they rode the gaps in the soundwaves, darting left and right. Evading the densest clusters of golden needles, they continued to pester and entangle Liu Jinke.

Many Cultivators who understood music theory furrowed their brows deeply at this moment!

It sounded awful. It was far too horrendous to listen to.

It was simply a massive torture to the eardrums.

It was pure noise!

"How can there be such a horrifying zither melody in this world? Is this even something a human can play?!" Someone covered their ears, on the verge of going mad.
