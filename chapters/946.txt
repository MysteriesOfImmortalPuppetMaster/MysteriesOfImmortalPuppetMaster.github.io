Chapter 946: Healing the Red-Robed Guest

Ignoring the blackness gathering in his vision, Liu Jinke ruthlessly forced out ten Golden Blood Pill Beads and threw them all into the beast statue’s mouth.

In the center of the Altar, the beast statue opened its eyes.

Those eyes were not golden, but black-gold. Deep within its pupils, there was no warmth of sentience, only pure, extreme greed.

All the sacrificial offerings collapsed simultaneously.

The Great White Golden Essence lost its luster, the Cold Edge Refined Gold cracked into ashen chunks, and the Geng Metal Sand scattered like weathered yellow earth. Even the precious rings placed at the four corners by Jin Mantang shattered with a crack, breaking two of them.

The phantom of a giant maw slowly opened above the Altar.

Soundlessly.

Everyone had the illusion that an invisible giant beast between Heaven and Earth was swallowing all the gold in the world!

A ray of golden-black light flew out from the beast statue’s abdomen and submerged into Liu Jinke’s body.

Severe pain!

The black-gold radiance landed on his chest, where a divine crest resembling a beast's maw rapidly surfaced. The edges of the crest interlocked like golden teeth, while the center was a deep, dark abyss, as if leading into an insatiable beast’s belly.

Liu Jinke fell into a hallucination.

He found himself in a world of metal.

The mountains were golden mountains, the rivers were golden rivers, the clouds were made of shattered gold, and the ground was paved with swords, armor, copper coins, and broken artifacts. Countless streams of metal qi peeled away from everything in the world, turning into thin streams that converged into the belly of a boundlessly massive Gold-Swallowing Giant Beast.

The giant beast lay at the end of the world, its belly as large as a mountain, its mouth full of golden teeth.

It did not speak.

It merely lowered its eyes and glanced at Liu Jinke.

That single glance made all the golden blood in Liu Jinke’s body nearly solidify!

A thought fell into his mind: "The Divine Domain may be borrowed, with gold as the Sacrifice. Take the weapons of your enemies as my food."

Liu Jinke suddenly awoke.

He knelt before the Altar, panting heavily, his body drenched in sweat, as if he had just broken free from a nightmare.

The other Cultivators gathered around him, staring burningly at the divine crest on his chest.

It was a success!

The Gold-Swallowing Divine Domain had been borrowed!

"Congratulations, Fellow Daoist Liu Jinke."

"The Gold-Swallowing Beast God has favored you!"

"Once this Divine Domain is opened, any metallic object within it will have its metal qi slowly extracted. When the metal qi enters the domain, it first goes to the Beast God, and then a portion is fed back to you."

"If Ning Zhuo's Profound Armament Armor, mechanical flying birds, and mechanism accessories are still of the metal attribute, they will all be suppressed within this domain! The third battle is secured!"

The Gold-Swallowing Beast statue on the Altar closed its eyes, the golden-black light in its abdomen slowly retracting. The miraculous sight of it opening its eyes and looking down seemed like nothing but a dream.

The beast-maw divine crest on Liu Jinke’s chest remained, its lines interlocking like golden teeth, hidden beneath his flesh. With every breath, a faint golden light circulated within it.

The Cultivators felt their hearts heat up upon seeing this.

They had invested far too many resources in Liu Jinke. Elevating his Innate Talent to the level of a quasi-Divine Art, providing the Golden Network Garment, Blood Body Talisman, Fire Evasion Pill, and Chaotic String Sound-Splitting Fork - and now, summoning the Gold-Swallowing Beast God to bestow the Gold-Swallowing Divine Domain!

Almost everyone harbored immense confidence in Liu Jinke’s third battle against Ning Zhuo.

Lei Wangyue wore a solemn expression. He looked at Liu Jinke, lightning flickering in the depths of his eyes.

While it was true that Liu Jinke was vastly different from before, the impression Ning Zhuo had left on him could not be easily erased either.

Lei Wangyue suddenly said, "It’s still not secure enough."

As soon as these words were spoken, many Cultivators in the cloud cave frowned slightly.

Jin Mantang had just hosted the sacrificial ritual at great cost. He was currently so heartbroken over the expense that the fat on his face was quivering slightly. Hearing this, he immediately looked at him. "Fellow Daoist Lei, this still isn't enough?"

Lei Wangyue spoke slowly, "Just as we discussed earlier: Liu Jinke is the main blade, but we cannot rely on only this one blade."

"It is inappropriate to pin all our hopes on Liu Jinke alone."

The crowd fell silent.

Liu Jinke snorted coldly in displeasure.

The old woman with the golden hairpin leaned on her walking stick and asked hoarsely, "What thoughts does Fellow Daoist Lei have?"

Lei Wangyue said, "The fellow daoist from the Facing Death Sect?"

He looked toward the Cultivator from the Facing Death Sect in the room.

Lei Wangyue continued, "I have long heard the great name of the Three Valiants of the Xiang Sect. Triplets born from the same womb, all at the Golden Core-level. Their Innate Talent 'Live and Die Together' has already been cultivated into a Divine Art! Fellow Daoist Liu Jinke is ultimately only at the quasi-Divine Art level. If they strike, Ning Zhuo will undoubtedly face defeat."

Jin Mantang’s eyelids twitched. "The Three Valiants of the Xiang Sect? Those three brothers are not easy to hire."

Everyone’s gaze shifted to the Cultivator from the Facing Death Sect.

The one who had come this time was a deacon of the Facing Death Sect, named Yin Baiming.

This person wore long gray hemp clothes, his hair half-white and half-black, and his face pale as a corpse that hadn't seen sunlight for years. A string of bone tiles hung at his waist, each carved with a name. When the wind blew, the bone tiles collided without a crisp sound, instead emitting dull rattling noises, like teeth softly chattering.

Yin Baiming slightly raised his eyelids. "The Three Valiants are our Sect's trump card; they are never mobilized lightly. To hire them to strike is possible, but you must follow our Facing Death Sect’s rules and pay the full fee."

Lei Wangyue said, "State your price first."

Yin Baiming replied, "First, three hundred thousand mid-grade Spirit Stones just to deploy them."

"Second, three Golden Core-level magic treasures to enter the Facing Death Sect’s vault, regardless of the mission's success or failure."

"Third, if any of the Three Valiants die in battle, you must compensate with one Soul-Sustaining Golden Core, one Life-Returning Yin Mushroom, and one jar of Hundred-Year Dead Spring Water."

"Fourth, if the 'Live and Die Together' Divine Art is triggered, resulting in the three losing lifespan, their Golden Cores cracking, or their Divine Souls being damaged, the employers must provide additional compensation according to the injuries."

"Fifth, if the mission involves a Nascent Soul intervening, the price doubles."

Lei Wangyue’s eyebrows twitched.

The other Cultivators looked at each other, all feeling the sting of the steep cost.

Yin Baiming had long anticipated their reaction, and his expression remained calm. "The Facing Death Sect is not a charity. We sell lives, so we must charge the price of a life."

Lei Wangyue was silent for a moment before looking around and saying, "This matter does not solely concern my Thunder Cloud Association. Naming Stronghold threatens all the factions of Flowing Cloud Peak; everyone is involved. We can crowdfund this money."

Lei Wangyue continued, "The Facing Death Sect is also a faction of Flowing Cloud Peak. Your side should also make a show of support, no?"

Yin Baiming’s bone tiles rattled softly. He remained silent for a moment before saying, "The Facing Death Sect will also share the burden, taking an equal cut among us."

Seeing him agree, Lei Wangyue looked at the others present.

Jin Mantang was the first to sigh. "Everyone, this is not a small sum."

The old woman with the golden hairpin sneered. "If Liu Jinke is defeated again, what does this small price matter?"

Liu Jinke snorted coldly in displeasure.

Zhao Ni said in a deep voice, "Beast and Bird Villa can contribute a share. If Naming Stronghold isn't suppressed, we will have no peace in the future."

Ye Qingming gently picked up her tea and said softly, "Green Tea Society can also contribute a share. Naming Stronghold’s momentum is too great; they indeed need to be suppressed."

Wen Suzhen pondered for a moment. "Xuanhu Residence agrees."

Jin Mantang spread his fat hands. "Fusheng Society also accepts this. Who asked us to invest so much in Liu Jinke already? Having brought the business to this point, we must follow through."

The crowd voiced their agreement one by one.

Only the representative of the Territory Expansion Alliance, Qiu Lei, remained silent.

On the side of Baiyun Township, Old Man Wandering Cloud was also silent.

Lei Wangyue looked at the two. "You two Fellow Daoists?"

Qiu Lei's expression was steady, his voice slightly deep. "The Territory Expansion Alliance needs to discuss this further."

Old Man Wandering Cloud also said, "The same goes for Baiyun Township."

Lei Wangyue frowned. "You're still hesitating at a time like this?"

Qiu Lei glanced at him, then at Old Man Wandering Cloud. "It indeed requires some consideration."

Old Man Wandering Cloud said nothing more, only nodding slightly.

The two men's gazes met obscurely in mid-air.

The Cultivators present already understood in their hearts - the sudden exposure of the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar had sparked a hidden confrontation between the Territory Expansion Alliance and Baiyun Township.

Both factions desired this treasured land of feng shui.

The Territory Expansion Alliance had secretly managed it for many years, but the territory fundamentally belonged to Baiyun Township.

In truth, the Territory Expansion Alliance had made several attempts before, mostly beating around the bush to buy this territory, but none had succeeded.

The two sides had not yet formally fallen out, but they had already faintly begun to face off.

Both were mobilizing manpower, organizing account books, and gathering evidence, preparing to fight for a legitimate claim over the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar.

Under these circumstances, asking them to shell out money to crowdfund the hiring of the Three Valiants wasn't an issue of reluctance, but rather that their focus was clearly meant to be placed on the contest for the Sun-Supporting Yang-Locking Cloud Ascension Altar.

At a time like this, splitting their energy and financial resources to simultaneously deal with Naming Stronghold could lead to a deficit in strength during their future confrontation. If they lost the treasured land because of that, what would they do?

Ye Qingming couldn't help but softly remind them, "You two, infighting at this time will only benefit Naming Stronghold."

Wen Suzhen suddenly sighed. "These past few days, I have increasingly marveled at the methods and schemes of this junior, Ning Zhuo. The more I ponder them, the more my spine chills."

"He has bypassed taking Liu Jinke's life twice in a row. Such scheming and foresight are truly too terrifying."

"We have already invested once before, and now we are supporting Liu Jinke like this again. If we go on to hire the Three Valiants, how much total investment will that be?"

"We have consumed so much, but what has Ning Zhuo consumed?"

"We can't even be certain if Liu Jinke can win the third battle. In other words, our massive investment may not even bear fruit."

"And all of this is an overt scheme by Ning Zhuo that we have no choice but to accept."

Liu Jinke snorted coldly in displeasure.

The atmosphere in the room became even heavier because of Wen Suzhen's words.

Ye Qingming tilted her head back and let out a long sigh. "This is why Zhong Dao values Ning Zhuo so highly. To possess such methods at such a young age, how formidable will he be in the future?"

Jin Mantang added, "The key is that he also has exceptional Cultivation Innate Talent. His comprehension is too astonishing. Without even majoring in Confucian methods, he was able to forcefully secure first place in several Confucian minor trials."

Lei Wangyue: "This kind of person, as long as he's given a sliver of opportunity, he can stir up the winds and clouds. When the Naming Fire Furnace minor trial was announced, I never would have thought that our entire Flowing Cloud Peak would suffer such an impact because of it!"

Qiu Lei waved his hand. "Alright, Naming Stronghold is indeed a massive threat. I cannot make the final decision regarding the hiring of the Three Valiants, but I will report back and strive for it to the utmost."

Old Man Wandering Cloud said, "Baiyun Township is not ignoring the bigger picture. It's just that having previously funded Liu Jinke, combined with the need to repair the ferry crossing and compensate for the cloud path, our future expenses are truly terrifying."

"However, strictly from my personal perspective, I am also willing to follow up on the matter of the Three Valiants."

Seeing the two express their stances one after another, Lei Wangyue couldn't help but glance at Wen Suzhen, marveling that the latter truly lived up to his reputation for intellect. A few words had magnified the threat of Naming Stronghold in everyone's hearts, and the clever part was that he focused on Ning Zhuo alone, rather than the entire Naming Stronghold.

Because of this, it felt more real and struck deeper into people's hearts.

Lei Wangyue: "Since that is the case, we will quietly await good news from you two. It's best to finalize the matter of the Three Valiants as quickly as possible; it is better to be prepared. The longer time drags on, the more likely it is that Ning Zhuo will make a new move."

Liu Jinke snorted coldly in displeasure.

-----

Meanwhile.

Ning Zhuo’s thoughts raced like lightning. For a brief moment, he almost resorted to oppressive tactics to push the Red-Robed Guest into making a decision.

But when his gaze fell upon the smear of dark red blood on the back of the Red-Robed Guest’s hand, another layer of consideration arose in his heart.

The Red-Robed Guest was a Demonic Cultivator. His temperament was explosive, fierce, and untamed. He acted without restraint, and by taking the initiative to charge into the Formation, he had indeed built up prestige for Naming Stronghold.

This was a merit.

"Can I treat a meritorious subject like this?" Emotionally, Ning Zhuo was unwilling to do so.

Rationally, there was also another layer-

If Ning Zhuo took advantage of his injuries to pressure him today, the Red-Robed Guest would likely see right through it. Even if he agreed on the surface, he would harbor a grudge in his heart. Naming Stronghold's people were already divided in spirit. If a seed of discord was planted here, it might come back to bite them in the future.

Ning Zhuo slowly exhaled a breath. "Senior’s injuries are not light. Why not allow this junior to show his meager skills and heal Senior first?"

The Red-Robed Guest raised an eyebrow, a trace of surprise flashing in the depths of his eyes. "You, a mid-Foundation Establishment cultivator, are going to heal me?"

Ning Zhuo smiled. "Senior is perceptive. This junior’s cultivation is meager, so naturally, I cannot. However, the foundation of my clan might be able to."

Saying this, he patted his Storage Belt and released a humanoid mechanism.

Snow-Colored Maiden Hui!

She stood six feet three inches tall, built in the form of a woman. Her figure was slender, her curves graceful; snow-white porcelain covered her face, and ice silkworm silk served as her hair, hanging over her shoulders like black silk with strands of cold light. Her body was draped in three-colored enamel armor, where the crimson, blue, and white radiance flowed like a cold sun shining upon colored glaze - pure, stunning, and refined.

What was particularly strange was that she had six arms. Her primary arms hung at the front, while her secondary arms were tucked by her sides, as still as a jade carving.

With a stir of Ning Zhuo’s divine sense, the three pairs of jade arms suddenly stretched out, like a snow lotus blooming for the first time, or six icy moons rising simultaneously.

The Red-Robed Guest raised his eyebrows slightly.

With his breadth of experience, he had naturally seen many mechanisms. But one like the Snow-Colored Maiden Hui before him was rare - far too rare!

The Red-Robed Guest faintly felt that this mechanism was incredibly unique - it almost seemed alive.

"Is this your clan’s foundation? It does have some quality. Come, try your hand." After seeing the Snow-Colored Maiden Hui, the Red-Robed Guest changed his mind and allowed Ning Zhuo to treat him.

Snow-Colored Maiden Hui floated forward.

The tips of her toes did not touch the ground; instead, she hovered slightly as layers of Formation patterns lit up on her three-colored enamel armor. Beneath the colorful armor, an inner lining of seven-rainbow ice silkworm silk accelerated the flow of her spiritual energy. The bones and sinews forged from warm-cold jade essence linked her six arms into a unified whole, making her every movement a blend of hardness and softness, so delicate that it was almost completely silent.

She raised her six jade hands simultaneously.

Innate Talent - Ice Jade Hands.

A lustrous white light flowed across the fingertips of her six palms. Within that white light, a faint smear of vital jade-green was barely visible. A cool sensation wafted over through the air, yet it lacked any hint of deathly stillness. Instead, it was like the melting snow of early spring - hiding moisture within the chill, containing life within the cold.

The Red-Robed Guest’s expression immediately changed.

The white light, concealing life within the cold, formed a thick layer of ice over his body.

A moment later, the Red-Robed Guest’s body shuddered, directly shattering the ice blocks encasing him as he stood straight up.

He exhaled a mouthful of turbid breath, pleasantly surprised to discover that his internal injuries had been mostly healed. He could handle the rest on his own.

The Red-Robed Guest stared at the Snow-Colored Maiden Hui, then couldn't help but look at Ning Zhuo. "This mechanism... did you refine and forge it?"

Ning Zhuo replied, "It was obtained from my clan’s foundation, and this junior has subjected it to numerous repairs and remodelings."

These words were spoken humbly, yet they did not deny his involvement.

The Red-Robed Guest’s heart stirred.

After a moment of silence, the Red-Robed Guest finally said, "Kid, I owe you a favor."

Ning Zhuo cupped his hands. "Senior speaks too seriously. Naming Stronghold shares the same breath and branches. If Senior is injured and this junior can help, it is only natural to offer a hand."

The Red-Robed Guest sat back down unhurriedly, half-reclining as he said to Ning Zhuo, "Since that is the case, I will also give you an opportunity. It will just depend on whether you can grasp it."

Ning Zhuo was puzzled. "Please explain clearly, Senior."

The Red-Robed Guest did not answer. Instead, using his divine sense to control objects, he moved a broken stone lamp over.

The stone lamp was not large; it was entirely black-red, as if it had been soaked in endless blood and then baked dry by earth fire. The body of the lamp was covered in incomplete sacrificial runes. Some parts were already half-melted, and its edges were pitted, as if it had been dug out of an extremely ancient tomb.

Ning Zhuo's heart skipped a beat.

Inside the lamp, there was a tiny wisp of flame.

It was a Fire Seed!
